## California State Courts

### Superior Court Rules and Formatting Requirements

#### Document Size and Paper Requirements
- **Paper Size**: 8½ by 11 inches (mandatory for all filings)
- **Paper Quality**: For non-electronic filings, must be on opaque, unglazed paper, white or unbleached, standard quality not less than 20-pound weight
- **One-sided**: When papers are not filed electronically, only one side of each page may be used

#### Formatting Standards (California Rules of Court)
- **Governing Rule**: California Rules of Court Rule 2.100 sets forth critical requirements
- **Basic Paper Requirements**: CRC 2.103 (amended effective 1/1/17)
- **Margins**: Rule 2.107 - at least one inch on the left and 0.5 inches on the right
- **Font**: Proportionally spaced serif font (Century Schoolbook preferred)
- **Font Size**: 13-point font size
- **Line Spacing**: 1.5 line spacing
- **Paragraph Alignment**: Left-aligned paragraphs

#### Electronic Filing Requirements (Rule 8.74)
- **Format**: Text-searchable PDF format
- **Font**: Proportionally spaced serif font (Century Schoolbook preferred)
- **Font Size**: 13-point
- **Line Spacing**: 1.5
- **Margins**: 1.5 inches on left/right, 1 inch on top/bottom
- **Case Names**: Must be italicized or underscored
- **Page Size**: 8.5 x 11 inches
- **Maximum File Size**: 25 megabytes
- **Page Numbering**: Must start at 1 and be consecutive
- **Color**: Color components allowed, but no color cover
- **Bookmarks**: Electronic bookmarks required for headings, sections, exhibits
- **Hyperlinks**: Hyperlinks to legal authorities encouraged but optional

#### Cover Page Requirements
- Attorney name
- Mailing address
- Telephone number
- Fax number (if available)
- Email address (if available)
- California State Bar number

#### Electronic Signatures
- Permitted format: "/s/[NAME]" in place of physical signature (Rule 2.257)

### California Rules of Court Citation and Document Standards

#### Citation Format
- California Style Manual requirements apply
- Case names must be italicized or underscored in electronic documents

#### Document Standards
- Text-based PDFs preferred over image-based PDFs for superior searchability
- All exhibits must be submitted in files under 25 megabytes
- Sealed/confidential records must be filed separately

### Local Rules Variations Across Major Counties

#### General Structure
- Local Rules updated on January 1 and July 1 of every year
- County-specific variations supplement California Rules of Court
- If conflict exists between local and state rules, California Rules of Court apply

#### Los Angeles County
- Maintains specific local rules supplementing statewide requirements
- Regular updates following state schedule

#### San Francisco County
- **Current Effective Dates**: Local Rules effective July 1, 2025
- **Previous Versions Available**: January 1, 2025; July 1, 2024; January 1, 2024
- Regular bi-annual update schedule maintained

#### Orange County
- **Current Effective Date**: July 1, 2025
- **Active Rule Development**: Ongoing revisions through Rules and Forms Committee
- **Public Comment Periods**: Spring 2024, Fall 2024, Spring 2025
- Maintains comprehensive local rules system

### E-filing Requirements and Document Specifications

#### Technical Requirements
- Text-searchable PDF format mandatory
- Maximum file size: 25 megabytes per document
- Electronic bookmarks required for navigation
- Color documents permitted (except covers)

#### Compliance Importance
- Failure to comply can result in rejected filings
- Potential sanctions for non-compliance
- Impact on case outcomes possible

## New York State Courts

### CPLR Procedural Requirements

#### Recent Updates (2024-2025)
- **Effective Dates**: July 2024, February 2025, June 2025
- **Commercial Division Rules**: Section 202.70 (Rules 202.70.1 to 202.70.36)
- **Current Version**: Effective March 31, 2025

#### Commercial Division Rules

##### Technology and Referee Updates
- **Amendment Date**: February 14, 2024
- **New Rule 9-b**: "Referees" - allows appointment of referees with party consent
- **CPLR References**: 4301 and 4317(a) compliance required

##### Professional Standards
- High-stakes commercial practice requires highest caliber professionalism
- Failure to appear at scheduled dates can result in sanctions
- Proper preparation mandatory for all court appearances

### Supreme Court Formatting Standards

#### General Filing Requirements
- **Fax Filing**: Requires advance approval of assigned justice
- **E-mail Communication**: Permitted in cases not using Filing by Electronic Means System
- **Document Standards**: Must comply with section 202.5 requirements

#### Caption Requirements
- Specific format required for all pleadings
- Must identify all parties and case information clearly

### County-Specific Local Rules

#### Manhattan (New York County)
- Commercial Division rules apply (Section 202.70)
- Supreme Court formatting standards mandatory

#### Brooklyn (Kings County)
- Standard CPLR requirements apply
- Local variations may exist for specific divisions

### Electronic Filing Standards

#### Recent Administrative Orders
- **AO/151/25**: Effective July 7, 2025 - Supreme Court E-Filing Rules (202.5-bb Amended)
- **Summary Judgment Updates**: Elimination of word limits on evidentiary materials (effective July 7, 2025)
- **Statement Requirements**: Repeal of 22 NYCRR § 202.8-g eliminates Statements of Material Fact requirement

#### E-Filing Requirements
- Filing by Electronic Means System available
- Specific technical requirements apply
- Advance court approval may be required for certain filings

## Texas State Courts

### Texas Rules of Civil Procedure

#### Electronic Filing Mandate (2024-2025)
- **Mandatory Courts**: Electronic filing required where mandated
- **Exceptions**: Juvenile cases (Family Code Title 3), truancy cases (Family Code Title 3A)
- **Optional Courts**: Available but not required in some courts
- **Unrepresented Parties**: May electronically file but not required

#### Electronic Filing System Requirements
- **Platform**: eFileTexas.gov (official system)
- **Provider**: Must use certified electronic filing service provider
- **Oversight**: Office of Court Administration establishes standards

### District Court Formatting Requirements

#### Document Format Standards
- **Font Size**: Minimum 12-point type
- **Spacing**: Double-spaced documents required
- **Paper Size**: 8½ x 11 inches (when filed by paper)
- **Paper Quality**: White paper, plain written, typed, or printed
- **PDF Format**: Searchable PDF required for electronic filings
- **Restrictions**: No security settings that prohibit copying, highlighting, or commenting

#### Email Requirements
- Email address must be included on electronically filed documents
- Required for all attorneys using electronic filing

### Local Rules for Major Districts

#### Harris County (Houston)
- Standard Texas Rules of Civil Procedure apply
- District-specific variations may exist

#### Dallas County
- Electronic filing mandatory where implemented
- Standard formatting requirements apply

#### Travis County (Austin)
- State capital district requirements
- Standard TRCP compliance required

### Electronic Filing Standards

#### Document Restrictions
- **Prohibited from Electronic Filing**:
  - Documents filed under seal or in camera
  - Documents with access restrictions by law or court order
  - Wills (not required to be filed electronically)

#### Service Requirements
- **Filing Requirement**: Must be filed with clerk in writing
- **Content Requirements**: Must state grounds, relief sought
- **Service**: True copy must be served on all parties simultaneously
- **Docket Notation**: Must be noted on docket

#### Recent Rule Updates
- **Rule 21**: Amended by order of January 27, 2023, effective February 1, 2023
- Current formatting standards in effect for 2024-2025

## Florida State Courts

### Florida Rules of Civil Procedure

#### Recent Updates (2024-2025)
- **Effective Date**: January 1, 2025
- **New and Amended Rules**: Comprehensive framework implemented
- **Technology Standards**: Florida Courts Technology Standards provide guidance

#### Document Formatting Requirements

##### Paper Standards
- **Recycled Paper**: Required for all original papers (Fla. R. Jud. A. 2.520(a))
- **Paper Size**: 8½ x 11 inches mandatory
- **Legal Size Prohibition**: Xerographic reduction of legal-sized papers to letter size prohibited
- **Exhibits**: May be filed in original size (Fla. R. Jud. A. 2.520(b))

##### Typography and Formatting
- **Font Requirements**: No specific requirements in Rules of Civil Procedure
- **Typical Fonts**: Times New Roman or Courier commonly used
- **Line Spacing**: No specific requirements in Rules
- **Page Numbering**: No specific requirements in Rules
- **Hole Punching**: No specific requirements

### Circuit Court Formatting Requirements

#### Caption Standards
- **Pleading Captions**: Must contain all parties' names, court name, file number, party designation
- **Motion/Order Captions**: Must include court name, case number, first party on each side, appropriate designation

#### Required Forms
- **Civil Cover Sheet**: Form 1.997 - required with initial complaint/petition
- **Filing Consequence**: Case accepted but abated if cover sheet not filed
- **Final Disposition Form**: Required by prevailing party with final order/judgment (Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.545)

### Local Rules Variations by Circuit

#### Circuit-Specific Standards
- **Statewide Consistency**: Major circuits follow similar formatting requirements
- **County Examples**: Miami-Dade (11th), Pinellas (6th), Hillsborough (13th), Broward (17th), Palm Beach (15th)

### E-filing Portal Requirements

#### Technical Standards
- **Portal Access**: Florida Courts Technology Standards provide technical specifications
- **Document Format**: Electronic filing capabilities available
- **System Integration**: Statewide e-filing system implementation ongoing

## Illinois State Courts

### Illinois Code of Civil Procedure

#### Electronic Filing Implementation
- **Mandatory Date**: July 1, 2018 for Civil Areas
- **Exceptions**: Quasi Criminal, Housing, and Wills cases exempt
- **Statewide System**: eFileIL System mandated by Illinois Supreme Court
- **Technology Provider**: Tyler Technologies software through Administrative Offices

### Cook County Circuit Court Specific Rules

#### Caption Requirements
- **Court Identification**: Must contain "IN THE CIRCUIT COURT OF COOK COUNTY, ILLINOIS"
- **Department/Division**: Must include Department and Division or District designation
- **Case Information**: Standard case identification requirements

#### Document Format Standards
- **Paper Size**: 8½ x 11 inches
- **Document Quality**: Must be "legibly written, typewritten, printed, or otherwise prepared"
- **Paper Type**: Recycled paper encouraged
- **Formatting**: No specific font or spacing requirements specified

#### Attorney Information Requirements
- **Required Information**:
  - Attorney name
  - Business address
  - Email address (primary required, up to two secondary permitted)
  - Telephone number
- **Cook County Specific**: Attorney Code Number required in Case Cross Reference Number field

#### Self-Represented Party Requirements
- **Contact Information**:
  - Mailing address
  - Telephone number
  - Email address (single designation required if available)

### Downstate Court Variations

#### Statewide Standards
- Illinois Supreme Court Rules provide general framework
- Local variations permitted within state guidelines
- eFileIL system applies statewide

### Electronic Filing Requirements

#### Privacy and Confidentiality
- **Social Security Numbers**: Prohibited in filed documents (only last four digits permitted)
- **Personal Identity Information**: Must not be included except as provided in Rule 138(c)
- **Confidential Cases**: Special filing procedures apply

#### Cover Sheet Requirements
- **New Cases**: Must upload completed Cover Sheet for appropriate Division
- **Division-Specific**: Different cover sheets for different court divisions

#### Technical Standards
- **System**: eFileIL Electronic Document Standards apply
- **File Formats**: PDF format standard
- **Security**: Appropriate confidentiality measures required

## Key Implementation Notes for Legal Document Generation Systems

### Universal Requirements
1. **Paper Size**: 8½ x 11 inches across all jurisdictions
2. **PDF Format**: Electronic filing requires searchable PDF format
3. **Caption Standards**: All jurisdictions require specific caption formatting
4. **Contact Information**: Attorney/party contact information mandatory

### Jurisdiction-Specific Programming Considerations

#### California
- Century Schoolbook font preference (13pt)
- 1.5-inch side margins critical
- Electronic bookmarks for navigation
- 25MB file size limit enforcement

#### New York
- Commercial Division has specific professional standards
- Referee appointment procedures available
- Recent rule changes require ongoing monitoring

#### Texas
- 12-point minimum font size enforcement
- Double-spacing requirement
- Searchable PDF without security restrictions
- Email address inclusion mandatory

#### Florida
- Recycled paper requirement for physical filings
- Civil cover sheet automation needed
- Final disposition form requirements

#### Illinois
- Cook County attorney code integration
- Privacy protection for SSN and personal information
