## IV. DETAILED ANALYSIS

### A. FMC Regulatory Framework for Vessel Sharing Agreements

#### 1. Statutory Requirements Under the Shipping Act

Vessel sharing agreements (VSAs) between ocean common carriers are subject to the Federal Maritime Commission's jurisdiction under 46 U.S.C. Chapter 403 (Agreements). The statutory framework establishes the following requirements:

**Filing Requirement (46 U.S.C. Â§ 40302):** "A true copy of every agreement...shall be filed with the Federal Maritime Commission." This mandatory filing requirement applies to all VSAs unless specifically exempted by Commission regulations at 46 CFR Part 535. [VERIFIED: 46 U.S.C. Â§ 40302, https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?path=/prelim@title46/subtitle4&edition=prelim]

**Effective Date and Waiting Period (46 U.S.C. Â§ 40304):** Filed VSAs become effective on "the 45th day after filing, or on the 30th day after notice...is published in the Federal Register, whichever is later." The FMC must publish notice within seven days of filing and may request additional information, potentially extending the review period to 45 days after receiving complete submissions. Parties may request expedited effectiveness "not to a date that is less than 14 days after notice" publishes. [VERIFIED: 46 U.S.C. Â§ 40304, https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?path=/prelim@title46/subtitle4&edition=prelim]

**Antitrust Immunity (46 U.S.C. Â§ 40307(a)):** Filed and effective VSAs receive antitrust immunity for operational cooperation including vessel sharing, schedule coordination, and terminal facility sharing. However, this immunity has critical limitations: 46 U.S.C. Â§ 40307(b) provides that immunity does NOT extend to rate-fixing discussions or mandatory service contract terms. [VERIFIED: 46 U.S.C. Â§ 40307, https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?path=/prelim@title46/subtitle4&edition=prelim]

**Indefinite Duration (46 U.S.C. Â§ 40304(f)):** "The Commission may not limit the effectiveness of an agreement to a fixed term," which means VSAs can operate indefinitely absent formal cancellation or FMC disapproval. This provision is significant for change of control analysis because VSAs are not required to renew periodically, reducing natural opportunities for parties to exit without triggering contractual termination provisions. [VERIFIED: 46 U.S.C. Â§ 40304(f), https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?path=/prelim@title46/subtitle4&edition=prelim]

#### 2. Amendment Procedures Under 46 CFR Part 535

**Amendment Filing Requirements (46 CFR Â§ 535.401):** The regulations establish specific procedures for modifying existing VSAs:

- Modifications must be filed by "reprinting the entire page on which the matter being changed is published ('revised page'), with the revised page indicating the consecutive denomination of the revision (e.g., '1st Revised Page 7')"
- New content inserted between existing pages must be numbered with alpha suffixes (e.g., page inserted between page 7 and 8 becomes "7a")
- Amendments must be submitted to FMC either in paper form or electronically through the Commission's automated agreement filing system at www.fmc.gov

[VERIFIED: 46 CFR Part 535, Subpart D, https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-46/chapter-IV/subchapter-B/part-535]

**FMC Review of Amendments:** The Commission conducts a "preliminary review of each filed agreement to determine whether the agreement is in compliance with the requirements of the Act and this part." The same 45-day waiting period applies to amendments, though parties may request expedited effectiveness subject to the 14-day minimum. [VERIFIED: 46 CFR Â§ 535.601-602, https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-46/chapter-IV/subchapter-B/part-535]

**Critical Issue for PMSC Transaction:** Whether the acquisition of PMSC by Global Logistics Partners constitutes a "reportable change" requiring VSA amendment filing depends on two factors:

1. **Change of Party Identity:** If PMSC remains the named party to the VSA (as the same legal entity despite change in ownership), FMC regulations do not require amendment filing solely for change of corporate control.

2. **Material Change in Operational Capability:** If the ownership change materially affects PMSC's operational capabilities, financial stability, or competitive positioning vis-Ã -vis VSA partners, the FMC may require notification under reporting requirements in 46 CFR Part 535, Subpart G.

**Industry Practice (Standard VSA Withdrawal Provisions):** Research into filed VSA agreements reveals standard industry practices for withdrawal and termination:

**Ocean Alliance Agreement (FMC Agreement No. 012426):** The Ocean Alliance (CMA CGM, COSCO, Evergreen, OOCL) includes the following standard provisions:

- **Withdrawal Notice:** "Any Party may resign from this Agreement on not less than thirty (30) days' notice to the other Parties"
- **Slot Sale Consent:** "The slot sale to any third party VOCC on a more permanent basis shall require the unanimous written consent of the other Parties having a BSA on the concerned service, not to be unreasonably withheld or unduly delayed"
- **Affiliate Exception:** "Any sale or assignment of space by a Party to a subsidiary or affiliate shall be permitted and not subject to any consent requirement"

[VERIFIED: Ocean Alliance Agreement, FMC Agreement No. 012426, https://www2.fmc.gov/agreement_lib/012426-000.pdf]

**THE Alliance Agreement (FMC Agreement No. 012439):** THE Alliance (Hapag-Lloyd, ONE, Yang Ming prior to 2025 restructuring) includes similar provisions plus additional termination rights:

- **Standard Withdrawal:** 30-day notice requirement
- **Material Adverse Change Termination:** Agreement provides termination rights upon "insolvency or material adverse change" affecting a party's ability to perform
- **Continuation of Obligations:** "When the agreement is terminated with respect to a party pursuant to insolvency or material adverse change provisions, certain obligations continue to apply for 360 days"
- **Amendment Upon Termination:** Remaining parties must "discuss in good faith and agree to any amendments to this Agreement necessitated by such termination"

[VERIFIED: THE Alliance Agreement, FMC Agreement No. 012439, https://www2.fmc.gov/FMC.Agreements.Web/Public/Document/6244]

### E. FMC Filing Requirements for Transaction-Triggered VSA Changes

#### 1. Change of Control Notification to FMC

**Regulatory Question:** Does PMSC's acquisition by Global Logistics Partners require notification to FMC under 46 CFR Part 535 reporting requirements?

**Analysis:**

**46 CFR Part 535, Subpart G (Reporting Requirements):** The regulations require parties to ocean carrier agreements to report certain "material changes" to the FMC. However, the regulations do NOT explicitly define "change of control" or "change of ownership" as triggering events for reporting.

**46 U.S.C. Â§ 40304 (Commission Action on Agreements):** The statute provides FMC with authority to review filed agreements for compliance but does not mandate amendment filing solely for change in party ownership where the party identity remains unchanged (i.e., PMSC remains Pacific Maritime Services Corporation, same legal entity, same FMC Ocean Common Carrier License No. 024587).

**Industry Practice:** Based on research, ocean carriers typically do NOT file VSA amendments with FMC solely for change of corporate ownership, unless:
1. The change affects party identity (e.g., merger changes corporate name or EIN)
2. The change materially affects operational capability (e.g., fleet reduction, terminal closures)
3. VSA partners request FMC filing to formalize changes to party membership

**Conclusion:** PMSC is **NOT required** to file VSA amendments with FMC based solely on acquisition by Global Logistics Partners, PROVIDED:
- PMSC remains same legal entity (Delaware corporation, same EIN, same FMC license)
- Fleet, terminals, and operational capabilities remain substantially similar
- No VSA partner formally requests FMC amendment filing

**However:** PMSC **MUST notify** VSA partners under contractual notice provisions (typically 30 days) of change of ownership, and partners may then exercise termination rights under VSA terms.

#### 2. VSA Amendment Filing if Partners Terminate

**Scenario:** If 2-3 VSA partners (e.g., Maersk, Hapag-Lloyd, MSC) exercise termination rights, must remaining VSA be amended and refiled with FMC?

**Regulatory Requirement:** Yes. 46 CFR Â§ 535.401 requires amendment filing when "material changes" occur to effective agreements. Withdrawal of parties to a VSA constitutes material change requiring:

1. **Amended VSA Filing:** Remaining parties must file revised agreement removing withdrawn parties and adjusting slot allocations, sailing schedules, and terminal sharing arrangements

2. **45-Day Waiting Period:** Amendment becomes effective 45 days after filing (or 30 days after Federal Register publication, whichever later), UNLESS FMC grants expedited effectiveness (minimum 14 days)

3. **Potential FMC Scrutiny:** If multiple parties withdraw simultaneously, FMC may inquire whether remaining agreement still serves pro-competitive purpose or if withdrawal undermines alliance structure

**Timeline Impact on Transaction Closing:**

If VSA partners exercise termination rights upon learning of PMSC acquisition:
- **T+0 (Closing Date):** PMSC notifies VSA partners of change of ownership
- **T+30-60 days:** VSA partners provide withdrawal notice (30-60 day notice periods typical)
- **T+30-60 days:** Withdrawal effective, PMSC must file VSA amendments with FMC
- **T+75-120 days:** Amended VSAs effective after 45-day FMC waiting period
- **T+90-180 days:** 360-day wind-down obligations continue for withdrawn parties

**Transaction Closing Condition Risk:** If acquisition agreement includes condition precedent that "material VSA arrangements remain in force" or "no material adverse change in operational capacity," VSA partner terminations could delay or prevent closing.

**Recommendation:** Acquisition agreement should include:
- **Diligence Confirmation:** Pre-closing outreach to VSA partners to assess termination risk
- **Closing Condition Carve-Out:** VSA terminations do NOT constitute material adverse change unless >50% of VSA capacity terminates
- **Post-Closing Covenant:** PMSC commits to negotiate bilateral slot charters or new VSA within 180 days of closing to replace terminated capacity
