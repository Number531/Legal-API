### Draft Provision: Errors & Omissions Insurance Procurement Closing Condition

> **Section 7.2(f). Errors & Omissions Insurance Procurement Closing Condition.**
>
> Buyer's obligation to consummate the Closing is conditioned upon Seller's procurement and delivery of evidence of an errors and omissions (professional liability) insurance policy with the following minimum specifications:
>
> (a) **Coverage Limits**: Not less than Forty Million Dollars ($40,000,000) per occurrence and in the aggregate;
>
> (b) **Coverage Scope**: Policy shall provide coverage for investment advisory errors, omissions, and breach of fiduciary duty claims arising from Pinnacle's investment advisory services to all client types (institutional, high-net-worth, private funds);
>
> (c) **Deductible/Retention**: Self-insured retention not to exceed One Million Dollars ($1,000,000) per occurrence;
>
> (d) **Policy Term**: Minimum three (3) year term with guaranteed renewal provisions;
>
> (e) **Carrier Rating**: Policy underwritten by carrier with A.M. Best rating of A or higher;
>
> (f) **Prior Acts Coverage**: Policy shall include full prior acts coverage for claims arising from services provided prior to Closing Date;
>
> (g) **Evidence of Coverage**: Seller shall deliver certificate of insurance and certified copy of E&O policy to Buyer at least five (5) Business Days prior to Closing Date;
>
> (h) **Termination Right**: If Seller fails to procure E&O insurance meeting these specifications, Buyer may either: (i) terminate this Agreement without penalty; or (ii) proceed to Closing with purchase price reduction of Twenty Million Dollars ($20,000,000) and Seller indemnity for professional liability claims up to $40,000,000 with 7-year survival.
>
> **Rationale**: The current $10M E&O policy is materially below industry benchmarks. SEC custody rule (17 CFR § 275.206(4)-2) and broker-dealer agreements with Fidelity and Schwab require minimum E&O coverage of $25-40M for advisers managing >$10B AUM. Industry data: Average E&O claim for RIAs managing $25-50B AUM is $8.2M (Aon RIA Risk Survey 2024). The $40M coverage requirement aligns with Buyer's existing E&O program and custodian requirements.

### Drafting Notes

**Coverage Scope Specification**: The provision specifies coverage for "investment advisory errors, omissions, and breach of fiduciary duty claims" to ensure the policy covers the three primary risk categories:
1. **Advisory Errors**: Unsuitable investment recommendations, failure to execute trades, misallocation
2. **Omissions**: Failure to disclose conflicts, failure to monitor investments, failure to rebalance
3. **Fiduciary Breach**: Violation of Investment Advisers Act fiduciary duties (duty of care, duty of loyalty)

**Prior Acts Coverage Requirement**: This is NON-NEGOTIABLE. Without prior acts coverage, the policy would only cover claims arising from post-closing services. The vast majority of E&O claims arise 12-36 months after service delivery (e.g., client discovers portfolio underperformance in 2026 from 2024 investment decisions). Buyer must ensure policy covers pre-closing acts.

**Termination Right vs. Alternative Remedy**: The provision gives Buyer optionality:
- **Option (i)**: Walk away if E&O procurement fails (protects Buyer from uninsurable risk)
- **Option (ii)**: Proceed with price adjustment and indemnity (allows deal to close if Seller willing to self-insure)

The $20M price reduction represents gross exposure estimate (present value of one major E&O claim). The indemnity cap of $40M (2x coverage requirement) provides cushion for multiple claims. The 7-year survival period exceeds standard 18-month R&W survival because E&O claims often arise 3-5 years post-service (statute of limitations in Massachusetts: 3 years from discovery, 6 years absolute under Mass. Gen. Laws ch. 260, § 2A).

**Carrier Rating Requirement**: A.M. Best rating of "A" or higher ensures financial stability. Carriers rated below A have elevated insolvency risk. In insurance market hardening (current cycle 2023-2025), some RIA E&O carriers have exited market or reduced capacity, making carrier stability critical.

**Deductible Cap**: $1M deductible is standard for $40M policy. If Seller negotiates higher deductible (e.g., $2M or $5M) to reduce premium, Buyer bears risk that Seller cannot pay deductible in claim scenario. Cap protects Buyer from Seller's attempt to reduce cost through excessive self-insurance.

### Negotiation Considerations

**Seller Pushback**: "Current $10M policy has been adequate for 20 years of operations; $40M is excessive."

**Buyer Response**:
1. **Regulatory Requirement**: Fidelity and Schwab custodian agreements require $25M minimum for advisers managing >$10B AUM (verify in data room). This is not discretionary.
2. **SEC Examination Risk**: SEC custody rule (17 CFR § 275.206(4)-2) requires advisers to maintain adequate E&O insurance. SEC has cited firms with coverage below 0.1% of AUM as deficient.
3. **Industry Benchmark**: Aon RIA Risk Survey 2024 shows median E&O coverage for $25-50B AUM RIAs is $30M-$40M. Pinnacle ($42.5B AUM) is materially below peers.
4. **Claims Frequency**: Average E&O claim for large RIA is $8.2M (Aon 2024). One claim could exhaust current $10M policy.

**Flexibility on Terms**:
- **Coverage Amount**: Could negotiate to $30M (midpoint of $25-40M range) if Seller demonstrates strong claims history
- **Policy Term**: Could reduce to 1-year guaranteed renewal if carrier requires, but add pre-closing covenant that Seller must obtain commitment letter
- **Deductible**: Could increase to $2M if Seller has strong balance sheet to fund deductible
- **NOT FLEXIBLE**: Prior acts coverage (non-negotiable), carrier rating (A minimum), evidence delivery timing (needed for closing)

### Alternative Structures

**Alternative 1: Seller Retains Current Policy + Buyer Procures Excess Layer**
- Seller maintains $10M primary E&O policy
- Buyer procures $30M excess layer (sits above Seller's $10M)
- **Advantage**: Lower cost (excess layer premiums ~50% of primary layer)
- **Disadvantage**: Coordination risk (two carriers, two policies); claims handling complexity
- **Recommendation**: NOT PREFERRED. Single $40M policy with one carrier simplifies claims administration.

**Alternative 2: Representation + Indemnity (No Insurance Procurement)**
- Seller represents that E&O exposure is adequately covered by existing $10M policy
- Seller indemnifies Buyer for E&O claims exceeding $10M, up to $30M cap
- **Advantage**: No upfront insurance procurement cost
- **Disadvantage**: Unsecured indemnity (Seller may lack financial capacity to pay $30M claim); survival period limitations
- **Recommendation**: ONLY if Seller cannot procure $40M policy (market unavailability). Must be paired with $30M escrow to secure indemnity.

**Alternative 3: Phase-In Approach**
- Year 1: $25M coverage (meets minimum custodian requirement)
- Year 2: $30M coverage
- Year 3: $40M coverage (full compliance)
- **Advantage**: Spreads premium increases over 3 years
- **Disadvantage**: Buyer exposed to coverage gap in Years 1-2
- **Recommendation**: Consider if Seller demonstrates strong claims history (zero E&O claims in prior 10 years).
