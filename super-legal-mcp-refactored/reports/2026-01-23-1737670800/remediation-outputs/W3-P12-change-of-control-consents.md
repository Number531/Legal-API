#### (c) Minimum Consent Threshold — Closing Condition

Buyer's obligation to close the Transaction is expressly conditioned upon satisfaction of the following requirements (the "**Consent Closing Condition**"):

(i) **Minimum Threshold**: Receipt of written Change of Control Consents from Consent Clients representing at least **ninety percent (90%)** of the aggregate Consent Client AUM set forth on Schedule [X] (i.e., **$8.46 billion** of $9.4 billion AUM) (the "**Minimum Consent Threshold**").

[VERIFICATION: Task instructions recommend 85% threshold as "mid-range" of industry standard 70-90%. This provision adopts 90% threshold to reflect strong buyer preference for revenue certainty while remaining within market parameters. Section IV.J.B.2 states "Industry precedent from PEI 2023 survey of 47 comparable PE acquisitions of RIAs managing institutional assets; historical termination rates range 4.8%-10.2% by AUM, median 7.3%," supporting feasibility of 90% consent rate (inverse of 7-10% expected termination).]

[METHODOLOGY: 90% threshold calculation: $9.4B × 90% = $8.46B. This permits loss of 1 client holding up to $940M AUM (10% of total) while still satisfying condition. Per Section IV.J.B.2 client-specific risk assessment, losing highest-risk clients (e.g., one large public pension with 15-20% termination probability) would not defeat closing condition if other 10 clients consent.]

(ii) **Timing Requirement**: All Consents constituting the Minimum Consent Threshold must be delivered to Buyer in fully executed written form (original, PDF, or electronic signature acceptable under applicable law) at least **ten (10) Business Days** prior to the anticipated Closing Date, to permit Buyer to:
   - Verify authenticity and authority of signatories;
   - Review any conditions or qualifications attached to Consents;
   - Determine whether to proceed to Closing or invoke termination or renegotiation rights under subsection (d) below.

(iii) **Unconditional Consent Requirement**: No Consent counted toward the Minimum Consent Threshold may contain conditions, qualifications, or requirements that are materially adverse to Buyer or Seller, including without limitation:
   - Management fee reductions exceeding **ten (10) basis points** below the fee rate in effect as of the date of this Agreement;
   - Assignment of Seller liabilities, indemnification obligations, or co-investment commitments not expressly assumed by Buyer in this Agreement;
   - Abbreviated termination notice periods (less than **90 days**) or unilateral termination rights not contained in the Client Contract as of the date hereof;
   - "Key person" termination triggers tied to specific post-Closing personnel retention beyond those, if any, contained in existing Client Contracts;
   - Reporting, auditing, or compliance obligations materially more burdensome than those existing under current Client Contracts;
   - Consent expiration or withdrawal rights permitting client to revoke consent after Closing.

**Buyer Determination**: Buyer shall have sole and absolute discretion to determine whether any Consent contains materially adverse conditions and whether to accept or reject any conditioned Consent for purposes of calculating satisfaction of the Minimum Consent Threshold. Buyer shall notify Seller in writing within **three (3) Business Days** of receipt of any conditioned Consent whether Buyer accepts or rejects such Consent. If Buyer rejects a conditioned Consent, such Consent shall not count toward the Minimum Consent Threshold, and the rejecting client's AUM shall be excluded from both numerator and denominator for threshold calculation purposes.

#### (e) Post-Closing Indemnity for Lost Consent Clients

Seller shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Buyer and its Affiliates from and against any and all Losses (as defined in Section [Indemnification Article]) arising from or relating to termination of any Consent Client listed on Schedule [X] within **eighteen (18) months** after the Closing Date, if such termination results from or is materially influenced by:
- Client's objection to the change of control of Seller not disclosed to Buyer prior to Closing;
- Client's invocation of change-of-control termination rights not disclosed on Schedule [X];
- Misrepresentation in Seller's representation that client consented to the Transaction; or
- Breach of Seller's covenant to use commercially reasonable efforts to obtain Consents.

**Indemnifiable Losses** shall include:

(i) **Lost Management Fee Revenue**: The present value of foregone management fee revenue for the remainder of the 18-month indemnity survival period, calculated as:

**Losses = [Terminated Client AUM as of Termination Date] × [Management Fee Rate in Client Contract] × [Remaining Months in 18-Month Period ÷ 12] × [0.92 PV Factor]**

Where the PV Factor of 0.92 reflects discounting remaining monthly revenue to present value at 8% WACC (approximate mid-point discount for 9-month average remaining period).

**Example**: If $600M AUM client paying 0.50% fee terminates 6 months post-Closing:
- Losses = $600M × 0.50% × (12 months remaining ÷ 12) × 0.92
- = $600M × 0.005 × 1.0 × 0.92
- = **$2.76 million** indemnity payment

(ii) **Transition Costs**: Reasonable, documented out-of-pocket costs actually incurred by Buyer to:
- Conduct replacement client search and business development efforts (capped at **$50,000** per terminated client);
- Transition terminated client's account data, custody arrangements, and reporting systems (capped at **$25,000** per terminated client);
- Allocate internal personnel time to client retention efforts and replacement sourcing (capped at **$15,000** per terminated client, calculated at $250/hour for senior personnel time).

**Aggregate Transition Cost Cap**: $90,000 per terminated Consent Client.

**Exclusions from Indemnity**: Seller shall have no indemnification obligation under this Section (e) for any Consent Client termination if:
- Buyer increases the management fee charged to such client by more than **five (5) basis points** above the fee rate in effect at Closing, and the client's termination notice explicitly references the fee increase;
- Buyer materially breaches the Client Contract (e.g., fails to provide required reporting, breaches investment guidelines, commits gross negligence), and such breach is the primary cause of termination;
- The terminated client represents less than **$100 million** in AUM (de minimis exclusion to avoid administrative burden of small claims);
- Termination occurs more than **18 months** after Closing Date (outside survival period);
- Client termination is due to Buyer's replacement of key investment personnel contrary to retention obligations in Section [Employment Article] of this Agreement.

**Indemnity Limitations**:

(i) **Basket**: Seller shall not be liable under this Section (e) until aggregate Losses exceed **$750,000** (the "**Basket**"), after which Seller shall be liable for all Losses from the first dollar (first-dollar liability, not dollar-for-dollar above Basket).

[METHODOLOGY: Task instructions recommend $500,000 basket. (ii) **Cap**: Seller's aggregate liability under this Section (e) shall not exceed **$16.8 million** (the "**Indemnity Cap**").

[VERIFICATION: Section IV.J.B.2 calculates high-case NPV exposure of $16.8M based on 10% termination rate across $9.4B AUM: "$940M AUM × 0.45% fee = $4.23M annual revenue loss. NPV = $4.23M × 3.993 = $16.89M." This provision adopts $16.8M cap consistent with high-end exposure scenario. Task instructions reference $18.8M weighted exposure for broader 30% consent-denial probability; this provision uses lower $16.8M figure tied to actual post-Closing terminations (10% expected rate) rather than pre-Closing consent denial risk.]

(iii) **Survival Period**: All representations, warranties, and covenants related to Consent Clients (including without limitation Sections [specify], Schedule [X] accuracy, and subsection (b) covenant compliance) shall survive Closing for **eighteen (18) months**, terminating on the date that is 18 months after the Closing Date. No indemnification claim under this Section (e) may be asserted after expiration of the survival period, except for claims asserted in writing (with reasonable detail of the factual basis) prior to expiration and diligently pursued thereafter.

[METHODOLOGY: 18-month survival period aligns with industry precedent cited in Section IV.J.B.2: "Typical Survival Period: 18-24 months for commercial contracts representations (client consent obtained, LPA consent obtained, no MFN triggering). Longer than general rep survival (12-18 months) due to delayed manifestation of client/LP terminations (many occur 6-18 months post-COC, not immediately)."]

(iv) **Offset Against Escrow**: Any indemnification payments due under this Section (e) shall first be satisfied from the Client Consent Escrow established under Section (f) below, and only to the extent such payments exceed the remaining escrow balance shall Seller be obligated to pay out-of-pocket.

(v) **Exclusive Remedy**: The indemnification rights set forth in this Section (e) shall be Buyer's sole and exclusive remedy for any Losses arising from Consent Client terminations, and Buyer expressly waives any right to seek damages, rescission, or other relief under contract law, tort law, or equity for such terminations (except for claims of fraud, willful misconduct, or criminal conduct, which are not subject to this exclusivity provision).
