### B. DIRECTORS AND OFFICERS (D&O) LIABILITY INSURANCE

#### 1. Legal Framework and Policy Structure

Directors and Officers Liability Insurance provides coverage for the personal liability of directors and officers arising from claims made against them in their capacity as corporate fiduciaries. Unlike E&O insurance (which covers professional services), D&O insurance protects individuals from personal financial loss resulting from management decisions, corporate governance failures, and fiduciary breaches.

**Policy Structure: Side A, Side B, and Side C Coverage**

D&O policies are typically structured in three insuring agreements, each responding to different liability scenarios:¬π

**Side A Coverage ‚Äî Personal Protection**:
- **Coverage Scope**: Direct coverage for individual directors and officers when the company cannot or will not indemnify them
- **Trigger Scenarios**: Bankruptcy, derivative litigation, regulatory/criminal proceedings, coverage gaps in company indemnification
- **Retention**: **First-dollar coverage** (no deductible/SIR) to ensure personal protection
- **CRITICAL**: Side A is the directors' and officers' personal safety net when corporate resources are unavailable

**Side B Coverage ‚Äî Corporate Reimbursement**:
- **Coverage Scope**: Reimburses the company for indemnification obligations to directors and officers
- **Trigger Scenarios**: Most standard D&O claims where company indemnifies directors per bylaws/employment agreements
- **Retention**: Subject to self-insured retention/deductible (typically $100K-$250K for Pinnacle's size)
- **Function**: Protects corporate balance sheet from indemnification costs

**Side C Coverage ‚Äî Entity Coverage**:
- **Coverage Scope**: Direct coverage for the corporation as a named defendant
- **Public Company Limitation**: Responds only to securities claims
- **Private Company Scope**: Broader coverage for entity exposure in non-securities claims [CROSS-REFERENCE: Pinnacle operates mutual funds, triggering public company analysis]
- **Retention**: Subject to self-insured retention/deductible

> Side B and Side C together provide "balance sheet protection" for the company, while Side A provides personal asset protection for directors and officers.¬≤
