#### 1. STB Merger Approval Timeline: 21-36 Months (Base Case: 21-24 Months)

| Phase | Duration | Key Activities |
|-------|----------|---------------|
| **Phase 1: Completeness Determination** | 60-90 days | STB reviews application for compliance with 49 CFR Â§Â§ 1180.6-1180.11; accepts as complete via Federal Register notice |
| **Phase 2: Public Comment Period** | 90-120 days | Shippers, competing railroads, labor unions, state agencies file initial comments (60-90 days), rebuttal (30 days), replies (30 days) |
| **Phase 3: NEPA Environmental Impact Statement** | 12-18 months | Office of Environmental Analysis prepares Draft EIS (6-12 months), public comment (60-90 days), Final EIS (6-12 months) |

#### 2. Voting Trust Approval: 55% Probability (High Risk)

A **voting trust** allows Continental to complete **financial closing** (purchase GPRC shares) 4-6 months after application filing, while STB review continues for 18-24 additional months. KCS shares placed in independent trust; GPRC operates independently until final STB approval.

**Statutory Standard:** 49 U.S.C. Â§ 11323(c) and 49 CFR Part 1013 require STB approval if voting trust is "**consistent with the public interest**." Board evaluates:
1. **Competitive impact** during trust period (does single holding company control reduce competition even before operational integration?)
2. **Financial stability** (can acquirer service debt without jeopardizing target operations?)
3. **Trustee independence** (will trustee exercise voting rights solely in target's interests, not acquirer's?)
4. **Public benefits** (does voting trust serve legitimate business purpose beyond financial convenience?)

**CP-KCS Approval (May 6, 2021) - Benchmark:**
- **Approved** because: minimal route overlap (~50 customers), strong CP financial capacity (investment grade maintained), independent trustee (former KCS CEO David Starling), geographic complementarity (Canada-Mexico single-line)
- **Timeline:** Application April 5, 2021; approval May 6, 2021 (31 days - exceptionally fast due to minimal controversy)
- **Trust Period:** December 14, 2021 (financial closing) to April 14, 2023 (final merger approval) = **16 months**

**CN-KCS Rejection (August 31, 2021) - Critical Precedent:**
- **Unanimously rejected** because: substantial route overlap (~300 customers), excessive debt ($19B additional), gateway competition concerns (CN-KCS would dominate Midwest-Gulf routes), financial engineering concerns (loading KCS with debt before STB determines merger is in public interest)
- **Outcome:** CN abandoned KCS bid September 12, 2021

**Application to Continental-GPRC:**

|-----------------|-------------------|-------------------|----------------------------------|
| **Competitive Overlap** | Minimal | Substantial (300 customers) | **UNKNOWN - CRITICAL DATA GAP:** Continental's existing railroad holdings not disclosed. If Continental owns other railroads with GPRC overlap, voting trust approval probability drops to 20-30%. |
| **Debt Level** | Moderate (investment grade) | Excessive ($19B) | **HIGH RISK:** PE-backed structure likely involves significant leverage. If debt-to-EBITDA >5.0x, STB will question infrastructure investment capacity. |
| **Trustee Independence** | Strong (former KCS CEO) | N/A | **Must be established:** Cannot be Continental affiliate or PE fund representative. Recommend retired Class I railroad CEO (e.g., former UP, BNSF, or NS executive). |

If GPRC characterized as exercising market power, STB will scrutinize voting trust more heavily. |

**Probability Assessment:**

- **BASE CASE (55% probability): APPROVAL WITH CONDITIONS**
  - Continental demonstrates minimal competitive overlap, debt <4.5x EBITDA, appoints independent trustee, commits to rate freeze during trust period (18-24 months)
  - **Financial Closing:** 4-6 months after application filing
  - **Integration Begins:** 22-30 months after application filing (18-24 months in trust)

- **DOWNSIDE CASE (35% probability): DELAYED OR CONDITIONAL DENIAL**
  - STB expresses concerns about PE ownership structure, debt leverage >5.0x EBITDA, GPRC operational challenges (BLET strike, derailment litigation), shipper opposition
  - **Voting trust approval delayed 8-12 months** while Continental addresses concerns OR **denied**, requiring Continental to wait for final STB approval before financial closing
  - **Financial Closing:** 24-36 months (no trust) or 32-42 months (delayed trust approval)

- **WORST CASE (10% probability): OUTRIGHT REJECTION**
  - STB finds substantial competitive overlap, financial engineering concerns, premature control risks
  - **No financial closing until final STB approval** (~24-36 months)
  - **Deal Termination Risk:** Continental financing commitments may expire; competing bidders may emerge

**Revenue Impact of Voting Trust Denial:**
- **Financing costs increase:** $180M-$240M (base case with trust) to $300M-$360M (denied trust, 24-36 month review) due to extended debt commitment fees and interest carry
- **Retrading risk:** If market conditions deteriorate during extended review period, Continental may seek purchase price reduction or GPRC may demand increased consideration
- **Operational disruption:** GPRC management uncertainty for 24-36 months; capital investment decisions deferred; employee turnover risk

**CRITICAL RECOMMENDATION:** **Settle TransAmerica rate case before STB application filing** to neutralize primary shipper complaint and improve voting trust approval probability from 55% to 70%.

#### 3. Enhanced Competition Standard (Post-2001): Significantly Higher Bar

STB **revised merger regulations in 2001** following service failures after UP/SP merger (1996). Under **pre-2001 standards**, Class I carriers had to demonstrate competition would **remain unchanged**. Since **2001**, STB approves major mergers **only if competition will be ENHANCED** (49 CFR Â§ 1180.1).

**Public Interest Factors (49 CFR Â§ 1180.1):**

1. **Benefits:** Operational efficiencies, service improvements, capital investment, network optimization, new single-line routes
2. **Harms:** Reduction of competition, harm to essential services, transitional service problems, captive shipper impacts, gateway closures
3. **Enhanced Competition:** Transaction must **affirmatively enhance competition** through conditions such as:
   - Trackage rights for competing railroads (granting BNSF/UP access to GPRC lines)
   - Reciprocal switching (allowing captive shippers to reach competing railroads)
   - Gateway preservation (maintaining competitive interchange points)
   - Rate protections for captive shippers (rate freeze, maximum rate commitments)

**Application to Continental-GPRC:**

Continental must demonstrate how the merger **enhances competition**, not merely preserves it. Potential arguments:

| Enhanced Competition Claim | Supporting Evidence Required | STB Scrutiny Level |
|---------------------------|----------------------------|-------------------|
| **New single-line routes** | If Continental owns other railroads, GPRC acquisition creates new origin-destination pairs without interchange delays | **Depends on Continental holdings** (CRITICAL DATA GAP) |
| **Improved service reliability** | GPRC has operational challenges (March 2024 derailment, BLET strike risk); Continental investment improves performance | **HIGH:** STB will require concrete performance commitments (on-time delivery targets, FRA safety compliance improvements) |
| **Capital investment** | Continental commits to $1.2B+ annual capex (rail replacement, PTC maintenance) vs. GPRC's deferred maintenance backlog | **MEDIUM:** STB views positively if credibly committed; skeptical if PE-backed structure prioritizes financial returns over infrastructure |
| **Gateway expansion** | Continental proposes new gateways or expanded interchange facilities | **LOW:** Difficult to credibly claim gateway expansion when industry trend is gateway rationalization |
| **Voluntary trackage rights** | Continental proactively offers trackage rights to BNSF/UP for 10-15 high-traffic corridors | **HIGH VALUE:** Demonstrates good faith; preempts STB-imposed conditions |

**Risk:** If Continental **cannot demonstrate enhanced competition**, STB may **deny the merger** or impose **severe conditions** that offset the claimed public interest benefits (e.g., extensive trackage rights that eliminate merger synergies).

#### 4. Captive Shipper Analysis: 70% Probability of Rate Freeze/Moratorium

**Captive Shipper Definition:** Shipper with no economically viable transportation alternative to a single railroad. Typically ships bulk commodities (coal, grain, chemicals, aggregates) unsuitable for trucking; lacks barge access; located on single railroad's tracks.

**Statistical Context:**
- **78% of rail shippers** are served by only one railroad (Rail Customer Coalition data)
- **50% of rail revenue** generated from captive shippers (up from 27% in 2004)
- **Market power concentration** is primary STB concern in merger review

**GPRC Captive Shipper Exposure:**

| Commodity | % of GPRC Revenue | Captive Shipper Risk | Rationale |
|-----------|------------------|---------------------|-----------|
| **Coal** | 28% ($2.3B) | **HIGH** | Powder River Basin coal mines single-line captive to GPRC or joint-line with BNSF; no truck/barge alternative for 100+ mile hauls to power plants |
| **Agricultural** | 18% ($1.48B) | **HIGH** | Rural Great Plains grain elevators predominantly captive; truck-competitive only within 100-150 miles of final destination |
| **Chemicals** | 12% ($984M) | **MEDIUM** | Chemical plants with rail sidings lack truck-competitive alternatives for bulk shipments (tank cars); some barge access on Mississippi River system |
| **Intermodal** | 25% ($2.05B) | **LOW** | Truck-competitive; shippers can switch to over-the-road trucking if rail rates increase |
| **Other** | 17% ($1.39B) | **VARIES** | Automotive (volatile, truck-competitive); aggregates (captive within 50-mile radius) |

**TransAmerica Rate Case (STB Docket FD 36458) - Critical Variable:**

TransAmerica (grain elevator, captive shipper) alleges GPRC charged **$34.16/ton** vs. **maximum reasonable rate of $29.70/ton** (180% of variable cost $16.50/ton), resulting in **$44.6M overcharges** (2021-2024) plus **$11.15M annual prospective overcharge**.

**If TransAmerica Wins (50% probability):**
- Establishes precedent that GPRC **exercised market power** against captive shippers
- Triggers **25-30 additional rate complaints** from similarly situated shippers ($100M-$200M additional exposure)
- STB will **presume competitive harm** from Continental-GPRC merger (further consolidation of market power)
- **Rate freeze/moratorium probability increases from 70% to 95%**

**If TransAmerica Loses or Settles (50% probability):**
- No adverse precedent; GPRC maintains defense that rates are revenue adequate (180% constraint complied with)
- **Rate freeze probability remains 70%** (STB will still impose as standard merger condition for captive shipper protection)

**Projected STB Conditions for Captive Shippers:**

| Condition | Probability | Duration | Revenue Impact (Annual) | NPV Impact (10-year, 8% discount) |
|-----------|------------|----------|------------------------|---------------------------------|
| **Rate Freeze** (CPI-only increases) | 70% | 3-5 years | $50M-$100M | $280M-$560M |

#### 5. Gateway Preservation Conditions: 80% Probability

**Gateway Definition:** Interchange point where two railroads transfer traffic. Preserving gateway competition ensures shippers retain ability to route shipments via multiple railroad combinations (e.g., GPRC to Kansas City gateway, then BNSF to final destination).

**CP-KCS Gateway Preservation Precedent (March 15, 2023):**

STB imposed **unprecedented gateway protections** in CP-KCS approval:

1. **Rate Increase Justification Requirement:** If CPKC increases rates to affected gateway by >CPI, must provide shippers **written justification within 15 days**
2. **Pre-Merger Gateway Rights:** Shippers retain right to route traffic via pre-merger gateways; CPKC cannot force shippers onto single-line routes
3. **Seven-Year Oversight Period:** CPKC must file quarterly reports on gateway traffic volumes, rates, service performance, shipper complaints

| **Minneapolis** | CP, CN | MEDIUM (northern gateway) | **MEDIUM:** Agricultural, intermodal |

**Projected STB Conditions:**

#### 6. Labor Protective Provisions: $35M-$70M Exposure (Base Case)

49 U.S.C. Â§ 11326 **mandates** STB impose **labor protective conditions** on railroad mergers. Standard conditions known as **"New York Dock"** (adopted by ICC 1979) provide:

| Protection | Standard |
|-----------|----------|
| **Income Protection** | Up to **6 years** for displaced/dismissed employees |
| **Relocation Allowances** | Full reimbursement for moving costs |
| **Home Sale Protection** | Compensation for losses from home sale due to relocation |
| **Seniority Rights** | Employees not in worse position for **4 years** post-approval |
| **Arbitration** | Expedited binding arbitration for disputes |

**GPRC Labor Profile:**
- **28,000 total employees** (18,500 unionized operating craft, 9,500 non-union administrative/management)
- **12 collective bargaining agreements** (BLET, SMART-TD, BMWED, etc.)

| Scenario | Job Eliminations | Rationale | Dismissal Allowances (6-year) |

**Precedent:**
- **UP/SP (1996):** 200-500 job eliminations, $25M-$75M labor protective costs

**Seniority Integration Risk:**

If Continental owns other railroads, **National Mediation Board (NMB) may determine "single carrier"** status, requiring:

**Cost:** Seniority integration arbitration and related disputes add **$15M-$50M** to labor protective costs.

- **If BLET goes on strike during STB review:** Delays STB approval; STB views as operational instability; increases STB scrutiny of labor integration plans

#### 2. Public Interest Standard and Enhanced Competition Requirement (Post-2001)

**Statutory Standard:**

### C. Voting Trust Analysis Under 49 U.S.C. Â§ 11323(c) and 49 CFR Part 1013

#### 1. Purpose and Mechanics of Voting Trusts in Railroad Mergers

A **voting trust** allows the acquiring railroad to **complete the financial closing** (purchase KCS shares) while **STB review continues**, with KCS shares placed in an independent trust to prevent unlawful control before final merger approval.Â³â· This mechanism addresses two competing concerns:

1. **Acquirer's Need for Certainty:** Securing target shareholder approval and financing before spending 18-36 months in STB review.
2. **STB's Public Interest Review:** Ensuring the target operates independently until the Board determines the merger is in the public interest.

**Statutory Authority:**
49 U.S.C. Â§ 11323(c) authorizes the Board to approve a voting trust **if consistent with the public interest**. The Board's "Guidelines for the Proper Use of Voting Trusts" are codified at **49 C.F.R. Part 1013**.Â³â¸

**Key Voting Trust Requirements (49 CFR Â§ 1013):**Â³â¹

| Requirement | Standard |
|------------|----------|
| **Trustee Independence** | Trustee must maintain **complete independence** from the acquirer (settlor). Cannot take direction from acquirer on governance or operational matters. |
| **Irrevocability** | Trust must be **irrevocable** during STB review period to prevent acquirer from manipulating timing. |
| **No Operational Control** | Target railroad continues to operate **independently** under existing management. No integration with acquirer's operations, systems, or labor agreements. |
| **Financial Isolation** | Acquirer's debt **cannot be secured by target's assets**. Financial results reported separately. |

**Procedural Timeline:**
- Voting trust application filed **simultaneously with or shortly after merger application**â´â°
- **Brief public comment period** (30-60 days)â´Â¹
- **Board decision within 4-6 months** of application filingâ´Â²
- If approved, **financial closing can occur** before final merger approval (saving 18-24 months of limbo)

#### 2. Captive Shipper Doctrine and Rate Regulation Concerns

**Definition of Captive Shipper:**
A shipper is considered "captive" if it has **no economically viable transportation alternative** to a single railroad.â¶Â¹ These shippers typically:
- Ship bulk commodities (coal, grain, aggregates, chemicals) unsuitable for trucking due to volume/weight
- Lack access to navigable waterways for barge transport
- Are located on a single railroad's tracks with no connection to competing carriers

**Statistical Context:**
According to the Rail Customer Coalition, **78% of rail shippers are served by only one railroad** (single-line captive).â¶Â² In 2019, **50% of rail revenue was generated from captive shippers**, up from just 27% in 2004.â¶Â³ This concentration of market power is a **primary STB concern** in merger review.

**Application to GPRC:**
GPRC's revenue composition suggests **significant captive shipper exposure:**
- **Coal: 28% of revenue ($2.3B)** - Many coal mines (especially Powder River Basin) are single-line captive to GPRC or joint-line with BNSF.â¶â´
- **Agricultural: 18% of revenue ($1.48B)** - Grain elevators in rural Great Plains are predominantly captive.â¶âµ
- **Chemicals: 12% of revenue ($984M)** - Chemical plants with rail sidings often lack truck-competitive alternatives for bulk shipments.â¶â¶
