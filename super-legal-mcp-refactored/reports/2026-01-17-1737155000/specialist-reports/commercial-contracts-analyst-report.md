> (c) **Termination Right**: Following receipt of notice of a Change of Control, the Reinsurer shall have the right, but not the obligation, to terminate this Agreement upon 90 days' written notice to the Company. Termination shall be effective as of the end of the then-current policy year.
>
> (d) **Run-off**: If the Agreement is terminated pursuant to subsection (c), the Reinsurer shall remain liable for all claims incurred prior to the termination date, and the Company shall continue to pay reinsurance premiums for any policies in force as of the termination date until such policies terminate or expire.

**Key Differences from Coinsurance Treaties:**

1. **No Consent Requirement**: Munich Re does not have the right to consent to or block the change of control. LLIC only has a **notification obligation**.

2. **Post-Closing Termination**: Munich Re's only remedy is to terminate the treaty **after** the change of control occurs. This does not block the M&A transaction but creates **going-forward risk** (loss of reinsurance coverage for new group life policies).

3. **Run-off Provisions**: Munich Re remains liable for claims incurred prior to termination, ensuring LLIC is not exposed to retroactive coverage loss.
