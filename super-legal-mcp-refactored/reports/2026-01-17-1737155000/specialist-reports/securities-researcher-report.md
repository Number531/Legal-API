#### 3. Section 12(a)(2) Rescission Liability

**15 U.S.C. ¬ß 77l(a)(2)** creates strict liability for sellers who offer securities "by means of a prospectus or oral communication" containing material misstatements or omissions.‚Å∏

**Elements:**
1. Defendant offered or sold a security
2. By means of a prospectus or oral communication
3. Containing a material misstatement or omission
4. Purchaser did not know of the misstatement/omission
5. Statute of limitations not expired (1 year from discovery, 3 years from sale)

**Remedy:**
Rescission damages = **Purchase price + interest - sale price - income received**‚Åπ

**Defenses:**
- **Reasonable care defense**: Seller "did not know, and in the exercise of reasonable care could not have known, of such untruth or omission" (¬ß 12(a)(2))
- **Non-materiality**: Depreciation not caused by prospectus deficiency

**Application to LLIC's Prospectus Delivery Violations:**

If prospectuses were delivered **late** but were otherwise accurate, rescission liability is **limited**:
- Late delivery alone does not create ¬ß 12(a)(2) liability unless prospectus contained misstatements
- Standing limited to **direct purchasers** in initial offering (no aftermarket standing)¬π‚Å∞
- LLIC's primary exposure is **Section 5 civil penalties**, not rescission

**However**, if prospectuses contained **material misstatements** about:
- Fee structures (M&E charges, investment advisory fees, GMWB rider charges)
- Investment risks
- GMWB benefit calculations
- Historical performance illustrations

**Then rescission liability could be substantial:**
