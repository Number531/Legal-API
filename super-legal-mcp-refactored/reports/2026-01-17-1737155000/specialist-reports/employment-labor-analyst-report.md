## IV. DETAILED ANALYSIS

### A. Nebraska Non-Compete Law: Enforceability Assessment

#### 1. Legal Framework

**NO STATUTORY PROVISIONS:** Nebraska has no specific statute regulating non-compete agreements in employment relationships. All applicable law derives from common law court decisions. [VERIFIED via Nebraska Revised Statutes search â€” no employment non-compete statute exists]

**IMPORTANT CLARIFICATION:** Neb. Rev. Stat. Â§ 25-21,187 addresses indemnity provisions in construction contracts, NOT non-compete agreements. This statute is inapplicable to LLIC agent employment agreements.

#### 2. Nebraska Common Law Non-Compete Standards

**Three-Part Enforceability Test** (Nebraska Supreme Court):
1. Restrictions must be **reasonable and not injurious to the public**
2. Limitations must be **only as restrictive as necessary** to protect employer's legitimate business interests
3. Terms must be **reasonable** and **not unduly harsh and oppressive** on the employee

**Legitimate Business Interests Protected:**
- Customer goodwill
- Confidential information
- Trade secrets

**Nebraska's Strict Approach â€” CRITICAL LIMITATION:**

> "Nebraska courts have adopted a strict view of [non-compete agreements] and have concluded that a post-employment restriction on an employee must be no greater than reasonably necessary to protect an employer's legitimate interest (such as its goodwill, confidential information, or trade secrets)." *Polly v. Ray D. Hilderman & Co.*, 225 Neb. 662, 407 N.W.2d 751 (1987) [VERIFIED via CourtListener Case ID 1235956]

**NO GEOGRAPHIC RESTRICTIONS PERMITTED:**

> "Traditional non-compete clauses with geographic restrictions are not allowed in post-employment restrictions on employees." Instead, Nebraska courts recognize only a **narrow customer non-solicitation restriction**, which must be properly limited to prevent solicitation of only those customers that the employee **specifically did business with and had personal contact**.

#### 3. Insurance Agent Case Law â€” *Mertz v. Pharmacists Mutual Insurance Co.*

**Case:** *Mertz v. Pharmacists Mutual Ins. Co.*, 261 Neb. 704, 625 N.W.2d 197 (2001) [VERIFIED Nebraska Supreme Court]

**Facts:**
- Employment agreement for insurance agent in Iowa
- Covenant restricted agent from competing with employer
- Nebraska law applied (employer authorized to sell insurance in Nebraska)

**Holding:**
> "Applying Nebraska law, the covenant's restriction is **greater than is reasonably necessary to protect Pharmacists' interest**, and the covenant was **not enforced**."

**Key Precedent:**
- A **2-year, 95-mile radius restriction** against an insurance salesperson from competing with or soliciting customers was found **unreasonable** by Nebraska courts
- A restriction against a former insurance agent from soliciting or selling to **entities that were not customers** of the former employer was deemed **unreasonable**

#### 4. The "Magic Words" Standard â€” Customer Non-Solicitation

**Enforceable Language (Nebraska Courts):**

Nebraska courts have consistently upheld non-solicitation agreements that use the precise formulation:

> "The former employee is restricted from working for or soliciting the former employer's clients or accounts with whom the former employee **'actually did business and had personal contact'** while employed by the former employer."

**No Blue Penciling:** Nebraska courts will NOT modify or amend unenforceable non-compete agreements. If a non-compete is deemed unreasonable, it will be **entirely void** â€” courts cannot salvage overbroad provisions by narrowing scope.

#### 5. Application to LLIC Captive Agents

- 2-year duration post-termination
- 50-mile geographic radius from agent's office
- Prohibition on soliciting LLIC customers

**Enforceability Analysis:**

### D. WARN Act Applicability to Agent Departures

#### 1. WARN Act Statutory Framework

**Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification Act** (29 U.S.C. Â§Â§ 2101-2109; 20 C.F.R. Part 639)

**Coverage:** Employers with **100 or more full-time employees** OR 100+ employees who work at least **4,000 hours per week** (exclusive of overtime).

**Full-Time Employee Definition:** Employees who work at least **20 hours per week** and have been employed for at least **6 of the preceding 12 months**.

#### 2. Triggering Events

**Plant Closing:** Permanent or temporary shutdown of a **single site of employment** (or one or more facilities/operating units within a single site) that results in employment loss for **50 or more employees** during any 30-day period.

**Mass Layoff:** Employment loss at a **single site of employment** during any 30-day period for:
- **50-499 employees** if they make up **at least 33%** of the workforce at that site, OR
- **500 or more employees** (regardless of percentage)

**Notice Requirement:** **60 days' advance written notice** to:
- Affected employees (or their union representatives)
- State dislocated worker unit
- Chief elected official of local government
