### CRITICAL FINDING #1: STARK/AKS ASC Joint Venture Violation (DEAL-BLOCKING RISK)

**Issue:** Mercy Endoscopy Center LLC violates both the Physician Self-Referral Law (STARK) and Anti-Kickback Statute due to employed physician ownership structure.

**Facts:**
- **Ownership:** 50% Mercy Regional + 50% twelve gastroenterologists (each physician 4.17%)
- **Critical violation:** Eight (8) of twelve physician-owners are W-2 employees of Mercy Medical Group PC
- **Aggregate employee-physician ownership:** 33.36% (8 physicians √ó 4.17%)
- **Financial relationship:** Physicians receive $35,000 annual profit distributions PLUS W-2 employment compensation
- **Referral pattern:** 85% of ASC cases (2,200 of 2,588 annual procedures) referred by the 12 physician-owners
- **Federal healthcare program involvement:** $2.2M Medicare/Medicaid annual billing, 8-year operation (2016-2024), **$17.6M total federal payments**

**STARK Law Analysis:**

The arrangement violates 42 U.S.C. ¬ß 1395nn because:

1. **Financial relationship exists:** Employed physicians have both employment relationship (W-2) AND ownership interest (4.17% ASC ownership)
2. **Referrals for designated health services (DHS):** Colonoscopies/endoscopies = surgical services (explicit DHS category)
3. **No exception applies:**
   - **In-office ancillary services exception (¬ß1395nn(b)(2)) FAILS:** Requires DHS furnished "in a building in which the referring physician furnishes physicians' services." ASC is 2 miles away in separate building‚Äîdoes not satisfy same building requirement
   - **Whole hospital exception (¬ß1395nn(d)(3)) FAILS:** Applies only to hospital ownership. ASC is licensed under 42 CFR Part 416 (ASC conditions for coverage), NOT 42 CFR Part 482 (hospital conditions of participation)
   - **Rural provider exception FAILS:** Columbus is Metropolitan Statistical Area (urban/suburban)
   - **Academic medical center exception FAILS:** ASC not on academic medical center campus

**Anti-Kickback Statute Analysis:**

The arrangement violates 42 U.S.C. ¬ß 1320a-7b(b) because:

1. **Remuneration:** $35,000 annual distributions √ó 8 employed physicians = $280,000 annually
2. **One purpose to induce referrals:** 85% referral concentration demonstrates financial incentive to refer. OIG Special Fraud Alerts identify this fact pattern as suspect physician-owned entity.
3. **ASC safe harbor (42 CFR ¬ß 1001.952(r)) UNCERTAIN:** Safe harbor requires investment returns "not related to volume or value of referrals." Employed physicians referring to entity partially owned by employer raises heightened scrutiny per OIG Advisory Opinion 21-02. If distributions correlate with overall ASC profitability (driven by physician referrals), arguably "related to volume."

**Penalties and Exposure:**

**STARK Law:**
- **Mandatory refund:** $17.6M (8-year Medicare/Medicaid payments, 6-year lookback)
- **Civil monetary penalties:** $27,017 per prohibited service (2024 inflation-adjusted) √ó 17,600 procedures = $475M maximum theoretical; realistic: $5-$50M based on sampling
- **Exclusion risk:** CMS may exclude Mercy from Medicare/Medicaid (would terminate hospital operations‚ÄîMedicare = 48% of revenue)

**Anti-Kickback Statute:**

## IV. DETAILED ANALYSIS

### A. STARK Law and Anti-Kickback Statute - ASC Joint Venture (CRITICAL)

#### 1. Legal Framework

**STARK Law (42 U.S.C. ¬ß 1395nn)**

The Physician Self-Referral Law, commonly known as the Stark Law, prohibits a physician from making referrals for certain designated health services (DHS) payable by Medicare or Medicaid to an entity with which the physician (or an immediate family member) has a financial relationship (ownership/investment interest or compensation arrangement), unless an exception applies.¬π

The statute establishes a strict liability standard‚Äîif a financial relationship exists and referrals are made for DHS, the arrangement violates Stark unless an exception applies. Intent is irrelevant.¬≤

**Designated Health Services (DHS)** under 42 U.S.C. ¬ß 1395nn(h)(6) include:¬≥
- Clinical laboratory services
- Physical therapy services
- Occupational therapy services
- Radiology services
- Radiation therapy services
- Durable medical equipment
- Parenteral and enteral nutrients
- Prosthetics and orthotics
- Home health services
- Outpatient prescription drugs
- Inpatient and outpatient hospital services
- **Surgical services (including endoscopy and colonoscopy procedures)**

**Anti-Kickback Statute (42 U.S.C. ¬ß 1320a-7b(b))**

The federal Anti-Kickback Statute (AKS) prohibits knowingly and willfully soliciting, receiving, offering, or paying any remuneration (including kickbacks, bribes, or rebates) to induce or reward referrals of items or services reimbursable by federal healthcare programs.‚Å¥

Unlike Stark Law, AKS requires proof of intent‚Äîat least one purpose of the remuneration must be to induce referrals. However, the statute applies broadly to any "remuneration," including profit distributions from physician-owned entities.‚Åµ

#### 3. STARK Law Violation Analysis

**Step 1: Does a Financial Relationship Exist?**

YES. The eight employed physicians have two financial relationships with Mercy:
1. **Employment relationship** (W-2 compensation from Mercy Medical Group PC)
2. **Ownership interest** in Mercy Endoscopy Center LLC (4.17% ownership each, 33% aggregate)

Per 42 U.S.C. ¬ß 1395nn(a)(2)(A), "financial relationship" includes both ownership/investment interests AND compensation arrangements.‚Å∂

**Step 2: Do Physicians Refer for Designated Health Services?**

YES. Colonoscopies and endoscopies are **surgical services**, which are explicitly designated health services under 42 U.S.C. ¬ß 1395nn(h)(6)(D).‚Å∑

The employed physicians refer their own patients to Mercy Endoscopy Center LLC for these procedures, constituting "referrals" under the statute.

**Step 3: Does an Exception Apply?**

The burden is on Mercy to prove that an exception applies. We analyze the two most potentially applicable exceptions:

**Exception A: In-Office Ancillary Services (42 U.S.C. ¬ß 1395nn(b)(2))**

This exception allows physicians to refer for certain DHS if the services are:
1. Furnished personally by the referring physician, or
2. Furnished personally by another physician in the same group practice, or
3. Furnished under direct supervision

AND the services are furnished:
- **"in a building in which the referring physician (or another physician who is a member of the same group practice) furnishes physicians' services unrelated to the furnishing of DHS"** (same building requirement), OR
- In a centralized building used by the group practice for DHS provision‚Å∏

**ANALYSIS:** The exception **DOES NOT APPLY** because:
- The ASC is located **2 miles away** from Mercy Regional Medical Center
- The ASC is in a **separate building** from where the physicians provide their professional services
- The statute requires DHS to be furnished "in a building in which the referring physician...furnishes physicians' services"
- CMS Phase IV Final Rule (85 Fed. Reg. 77492, December 2, 2020) clarified that "same building" means the same physical structure‚Åπ

**Exception B: Whole Hospital Exception (42 U.S.C. ¬ß 1395nn(d)(3))**

This exception allows physician ownership in a "whole hospital" if certain conditions are met, including:
- Physician ownership must be in the entire hospital (not just a department)
- Ownership interests must comply with Medicare hospital ownership requirements

**ANALYSIS:** The exception **DOES NOT APPLY** because:
- Mercy Endoscopy Center LLC is an **ambulatory surgery center (ASC)**, NOT a hospital
- ASCs are licensed under 42 CFR Part 416 (Medicare conditions for coverage of ASCs)
- Hospitals are licensed under 42 CFR Part 482 (Medicare conditions of participation for hospitals)
- The whole hospital exception applies only to "hospital" ownership as defined by Medicare regulations¬π‚Å∞

**Exception C: Rural Provider Exception (42 U.S.C. ¬ß 1395nn(d)(2))**

Allows physician ownership in entities located in rural areas (outside Metropolitan Statistical Area).

**ANALYSIS:** The exception **DOES NOT APPLY** because:
- Columbus, Ohio is a Metropolitan Statistical Area (Columbus MSA, population ~2.1 million)
- Mercy facilities are located in urban/suburban areas (Columbus, Dublin, Reynoldsburg, Lancaster)

**Exception D: Academic Medical Center Exception (42 U.S.C. ¬ß 1395nn(d)(1))**

**ANALYSIS:** The exception **DOES NOT APPLY** because:
- Exception requires DHS entity to be located on the main campus of an academic medical center
- Mercy Endoscopy Center is 2 miles from main hospital campus
- Exception does not cover free-standing ASCs

**CONCLUSION:** No Stark Law exception applies. The arrangement constitutes a **STARK LAW VIOLATION**.

YES. The $35,000 annual profit distributions to each physician-owner constitute "remuneration" under 42 U.S.C. ¬ß 1320a-7b(b).¬π¬π

The ASC safe harbor protects physician investment in ASCs if certain conditions are met, including:¬π‚Å¥

### B. EMTALA Compliance

#### 1. Legal Framework

1. **Medical Screening Examination (MSE):** Provide an appropriate medical screening examination to any individual who comes to the emergency department requesting examination or treatment, to determine whether an emergency medical condition exists.

2. **Stabilizing Treatment:** If an emergency medical condition exists, provide stabilizing treatment or an appropriate transfer.

3. **No Delay for Financial Inquiry:** The examination and treatment **cannot be delayed to inquire about the individual's method of payment or insurance coverage**.¬≤‚Å∂

**Key Prohibition (42 U.S.C. ¬ß 1395dd(h)):**
> "A participating hospital may not delay provision of an appropriate medical screening examination...or further medical examination and treatment...in order to inquire about the individual's method of payment or insurance status."

### C. Physician Compensation Fair Market Value

#### 1. STARK Employment Exception Requirements (42 U.S.C. ¬ß 1395nn(e)(2))

The employment exception allows hospitals to employ physicians and pay compensation if:¬≥¬π

2. **Employment is for identifiable services**

### G. Joint Commission Accreditation and Deemed Status

**Joint Commission Deemed Status (42 CFR ¬ß 488.5)**‚Åµ‚Å∑

- CMS accepts Joint Commission survey **in lieu of** direct CMS survey
- Hospital maintains Medicare participation without annual CMS validation surveys
- Significant cost/administrative savings (Joint Commission survey every 3 years vs. CMS annual surveys)

### H. Medicare Hospital Readmission Reduction Program

#### 1. Legal Framework

**Hospital Readmissions Reduction Program (HRRP)**‚Å∂‚Å∑

Established by Affordable Care Act ¬ß 3025 (2010), codified at 42 U.S.C. ¬ß 1395ww(q):
- CMS reduces Medicare payments to hospitals with **excess readmissions** (unplanned readmissions within 30 days of discharge)
- Applies to six conditions: Acute myocardial infarction (AMI), heart failure (HF), pneumonia (PN), chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), coronary artery bypass graft (CABG), total hip/knee arthroplasty (THA/TKA)
- **Maximum penalty:** 3% reduction in Medicare payments (payment adjustment factor 0.97)‚Å∂‚Å∏

**Readmission Definition:**‚Å∂‚Åπ
- Any **unplanned admission** to **any hospital** within 30 days after discharge
- Includes readmissions for unrelated conditions (e.g., patient discharged for heart failure, readmitted for hip fracture = counted)
- Includes readmissions to different hospitals (not just same hospital)

**Performance Measurement:**‚Å∑‚Å∞
- CMS compares hospital's readmission rates to expected readmission rates (risk-adjusted for patient demographics, comorbidities)
- **Excess readmission ratio (ERR):** Actual readmissions √∑ Expected readmissions
- ERR >1.0 = excess readmissions (hospital performs worse than expected)
- ERR <1.0 = fewer readmissions (hospital performs better than expected)
