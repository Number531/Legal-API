**If Unions Exist: Successorship Obligations**

**Legal Standard:** *Fall River Dyeing & Finishing Corp. v. NLRB*, 482 U.S. 27 (1987)

New employer (successor) must recognize and bargain with union if:
1. **Majority continuity:** Majority of successor's workforce were predecessor's employees ‚úì (7,500-8,000 of 8,500 retained = 88-94%)
2. **Substantial continuity:** Same business operations, same locations ‚úì (4 hospitals, acute care, Columbus area)

**Conclusion:** If unions exist, National Healthcare Partners is **Burns successor** obligated to:
- **Recognize union** as bargaining representative (no new election required)
- **Bargain in good faith** (not required to adopt predecessor's CBA, but must negotiate new terms)
- **Effects bargaining** for layoffs: severance, bumping rights, recall rights, benefits continuation
- **Successor bar:** Union protected from challenge 6-12 months (recent 2024 Supreme Court remand under *Loper Bright* may affect doctrine)

**2024 NLRB Developments:**
- *Tramont Manufacturing*: Successor required to bargain over **effects** of layoffs even if not required to bargain over **decision** to lay off
- Supreme Court remand of successor bar case for reconsideration under *Loper Bright* (Chevron deference overturned) - may signal future changes

**Union Presence Impact on PE Integration:**
- **Constrains flexibility:** Seniority-based layoff procedures (last hired, first fired)
- **Increases costs:** CBA wage/benefit enhancements, effects bargaining severance negotiations, legal fees
- **Extends timeline:** Effects bargaining 30-90 days before layoffs, grievance procedures for disputes

#### 3. WARN Act Compliance Requirements

**60-Day Notice Obligation:**

Employer must provide written notice at least 60 calendar days before termination date to:
1. **Affected employees** or their union representatives (if unionized)
2. **State dislocated worker unit** (Ohio Department of Job and Family Services)
3. **Chief elected official** of local government where facility is located¬π¬π

**Notice Content Requirements:**

For employees:
- Statement whether action is plant closing or mass layoff
- Expected date of first separation and anticipated schedule
- Whether layoffs are expected to be permanent or temporary (if temporary, expected duration)
- Name and telephone number of company official to contact for further information
- Statement of any bumping rights (union seniority-based displacement)¬π¬≤

**Exceptions to 60-Day Notice (Rarely Applicable):**

| Exception | Requirements | Applicability to PE Acquisition |
|-----------|--------------|--------------------------------|
| **Faltering Company** | Company actively seeking capital/business; reasonable belief giving notice would prevent obtaining capital | **LOW** - Exception rarely applies post-acquisition when PE already secured financing |
| **Unforeseeable Business Circumstances** | Sudden, dramatic, unexpected circumstances not reasonably foreseeable 60 days prior | **LOW** - Planned PE integration is foreseeable at closing |
| **Natural Disaster** | Flood, earthquake, tornado, etc. | **NOT APPLICABLE** |

#### 2. Union Successorship Obligations (If Applicable)

**Legal Standard: Fall River Dyeing & Finishing Corp. v. NLRB, 482 U.S. 27 (1987)**

A new employer (successor) must recognize and bargain with an incumbent union if:
1. **Majority continuity:** Majority of successor's workforce in same bargaining unit were employees of predecessor, AND
2. **Substantial continuity:** Successor continues substantially same business operations in same location¬≤‚Å∂

**Application to Mercy Acquisition:**
- National Healthcare Partners continues operating 4 hospitals, same locations, same business (acute care)
- Majority of 8,500 employees retained (even if 500-1,000 laid off, 7,500-8,000 remain = 88-94% continuity)
- **CONCLUSION:** If unions exist at Mercy, National Healthcare Partners is a **Burns successor** obligated to recognize unions¬≤‚Å∑

**Burns Successorship Bargaining Obligations:**

**1. Recognition Obligation:**
- Successor must recognize union as collective bargaining representative
- Union retains certification/recognition status
- No need for new union election¬≤‚Å∏

**2. Bargaining Obligation:**
- Successor must bargain in good faith with union
- **NOT required to adopt predecessor's CBA terms** (wages, benefits, working conditions)
- May implement new terms and conditions, but must bargain to impasse first¬≤‚Åπ

**3. Successor Bar Doctrine:**
- Union entitled to represent employees for "reasonable period" (6-12 months) without challenge to representative status
- Successor cannot withdraw recognition or file decertification petition during successor bar period¬≥‚Å∞
- **2024 Development:** Supreme Court remanded NLRB successor bar case to D.C. Circuit for reconsideration under *Loper Bright* (Chevron deference overturned) - doctrine may be subject to future challenge¬≥¬π

#### 3. Effects Bargaining for Layoffs

**NLRB Tramont Manufacturing Decision (2024):**

Even if successor not required to bargain over **decision** to lay off employees, successor **MUST bargain over effects** of layoffs, including:
- Severance pay amounts
- Bumping rights (seniority-based displacement)
- Recall rights (priority for future openings)
- Benefits continuation (health insurance, vacation payout)
- Advance notice period (beyond WARN Act 60 days)¬≥¬≤

**Application to PE Integration:**
- If Mercy unionized, National Healthcare Partners must engage in effects bargaining before implementing 500-1,000 employee layoffs
- Union may negotiate enhanced severance, recall rights, job placement assistance
- Failure to bargain = unfair labor practice under NLRA ¬ß 8(a)(5)¬≥¬≥

#### 4. WARN Act + Union Interaction

If CBAs exist, WARN Act notice requirements **supplement** (not replace) union contractual notice requirements:
- Many CBAs require **90-180 days notice** for mass layoffs (longer than WARN 60 days)
- CBAs may require **seniority-based layoff order** (last hired, first fired)
- CBAs may require **union consultation** before finalizing layoff decisions¬≥‚Å¥

**Compliance Strategy:**
- Provide whichever notice period is LONGER (WARN 60 days vs. CBA 90 days = use 90 days)

#### 3. 401(k) Plan: Termination vs. Continuation

**Option A: Terminate Mercy 401(k) Plan, Transition to National Healthcare Partners' Plan**

**Process:**
1. **Board resolution** to terminate Mercy 401(k) plan
2. **Notice to participants** 60 days before termination (ERISA ¬ß 4041)
3. **100% vesting** of all employer contributions (accelerated vesting upon plan termination)‚Åµ‚Åµ
4. **Distribution options:** Roll over to National Healthcare Partners' plan, roll over to IRA, or take cash distribution (taxable)
5. **IRS/DOL approval** - standard termination requires plan fully funded, all benefits distributed

**Advantages:**
- Simplifies administration (single plan for all National Healthcare Partners entities)
- Reduces costs (economies of scale, lower recordkeeping fees)

**Disadvantages:**
- **Accelerated vesting** = increased cost if employees <100% vested (employees gain immediate ownership of employer contributions they would otherwise forfeit)
- Employee disruption (new fund menu, new provider, new website/app)
- Morale impact (change perceived negatively during integration uncertainty)

**Accelerated Vesting Cost Example:**
- Assume 20% of 8,500 employees (1,700) not fully vested in employer contributions

- Accelerated vesting cost: 1,700 √ó $5,000 = **$8.5 million one-time cost**‚Åµ‚Å∑

**Option B: Continue Mercy 401(k) Plan as Subsidiary Plan**

**Process:**
- National Healthcare Partners adopts Mercy 401(k) plan as subsidiary/affiliated plan
- Maintains separate plan document, trust, recordkeeping
- No accelerated vesting (employees continue vesting per existing schedule)
- Less disruption to employees

**Advantages:**
- No accelerated vesting cost
- Employee continuity (same fund menu, provider, account access)
- Gradual transition option (continue 12-24 months, then merge)

**Disadvantages:**
- Duplicate administration costs (two plan sponsors, two audits, two Form 5500 filings)
- Complexity for National Healthcare Partners (must maintain expertise in Mercy plan terms)

**Recommendation:** **Continue Mercy 401(k) plan 12-24 months** post-closing, then evaluate merger after integration stabilizes. Avoids accelerated vesting cost, reduces employee disruption.‚Åµ‚Å∏

### E. STATE EMPLOYMENT LAW (OHIO)

#### 1. Final Paycheck Timing

**Ohio Revised Code ¬ß 4113.15:**
- No specific statute requiring immediate final paycheck upon termination
- **General rule:** Employer must pay final wages by **next regular payday** (or within 30 days)
- If employer fails to pay, employee may file wage complaint with Ohio Department of Commerce‚Å∑‚Å∞

#### 2. Accrued Vacation/PTO Payment

**Ohio Law - "Deferred Payment of Earned Benefit":**

Accrued unused vacation is considered **earned compensation** that employee has legal entitlement to:
- **Default rule:** Employer must pay accrued vacation upon termination (treated as wage)
- **Exception:** Employer may adopt written "use-it-or-lose-it" forfeiture policy, IF:
  1. Policy clearly communicated to employees (employee handbook, signed acknowledgment)
  2. Policy applied prospectively (not retroactive to forfeit already-accrued time)‚Å∑¬π

**Application to PE Integration:**
- If Mercy pays out accrued vacation at termination (no forfeiture policy), PE must honor for 500-1,000 laid-off employees
- Average accrued vacation: 10-15 days (2-3 weeks)
- **Calculation (500 employees):**
  - 500 employees √ó 12.5 days average √ó $192/day (50K salary √∑ 260 work days) = **$1.2 million**‚Å∑¬≤

#### 3. Unemployment Insurance

**Mass Layoff UI Impact:**
- Employers pay UI taxes based on "experience rating" (higher claims = higher tax rate)
- If 500-1,000 employees laid off file UI claims, Mercy's (now National Healthcare Partners') Ohio UI tax rate increases
- UI tax rate increase: 0.5-1.5% of taxable wage base ($9,000 per employee in Ohio)
- **Cost increase:** 8,500 employees √ó $9,000 √ó 0.5-1.5% rate increase = **$382,500-$1.15 million annually**‚Å∑¬≥

**Misconduct Disqualification:**
- Employees terminated for cause (misconduct) disqualified from UI benefits
- Reduces employer UI tax impact
- PE should document performance issues, attendance violations, policy breaches for any "for cause" terminations to support misconduct disqualification‚Å∑‚Å¥

#### 4. Ohio COBRA (State Continuation)

**Federal COBRA:** 18 months continuation for employers 20+ employees (Mercy covered)
**Ohio State Continuation:** Ohio does not have separate state continuation law beyond federal COBRA (unlike California, Massachusetts, Connecticut)‚Å∑‚Åµ
