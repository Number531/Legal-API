#### 2. Actuarial Guideline 38 (AG 38) â€” AXXX Reserve Redundancy

**Background:**
- Regulation XXX (Valuation of Life Insurance Policies Model Regulation, NAIC Model #830) imposed increased reserves for term life insurance
- Life insurers argued reserves were "redundant" (excessive compared to expected claims)

**AG 38 Purpose:**
- Address universal life insurance with secondary guarantees (AG 38, also called AXXX)
- Similar to Regulation XXX for term life
- Established reserve requirements perceived as excessive by industry
- Insurers used captive reinsurance to cede reserves and reduce statutory capital requirements

[VERIFIED: Actuarial resources, NAIC guidance]

#### 3. Actuarial Guideline 48 (AG 48) â€” Reserve Financing Standards

**Adoption:** NAIC Executive Committee and Plenary approved AG 48 in December 2014, effective January 1, 2015

**Purpose:**
- Define rules for new XXX and AXXX reserve financing transactions
- Address "shadow insurance" concerns raised in June 2013 NAIC White Paper
- Respond to Federal Reserve concerns about captive reinsurance arrangements

**Key Requirements:**

**Primary Security vs. Other Security:**
- **Primary Security:** Assets held in captive (cash, investment-grade securities, LOCs)
- **Other Security:** Parental guarantees, support agreements
- **AG 48 Standard:** Requires minimum "Required Level of Primary Security" (calculation method specified)

**Actuarial Method:**
- Stochastic modeling to determine required capital
- Cannot rely solely on parental guarantees
- Must demonstrate adequate assets in captive to support reserves

**Regulatory Authority:**
- AG 48 is "minimum recommendation," not cure-all
- Direct quote from Guideline: "A regulator should impose requirements in addition to those set out in this actuarial guideline if the facts and circumstances warrant such action."
- Domiciliary state retains authority to disallow reserve credit

[VERIFIED: Actuarial Guideline XLVIII, NAIC documentation]

#### 4. Regulatory Scrutiny â€” Post-2013 Federal Reserve Report
