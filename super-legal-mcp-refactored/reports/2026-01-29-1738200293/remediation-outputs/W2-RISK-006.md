## Key Findings Summary

**Strategic Option Analysis** (Section IV.F) evaluates three restructuring paths with the following risk-adjusted outcomes:

1. **Option 1 - § 363 Going-Concern Sale**: $425M NPV, 70% success rate, 6-12 month timeline, 65-78% environmental offset. **Advantages**: Speed, IP value preservation (2-5× multiplier), creditor certainty, DIP financing viability. **Disadvantages**: Limited creditor negotiation, CERCLA successor liability risk (*Trainer Custom Chemical*), suboptimal NPV. **Application**: Recommended for operating businesses with valuable IP requiring expedited timeline.

2. **Option 2 - Plan-Based Reorganization**: $398M NPV, 50% success rate, 18-36 month timeline, 70-83% environmental offset. **Advantages**: Maximum discharge leverage, negotiated EPA settlement flexibility, differential creditor treatment, higher unsecured recovery (30-60%). **Disadvantages**: Extended timeline ($45M+ professional fees), 50% confirmation failure risk, feasibility demonstration challenges, going-concern value erosion. **Application**: Recommended for complex liability portfolios requiring differentiated creditor treatment and sustained operational viability.

3. **Option 3 - Hybrid Approach (RECOMMENDED)**: **$448M NPV**, 65% success rate, 8-14 month timeline, **85-95% combined environmental offset**. **Advantages**: Highest NPV ($23M-$50M premium vs. alternatives), maximum environmental offset (triple mechanism: abandonment + sale segregation + settlement), compressed timeline, IP value preservation, optimal risk allocation. **Disadvantages**: Requires 90-day pre-filing preparation, EPA pre-negotiation, secured creditor abandonment consent, stalking horse bidder identification. **Application**: Recommended for multi-facility manufacturing operations with segregable clean/contaminated assets.

**Environmental Escrow Sizing**: $85M-$135M total requirement calibrated to facility-type remediation cost ranges (Small $3-8M → $5-12M escrow; Medium $10-35M → $15-50M escrow; Large $40-200M → $60-250M escrow). Phased release over 7-10 years tied to remediation milestones: Phase 1 (Years 1-2, 20% release) upon RI/FS completion; Phase 2 (Years 3-5, 40% release) upon active remediation completion; Phase 3 (Years 6-10, 40% release) upon long-term monitoring completion. Alternative structures include purchase price adjustment for low-risk facilities and environmental insurance substitution for partial escrow reduction.

**Risk Mitigation Effectiveness**: Comprehensive strategy achieves **95% risk reduction** from $808.1M gross aggregate exposure to $41M net residual exposure through 7-layer protection framework: (1) Bankruptcy offset mechanisms 46-83% (discharge, classification, EPA settlement, abandonment, § 363 segregation); (2) Environmental escrow $85M-$135M; (3) EPA prospective purchaser agreement; (4) R&W insurance $25M-$45M; (5) Purchase price adjustment $25M-$50M; (6) Indemnification provisions ($500K basket, $100M cap, 10-year survival); (7) Regulatory compliance framework (PA Act 2, deed restrictions, financial assurance). ### Environmental Escrow Agreement - Phased Release Structure

**ENVIRONMENTAL ESCROW AGREEMENT**

THIS ENVIRONMENTAL ESCROW AGREEMENT (this "**Agreement**") is entered into as of [CLOSING DATE], by and among [BUYER ENTITY] ("**Buyer**"), [SELLER ENTITY] (the "**Seller**"), and [ESCROW AGENT] (the "**Escrow Agent**").

**WHEREAS**, pursuant to that certain Asset Purchase Agreement dated [DATE] (the "**APA**"), Buyer is acquiring certain manufacturing operations, intellectual property, and real property from Seller; and

**WHEREAS**, the Parties acknowledge that certain owned or operated facilities may require environmental investigation, remediation, and long-term monitoring under the Comprehensive Environmental Response, Compensation, and Liability Act (CERCLA), the Resource Conservation and Recovery Act (RCRA), the Clean Water Act, the Clean Air Act, and applicable state environmental laws; and

**WHEREAS**, to provide financial assurance for such environmental obligations and protect Buyer from undisclosed environmental conditions, the Parties have agreed to establish an environmental escrow with phased release tied to remediation milestones;

**NOW, THEREFORE**, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements contained herein, the Parties agree as follows:

**ARTICLE I - ESCROW ESTABLISHMENT AND FUNDING**

**Section 1.1 Escrow Amount**. At the Closing, Seller shall deposit with Escrow Agent, from the proceeds of the § 363 sale, the sum of **$[85,000,000 - $135,000,000]** (the "**Escrow Amount**"), to be held and disbursed in accordance with this Agreement. The Escrow Amount has been calculated based on the facility-specific remediation cost analysis set forth in Section IV.C of the Legal Research Memorandum dated January 29, 2026, reflecting lifecycle costs for [specify facility portfolio: e.g., "two medium-sized manufacturing facilities (5-50 acres) and one large steel/coke production facility (>50 acres)"] applying a 1.5× contingency multiplier for unknown environmental conditions.

**Section 1.2 Escrow Account**. Escrow Agent shall deposit the Escrow Amount in a separate interest-bearing account (the "**Escrow Account**") at [BANK NAME], with interest accruing to the benefit of Seller and released upon distribution of the corresponding Escrow Amount pursuant to the milestone schedule in Article II.

**Section 1.3 Permitted Investments**. Escrow Agent shall invest the Escrow Amount in: (a) direct obligations of the United States government with maturities not exceeding the anticipated disbursement timeline; (b) AAA-rated money market funds; or (c) FDIC-insured bank deposits, provided that such investments are readily liquidable to meet draw requests and milestone releases without penalty or material loss of principal.

**ARTICLE II - PHASED RELEASE SCHEDULE**

**Section 2.1 Three-Phase Release Structure**. The Escrow Amount shall be released to Seller in three phases over a period of seven to ten years following the Closing Date, contingent upon achievement of the environmental remediation milestones set forth below and satisfaction of the conditions precedent in Section 2.5.

**Section 2.2 Phase 1 Release (Investigation and Planning Phase - Years 1-2)**

**(a) Release Amount**: Twenty percent (20%) of the Escrow Amount = **$[17,000,000 - $27,000,000]**

**(b) Milestone Requirements** (all conditions must be satisfied):

(i) **Phase II Environmental Site Assessment Completion**: Buyer has completed comprehensive Phase II ESAs for all facilities identified in Schedule A, conducted by qualified environmental consultant (ASTM E1527-21 and E1903-19 standards), including soil borings, groundwater monitoring well installation and sampling, and analytical testing for Hazardous Substances (as defined in the APA);

(ii) **Remedial Investigation / Feasibility Study (RI/FS) Completion**: Buyer has submitted to EPA Region 3 and Pennsylvania Department of Environmental Protection (PA DEP) a completed RI/FS report documenting: (A) nature and extent of contamination at each facility; (B) risk assessment under EPA Regional Screening Levels for industrial use; (C) remedial alternatives analysis evaluating excavation, in-situ treatment, containment, and institutional controls; (D) comparative analysis of alternatives using EPA nine criteria (40 C.F.R. § 300.430(e)(9)); (E) recommended remedy with cost estimate and implementation timeline;

(iii) **Remedial Action Plan (RAP) Approval**: EPA Region 3 or PA DEP (as applicable) has issued written approval of the selected remedy through: (A) Record of Decision (ROD) for CERCLA sites; (B) Pennsylvania Act 2 Notice of Intent to Remediate approval for state voluntary cleanup program; or (C) RCRA Corrective Action Plan approval; and such approved remedy is consistent with the cost estimates and timelines used to establish the Escrow Amount;

(iv) **No Materially Adverse Remediation Findings**: The Phase II ESAs and RI/FS have not revealed contamination requiring remediation costs exceeding 125% of the facility-specific cost estimates set forth in Schedule B (i.e., contamination severity is within the contemplated range used to size the Escrow Amount); for purposes of this condition, "materially adverse" contamination includes: groundwater contamination exceeding EPA Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs) extending beyond facility boundaries, presence of dense non-aqueous phase liquid (DNAPL) requiring enhanced remediation, identification of additional Hazardous Substances not previously disclosed in Seller's environmental disclosures;

(v) **Regulatory Compliance Status**: Buyer has maintained all required environmental permits (NPDES, air emissions, RCRA) in good standing during Phase 1 period, with no unresolved Notice of Violation or enforcement actions from EPA or PA DEP related to ongoing facility operations (as distinguished from pre-Closing contamination).

**(c) Milestone Documentation**: Buyer shall provide to Escrow Agent and Seller:
- Certified copies of Phase II ESA reports with professional engineer/geologist seal
- EPA or PA DEP approval letter for Remedial Action Plan with file-stamped date
- Third-party environmental consultant certification that milestone conditions are satisfied
- Updated cost estimate for approved remedy demonstrating consistency with Escrow Amount adequacy

**(d) Timeline**: Phase 1 milestone achievement anticipated 18-24 months post-Closing based on typical RI/FS regulatory review timeline; Buyer shall provide quarterly written updates to Seller and Escrow Agent on Phase 1 milestone progress.

**Section 2.3 Phase 2 Release (Active Remediation Phase - Years 3-5)**

**(a) Release Amount**: Forty percent (40%) of the Escrow Amount = **$[34,000,000 - $54,000,000]**

**(b) Milestone Requirements** (all conditions must be satisfied):

(i) **Active Remediation Completion**: Buyer has completed all physical remediation activities specified in the approved Remedial Action Plan, including: (A) soil excavation and off-site disposal or treatment (if applicable); (B) groundwater extraction and treatment system installation and startup (if applicable); (C) soil vapor extraction or air sparging system installation (if applicable); (D) engineered cap or barrier installation with required depth, permeability, and vegetation specifications; (E) installation of monitoring wells and institutional control infrastructure;

(ii) **Regulatory Closeout of Active Phase**: EPA Region 3 or PA DEP has issued written determination that active remediation is complete and facility has transitioned to long-term monitoring phase through: (A) Preliminary Closeout Report acceptance for CERCLA sites; (B) Remedial Action Completion Report approval for Pennsylvania Act 2 sites; or (C) satisfactory completion inspection documentation for RCRA Corrective Action;

(iii) **Confirmation Sampling Results**: Post-remediation confirmation sampling conducted by independent third-party laboratory demonstrates contaminant concentrations below applicable cleanup standards: (A) EPA Regional Screening Levels for industrial use; (B) Pennsylvania Act 2 Statewide Health Standards for soil and groundwater; (C) site-specific risk-based standards approved by regulatory authority; and such sampling results are documented in report submitted to and accepted by regulatory authority;

(iv) **Institutional Controls Recorded**: All required institutional controls have been properly recorded and are legally enforceable, including: (A) deed restrictions prohibiting residential use and limiting groundwater use (recorded in county land records with perpetual duration); (B) environmental covenant pursuant to Pennsylvania Uniform Environmental Covenants Act (27 Pa. C.S. §§ 6501-6517); (C) groundwater use restriction area designation (if applicable); (D) vapor barrier maintenance requirements for occupied structures;

(v) **Cost Consistency**: Total costs incurred for Phase 2 active remediation do not exceed 125% of the Phase 2 budget allocation from the facility-specific cost estimates in Schedule B; if costs exceed this threshold, Phase 2 release amount shall be reduced pro rata to maintain adequate remaining escrow for Phase 3 long-term monitoring.

**(c) Milestone Documentation**: Buyer shall provide to Escrow Agent and Seller:
- Remedial Action Completion Report with regulatory acceptance letter
- Confirmation sampling analytical results certified by independent laboratory
- Copies of recorded deed restrictions and environmental covenants with recording information
- Cost accounting report documenting Phase 2 expenditures with supporting invoices
- Third-party environmental consultant certification of milestone satisfaction

**(d) Timeline**: Phase 2 milestone achievement anticipated 3-5 years post-Closing (cumulative 4.5-6.5 years from Closing) based on typical active remediation duration for medium-to-large manufacturing facilities; timeline may be extended for complex groundwater treatment systems requiring multi-year operation before cleanup standards achieved.

**Section 2.4 Phase 3 Release (Long-Term Monitoring Phase - Years 6-10)**

**(a) Release Amount**: Forty percent (40%) of the Escrow Amount = **$[34,000,000 - $54,000,000]** plus all accrued interest

**(b) Milestone Requirements** (all conditions must be satisfied):

(i) **Long-Term Monitoring Completion**: Buyer has completed the full long-term monitoring period specified in the approved Remedial Action Plan (typically 5-10 years post-active remediation, or 30 years total for groundwater contamination cases), including: (A) semi-annual or annual groundwater monitoring well sampling per approved plan; (B) biennial soil vapor monitoring for volatile organic compound contamination; (C) five-year reviews for CERCLA sites (40 C.F.R. § 300.430(f)(4)(ii)) with satisfactory regulatory acceptance; (D) institutional control maintenance and inspection reports;

(ii) **Final Regulatory Closeout**: EPA Region 3 or PA DEP has issued final closeout documentation confirming no further action required: (A) Final Closeout Report acceptance and Superfund site delisting for CERCLA sites; (B) Pennsylvania Act 2 Release of Liability under 25 Pa. Code § 245.312 or Site-Specific Standard attainment; (C) RCRA Corrective Action Complete with Controls determination; and such final determination contains no ongoing monitoring, maintenance, or financial assurance requirements;

(iii) **No Environmental Claims**: During the Phase 3 monitoring period, no third-party environmental claims, governmental enforcement actions, or citizen suits have been filed relating to pre-Closing contamination at the facilities, and no contamination migration beyond facility boundaries has occurred requiring off-site response action;

(iv) **Contaminant Trend Analysis**: Groundwater and soil vapor monitoring data demonstrate: (A) stable or decreasing contaminant concentrations over the monitoring period (no increasing trends indicating remedy failure); (B) no exceedances of cleanup standards in final three consecutive sampling events; (C) monitored natural attenuation validation (if MNA is component of remedy) showing plume stability and degradation consistent with remedy design assumptions;

(v) **Institutional Control Compliance**: All institutional controls remain in effect and have been properly maintained throughout Phase 3 period, with no violations, unpermitted uses, or unauthorized modifications; Buyer has submitted all required institutional control compliance reports to regulatory authority on schedule.

**(c) Milestone Documentation**: Buyer shall provide to Escrow Agent and Seller:
- Final Closeout Report or Release of Liability documentation from EPA/PA DEP
- Complete monitoring data package for Phase 3 period with trend analysis
- Certification that no environmental claims filed during monitoring period
- Institutional control compliance reports and inspection documentation
- Third-party environmental consultant final certification

**(d) Timeline**: Phase 3 release anticipated 7-10 years post-Closing (cumulative 10-15 years from Closing) depending on monitoring duration requirements; for sites with groundwater contamination requiring 30-year post-remediation monitoring, parties may negotiate alternative release schedule or substitution of financial assurance mechanism (environmental trust, surety bond) for remaining escrow amount after Year 10.

**Section 2.5 Conditions Precedent to All Releases**

No release of Escrow Amount shall occur unless the following conditions precedent are satisfied at the time of the requested release:

(a) **No Material Breach**: Neither Buyer nor Seller is in material breach of any provision of the APA, this Agreement, or any related transaction document, and no event of default has occurred and is continuing under any such agreement;

(b) **Survival Period**: The environmental representations and warranties in Article III of the APA remain in full force and effect (10-year survival period), or if the applicable survival period has expired, Buyer has not provided timely notice of any environmental claim prior to survival period expiration that remains unresolved;

(c) **Escrow Adequacy**: The remaining Escrow Amount (after proposed release) is adequate to fund the anticipated costs for remaining remediation milestones based on updated cost estimates; if updated cost estimates project a shortfall, Seller may cure by depositing additional funds to maintain adequate escrow, or the requested release amount shall be reduced to maintain minimum 110% escrow adequacy ratio;

(d) **Regulatory Stability**: No material adverse change in applicable environmental laws or regulatory requirements that would impose materially increased remediation obligations beyond those contemplated in the approved Remedial Action Plan; for purposes of this condition, "material adverse change" means regulatory requirement increasing total remediation costs by more than 15% (e.g., EPA lowering cleanup standards, requiring additional contaminants to be addressed, mandating enhanced remedy).

**Section 2.6 Dispute Resolution for Milestone Determination**

**(a) Joint Determination**: Buyer and Seller shall jointly review milestone documentation and provide written consent to Escrow Agent for each phased release within 30 days of Buyer's submission of milestone documentation.

**(b) Dispute Procedure**: If Seller disputes that milestone conditions are satisfied, Seller shall provide written objection to Buyer and Escrow Agent within 30 days of receiving milestone documentation, specifying with particularity which condition(s) are not satisfied and the factual basis for objection.

**(c) Environmental Expert Determination**: If parties cannot resolve dispute within 15 days of Seller's objection, the dispute shall be submitted to binding determination by an independent environmental expert (the "Expert") jointly selected by parties (or if parties cannot agree, appointed by American Arbitration Association). Expert shall: (i) have minimum 20 years experience in CERCLA remediation and Pennsylvania environmental compliance; (ii) review milestone documentation, site inspection reports, regulatory correspondence, and any supplemental information requested; (iii) issue written determination within 45 days of appointment stating whether milestone conditions are satisfied and, if not, what additional actions are required for satisfaction; (iv) allocate fees and expenses between parties based on reasonableness of positions.

**(d) Partial Release**: If Expert determines that milestone conditions are substantially satisfied but minor deficiencies exist, Expert may authorize partial release (e.g., 75-90% of phase amount) with remaining balance released upon cure of identified deficiencies.

**ARTICLE III - ESCROW DRAWS FOR REMEDIATION COSTS**

**Section 3.1 Draw Eligibility**. Buyer may submit draw requests against the Escrow Amount to fund environmental investigation, remediation, and monitoring costs for pre-Closing contamination at the facilities, subject to the limitations and procedures in this Article III. Draw requests are available only for costs directly related to environmental conditions existing as of the Closing Date, not for costs arising from post-Closing operations, regulatory non-compliance, or new contamination.

**Section 3.2 Draw Request Procedure**. Buyer shall submit draw requests to Escrow Agent and Seller containing: (a) itemized invoice listing costs incurred, with copies of vendor invoices and payment documentation; (b) certification that costs relate to pre-Closing contamination and are consistent with approved Remedial Action Plan; (c) environmental consultant review letter confirming costs are reasonable and necessary; (d) certification that Buyer has paid the costs from its own funds and seeks reimbursement from escrow (or invoices are due within 15 days).

**Section 3.3 Seller Review Period**. Seller shall have 15 business days to review draw request and provide written objection to Escrow Agent and Buyer. If no objection received within 15 days, Escrow Agent shall disburse requested amount to Buyer. If Seller objects, parties shall meet and confer for 10 days to resolve dispute; if unresolved, dispute resolution procedure in Section 2.6(c) shall apply.

**Section 3.4 Draw Limitations**.
(a) **Maximum Draw Percentages**: Draws from each phase allocation shall not exceed: Phase 1: 100% of Phase 1 release amount before Phase 1 milestones achieved; Phase 2: 100% of Phase 2 release amount before Phase 2 milestones achieved; Phase 3: 100% of Phase 3 release amount.

(b) **Minimum Retention**: Escrow Agent shall retain in Escrow Account at all times an amount equal to 110% of the estimated costs for remaining milestones (based on approved Remedial Action Plan cost estimate), to ensure adequate funds available for completion.

(c) **Aggregate Draw Cap**: Total draws shall not exceed the Escrow Amount; if actual remediation costs exceed Escrow Amount, Buyer bears the excess (subject to any indemnification claims against Seller under the APA for undisclosed environmental conditions exceeding materiality thresholds).

**ARTICLE IV - TERM AND TERMINATION**

**Section 4.1 Term**. This Agreement shall continue until the earlier of: (a) disbursement of all Escrow Amount and accrued interest pursuant to phased releases and draws; (b) 15 years from the Closing Date (at which time any remaining balance shall be released to Seller unless environmental obligations remain uncompleted due to Buyer delay or regulatory requirements beyond parties' control); (c) written agreement of Buyer and Seller to terminate.

**Section 4.2 Early Release - Full Remediation Completion**. Notwithstanding the phased release schedule, if Buyer completes all environmental investigation, remediation, and monitoring obligations and obtains final regulatory closeout before the scheduled Phase 3 release date, all remaining Escrow Amount plus accrued interest shall be released to Seller within 30 days of receipt of final closeout documentation, provided no unresolved environmental claims exist.
