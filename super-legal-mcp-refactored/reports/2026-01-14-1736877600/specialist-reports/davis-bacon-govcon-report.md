## I. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### Overview

This research memorandum analyzes the Davis-Bacon Act compliance risks and government contract implications arising from the Department of Labor (DOL) Wage and Hour Division investigation of United Environmental Services Corporation (UESC), initiated October 2024. The investigation alleges systematic underpayment of prevailing wages on 12 federal contracts from 2021-2024, affecting an estimated 200-400 employees and exposing UESC to $2.8M-$8.5M in back wage liability. More significantly, the investigation creates 25-35% probability of federal contractor debarment, which would eliminate $285M in annual federal contract revenue and potentially impact $135M in state/local government contracts.

#### 5. Novation Requirements and Transaction Structure Optimization (FAR 42.12)

**Anti-Assignment Act (41 U.S.C. Â§ 6305):** Federal law prohibits transfer of government contracts without express Government consent. "Despite the executed buy-sell agreement or asset purchase agreement, the transfer of a federal government contract is illegal unless the government gives its expressed consent under FAR Subpart 42.12."

**When Novation Required vs. NOT Required:**
- **REQUIRED** (FAR 42.1204(a)): Sale of assets with assumption of liabilities; transfer of assets incident to merger/consolidation; incorporation of proprietorship/partnership
- **NOT REQUIRED** (FAR 42.1204(d)): Change in ownership via **stock purchase** with no legal change in contracting party

**Stock Purchase Advantage - No Novation Required:**
- Same legal entity (UESC) continues as contractor; no change in contracting party
- Avoids 2-6 month novation approval process post-closing
- Eliminates contracting officer discretion risk (approval contingent on DOL investigation resolution)
- Maintains contract continuity without disruption

**Asset Purchase Disadvantage - Novation Required:**
- Contracting officer discretion: "The contracting officer is not obligated to approve a novation agreement, even though FAR 42.1204 allows for it." Factors considered include: (i) transferee's ability to perform, (ii) pending investigations or enforcement actions, (iii) responsibility determination under FAR 9.104, (iv) past performance.
- Timeline: Contracting officer notifies contract administration offices, requests comments within 30 days, but **no mandatory approval deadline** - typically 2-6 months overall. ABA recommended 90-day standard not yet adopted.
- DOL Investigation Impact: If investigation unresolved at novation request, contracting officer likely delays approval pending investigation outcome. If debarment occurs before approval, contracting officer **MUST deny novation** (transferee ineligible).

**Novation Process Documentation (FAR 42.1204(b)):**
- Three signed copies of proposed novation agreement
- Purchase/sale agreement or memorandum of understanding
- Transferee responsibility determination documentation: financial statements, past performance information, organizational structure, **FAPIIS disclosure of DOL investigation**

**Successor Liability Under Novation:**
Standard novation agreement (FAR 42.1203(e)): "The transferee assumes all the transferor's obligations under the contract." This includes:
- Outstanding Davis-Bacon back wages owed on novated contracts
- Potential liquidated damages accruing during investigation
- Compliance with enhanced reporting requirements if settlement imposed

**Small Business Set-Aside Affiliation Risk:**
"The legal effect of a transfer by novation could be that the seller is now affiliated with the buying company, which can be a disastrous result and violation of SBA affiliation rules." Novation triggers size recertification within 30 days (SBA regulations). If affiliation with acquirer exceeds SBA size standards, UESC loses small business set-aside contracts (**$30M-$50M estimated exposure**).
