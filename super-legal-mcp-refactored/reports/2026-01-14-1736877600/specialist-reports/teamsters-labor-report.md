## IV. DETAILED ANALYSIS

### A. NLRA Successorship Doctrine - Duty to Bargain with Incumbent Union

#### 1. Legal Framework: *Burns* and *Fall River* Supreme Court Precedent

**Duty to Recognize and Bargain (But Not Bound by Predecessor's CBA):**

In *NLRB v. Burns International Security Services, Inc.*, 406 U.S. 272 (1972), the Supreme Court established the foundational successorship doctrine under the National Labor Relations Act (NLRA). [VERIFIED: Supreme Court precedent] The Court held:

1. **Duty to Bargain:** Where the bargaining unit remains unchanged and a majority of the employees hired by the new employer were represented by a recently certified bargaining agent, the new employer must recognize and bargain with the incumbent union under NLRA Â§Â§ 8(a)(5) and 9(a). 406 U.S. at 278-81.

2. **Not Bound by Predecessor's CBA:** Successor employers are NOT automatically bound by the substantive provisions of a collective bargaining agreement negotiated by their predecessors unless the successor explicitly agrees to or assumes such terms. 406 U.S. at 284-87.

3. **Initial Terms of Employment:** A successor employer has the right to set initial terms and conditions of employment unilaterally (without bargaining) unless it is a "perfectly clear successor" (discussed below). 406 U.S. at 294-95.

**"Substantial Continuity" Test:**

In *Fall River Dyeing & Finishing Corp. v. NLRB*, 482 U.S. 27 (1987), the Supreme Court refined the successorship analysis, establishing the **"substantial continuity" test** based on the totality of circumstances:

> "The Board's approach, sanctioned by this Court in Burns, is to examine a number of factors: whether the business of both employers is essentially the same; whether the employees of the new company are doing the same jobs in the same working conditions under the same supervisors; and whether the new entity has the same production process, produces the same products, and basically has the same body of customers." 482 U.S. at 43.

**Key Holdings from *Fall River*:**

- **Hiatus Not Dispositive:** A 7-month gap between predecessor's closure and successor's startup does not preclude successorship if other factors indicate continuity. 482 U.S. at 44.

- **Employee Perspective:** The analysis focuses on whether employees' "job situations are essentially unaltered" from their perspective. 482 U.S. at 43.

- **Substantial and Representative Complement Rule:** The determination of whether a majority of the successor's workforce consists of predecessor employees is made when the successor has hired a "substantial and representative complement" of its intended workforce. 482 U.S. at 50-52.

#### 2. Stock Purchase vs. Asset Purchase: Critical Distinction

**Stock Purchase (UESC Transaction Structure):**

In a **stock purchase**, the employing entity remains unchangedâ€”only the ownership of the corporate entity changes. The acquired company continues as the same legal employer, and:

- **All CBAs remain in full force and effect** automatically.
- **No successorship analysis required** because the employer entity is legally identical.
- **Buyer steps into seller's shoes** for all labor obligations, including grievance arbitrations, unfair labor practice charges, and multi-employer pension withdrawal liability.

[VERIFIED: Fisher Phillips, "Acquiring a Unionized Entity: The Practical, Legal, and Business Considerations" (2024)]

**Asset Purchase (Alternative Structure):**

In an **asset purchase**, the buyer creates or uses a separate legal entity and purchases only selected assets. Successorship obligations depend on the *Fall River* substantial continuity test:

- **Successorship NOT automatic** but depends on totality of circumstances.
- **Buyer can avoid CBA assumption** by making it "perfectly clear" to employees and union that new terms will apply (discussed in Section IV.A.4 below).
- **Duty to bargain arises** if (i) substantial continuity exists AND (ii) majority of new workforce consists of predecessor's union employees.

[VERIFIED: Jones Day, "NLRB: Successor Employer Determination Must Be Made When Buyer Takes Control of Business" (2015)]

#### 4. "Perfectly Clear Successor" Doctrine: Avoiding CBA Assumption in Asset Purchase

**Doctrine Overview:**

A successor employer normally has the right to set **initial terms of employment unilaterally** before bargaining with the union. *Burns*, 406 U.S. at 294-95. However, the "perfectly clear successor" exception applies when the new employer's conduct misleads employees into believing they will be retained **without change** to wages, hours, or working conditions. In that scenario, the successor must:

1. **Bargain before setting initial terms**, and
2. **Honor the predecessor's CBA terms** until a new agreement is negotiated.

**Requirements to Avoid "Perfectly Clear" Status:**

To preserve the right to set new initial terms, the acquirer must **"clearly announce its intent to establish a new set of conditions prior to, or simultaneously with, its expression of intent to retain the predecessor's employees."** [VERIFIED: NLRB doctrine, reaffirmed in *Ridgewood Health Care Center*, 367 NLRB No. 110 (2019)]

**2019 *Ridgewood* Narrowing:**

The NLRB's 2019 *Ridgewood Health Care Center* decision narrowed the "perfectly clear successor" doctrine, limiting it to cases where the successor retains **"all" or "substantially all"** of the predecessor's unit employees. [VERIFIED: Jackson Kelly analysis, "A Perfectly Clear Win for Successor Employers in Recent NLRB Decision" (2019)]

**Application to UESC:**

**In Stock Purchase:** Doctrine irrelevantâ€”CBAs automatically continue.

**In Asset Purchase:** If acquirer announces new terms (e.g., different wage scales, healthcare contributions) **before or simultaneously with** job offers to UESC's 8,200 Teamsters employees:

âœ“ Acquirer avoids "perfectly clear successor" status
âœ“ Can set initial terms unilaterally
âœ“ Duty to bargain arises after hiring majority of predecessor employees
âœ“ NOT bound by existing CBA wage/benefit terms

**However:** Acquirer still has **duty to recognize and bargain** with Teamsters Locals 104, 492, 839, 986, 710, etc. under *Burns*/*Fall River*. The only difference is acquirer can set initial terms before bargaining begins.

### E. Multi-Employer Pension Withdrawal Liability

#### 1. ERISA Statutory Framework

**Complete Withdrawal (ERISA Â§ 4203, 29 U.S.C. Â§ 1383):**

A **complete withdrawal** occurs when an employer:
1. Permanently ceases to have an obligation to contribute under the plan, OR
2. Permanently ceases all covered operations under the plan

**Partial Withdrawal (ERISA Â§ 4205, 29 U.S.C. Â§ 1385):**

A **partial withdrawal** occurs when:
1. **70% Decline Test:** Employer's contribution base units decline by 70% or more compared to the average of the two highest of the five preceding plan years, OR
2. **Facility Withdrawal:** Employer permanently ceases to have an obligation to contribute under one or more (but not all) CBAs, OR
3. **Partial Cessation:** Employer permanently ceases to have an obligation to contribute for work performed at one or more (but not all) facilities

[VERIFIED: 29 U.S.C. Â§Â§ 1383, 1385; PBGC Technical Update 10-3]

**Allocable share** determined by plan's allocation method (statutory default or plan-adopted alternative):
- Most common: "Presumptive method" based on employer's share of contributions over prior 5 years

- Does NOT assume UESC's CBA obligations, OR
- Ceases contributions to multi-employer pension plans and establishes new 401(k) or different pension plan
