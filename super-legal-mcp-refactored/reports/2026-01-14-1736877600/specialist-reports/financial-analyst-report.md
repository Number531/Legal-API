## IV. MONTE CARLO SCENARIO MODELING

### Methodology Overview

This section models three probability-weighted scenarios to quantify expected transaction exposure under different risk materialization assumptions:

- **Base Case (50th Percentile)**: Expected outcome using midpoint estimates and stated probabilities
- **Downside Case (75th Percentile)**: Adverse but plausible outcome using upper-midpoint estimates (75% of range) with probability increases of 15-20%
- **Severe Downside (90th Percentile)**: Multiple adverse events with upper bound estimates and near-maximum probabilities (80-95%)

**Key Modeling Assumptions**:
1. **Independence vs. Correlation**: Base Case assumes partial independence; Downside scenarios model correlation effects (see Section V)
2. **Probability Distributions**: Triangular distributions used (min, most likely, max) for continuous variables; binomial for binary events
3. **Time Horizon**: 7-year hold period for IRR calculations; NPV discounted at 10% WACC
4. **Tax Effects**: All exposures netted for tax benefits at 25.2% combined rate where deductible
