## I. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### Overview

This report analyzes climate change regulations and ESG compliance obligations for United Environmental Services Corporation (UESC) in connection with the proposed $3.6 billion acquisition by American Sustainability Partners LLC (ASP LLC). UESC operates 6 municipal solid waste (MSW) landfills and 2,400 collection trucks across Arizona, Nevada, New Mexico, and California, generating **895,000 metric tons CO2e annually** (Scope 1+2 emissions). The waste management sector faces increasing federal and state climate regulations, private equity ESG reporting requirements, and physical climate risks.

**CRITICAL FINDING:** California Advanced Clean Fleets regulations requiring 100% zero-emission vehicle conversion by 2042 were **REPEALED in January 2025**, eliminating the anticipated **$450 million fleet electrification mandate**. This dramatically reduces UESC's near-term climate capital obligations from $450M to $15M-$25M voluntary investment for competitive positioning.

### Key Findings

#### 1. SEC Climate Disclosure Rules: Stayed, Contingent Risk ($325K-$650K)

The SEC adopted final climate disclosure rules (17 C.F.R. ¬ß 229.1500) in March 2024 requiring large accelerated filers to disclose Scope 1 and Scope 2 GHG emissions if material. However, the SEC voluntarily stayed implementation in April 2024 amid multi-circuit litigation, and in March 2025 withdrew defense of the rules. Litigation is held in abeyance as of January 2026. **No current compliance obligation.**

**Applicability to UESC:**
- **Critical Question:** Is ASP LLC a publicly reporting company (Forms 10-K, 10-Q)?
- **If YES:** UESC's 895,000 MT CO2e emissions would be **material** and require disclosure if rules are reinstated (36x the EPA's 25,000 MT mandatory reporting threshold)
- **If NO:** No SEC obligations; PE limited partner ESG requirements apply instead (see #4 below)

**Compliance Cost (if rules reinstated and ASP LLC publicly reporting):**
- First-year: $325,000-$650,000 (GHG inventory, third-party verification, TCFD climate risk assessment)
- Annual recurring: $75,000-$150,000 (GHG reporting and assurance)

**Probability Assessment:** 30-40% rules reinstated by 2027 [METHODOLOGY: Policy uncertainty under current administration; historical pattern of climate rule reinstatement under Democratic administrations]

**Recommendation:** If ASP LLC is SEC-reporting, begin voluntary GHG measurement and TCFD disclosures to position for potential reinstatement and meet investor expectations.
