### A. Current Insurance Program Architecture (Policy Period Jan 1, 2024 - Jan 1, 2025)

**Environmental Impairment Liability (EIL) - Primary and Excess Layers:**

UESC maintains a multi-carrier environmental insurance tower providing $300M total coverage:
- **Primary Layer (AIG Environmental):** $100M per claim / $100M aggregate for sudden and gradual pollution, cleanup costs, third-party bodily injury/property damage claims
- **Excess Layer 1 (Zurich):** $50M excess of $100M (follow-form to primary)
- **Excess Layer 2 (Chubb):** $75M excess of $150M (follow-form to primary)
- **Excess Layer 3 (Lloyd's of London):** $75M excess of $225M (follow-form to primary)

**Coverage Scope:** The EIL policy provides claims-made-and-reported coverage for environmental contamination cleanup costs, CERCLA defense costs and response cost liability, RCRA corrective action expenses, and third-party claims for pollution-related bodily injury or property damage. Defense costs are covered in addition to policy limits (not eroded from the $300M tower).

**Commercial General Liability (CGL) - Environmental Exclusion:**

UESC's CGL policy (Liberty Mutual, $50M per occurrence / $100M aggregate) contains the standard **absolute pollution exclusion** adopted industry-wide in 1986. As established in *Quadrant Corp. v. American States Insurance Co.*, 154 Wash.2d 165 (2005), this exclusion "unambiguously precludes coverage" for all environmental contamination claims, meaning UESC has **zero CGL coverage** for landfill leachate, groundwater contamination, CERCLA liabilities, or RCRA corrective action. The company relies exclusively on its EIL policy for environmental claims.

**Professional Liability (E&O):** XL Catlin provides $25M per claim / $50M aggregate coverage for errors and omissions in environmental remediation services ($180M annual revenue, Fort Huachuca $350M IDIQ contract, Hanford $120M subcontract). Coverage extends to professional negligence claims such as site characterization errors, remediation design flaws, and regulatory closure failures. **Critical exclusions:** Warranties and guarantees are excluded, creating a $5-10M coverage gap for the Western Refinery 5-year performance warranty (expires 2033).

**Directors & Officers (D&O):** Chubb provides $50M Side A/B/C coverage for director and officer liability, including regulatory investigations (EPA, DOL, OSHA), derivative lawsuits, and breach of fiduciary duty claims. Change of control provisions require **6-year tail coverage** ($1.6M-$2.4M premium) to cover pre-closing wrongful acts discovered post-closing, such as Davis-Bacon violations leading to debarment or Superfund contribution claims against former officers.

### D. Insurance Archaeology: $50M-$150M Historical Recovery Potential

**Pre-1986 CGL Policies - No Pollution Exclusion:** Commercial general liability policies issued before 1986 did not contain the absolute pollution exclusion and provided broad occurrence-based coverage for environmental contamination. Policies from the 1960s-1970s are particularly valuable because they typically included no aggregate limits, unlimited defense costs, and no pollution exclusions whatsoever. UESC's operations dating to 1987 and 14 corporate acquisitions (1995-2022) create substantial potential for historical policy recovery.

**Continuous Trigger Doctrine:** Under *Owens-Illinois, Inc. v. United Insurance Co.*, 138 N.J. 437 (1994), environmental contamination that occurs progressively over multiple years triggers all CGL policies in effect from initial contamination exposure through manifestation of injury. For example, if landfill groundwater contamination began in 1990 and continued through 2020 before discovery in 2024, all CGL policies from 1990-2020 are potentially triggered and must contribute to cleanup costs.

**Coverage Opportunities:**

**a) UESC Policies (1987-1990s):** If UESC purchased CGL policies in 1987-1989 before the absolute pollution exclusion became universal, those policies may contain only the "sudden and accidental" pollution exclusion, which New Jersey courts have interpreted favorably to policyholders under the *Morton International, Inc. v. General Accident Insurance Co.*, 134 N.J. 1 (1993) regulatory estoppel doctrine. Potential coverage: $10M-$25M per policy year x 5-10 years = $50M-$250M.

**b) Acquired Companies (14 Entities, 1995-2022):** Each company UESC acquired likely maintained its own CGL insurance prior to acquisition. Under successor-in-interest principles, UESC may have coverage rights to those historical policies for pre-acquisition contamination that triggers liability post-acquisition. Potential coverage: 14 companies x 10 years average operations x $2M-$10M limits = $280M-$1.4B (subject to significant allocation disputes).

**c) Pre-1986 Predecessor Operations:** If any UESC predecessor entities or acquired companies operated waste facilities before 1986, their CGL policies would be especially valuable with no pollution exclusions. A single pre-1986 policy could provide $1M-$5M in limits with no aggregate cap.

**Realistic Recovery Estimate:** Based on insurance industry experience with companies of UESC's profile, insurance archaeology typically recovers $50M-$150M through a combination of policy identification, coverage litigation, and negotiated settlements with historical carriers. **Investment required:** $150K-$300K to retain specialized insurance archaeology firm (Restorical Research, PolicyFind, Insurance Archaeology Services). **Timeline:** 8-21 months from initiation through settlement/recovery.

**Critical Recommendation:** Buyer should engage insurance archaeology firm within 30 days of transaction announcement. Potential $50M-$150M recovery substantially exceeds the $150K-$300K cost and directly offsets the $48M-$130M uninsured Superfund exposure.

**Regulatory Framework:**¬≤‚Å¥

1. **Closure costs** (40 CFR ¬ß 264.142)
2. **Post-closure care costs** (40 CFR ¬ß 264.144)
3. **Corrective action** (40 CFR ¬ß 264.101)
4. **Third-party liability** (40 CFR ¬ß 264.147)

**Third-Party Liability Insurance Requirements:**¬≤‚Åµ
Owners or operators of hazardous waste TSDFs must maintain liability insurance coverage for:
- **Sudden accidental occurrences:** $1M per occurrence / $2M aggregate (minimum)

- **Coverage must be maintained:** During active life of facility and through closure/post-closure periods
