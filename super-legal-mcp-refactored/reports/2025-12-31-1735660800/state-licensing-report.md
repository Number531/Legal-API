## IV. DETAILED ANALYSIS

### A. NY BitLicense Regulatory Framework

#### 1. Overview of 23 NYCRR Part 200

The New York State Department of Financial Services (NYDFS) established the BitLicense regulatory framework under 23 NYCRR Part 200, effective June 24, 2015.¬π The regulation applies to entities engaged in "virtual currency business activity" involving New York or New York residents, including:
- Receiving virtual currency for transmission or transmitting virtual currency
- Storing, holding, or maintaining custody of virtual currency on behalf of others
- Buying and selling virtual currency as a customer business
- Performing exchange services as a customer business
- Controlling, administering, or issuing a virtual currency

The BitLicense framework imposes comprehensive regulatory requirements including capital adequacy, surety bonds or trust accounts, cybersecurity standards, anti-money laundering programs, consumer protection measures, and ongoing reporting obligations.

#### 2. Capital Requirements Under 23 NYCRR ¬ß 200.8

**Statutory Text:**

23 NYCRR ¬ß 200.8(a) provides:

> "Each Licensee shall maintain at all times such capital in an amount and form as the superintendent determines is sufficient to ensure the financial integrity of the Licensee and its ongoing operations based on an assessment of the specific risks applicable to each Licensee."¬≤

**Individualized Determination Standard:**

Unlike traditional banking regulations that prescribe formulaic capital ratios, the BitLicense regulation grants the NYDFS Superintendent discretion to determine capital requirements on a **case-by-case basis**.¬≥ The Superintendent considers nine statutory factors:

1. Composition of total assets (position, size, liquidity, risk exposure, volatility)
2. Composition of total liabilities (size and repayment timing)
3. Actual and expected volume of virtual currency business activity
4. Existing licenses or regulatory status under financial services, banking, or insurance laws
5. Leverage employed by the licensee
6. Liquidity position
7. Customer financial protection via trust accounts or bonds
8. Types of entities to be serviced (retail vs. institutional)
9. Types of products or services offered‚Å¥

**Form of Capital:**

Under 23 NYCRR ¬ß 200.8(b), required capital must be held in "cash, virtual currency, or high-quality, highly liquid, investment-grade assets, in such proportions as are acceptable to the superintendent."‚Åµ

**Supervisory Agreements:**

Upon BitLicense approval, NYDFS enters into detailed **Supervisory Agreements** with each licensee that establish individualized capital requirements, restricted activities, and reporting obligations tailored to the entity's specific business model and risk profile.‚Å∂ These supervisory agreements are updated periodically to reflect changing business models or market conditions.‚Å∑

**Capital Calculation Methodology:**

While the regulation does not prescribe a specific formula, industry guidance and the user-provided transaction documents indicate that NYDFS has applied capital requirements based on a **multiple of quarterly operating expenses**. For CTE, the calculation appears to be:

- **Required Capital:** Greater of $5,000 OR 2√ó quarterly operating expenses
- **CTE Quarterly Operating Expenses (Q3 2024):** $123 million
- **Calculated Requirement:** 2 √ó $123M = **$246 million**
- **CTE Current Capital (Sept 30, 2024):** $105 million
- **Capital Shortfall:** $246M - $105M = **$141 million**

**CRITICAL NOTE:** This calculation is based on transaction documents provided by the user. The actual capital requirement for CTE would be specified in its individual Supervisory Agreement with NYDFS, which is not publicly available. ### B. Surety Bond and Trust Account Requirements

#### 1. Regulatory Framework: 23 NYCRR ¬ß 200.9

**Statutory Requirement:**

23 NYCRR ¬ß 200.9 provides that "Each Licensee shall maintain a surety bond or trust account in United States dollars for the benefit of its customers in such form and amount as is acceptable to the superintendent."‚Å∏

**Minimum Amount:**

Industry guidance indicates that the minimum surety bond or trust account requirement is generally **$500,000**, though this figure can increase based on the licensee's business model, transaction volume, and risk profile.‚Åπ Like capital requirements, the actual bond amount is determined by the Superintendent on an individualized basis.

**Bond Premium Costs:**

- Low premium (1%): $5,000 annually

#### 2. Custody and Protection of Customer Assets

Beyond surety bonds, 23 NYCRR ¬ß 200.9 imposes strict custody obligations:

**Segregation Requirement:**
- Licensees must hold virtual currency "of the same type and amount as that which is owed or obligated to such other Person" when storing assets on behalf of customers¬π¬≤
- Virtual currency held in custody for customers must be segregated from the licensee's proprietary assets

**Prohibition on Commingling:**
- Licensees may not use, sell, or encumber customer assets except "at the direction of such other Person"¬π¬≥
- This prevents rehypothecation and unauthorized lending of customer crypto assets

**NYDFS Custodial Guidance (September 2025):**

On September 30, 2025, NYDFS issued updated guidance emphasizing sound custody practices and requiring clear disclosure regarding sub-custodians.¬π‚Å¥ This guidance reinforces that customer protection through proper asset segregation remains a top regulatory priority.

### D. Capital Raise Strategy Options

### E. Change of Control Approval Requirements

#### 1. Regulatory Framework: 23 NYCRR ¬ß 200.11

**Statutory Prohibition:**

23 NYCRR ¬ß 200.11(a) provides: "No action shall be taken, except with the **prior written approval of the superintendent**, that may result in a change of control of a Licensee."¬≥‚Åµ

**Definition of Control:**

"Control" means the power to direct the management and policies of a Licensee, whether through stock ownership or otherwise. Control is **presumed** when a Person owns, directly or indirectly, **ten percent (10%) or more** of voting stock.¬≥‚Å∂

For the proposed acquisition of CTE by Digital Finance Ventures LLC:
- Transaction structure: Asset purchase OR stock purchase (TBD)
- If **stock purchase**: Acquirer will own 100% of CTE ‚Üí Clear change of control
- If **asset purchase**: May not trigger 23 NYCRR ¬ß 200.11 if BitLicense remains with existing entity, BUT:
  - NYDFS likely views asset purchase with BitLicense transfer as equivalent to change of control
  - Practical effect: BitLicense cannot be "sold" separately from the licensed entity
  - **Conclusion:** Change of control approval required regardless of transaction structure

#### 2. Application Requirements

**Form and Substance:**

A Person seeking to acquire control must submit "a written application to the superintendent in a form and substance acceptable to the superintendent."¬≥‚Å∑ While NYDFS does not publish a standard form, applications typically include:

- **Acquirer Information:**
  - Corporate structure, ownership, capitalization
  - Financial statements (3 years audited)
  - Business plan for CTE post-acquisition
  - Source of funds for acquisition
  - Biographical and financial information for control persons (background checks)

- **Target Information:**
  - Current BitLicense status and compliance history
  - Financial condition and capital adequacy
  - Business plan continuity or changes contemplated
  - Customer impact assessment

- **Transaction Terms:**
  - Purchase price and payment structure
  - Definitive agreement (LOI insufficient)
  - Financing arrangements
  - Post-closing governance structure

#### 3. Approval Timeline

**Statutory Deadline:**

23 NYCRR ¬ß 200.11(c) provides: "The superintendent shall approve or deny every application for a change of control...within **120 days** from the filing of an application deemed by the superintendent to be complete."¬≥‚Å∏

**Clock Starts:** When NYDFS deems application "complete" (not filing date if deficient)
**Extensions:** Superintendent may extend for "good cause shown"¬≥‚Åπ
**De Facto Extension:** NYDFS can request additional information, restarting 120-day clock
