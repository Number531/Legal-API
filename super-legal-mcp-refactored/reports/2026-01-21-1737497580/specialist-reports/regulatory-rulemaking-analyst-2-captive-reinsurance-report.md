### B. NAIC Actuarial Guideline 48 (AG48) ‚Äî Reserve Credit Requirements

#### 1. AG48 Adoption and Effective Date

AG48 was adopted by the NAIC Executive (EX) Committee and Plenary in December 2014 and became effective **January 1, 2015**.¬π¬≤ AG48 defines the rules for new XXX and AXXX reserve financing transactions executed after the effective date and implements the XXX/AXXX Reinsurance Framework adopted in 2014.¬π¬≥

**Purpose:** AG48 established a consistent method for calculating the economic reserve (plus a margin for conservatism) and the level of "primary security" (i.e., higher quality assets), even when held at a captive reinsurer, to strengthen policyholder protections in captive reinsurance arrangements.¬π‚Å¥

#### 2. Primary Security vs. Other Security Framework

AG48 divides reserves under covered business into two layers:¬π‚Åµ

**Primary Security Layer:**
- **Definition:** The "Required Level of Primary Security" determined by an actuarial valuation model provided in AG48, effectively replacing the "economic reserve layer" in traditional reserve financing.¬π‚Å∂
- **Permitted Assets:** Restricted to cash, Securities Valuation Office (SVO)-listed securities, but **excludes synthetic letters of credit**. Letters of credit (including clean and unconditional LOCs) are **not allowed** as Primary Security.¬π‚Å∑
- **Purpose:** High-quality assets that provide strong assurance of policyholder protection, comparable to assets held by the ceding insurer.

**Other Security Layer:**
- **Definition:** The remainder of statutory reserves above the Required Level of Primary Security, effectively replacing the "excess reserve layer."¬π‚Å∏
- **Permitted Assets:** A wider variety of assets acceptable to the sponsor life insurance company, including assets that could be used for Primary Security, **plus** parental guarantees, letters of credit, and other forms of collateral.¬π‚Åπ
- **Regulatory Discounting:** Regulators are seeking to bolster policyholder protections by discounting the value of certain assets employed as Other Security in RBC calculations.¬≤‚Å∞
