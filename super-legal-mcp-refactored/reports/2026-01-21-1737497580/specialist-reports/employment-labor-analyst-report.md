#### 1. Legal Framework for Classification

Employment status classification determines critical legal rights and obligations including tax withholding, employee benefits, workers' compensation coverage, unemployment insurance, and‚Äîcritically for this analysis‚Äîenforceability of restrictive covenants such as non-compete agreements.

The common law control test remains the predominant framework for determining employee versus independent contractor status. The IRS applies a multi-factor test examining:

- **Behavioral Control:** Does the company control or have the right to control what the worker does and how the worker does their job?
- **Financial Control:** Are business aspects of the worker's job controlled by the company (expenses, investment in tools/facilities, opportunity for profit/loss)?
- **Relationship Type:** Are there written contracts, employee-type benefits (insurance, pension, paid leave), permanency of relationship, and whether services are key to business?

### B. WARN Act Analysis ‚Äî Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification Act

#### 1. Statutory Framework

The Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification Act, 29 U.S.C. ¬ß¬ß 2101-2109, requires covered employers to provide 60 days' advance written notice before implementing plant closings or mass layoffs. [VERIFIED: 29 U.S.C. ¬ß 2102(a), https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/29/2102]

**Covered Employers:** 29 U.S.C. ¬ß 2101(a)(1)
- Business enterprises with 100 or more employees (excluding part-time employees), OR
- 100 or more employees who in aggregate work at least 4,000 hours per week (exclusive of overtime)

**LLIC Status:** With 2,800 total employees, LLIC is a **covered employer** under WARN Act. [VERIFIED]

**Part-Time Employee Exclusion:** Part-time employees (working fewer than 20 hours per week average OR employed less than 6 of the preceding 12 months) are excluded from the 100-employee threshold calculation but ARE counted as "affected employees" who must receive notice if they experience employment loss. 29 U.S.C. ¬ß 2101(a)(8).

#### 2. Triggering Events

**Plant Closing:** 29 U.S.C. ¬ß 2101(a)(2)
- Permanent or temporary shutdown of a single site of employment, OR
- Shutdown of one or more facilities/operating units within a single site
- Resulting in employment loss during any 30-day period for 50 or more employees (excluding part-time)

**Mass Layoff:** 29 U.S.C. ¬ß 2101(a)(3)
- Reduction in force (not resulting from plant closing) during any 30-day period
- Causing employment loss at a single site for:
  - **Option A:** At least 500 employees (excluding part-time), OR
  - **Option B:** At least 50-499 employees (excluding part-time) AND such employees constitute ‚â•33% of the active workforce at that site

**Employment Loss Definition:** 29 U.S.C. ¬ß 2101(a)(6)
1. Employment termination (excluding discharge for cause, voluntary departure, or voluntary retirement)
2. Layoff exceeding 6 months, OR
3. Reduction in hours of work of more than 50% during each month of any 6-month period

#### 3. Notice Requirements

**60-Day Advance Notice:** 29 U.S.C. ¬ß 2102(a)

Employer must serve written notice **60 days before** ordering a plant closing or mass layoff to:

1. **Affected Employees:**
   - Each representative of affected employees (union), OR
   - If no union representation, directly to each affected employee

2. **Government Entities:**
   - State dislocated worker unit (designated state agency)
   - Chief elected official of local government unit where closure/layoff occurs

**Notice Content Requirements:** 20 C.F.R. ¬ß 639.7
- Name and address of employment site
- Statement whether action is plant closing or mass layoff
- Expected date of first separation
- Expected date when all separations will be completed
- Job titles of positions affected and names of workers holding those jobs
- Whether bumping rights exist (union seniority-based displacement)
- Name and contact information for company official

#### 7. WARN Act Penalties for Non-Compliance

**Back Pay and Benefits:** 29 U.S.C. ¬ß 2104(a)(1)

Employer violating notice requirements liable to each affected employee for:

- **Back pay** at higher of: (i) average regular rate during last 3 years of employment, OR (ii) final regular rate
- **Benefits:** Value of cost of employee benefits (medical expenses) the employee would have received during violation period

**Maximum Liability Period:** Up to 60 days, but cannot exceed half the employee's tenure

**Calculation Example:**
- 100 employees laid off without notice
- Average compensation: $75,000 annually = $6,250/month √ó 2 months = $12,500 per employee
- Benefits: $1,200/month √ó 2 months = $2,400 per employee
- **Total per employee:** $14,900
- **Aggregate liability:** 100 √ó $14,900 = **$1,490,000**

**Civil Penalties to Local Government:** Up to $500 per day of violation if employer fails to provide notice to chief elected official, unless employer pays affected employees within 3 weeks. 29 U.S.C. ¬ß 2104(a)(3).

**Attorney's Fees:** Courts may award reasonable attorney's fees to prevailing plaintiff employees. 29 U.S.C. ¬ß 2104(a)(6).

**Good Faith Defense:** Courts may reduce liability if employer proves it acted in good faith belief that conduct complied with WARN Act. 29 U.S.C. ¬ß 2104(a)(4).

**No Injunctive Relief:** Courts cannot enjoin plant closing or mass layoff; remedies limited to monetary damages. 29 U.S.C. ¬ß 2104(b).
