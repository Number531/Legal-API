### B. DOT Fitness Review and Foreign Ownership Compliance (HIGHEST RISK - POTENTIAL DEAL BLOCKER)

**CRITICAL ISSUE: CPPIB "Actual Control" Risk**

**Statutory Framework:**
49 U.S.C. ¬ß 40102(a)(15) requires air carriers be **"actually controlled"** by U.S. citizens. Definition includes:
- At least **75% of voting equity** held by U.S. citizens (foreign ‚â§25% voting)
- At least **two-thirds of board** and managing officers are U.S. citizens
- Airline under **actual control of U.S. citizens**

**GAH Ownership Structure:**
| Investor | Equity % | Voting % | Special Rights |
|----------|----------|----------|----------------|
| U.S. Sponsors | 60% | 60% | Standard |
| **CPPIB (Canadian)** | **30%** | **30%** | **Board observer + veto over capital structure changes** |
| European Institutional | 10% | 10% | None |
| **Total Foreign** | **40%** | **40%** | **‚ö†Ô∏è CPPIB Veto Issue** |

**Facial Compliance:** ‚úì Complies (60% U.S. voting vs. 75% requirement; 40% foreign vs. 49% DOT precedent cap)

**ACTUAL CONTROL CONCERN:** CPPIB's **veto over capital structure changes** could constitute "actual control" by foreign entity under DOT's case-by-case analysis, **despite facial compliance** with equity percentages.

**Precedent: Virgin America (2006-2007) - Directly Analogous**

**DOT Initial Denial (December 27, 2006):**
Virgin America **tentatively denied** despite compliance with equity percentages because:
1. Virgin Group (foreign) provided ~50% of startup funding
2. **Trademark license gave Virgin Group "undue influence" on operating decisions** (similar to CPPIB's capital structure veto)
3. Virgin Group had two board seats (25% of 8-member board)
4. Interlocking financial agreements

**DOT Approval After Restructuring (May 18, 2007 - Order 2007-5-11):**
Virgin America restructured to:
- Reduce Virgin Group to **22% equity** (below 25% threshold)
- Eliminate Virgin Group's operational influence
- Only 2 Virgin Group directors on 8-person board (limited influence)

**Comparative Analysis - GAH vs. Virgin America:**

Under 14 C.F.R. Part 119, **air carrier certificates are not transferable between owners.** An Air Carrier Certificate or Operating Certificate issued under Part 119 is effective until the certificate holder surrenders it to the Administrator or the Administrator suspends, revokes, or otherwise terminates the certificate. 14 C.F.R. ¬ß 119.5(g) [VERIFIED via eCFR.gov, accessed Dec. 27, 2025].

**Critical Implication:** When ownership of SkyWest Regional Airlines changes from current shareholders to Global Aviation Holdings LLC, the existing certificate **does not automatically transfer**. The FAA must approve the new ownership structure and may require significant operational continuity demonstrations.

**Source:** [eCFR :: 14 CFR Part 119 -- Certification: Air Carriers and Commercial Operators](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-14/chapter-I/subchapter-G/part-119)

#### 2. Citizenship Requirements (Deal-Blocking Risk)

**Statutory Requirement:** A person may not operate as a direct air carrier unless that person is a citizen of the United States, obtains an Air Carrier Certificate, and obtains operations specifications. 14 C.F.R. ¬ß 119.33(a)(1).

**Change of Ownership Review:** When a change of ownership occurs, the FAA Principal Inspector must ensure that the certificate holder meets the citizenship requirement in accordance with ¬ß 119.33(a)(1) and (b)(1). FAA Order 8900.1, Volume 3, Chapter 34, Section 3 [VERIFIED via FSIMS database, accessed Dec. 27, 2025].

**Analysis:** GAH's ownership structure includes 40% foreign equity (30% CPPIB + 10% European institutional investors). While this complies facially with the 49% foreign equity cap under 49 U.S.C. ¬ß 40102(a)(15), the FAA will scrutinize **actual control** factors (see Section IV.C below for DOT analysis, which parallels FAA review).

**Source:** [14 CFR ¬ß 119.33 - General requirements](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/14/119.33)

#### 3. Pre-Application Process and Timeline

**FAA Form 8400-6 Pre-Application Statement of Intent (PASI):**
- **Timing:** Must be submitted at least **30 calendar days** prior to the formal application meeting if the ownership change requires a new certificate or significant amendments.
- **Content:** General description of proposed operation, key management personnel identification, and operational scope.

**Additional Pre-Application Checklist (PAC) Items:**
- Must be submitted **at least 15 days** prior to the formal application meeting.
- After submission, the FAA Flight Standards District Office (FSDO) assigns Certification Project Managers, Principal Operations Inspector, Principal Airworthiness Inspector, and Principal Avionics Inspector.

**Source:** [FAA Form 8400-6](https://www.faa.gov/documentLibrary/media/Form/FAA_Form_8400-6.pdf), [Completing the Pre-Application Checklist | FAA](https://www.faa.gov/licenses_certificates/airline_certification/air_carrier/complete_preapp_chklist)

#### 4. FAA Order 8900.1 Certificate Transfer Process

**Procedural Guidance:** FAA Order 8900.1 (Flight Standards Information Management System) directs FAA aviation safety inspectors responsible for certification, technical administration, and surveillance of air carriers. Volume 2, Chapter 1, Section 5 specifically addresses certificate transfer processes.

**Key Process Steps:**
1. **Meeting with Certificate Holder:** FAA holds meeting to review certificate transfer process and certificate holder's transfer plan (acquiring Flight Standards office attendance recommended).
2. **Flowcharts and Job Aids:** Order 8900.1 contains specific flowcharts for certificate transfer process.
3. **Continuity Review:** FAA evaluates:
   - Extent of operational changes under new ownership
   - Ability to continue conducting authorized operations
   - Management personnel qualifications
   - Financial fitness

**Standard Timeline:** While not explicitly stated in the regulations, **industry practice indicates 90-120 days** for certificate transfer approval following change of ownership, based on complexity of operations.

**Source:** [FAA Order 8900.1](https://www.faa.gov/documentLibrary/media/Order/8900.1.pdf), [FAA Order 8900.1 - Dynamic Regulatory System](https://drs.faa.gov/browse/excelExternalWindow/DRSDOCID125310001920230721133818.0001%3FmodalOpened%3Dtrue)

#### 5. Operations Specifications (OpSpecs) Amendment Process

**Regulatory Authority:** 14 C.F.R. ¬ß 119.51 governs amendment of operations specifications for Part 121 carriers.

**FAA-Initiated Amendments:**
- FAA may initiate amendments if safety and public interest require
- Certificate holder receives written notice with at least **7 days** to submit written information/views/arguments
- Amendment becomes effective **not less than 30 days** after notice, unless emergency exists

**Certificate Holder-Initiated Amendments (for Ownership Change):**
- **Standard Filing:** Application must be filed **at least 90 days** before proposed effective date in cases of:
  - Mergers
  - Acquisitions of airline operational assets requiring additional showing of safety
  - Changes in kind of operation
- **Shorter Timeline:** 15 days for all other amendments not listed above

**Critical Implication for SkyWest Acquisition:**
The GAH acquisition likely qualifies as an "acquisition of airline operational assets" requiring the **90-day advance filing requirement**. This runs concurrent with (not additional to) the overall certificate transfer timeline.

**Source:** [14 CFR ¬ß 119.51 - Amending operations specifications](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-14/chapter-I/subchapter-G/part-119/subpart-C/section-119.51)

#### 7. Safety Management System (SMS) Continuity

- **Subpart D:** Safety Assurance

### B. DOT FITNESS REVIEW AND FOREIGN OWNERSHIP COMPLIANCE - CRITICAL ISSUE

#### 1. Statutory Framework: U.S. Citizenship Requirement for Air Carriers

**49 U.S.C. ¬ß 40102(a)(15) - "Citizen of the United States" Definition:**

For air carrier purposes, a "citizen of the United States" means:
- The president and **at least two-thirds of the board of directors** and other managing officers are U.S. citizens
- The airline is under the **actual control of U.S. citizens**
- At least **75 percent of voting interests** is owned or controlled by U.S. citizens

**49 U.S.C. ¬ß 41102:** An air transport certificate can only be held by a U.S. citizen as defined above.

**Key Limitation:** Foreign nationals' control of U.S. air carriers is **prohibited** under 49 U.S.C. ¬ß¬ß 40102(2), (15), 41101, 41102.

**Source:** [49 U.S. Code ¬ß 40102 - Definitions](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/40102), [GAO Report GAO-19-540R: Airline Foreign Ownership](https://www.gao.gov/assets/gao-19-540r.pdf)

#### 3. DOT "Actual Control" Doctrine - Precedent Analysis

**Legal Standard:** DOT interprets "actual control" to mean determining whether foreign interests have the ability to exercise **substantial influence** over the airline beyond numerical requirements. Determination is **case-by-case** involving examination of several factors and totality of circumstances.

**Factors DOT Officials Consider:**
- Company debt and funding sources
- Shareholder influence on business decisions
- Board composition and decision-making authority
- Protective provisions and veto rights
- Management appointments and oversight
- Trademark licenses and operational agreements

**Source:** [GAO Report GAO-19-540R](https://www.gao.gov/assets/gao-19-540r.pdf), [Medium: Navigating US DOT Foreign Ownership Rules](https://medium.com/new-heights-aviation-law-journal/navigating-us-dot-foreign-ownership-rules-for-us-air-carriers-ab955ab879e0)

#### 4. Precedent: Virgin America DOT Fitness Determination (2006-2007)

**Initial Denial (December 27, 2006):**

DOT **tentatively denied** Virgin America's application, finding:
- Less than 75% of voting equity held by U.S. citizens

1. **Virgin Group's "pervasive involvement"** in creation of Virgin America

5. **Virgin Group's ability to influence board decisions**

Virgin America restructured:
- **Voting shares** placed in DOT-approved trust
- **Board composition:** Only 2 Virgin Group directors on 8-person board
- **Virgin Group equity reduced:** Branson's Virgin Group held only **22% of shares**
- **Operational influence eliminated:** Little influence on corporate decision-making

**DOT approved** after accepting revised ownership structure.

#### 2. LaGuardia Airport Slots - Regulatory Framework

**14 C.F.R. Part 93 Subpart K - High Density Traffic Airports:**

Historically governed LaGuardia slot allocations. However, as of October 2024, FAA transitioned to "Operating Authorizations" that function similarly to historical slot system.

**Current Operating Order (2024-2026):**
- **Effective Date:** October 27, 2024
- **Expiration:** October 24, 2026
- FAA continues to manage LaGuardia operations through Operating Authorizations

**Slot Transfer Approval Process:**
- All transfers between operators subject to **prior FAA confirmation** per applicable rules
- U.S. and foreign air carriers must notify FAA of written consent and requests for slot transfer confirmation
- Requests must be submitted to **7-AWA-Slotadmin@faa.gov**
- Requests must come from designated representative of each carrier
- **FAA must confirm and approve transactions in writing prior to effective date**

**Timeline for Slot Transfer Approval:**
- Standard processing: **30-60 days** from complete application
- FAA review ensures transfers consistent with operational requirements and slot management policies

**Source:** [Federal Register :: Operating Limitations at New York LaGuardia Airport (May 13, 2024)](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2024/05/13/2024-10298/operating-limitations-at-new-york-laguardia-airport), [FAA: System Capacity and Evaluation](https://www.faa.gov/about/office_org/headquarters_offices/ato/service_units/systemops/perf_analysis/slot_administration/data)

#### 3. Reagan National Airport Slots - Regulatory Framework

**14 C.F.R. Part 93 Subpart S - Allocation of Commuter and Air Carrier IFR Operations:**

Governs slot allocations at high-density traffic airports including DCA.

**Slot System at DCA:**
- Regulations at 14 C.F.R. ¬ß 93.123 established **60 hourly slots** at DCA
- Statutes have granted an additional maximum of **7 slots** (beyond-perimeter exemptions)
- **Total: 67 hourly slots** at Reagan National

**Perimeter Rule (Critical Restriction):**
- Flights to/from DCA restricted by statutory "perimeter rule"
- Limits nonstop flights to **1,250-mile distance** unless granted waiver/exemption
- **49 U.S.C. ¬ß 41718** governs perimeter rules and slot allocations

**2024 Slot Expansion:**
FAA Reauthorization Act of 2024 (enacted May 16, 2024) authorized **10 additional DCA slot exemptions**. Five additional perimeter-exempt roundtrip slots added to Reagan National.

**Slot Transfer Requirements (14 C.F.R. ¬ß 93.221):**
- **Effective April 1, 1986:** Slots may be bought, sold, or leased for any consideration and time period
- Slots may be traded in any combination for slots at same airport or other high-density airport
