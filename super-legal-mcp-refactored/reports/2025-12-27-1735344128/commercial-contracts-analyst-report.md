### 2. Assignment and Change of Control Consent Requirements

**Standard Aircraft Lease Assignment Provisions:**

Under industry-standard IATA lease agreements,¬≤¬≥ lessees cannot assign, delegate, or otherwise transfer any rights or obligations under the lease **without prior written consent of the lessor**. However, there are typically exceptions allowing assignment to:
- Parent companies
- Majority-owned and controlled subsidiaries
- Companies under common ownership or control (upon written notice)

**Analysis for SkyWest Acquisition:**

The $2.8B acquisition by Global Aviation Holdings constitutes a **change of control** that will require lessor consent for **all 120 leased aircraft**.

**IATA Novation Standards:**¬≤‚Å¥
- Lessee (SkyWest) is typically considered an "accommodation party" in novation/assignment
- **Lessee's obligations should not increase** as consequence of lessor transfer or assignment
- Lessee should not bear increased obligations as result of ownership change
- Most leases contain provision that lessee obligations will not increase due to lessor transfer

**Critical Issue**: While IATA standards protect lessees from increased obligations in **lessor transfers**, the SkyWest acquisition involves a **lessee change of control**, which places SkyWest in a weaker negotiating position.

### 1. FAA Slot Usage Requirements - 14 CFR ¬ß 93.227

**Statutory Framework:**‚Å¥‚Å∞

Any Operating Authorization (slot) **not utilized 80 percent of the time over a 2-month period shall be recalled by the FAA**.

**Measurement:**
- **2-month periods**: Usage measured bimonthly beginning January 1
- **80% threshold**: Slot must be used for at least 48 of 60 days in 2-month period (80%)
- **"Use"**: Actual flight operations (takeoff or landing)

**Exceptions to 80% Rule:**‚Å¥¬π
1. **New entrants**: 90-day grace period after slot allocation
2. **Limited/other incumbents**: 60-day grace period
3. **Strikes**: Operators forced to cease operations
4. **Bankruptcy**: 60-day initial period plus extensions during antitrust reviews
5. **Seasonal holidays**: Thanksgiving, day after Thanksgiving, December 24 - first Saturday in January count as "used"

**Waiver Authority:**‚Å¥¬≤
The FAA Chief Counsel may waive the 80% requirement for:
- **Highly unusual and unpredictable conditions** beyond slot-holder's control lasting 9+ days
- Examples: Extended weather disruptions, aircraft type groundings
- COVID-19 impact: FAA granted extensive waivers during 2020-2024

**2024-2025 Slot Usage Relief:**‚Å¥¬≥

### 2. Slot Lease Enforceability Under Federal Law

**FAA Position on Slot Leases:**

The FAA permits **trades and leases of slots for consideration** at LaGuardia Airport.‚Å¥‚Å¥ Slot lease agreements are private commercial contracts between airlines, but slots remain subject to FAA's use-or-lose rule.

**Critical Question: Who Must Satisfy 80% Usage?**

**Typical Slot Lease Structure:**
- **Lessor** (SkyWest - hypothetically) retains legal ownership of slot
- **Lessee** (JetBlue) operates flights using the slot
- **Usage responsibility**: Typically assigned to **lessee** in lease agreement

**FAA Enforcement:**
- FAA tracks actual operator of each slot daily (flight number, equipment, operator)‚Å¥‚Åµ
- If 80% usage not met, FAA withdraws slot **from legal holder** (lessor)
- Lessor cannot lease withdrawn slot; **lessor bears ultimate risk of FAA withdrawal**

### 3. Hypothetical Breach Analysis - 80% Usage Violation

**Scenario:** JetBlue (lessee) claims SkyWest (lessor) breached minimum usage requirements.

**Legal Issues:**

**Issue 1: Standing - Who Can Assert Breach?**

If JetBlue was **assigned usage responsibility** under the lease:
- JetBlue's failure to use slot does **not breach SkyWest's obligations**
- JetBlue would be in breach of lease for failing to use slot as required
- SkyWest could terminate lease **for cause** (JetBlue's breach)

If SkyWest **retained usage responsibility**:
- SkyWest's failure to meet 80% = breach of lease obligation
- JetBlue could claim constructive eviction or material breach
- JetBlue's remedies: Termination, damages, or rent abatement

**Most Likely Scenario:** Slot leases typically transfer **operational control** to lessee, making lessee responsible for usage. JetBlue's claim that "SkyWest breached minimum usage requirements" would fail because **JetBlue controlled the usage**.
