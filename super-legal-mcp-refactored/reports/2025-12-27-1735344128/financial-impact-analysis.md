## IV. PURCHASE PRICE ADJUSTMENT ANALYSIS

### A. Valuation Impact Methodologies

Three methodologies for calculating appropriate purchase price adjustment based on Monte Carlo/probability analysis results:

**Method 1: Expected Value Adjustment (Probability-Weighted Mean)**

```
Original Purchase Price:        $2,800,000,000
Less: Mean Expected Exposure:   -$  612,000,000  (21.9% reduction)
Adjusted Fair Value:            $2,188,000,000
Implied Price Reduction:        21.9%
```

**Rationale**: Expected value represents the statistically most accurate estimate of total risk exposure. This approach is theoretically sound and commonly used in corporate finance for risk-adjusted valuation.

**Weaknesses**: Does not account for risk aversion; treats all probability scenarios equally regardless of severity; may undervalue catastrophic tail risks.

---

**Method 2: Conservative Adjustment (75th Percentile)**

```
Original Purchase Price:        $2,800,000,000
Less: 75th Percentile Exposure: -$  670,000,000  (23.9% reduction)
Conservative Fair Value:        $2,130,000,000
Implied Price Reduction:        23.9%
```

**Rationale**: Conservative approach ensures that in 75% of scenarios, the actual exposure will be less than the price reduction. Provides buffer against adverse outcomes while remaining within reasonable valuation bounds.

**Strengths**: Balances risk aversion with deal feasibility; addresses buyer's concern about downside protection; supported by precedent in distressed M&A transactions.

**Weaknesses**: May be difficult to justify to seller; 75th percentile somewhat arbitrary (why not 70th or 80th?).

---

**Method 3: Risk-Averse Adjustment (90th Percentile - Conservative Planning)**

```
Original Purchase Price:        $2,800,000,000
Less: 90th Percentile Exposure: -$1,025,000,000  (36.6% reduction)
Risk-Averse Fair Value:         $1,775,000,000
Implied Price Reduction:        36.6%
```

**Rationale**: Extremely conservative approach appropriate for highly risk-averse buyer or when seller has superior information about risks. Protects against all but the most catastrophic scenarios (1-in-10 worst case).

**Strengths**: Maximum downside protection for buyer; accounts for compounded risks and tail scenarios.

**Weaknesses**: **Likely deal-breaking**: 36.6% price reduction may be commercially unacceptable to seller; overweights low-probability scenarios; may preclude deal closure entirely.
