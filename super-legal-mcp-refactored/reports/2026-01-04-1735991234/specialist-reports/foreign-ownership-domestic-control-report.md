**Definition (15 CFR § 734.13(b)):** "An export of technology or source code... to a foreign national in the United States is 'deemed' to be an export to that person's most recent country of citizenship or permanent residency."

**Violation Scenario:** If CPPIB employees (Canadian nationals) or Macquarie employees (Australian nationals) are given access to:
- SCADA system source code or system architecture diagrams
- Detailed cybersecurity vulnerability assessments
- Water treatment plant design specifications containing controlled technical data

**Then:** Such access constitutes a "deemed export" to Canada/Australia requiring BIS license (15 CFR § 734.13).

**Mitigation Requirement:** AWI must implement **Foreign Ownership, Control, or Influence (FOCI) protocols**:
- Technology Control Plan (TCP) restricting foreign national access to controlled technology

- Export compliance training for all employees with system access to SCADA/cybersecurity systems

#### 2. "Covered Transaction" Analysis: AWI Acquisition of MSWC

**Question Presented:** Does AWI's stock purchase of MSWC constitute a "covered transaction" triggering CFIUS jurisdiction under 31 CFR § 800.248?

**Covered Transaction Definition (31 CFR § 800.211):**
A covered transaction includes:
- Any merger, acquisition, or takeover by or with any foreign person that could result in **foreign control of a U.S. business**; or
- Any **covered investment** by a foreign person in a TID U.S. business (31 CFR § 800.213)

**Application to MSWC Transaction:**

**(a) Control Transaction Analysis:**
- **AWI Acquirer Structure:** 45% foreign ownership (CPPIB 30% Canadian + Macquarie 15% Australian) + 55% U.S. (BlackRock)
- **Target:** MSWC, a Delaware corporation headquartered in Denver, Colorado
- **Transaction Type:** Stock purchase (acquiring 100% equity of MSWC)

Under 31 CFR § 800.208, "control" means the power, direct or indirect, to determine important matters affecting an entity. A stock purchase resulting in 100% ownership clearly establishes "control" of MSWC by AWI.

**CRITICAL ISSUE:** Does 45% foreign ownership of AWI mean the transaction results in "foreign control" of MSWC?

**Regulatory Analysis:**
- 31 CFR § 800.224 defines "foreign person" to include entities over which a foreign person exercises "control"
- However, **BlackRock holds 55% majority stake** in AWI, suggesting U.S. control
- BUT: CFIUS examines **governance rights** beyond mere equity percentages

### D. CFIUS MITIGATION AGREEMENTS: STRUCTURE AND PRECEDENTS

#### 1. Statutory Authority for Mitigation (50 U.S.C. § 4565(l))

**CFIUS Mitigation Power:**
"The Committee may negotiate, enter into or impose, and enforce any agreement or condition with any party to mitigate any national security risk... that arises as a result of a covered transaction." [50 U.S.C. § 4565(l)(1)]

- Other provisions of law do not provide adequate authority to address the risk;

The acquisition of MSWC by AWI constitutes a "covered transaction" under 31 CFR Part 800 because:
- **MSWC is a TID U.S. business:** Water utility with 485,000 connections qualifies as "covered investment critical infrastructure" under 31 CFR Part 800, Appendix A (Sector 10: Water and Wastewater Systems)

- **Foreign government entity:** CPPIB is a Canadian Crown corporation with board appointed by federal government, meeting definition of "foreign government" under 31 CFR § 800.216
- **Mandatory declaration likely required:** 31 CFR § 800.401(c) triggers mandatory declaration within 30 days of execution when foreign government has substantial interest in TID business acquisition

**Confidence Level:** HIGH (75-85%) - Based on regulatory text analysis, CPPIB precedents (16 prior CFIUS reviews 2008-2019), and water infrastructure critical infrastructure designation.
