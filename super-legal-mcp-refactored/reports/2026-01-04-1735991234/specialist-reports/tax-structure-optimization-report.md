## I. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### Overview

This tax structure optimization analysis examines whether American Water Infrastructure LLC ("AWI") should make an IRC § 338(h)(10) election in connection with its proposed $2.4 billion stock acquisition of Mountain States Water Company ("MSWC"), a Colorado-regulated water utility. The election would treat the transaction as a deemed asset sale for federal income tax purposes, generating significant stepped-up basis for future depreciation and amortization deductions. The analysis quantifies the net present value (NPV) of the step-up benefit at **$175 million to $200 million** after accounting for the purchase price adjustment required to compensate the seller for incremental tax costs.

**CRITICAL CONTINGENCY:** The § 338(h)(10) election is **available ONLY if** MSWC is currently owned by (1) a consolidated group of corporations (with MSWC as a subsidiary member), or (2) S corporation shareholders. If MSWC is owned directly by individuals or a partnership, the election is **not available** under Treasury Regulation § 1.338(h)(10)-1(c). **Seller eligibility verification in the data room is essential** within the first 30 days of due diligence.

## IV. DETAILED ANALYSIS

### A. IRC § 338(h)(10) Election: Eligibility and Requirements

#### 1. Statutory Framework

**IRC § 338(h)(10) Election Mechanics** [VERIFIED via 26 U.S.C. § 338(h)(10), accessed 2026-01-04]

Section 338(h)(10) permits an elective deemed asset sale treatment for qualified stock purchases where the target corporation is a member of a selling consolidated group or an S corporation. The election transforms what would otherwise be a stock sale (with no basis step-up to the acquirer) into a deemed asset sale for federal income tax purposes, while maintaining stock sale treatment for legal purposes.

**Key Statutory Provisions:**

1. **Deemed Asset Sale Treatment:** If the election is made, the target corporation "recognizes gain or loss with respect to the transaction as if it sold all of its assets...at fair market value in a single transaction" immediately before the acquisition. 26 U.S.C. § 338(h)(10)(A).

2. **No Shareholder Recognition:** The selling consolidated group members or S corporation shareholders "recognize no gain or loss on stock sold" in the qualified stock purchase. 26 U.S.C. § 338(h)(10)(B).

3. **Stepped-Up Basis to Acquirer:** The purchasing corporation receives a basis in the target's assets equal to the adjusted grossed-up basis of the stock (essentially, the purchase price allocated to assets under the residual method). 26 U.S.C. § 338(b).

**Citation:** 26 U.S. Code § 338, https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/338

#### 3. Qualified Stock Purchase (QSP) Verification

**80% Control Test (IRC § 1504(a)(2)):**

AWI's 100% stock acquisition of MSWC satisfies the "qualified stock purchase" requirement:

- **Voting Power:** 100% of voting stock acquired (exceeds 80% minimum)
- **Value:** 100% of stock value acquired (exceeds 80% minimum)
- **Acquisition Period:** Single closing within 12-month period
- **Purchase Requirement:** Cash consideration (not boot or reorganization)

**12-Month Acquisition Period (IRC § 338(h)(2)):**

The statute requires the acquiring corporation to purchase at least 80% of target stock "during the 12-month acquisition period," defined as beginning with "the date of the first acquisition by purchase of stock included in a qualified stock purchase." Since AWI is acquiring 100% of MSWC stock at a single closing, this requirement is conclusively satisfied.

**Citation:** 26 U.S.C. § 338(d)(3), (h)(2); [Section 338(h)(10) Election Guide](https://www.ehowardlaw.com/post/section-338-h-10-election-an-in-depth-guide-for-m-a-tax-planning)

#### 4. Joint Election Requirement

**Mandatory Mutual Consent:**

Unlike a regular § 338(g) election (which the buyer can make unilaterally), a § 338(h)(10) election requires **joint filing** by both the purchasing corporation and the seller:

- **Form 8023 Filing:** The election is made on IRS Form 8023, "Elections Under Section 338 for Corporations Making Qualified Stock Purchases," signed by both parties
- **Deadline:** Must be filed by the 15th day of the 9th month after the month of acquisition
- **Irrevocability:** Once made, the election is irrevocable

**S Corporation Special Rule:** If MSWC is an S corporation, **all shareholders** (not just those selling stock) must consent to the election. Treas. Reg. § 1.338(h)(10)-1(c)(2).

**Negotiation Dynamics:** Because the election benefits AWI (stepped-up basis for future depreciation) but imposes current tax costs on the seller (deemed asset sale gain), the purchase price must reflect a negotiated adjustment to compensate the seller for its incremental tax burden (see Section IV.B below).

**Citation:** [IRS Form 8023 Instructions](https://www.irs.gov/forms-pubs/about-form-8023), [338(h)(10) Structure: Pros, Cons for Sellers, Buyers - RKL LLP](https://www.rklcpa.com/338h10-transaction-structure-pros-cons-sellers-buyers/)

#### 5. Precedent Guidance: Revenue Ruling 2001-46

**Clarification of Eligible Transactions:**

Rev. Rul. 2001-46 addressed whether § 338(h)(10) elections were available in transactions involving newly formed wholly owned subsidiaries. The ruling analyzed two situations:

### B. Purchase Price Allocation Under IRC § 1060 Residual Method

#### 1. Statutory Allocation Framework

**IRC § 1060 Coordination with § 338:**

Section 1060 mandates that in an "applicable asset acquisition" (which includes § 338 elections), "consideration received for such assets shall be allocated among such assets...in the same manner as amounts are allocated to assets under section 338(b)(5)." 26 U.S.C. § 1060(a). This cross-reference requires application of the **residual method** set forth in Treas. Reg. § 1.338-6 and § 1.338-7.

**Residual Method Mechanics** [VERIFIED via 26 CFR § 1.1060-1, accessed 2026-01-04]:

The residual method allocates purchase price sequentially through seven asset classes, with each class receiving allocation only after all prior classes are satisfied up to their fair market values. Any residual consideration (amounts exceeding the FMV of Class I-VI assets) flows to Class VII (goodwill and going concern value).

**Citation:** 26 U.S.C. § 1060, https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/1060; [26 CFR § 1.1060-1](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/26/1.1060-1)

### E. IRC § 382 Limitation on Net Operating Loss Carryforwards

#### 1. Ownership Change Triggering Event

**100% Stock Acquisition = Automatic § 382 Trigger:**

AWI's 100% acquisition of MSWC stock constitutes an "ownership change" under IRC § 382(g), automatically subjecting any pre-acquisition net operating losses (NOLs) to annual utilization limitations.

**Ownership Change Definition (IRC § 382(g)):**

An "ownership change" occurs when the percentage of stock owned by one or more **5-percent shareholders** has increased by more than **50 percentage points** over the lowest percentage owned during the **3-year testing period**. 26 U.S.C. § 382(g)(1).

In this transaction:
- **Before acquisition:** MSWC shareholders own 100% of MSWC stock
- **After acquisition:** AWI owns 100% of MSWC stock
- **Percentage increase:** 100 percentage points (AWI goes from 0% to 100%)
- **Result:** Ownership change conclusively occurred

**Citation:** [26 U.S. Code § 382](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/382), [Overview of Section 382 Limitation on NOL Carryforwards](https://swcllp.com/overview-of-section-382-limitation-on-net-operating-loss-carryforwards/)

#### 2. Annual Limitation Calculation

**IRC § 382(b) Formula:**

When an ownership change occurs, the amount of pre-change NOLs that can be used to offset post-acquisition taxable income in any year is limited to:

**Annual Limitation = FMV of Loss Corporation Stock (immediately before ownership change) × Long-Term Tax-Exempt Rate**

**Application to MSWC:**

| Variable | Value | Source |
|----------|-------|--------|
| **FMV of MSWC Stock (pre-change)** | $2.4 billion | Purchase price (best evidence of FMV) |
| **Long-Term Tax-Exempt Rate** | 3.81% (Jan 2024) to ~4.5% (est. 2026-2027) | Published monthly by IRS in Revenue Rulings |
| **Annual § 382 Limitation (Base Case)** | **$96M-$108M per year** | $2.4B × 4.0%-4.5% |

**Key Insight:** If MSWC has $50M-$100M in NOL carryforwards (as hypothesized), the § 382 limitation of $96M-$108M per year would allow **full utilization** of the NOLs within 1-2 years post-acquisition, assuming sufficient taxable income.

**Citation:** [IRC Section 382 Net Operating Loss Limitation Rules](https://www.irstaxapp.com/irc-section-382-net-operating-loss-limitation-rules/), [Offset Taxable Income with Net Operating Losses and Navigate Section 382](https://www.mossadams.com/articles/2024/04/offset-tax-liability-with-section-382)

#### 3. Long-Term Tax-Exempt Rate (2024-2026 Range)

**IRS Monthly Publication:**

The "long-term tax-exempt rate" is published monthly by the IRS in Table 3 of the Revenue Rulings for purposes of § 382(f). The rate equals "the highest among the adjusted federal long-term rates applicable in any month within the three months preceding the ownership change." 26 U.S.C. § 382(f).

### F. Colorado Corporate Income Tax Conformity Analysis

#### 1. Colorado IRC Conformity Framework

**Rolling Conformity with Prospective-Only Application:**

Colorado conforms to the Internal Revenue Code on a **rolling basis**, meaning that Colorado automatically adopts federal tax law changes as they occur. However, Colorado has a critical limitation:

**Prospective Conformity Rule:** "Colorado clarifies that the term 'internal revenue code' incorporates changes to federal statute only on a **prospective basis** and does not, for any taxable year, incorporate federal statutory changes that are enacted **after the last day of that taxable year**." (Colorado Department of Revenue guidance)

**Implication:** If federal tax law changes occur after December 31 of a given tax year, those changes do not affect Colorado tax liability for that year—they apply only to future tax years.

**Citation:** [Colorado regulation changes impact NOLs, FSI exclusion, and Section 78 subtraction](https://www.pwc.com/us/en/services/tax/library/colorado-regulation-updates-impact-nols-and-other-items.html), [Colorado's rolling conformity to IRC](https://www.grantthornton.com/insights/alerts/tax/2023/salt/a-e/co-rolling-conformity-to-irc-includes-retroactive-federal-changes-01-24)

#### 2. C.R.S. § 39-22-304 — Corporate Net Income

**Statutory Authority:**

Colorado Revised Statutes § 39-22-304 governs the calculation of corporate income tax for C-corporations. The statute defines "net income" as **federal taxable income** with specific Colorado modifications (additions and subtractions).

**Starting Point = Federal Taxable Income:**

Because Colorado starts with federal taxable income, **any election that affects federal taxable income automatically flows through to Colorado**, unless Colorado law specifically decouples.

**Citation:** [Colorado Revised Statutes § 39-22-304](https://codes.findlaw.com/co/title-39-taxation/co-rev-st-sect-39-22-304/), [Corporate Income Tax Guide - Colorado Department of Revenue](https://tax.colorado.gov/corporate-income-tax-guide)
