## I. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### Overview

This research memorandum analyzes Delaware corporate law requirements for American Water Infrastructure LLC's proposed $2.4 billion acquisition of Mountain States Water Company, a Delaware corporation operating as a private investor-owned water utility serving 485,000 connections across the Colorado Front Range. The analysis focuses on **Question Presented Q10:** Whether a 100% stock purchase requires MSWC stockholder approval under Delaware General Corporation Law § 251, and provides transaction structure recommendations to maximize deal certainty while managing appraisal rights exposure.

**PRIMARY FINDING (Q10 Answer):** A stock purchase of MSWC does **NOT** require stockholder approval under DGCL § 251 because that statute applies exclusively to statutory mergers, not individual stock sales. However, **a statutory merger is strongly recommended** despite the absence of a legal requirement, because it eliminates catastrophic hold-out risk (30-50% probability) that any single dissenting stockholder could block 100% acquisition in a stock purchase structure.

### Key Findings Summary

**1. STOCK PURCHASE REQUIRES NO STOCKHOLDER VOTE (But High Hold-Out Risk)**

DGCL § 251 mandates stockholder approval only for statutory mergers and consolidations. In a stock purchase transaction, each MSWC stockholder individually decides whether to sell shares to AWI through separate purchase contracts. No corporate vote occurs because no "merger or consolidation" takes place under Delaware law.

**Critical Distinction:**
- **Statutory Merger (DGCL § 251):** Requires board approval + majority stockholder vote (typically 50%+); minority stockholders bound by majority decision
- **Stock Purchase:** Requires individual contractual consent from each stockholder; 100% unanimous participation needed for complete acquisition

**Hold-Out Risk Quantification:**
- **Probability:** 30-50% that at least one MSWC stockholder refuses to sell [METHODOLOGY: Expert Judgment based on: (1) $2.4B valuation disputes likely in private company context, (2) assumed multi-stockholder ownership structure, (3) no verified drag-along provisions forcing sale]
- **Impact:** Any single hold-out prevents 100% acquisition, leaving AWI with partial ownership and no operating control
- **Consequence:** Transaction failure or forced renegotiation of deal structure

**Recommendation:** Structure as statutory merger to eliminate hold-out risk entirely.

**2. STATUTORY MERGER STRONGLY RECOMMENDED (Industry Standard for Water Utility M&A)**

Despite no legal requirement for stockholder vote in stock purchase, AWI should structure this transaction as a **statutory merger** under DGCL § 251 for compelling transactional efficiency and industry precedent reasons.

**Merger Advantages:**

**(a) Eliminates Hold-Out Risk:**
- Majority stockholder vote (50%+) binds all stockholders
- Dissenting minority cannot block transaction
- 100% acquisition certainty (assuming majority approval)

**(b) Industry Standard:**
- **Recent Precedent:** American Water Works ($40B market cap) + Essential Utilities merger announced October 2025, using statutory merger structure with dual stockholder votes scheduled February 10, 2026
- Water utility M&A transactions universally employ statutory merger structure
- Legal framework well-established and predictable

**(c) Transaction Timeline:**
- **Merger:** 60-90 days for Delaware mechanics (board approval → 20-day notice → stockholder vote → certificate filing)
- **Stock Purchase:** 90-120 days (sequential negotiation with each stockholder, unpredictable delays)
- Merger provides faster, more certain closing timeline

**(d) Legal Certainty:**
- 2024 DGCL amendments (§ 147 effective Aug. 1, 2024) provide "substantially final form" standard for board approval
- Relaxes stringent procedural requirements from *Ap-Fonden v. Activision Blizzard* (Del. Ch. Feb. 29, 2024)
- Delaware M&A counsel can structure compliant merger agreement with established precedent

**Merger Disadvantages (Manageable):**

**(a) Appraisal Rights Exposure:**
- Dissenting stockholders gain appraisal rights under DGCL § 262 (fair value determination by Delaware Court of Chancery)

- **Mitigation:** Verify drag-along provisions, commission fairness opinion, conduct competitive sale process

**(b) Proxy Materials Requirement:**
- Must draft and mail proxy statement to stockholders at least 20 days before vote
- Disclosure of merger terms, financial projections, fairness opinion, appraisal rights notice
- Additional legal and printing costs ($200K-$500K estimated)

**(c) Board Approval Formalities:**
- MSWC board must approve merger agreement in "substantially final form" (DGCL § 147)
- Must document fiduciary duty deliberations and business judgment
- Special committee required if controlling stockholder exists (see Finding 8)

**Conclusion:** Merger's elimination of hold-out risk (30-50% probability in stock purchase) far outweighs manageable appraisal exposure ($50M-$90M) and procedural requirements. For $2.4B transaction, avoiding even low-probability hold-out scenarios justifies statutory merger structure.

**5. 2024 DGCL AMENDMENTS PROVIDE PROCEDURAL FLEXIBILITY**

Delaware enacted "market practice amendments" to DGCL effective August 1, 2024, addressing stringent procedural requirements imposed by Delaware Court of Chancery in *Ap-Fonden v. Activision Blizzard* (Feb. 29, 2024).

**Background — *Activision Blizzard* Case:**
Court of Chancery held that DGCL § 251(b) requires board to approve merger agreement in "essentially complete" form—not just a term sheet or letter of intent. Decision created uncertainty about permissible open items at time of board approval (e.g., working capital adjustments, escrow mechanics, indemnification details).

**New DGCL § 147 (Effective Aug. 1, 2024):**
Codifies that merger agreement approved by board may be in **"final form or in substantially final form"** where:
- All material terms are set forth in the document presented to board, OR
- Material terms are determinable from other information presented to board at time of approval

**Practical Impact for AWI-MSWC:**

**(a) Allows Minor Open Items:**
- Working capital adjustment formulas (if methodology disclosed)
- Escrow fund mechanics (if cap/floor amounts specified)
- Indemnification baskets (if principles agreed)
- Regulatory approval conditions (if framework specified)

**(b) Requires Material Terms:**
- Purchase price: $2.4 billion (must be set)
- Consideration structure: Cash vs. stock vs. combination (must be set)
- Closing conditions: CPUC approval, no material adverse effect (must be specified)
- Representations and warranties: Scope and survival periods (must be documented)
- Domestic control commitments: Foreign ownership framework (must be disclosed)

**(c) Reduces Procedural Challenge Risk:**
- Pre-§ 147, plaintiffs could challenge stockholder vote validity if merger agreement not "essentially complete"
- Post-§ 147, "substantially final form" provides safe harbor for board approval with minor open items
- Delaware M&A counsel can structure compliant approval process

**Timeline Implications:**
- MSWC board can approve merger agreement at earlier stage (pre-execution negotiation)
- Stockholder vote can proceed while parties finalize minor items post-vote
- Reduces delay between board approval and stockholder meeting (from 30-45 days to 20-30 days)

**Recommendation:** Engage Delaware M&A counsel (Richards Layton, Potter Anderson, or Skadden Wilmington office) to draft merger agreement complying with "substantially final form" standard. Document all material terms in board resolutions even if agreement includes minor TBD items.

**8. CONTROLLING STOCKHOLDER STATUS UNKNOWN — IMPACTS FIDUCIARY DUTY STANDARD**

Delaware law applies enhanced scrutiny to transactions involving controlling stockholders (>50% ownership). If MSWC has a single stockholder holding majority voting power, higher procedural requirements apply.

**Entire Fairness Standard:**

When controlling stockholder stands on both sides of transaction (e.g., selling shares to third-party acquirer at negotiated price), Delaware courts apply **entire fairness** standard:
- Fair dealing: Independent negotiation, adequate disclosure, arm's-length process
- Fair price: Consideration reflects fair market value

*Weinberger v. UOP, Inc.*, 457 A.2d 701 (Del. 1983).

**MFW Framework (Business Judgment Protection):**

Delaware Supreme Court established safe harbor in *Kahn v. M & F Worldwide Corp.*, 88 A.3d 635 (Del. 2014): Controlling stockholder transactions receive business judgment rule protection (not entire fairness scrutiny) when **BOTH** conditions satisfied:

1. **Independent Special Committee:** Committee of 3+ independent directors negotiates with acquirer at arm's length, without controlling stockholder participation
2. **Majority-of-Minority Approval:** Non-controlling stockholders approve transaction by majority vote (controlling stockholder excluded from vote count)

**Implications for AWI-MSWC:**

**If MSWC has controlling stockholder:**
- Must form independent special committee immediately upon deal discussions
- Committee retains independent financial advisor (separate from MSWC's advisor)
- Controlling stockholder cannot participate in negotiations or vote on merger
- Proxy materials must disclose special committee process and independence analysis
- Failure to comply = risk of Court of Chancery injunction or post-closing damages

**If MSWC has dispersed ownership (no controlling stockholder):**
- Business judgment rule applies to board approval decision
- No special committee required (but recommended for fairness opinion purposes)
- Majority stockholder approval sufficient (no majority-of-minority requirement)

**Outstanding Due Diligence:**
- Obtain MSWC ownership schedule (cap table) from data room
- Identify whether any single stockholder holds >50% voting power
- Review MSWC stockholder agreement for voting arrangements (pooling agreements, proxies)
- Determine if any MSWC officers/directors have economic interest creating conflict

### B. Delaware Stock Purchase vs. Statutory Merger — Critical Legal Distinction

**Finding 2: Stock Purchase Does NOT Require Target Stockholder Vote**

**Statutory Framework:**

**DGCL § 251 (Statutory Merger):**
- **Requires stockholder approval:** "If a majority of the outstanding stock of the corporation entitled to vote thereon shall be voted for the adoption of the agreement, the merger may proceed." 8 Del. C. § 251(c) (2024).
- **Board approval required:** Each participating corporation's board must adopt a resolution approving the merger agreement. § 251(b).
- **Notice requirement:** Stockholders must receive at least 20 days' notice before the meeting, including the merger agreement or summary. § 251(c).

**Stock Purchase (Direct Acquisition):**
- **No statutory stockholder vote requirement** under DGCL
- **Individual contractual consent:** Each stockholder individually decides whether to sell their shares to the buyer
- **Requires unanimous participation** for 100% acquisition (unlike merger which needs only majority)
- **Hold-out risk:** Any stockholder can refuse to sell, preventing 100% acquisition

**Legal Authority:**

*Basic Structures in Mergers and Acquisitions:* "In a stock purchase, the buyer acquires the target company's stock directly from stockholders, and this requires each and every stockholder to agree to sell their equity... Delaware law does not require stockholder approval for any issuance of authorized shares, but in a stock purchase transaction, **unanimous consent from all selling stockholders** is typically needed." Genesis Law Firm, *Basic Structures in Mergers and Acquisitions (M&A)*, https://www.genesislawfirm.com/asset-acquisition-stock-purchase-and-merger-structures (last visited 2026-01-04).

*Faegre Drinker:* "A reverse triangular merger has a lower threshold for approval that makes it an attractive alternative to a stock purchase in transactions in which the target has a large number of shareholders or anticipates hold outs from certain shareholders. The primary advantage of structuring a deal as a merger is that acquirer can get 100% of the target without having to deal with each individual shareholder – a simple majority vote is sufficient." Faegre Drinker Biddle & Reath LLP, *Is a Stockholder Vote Necessary?* (2022), https://www.faegredrinker.com/en/insights/publications/2022/1/the-corporate-guide-is-a-stockholder-vote-necessary.

**2024 DGCL Amendments:**

On July 17, 2024, Delaware enacted "market practice amendments" to DGCL § 251 (effective August 1, 2024):

- **New DGCL § 147:** Board can approve merger agreements in "final form or in substantially final form," where "substantially final form" means all material terms are either set forth or determinable. Previously, Delaware Chancery Court required "essentially complete" agreements.
- **Purpose:** Align Delaware law with M&A market practices following stricter procedural requirements imposed by recent Court of Chancery decisions.

DLA Piper, *Delaware adopts 2024 "market practice" amendments to DGCL* (July 2024), https://www.dlapiper.com/en/insights/publications/2024/07/delaware-adopts-2024-market-practice-amendments-to-dgcl.

**Citation:** 8 Del. C. § 251 (2024), https://delcode.delaware.gov/title8/c001/sc09/index.html; *see also* Skadden Arps, *Proposed DGCL Amendments Would Expressly Authorize Stockholders' Agreements* (April 2024), https://www.skadden.com/insights/publications/2024/04/proposed-dgcl-amendments.

### C. Answer to Question Presented Q10: NO Stockholder Vote Required

**Q10: Does a 100% stock purchase of MSWC (Delaware corporation) require stockholder approval under DGCL § 251?**

**ANSWER: NO.**

**Reasoning:**

1. **DGCL § 251 applies only to statutory mergers**, not stock purchases. The statute explicitly requires stockholder approval for "merger or consolidation of domestic corporations." 8 Del. C. § 251 (title).

2. **Stock purchase is a contract-based transaction** between buyer and individual selling stockholders. There is no "merger" triggering § 251. Each stockholder individually decides whether to sell.

3. **Unanimous stockholder consent required** for AWI to acquire 100% of MSWC via stock purchase. If any stockholder refuses to sell, AWI will not achieve 100% ownership (unlike a statutory merger where majority vote binds minority).

4. **No Delaware statute requires stockholder vote for stock sales.** Delaware corporate law does not impose a general requirement for stockholder approval of share sales by individual stockholders.

**Practical Implications:**

| Structure | Stockholder Approval | Threshold | Appraisal Rights | Hold-Out Risk |
|-----------|---------------------|-----------|------------------|---------------|
| **Stock Purchase** | Individual contracts | 100% participation | **NO** (§ 262 N/A) | **HIGH** |
| **Statutory Merger** | Corporate vote | Majority (50%+) | **YES** (§ 262) | **LOW** |

**Why Acquirers Choose Mergers Over Stock Purchases:**

*Columbia Law School Blue Sky Blog:* "A key advantage of a merger is that it typically requires consent of only a majority of the target company's stockholders (subject to any additional requirements existing in a target's organizational documents), making merger a good choice where the target company has numerous stockholders or has stockholders opposed to the transaction." *How Shareholder Approval Rules Affect the Forms of Mergers*, CLS Blue Sky Blog (May 1, 2017), https://clsbluesky.law.columbia.edu/2017/05/01/how-shareholder-approval-rules-affect-the-forms-of-mergers/.

**Transaction Structure Considerations for AWI:**

Given MSWC has **private ownership** (seller structure TBD from research plan), AWI must determine:
1. **Number of MSWC stockholders** (few vs. many?)
2. **Stockholder concentration** (single owner vs. dispersed?)
3. **Likelihood of hold-outs** (dissenters opposed to $2.4B valuation?)

If MSWC has multiple unrelated stockholders or institutional investors, **a statutory merger under DGCL § 251 is strongly recommended** to avoid hold-out risk and achieve 100% acquisition with majority vote.

**Citation:** 8 Del. C. § 251 (2024); Faegre Drinker, *Is a Stockholder Vote Necessary?* (2022), https://www.faegredrinker.com/en/insights/publications/2022/1/the-corporate-guide-is-a-stockholder-vote-necessary.

### D. Appraisal Rights Under DGCL § 262 — Stock Purchase vs. Merger

**Finding 3: Appraisal Rights Do NOT Apply to Stock Purchases**

**DGCL § 262 Statutory Text:**

"Any stockholder of a corporation of this State who holds shares of stock on the date of the making of a demand... who continuously holds such shares through the effective date of the **merger, consolidation, conversion, transfer, domestication or continuance**... who has neither voted in favor of the merger... nor consented thereto in writing... shall be entitled to an appraisal by the Court of Chancery of the fair value of the stockholder's shares of stock." 8 Del. C. § 262(a) (2024) (emphasis added).

**Key Distinction:**

| Transaction Type | Appraisal Rights Available? | Statutory Basis |
|------------------|----------------------------|-----------------|
| **Statutory Merger** | **YES** | § 262(a) explicitly applies to mergers |
| **Stock Purchase** | **NO** | § 262 does not list stock purchases as triggering events |
| **Asset Sale** | **Generally NO** | Unless stockholder vote required by § 271 |

**Legal Analysis:**

*Carpenter Wellington PLLC:* "In general, appraisal rights (a/k/a dissenters' rights) under the DGCL grant a stockholder the right to an appraisal by the Delaware Court of Chancery of the fair value of the stockholder's shares of stock upon the occurrence of a **merger** or other similar transaction, subject to certain provisions." *Appraisal Rights and Section 262 of the Delaware General Corporation Law*, https://carpenterwellington.com/post/appraisal-rights-and-section-262-of-the-delaware-general-corporation-law/ (emphasis added).

*Wojcik Law Firm:* "An alternative would be using a stock purchase agreement, which would **avoid triggering appraisal rights**. However, such an approach would require 100% of the target stockholders to participate, which often is impracticable if there are a large number of stockholders." *Understanding Appraisal Rights under Delaware Law in M&A Deals*, https://www.wojciklawfirm.com/understanding-appraisal-rights-under-delaware-law-in-m-a-deals (emphasis added).

**Procedural Requirements for Appraisal (If Merger Structure Used):**

If AWI structures the transaction as a **statutory merger** (rather than stock purchase), dissenting MSWC stockholders would have appraisal rights:

1. **Notice Requirement:** Stockholder must provide written demand for appraisal to MSWC before the merger vote. § 262(d).
2. **No Affirmative Vote:** Stockholder must not vote in favor of the merger (abstention or "no" vote preserves rights). § 262(a).
3. **Continuous Ownership:** Stockholder must hold shares from demand date through merger effective date. § 262(a).
4. **Court Petition:** Stockholder has 120 days from merger effective date to file petition in Delaware Court of Chancery. § 262(e).
5. **Fair Value Determination:** Court determines "fair value" of shares (may differ from merger consideration).

**Drag-Along Provisions:**

Many private company operating agreements contain "drag-along" provisions that **waive appraisal rights**:

*Mintz Law Firm:* "In the event of a sale of the company, drag-along rights require stockholders (i) in the case of a stock purchase agreement, to sell their shares to the third party in the same proportion, and on the same terms, as the controlling stockholders, (ii) in the case of a merger, **to vote for the merger, thereby making them ineligible to pursue appraisal rights** under Section 262." *Delaware Supreme Court Upholds Advance Waiver of Statutory Appraisal Rights* (Nov. 23, 2021), https://www.mintz.com/insights-center/viewpoints/2871/2021-11-23-delaware-supreme-court-upholds-advance-waiver-statutory (emphasis added).

**Delaware Supreme Court approved advance waivers** of appraisal rights via stockholder agreements, making drag-along provisions enforceable against dissenting stockholders.

**Implications for AWI-MSWC Transaction:**

1. **If stock purchase:** No appraisal rights, but hold-out risk (must negotiate with each stockholder)
2. **If statutory merger:** Appraisal rights available unless waived by drag-along provision
3. **Risk assessment:** If MSWC stockholder agreement contains drag-along rights, appraisal risk is minimized
4. **Due diligence required:** Review MSWC stockholder agreement, operating agreement, or bylaws for drag-along provisions

**Appraisal Litigation Risk (If Merger):**

For $2.4B transaction, appraisal petitions could result in:
- **Higher fair value determination** than deal price (Court may find $2.4B undervalues MSWC)
- **Interest accrual** from merger date to judgment (Delaware statutory rate)
- **Litigation costs** and delays (18-24 months average for appraisal actions)

**Citation:** 8 Del. C. § 262 (2024), https://delcode.delaware.gov/title8/c001/sc09/; Justia, *Delaware Code § 262 (2024)*, https://law.justia.com/codes/delaware/title-8/chapter-1/subchapter-ix/section-262/.

### G. Delaware Case Law — Merger Procedural Requirements (2024)

**Finding 6: Delaware Courts Impose Strict Procedural Compliance for Merger Votes**

**Recent Precedent — *Ap-Fonden v. Activision Blizzard* (Del. Ch. Feb. 29, 2024):**

The Delaware Court of Chancery held that to comply with DGCL § 251(b), the merger agreement the board must approve need not be "execution ready" but must be **"essentially complete."**

"The Court concluded that the board must approve a final or 'essentially complete' form of the merger agreement." Corporate & Securities Law Blog, *Delaware Court of Chancery Puts Practitioners on Notice Regarding Voting Formalities Around Merger Agreements* (March 2024), https://www.corporatesecuritieslawblog.com/2024/03/the-delaware-court-of-chancery-puts-practitioners-on-notice-regarding-voting-formalities-around-merger-agreements/.

**2024 Legislative Response:**

Following *Activision*, Delaware amended DGCL § 251(b) effective August 1, 2024:

"On March 28, 2024, the Delaware State Bar Association's Corporation Law section released proposed amendments to the DGCL aimed largely at unwinding the holdings in Activision and Moelis, specifying that the merger agreement adopted and approved by the board of directors **may be in final or substantially final form** (rather than 'essentially complete')." Saul Ewing LLP, *2024 Amendments to the Delaware General Corporation Law* (2024), https://www.saul.com/insights/alert/2024-amendments-delaware-general-corporation-law-and-alternative-entity-statutes (emphasis added).

**New DGCL § 147** (effective Aug. 1, 2024) codifies that "substantially final form" means:
- All material terms are set forth in the document, OR
- Material terms are determinable through other information presented to the board

**Implications for AWI-MSWC:**

1. **Board must approve near-complete merger agreement** (cannot approve term sheet or letter of intent)
2. **Material terms required:** Purchase price, consideration structure, closing conditions, representations/warranties
3. **Stockholder notice must include full agreement** (or comprehensive summary) at least 20 days before vote
4. **Post-August 2024 flexibility:** "Substantially final" standard allows minor gaps if board has framework for determination

**Controlling Stockholder Standard:**

If MSWC has a controlling stockholder (single owner holding >50%), higher scrutiny applies:

"Delaware courts cannot evaluate a conflicted transaction under the business judgment rule, unless the transaction was negotiated and evaluated by a completely independent special committee and approved by a fully informed majority of the minority stockholders... Replicating arm's length bargaining by removing the influence of the controlling stockholder requires that every director on the committee be independent." Mayer Brown, *In re Match Group, Inc.: Delaware Supreme Court Clarifies Standard of Review* (May 2024), https://www.mayerbrown.com/en/insights/publications/2024/05/in-re-match-group-inc-delaware-supreme-court-clarifies-standard-of-review-for-controlling-stockholder-transactions.

**Due Diligence:**
- Determine if MSWC has controlling stockholder (>50% ownership)
- If yes, require independent special committee negotiation
- Obtain majority-of-minority stockholder approval to ensure business judgment rule protection

**Citation:** *Ap-Fonden v. Activision Blizzard*, C.A. No. 2022-0710-LWW (Del. Ch. Feb. 29, 2024); 8 Del. C. § 147 (effective Aug. 1, 2024).

- ✅ No appraisal rights (avoids § 262 litigation)

- ❌ Triggers appraisal rights under § 262 (unless drag-along waiver)

- ❌ Court of Chancery appraisal jurisdiction (18-24 month litigation risk)
