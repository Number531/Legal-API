## X. METHODOLOGY DISCLOSURE

### A. Monte Carlo Simulation Parameters

**Simulation Configuration:**
- **Iterations:** 10,000 runs
- **Random Number Generator:** Mersenne Twister (industry standard)
- **Confidence Intervals:** P50 (median), P75 (conservative), P90 (severe downside), P95 (worst case)
- **Discount Rate:** 12% (PE aviation sector hurdle rate)
- **Time Horizon:** 3 years for multi-year exposures; 1 year for one-time events

**Distribution Assignments:**

| Risk Category | Distribution Type | Rationale |
|---------------|------------------|-----------|
| United CPA Renegotiation | Triangular (min, mode, max) | Known minimum (5% reduction), maximum (10% reduction), most likely (7.5%) based on Republic precedent |
| Delta CPA Renegotiation | Uniform | Equal probability across $0-$360M range given Delta's historically less aggressive stance |
| Aircraft Lease Costs | Normal | Large sample size (145 leases) justifies normal distribution around mean consent cost |
| Maintenance Reserves | Deterministic | 100% probability at $108M annual cost (contractual obligation revealed in due diligence) |
| JetBlue Slot Dispute | Triangular | Settlement outcome range with most likely value at midpoint |
| ALPA Strike | Triangular | Strike duration uncertainty (2 weeks to 2 months most likely) |
| ALPA Settlement | Normal | Wage increase percentage centered around 11% market rate |
| Unionization Risks | Uniform | Limited precedent data; equal probability across ranges |
| WARN Act | Triangular | Backpay calculation relatively certain; attorney fees variable |
| EEOC Class Action | Lognormal | Litigation outcomes exhibit right-skewed distribution (small settlements common; large verdicts rare) |
| Regulatory Items | Triangular/Uniform | Based on precedent penalty ranges |

### B. Correlation Modeling Methodology

Correlations estimated using **Expert Judgment** based on:
1. **Economic linkages:** CPA counterparties respond to common market conditions
2. **Contractual dependencies:** Strike triggers CPA force majeure provisions
3. **Operational relationships:** Union organizing campaigns share common catalyst (PE acquisition)
4. **Historical precedents:** Republic Airways bankruptcy demonstrated CPA-labor interaction effects

**Limitation:** Precise correlation coefficients cannot be calculated from limited precedent data. Sensitivity analysis (Section VIII) tests impact of ±20% correlation adjustment.

**12% Weighted Average Cost of Capital (WACC) Derivation:**

| Component | Weight | Cost | Contribution |
|-----------|--------|------|--------------|
| Equity (PE target return) | 65% | 15.0% | 9.75% |

**Aviation Sector Risk Factors Supporting 15% Equity Hurdle:**
- Cyclical industry with high fixed costs

- Regulatory risk (FAA/DOT compliance)

- Labor unionization (RLA constraints)

### D. Probability Assessment Methodology

All probability ranges disclosed in Section III.A were derived using one of four methodologies:

| Methodology | Risk Categories | Derivation Basis |
|-------------|-----------------|------------------|
| **Industry Precedent** | United/Delta CPA leverage, Aircraft lease consents | Republic Airways bankruptcy (2016-2017), Continental-UAL merger, Alaska-Virgin America |
| **Regulatory History** | DOT tarmac penalty, DOT foreign ownership | DOT enforcement orders 2015-2024 (United $1.9M, American $4.1M, ExpressJet $200K) |
| **Expert Judgment** | ALPA strike probability, Unionization campaigns | Based on (1) 7-point wage gap, (2) NMB 97% settlement rate, (3) pilot labor market tightness, (4) PE ownership catalyst |
| **Statutory Certainty** | Maintenance reserves (100%), WARN Act damages formula | Contractual obligations (maintenance reserves); 29 U.S.C. § 2104 (WARN backpay formula) |

**Confidence Intervals by Methodology:**
- **Statutory Certainty:** ±5% (narrow range)
- **Regulatory History:** ±15% (moderate precedent variability)
- **Industry Precedent:** ±20% (transaction-specific factors vary)
- **Expert Judgment:** ±30% (highest uncertainty)

### E. Known Limitations and Assumptions

**Critical Assumptions:**
1. **CPA consent provisions:** Assumed "materially adverse" standard in United CPA based on task memo reference to Section 12.4; actual contract language not reviewed
2. **CPPIB governance rights:** Assumed CPPIB has special rights (board appointments, veto authority) justifying 30% equity/15% voting disparity; actual operating agreement not reviewed
3. **Aircraft lease portfolio:** Assumed standard assignment consent provisions across all 145 leases; individual lease terms not reviewed
4. **ALPA strike timing:** Assumed strike possible May-August 2027 based on December 2026 CBA expiration + 5-7 months NMB mediation + 30-day cooling off
5. **No discovery of additional liabilities:** Analysis limited to issues identified in specialist reports; comprehensive document review may reveal additional exposures

**Data Gaps Affecting Confidence:**
- United/Delta/American CPA full contract terms (change of control provisions, force majeure, termination rights)
- CPPIB-KKR operating agreement (board composition, approval rights, co-investment provisions)
- Individual aircraft lease agreements (assignment fees, security deposits, return conditions)
- SkyWest maintenance reserve account statements by lessor
- ALPA negotiation proposals and management counterproposals (wage tables, work rules)

**Model Limitations:**
- **No second-order effects modeled:** E.g., CPA rate reductions → EBITDA decline → covenant breach → accelerated debt maturity
- **No macroeconomic scenarios:** Assumes stable aviation demand; recession could compound CPA and labor risks
- **Static probability distributions:** Does not model changing probabilities over time (e.g., strike probability increases if negotiations drag)
- **No management mitigation credit:** Conservative assumption that GAH takes no proactive steps to reduce risks
