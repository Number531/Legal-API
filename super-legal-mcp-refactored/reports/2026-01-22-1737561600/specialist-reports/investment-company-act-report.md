### B. Board Responsibilities and 15(c) Annual Advisory Contract Renewal

#### 1. Section 15(c) Requirements

Section 15(c) of the Investment Company Act requires a **majority of a fund's independent directors to annually approve the fund's advisory contract at an in-person meeting** called for that purpose.‚Å∑ This annual renewal process is a cornerstone of mutual fund governance and investor protection.

#### 2. Gartenberg Factors - The Excessive Fee Standard

The **Gartenberg v. Merrill Lynch Asset Management, Inc., 694 F.2d 923 (2d Cir. 1982)** case established the judicial standard for determining when mutual fund advisory fees violate fiduciary duty under Section 36(b):

**Gartenberg Test:** "Whether the fee schedule represents a charge within the range of what would have been negotiated at arm's-length in the light of all of the surrounding circumstances."‚Åπ

**More specifically:** An adviser violates Section 36(b) if it charges a fee "so disproportionately large that it bears no reasonable relationship to the services rendered and could not have been the product of arm's-length bargaining."¬π‚Å∞

**Supreme Court Endorsement - Jones v. Harris:**

In **Jones v. Harris Associates, 559 U.S. 335 (2010)**, the U.S. Supreme Court unanimously endorsed the Gartenberg standard, holding that boards must engage in "rigorous scrutiny of adviser compensation, taking into account all relevant factors."¬π¬π However, the Court emphasized this is **not rate regulation** and that "a measure of deference to board decisions is appropriate if they are fully informed and conscientious in their review."¬π¬≤

**The Six Gartenberg Factors Boards Must Review:**¬π¬≥

1. **Nature and quality of services** provided to fund shareholders
2. **Profitability of the fund** to the adviser
3. **Fall-out benefits** (indirect benefits to adviser from managing the fund)
4. **Economies of scale** (fee reductions as AUM grows)
5. **Comparative fee structures** (peer comparison)
6. **Independence and conscientiousness** of the directors

**Directors should be mindful that the Gartenberg factors are non-exhaustive** and should evaluate any other relevant factors when approving advisory agreements.¬π‚Å¥

### C. 12b-1 Distribution Fees and Revenue Sharing Controversy

#### 1. Rule 12b-1 Legal Framework

In 1980, the SEC adopted **Rule 12b-1 under the Investment Company Act**, which permits funds to compensate brokers and other financial intermediaries out of fund assets for services they provide shareholders related to the distribution of fund shares.¬π‚Åµ

**Permitted Uses of 12b-1 Fees:**¬π‚Å∂

**Prohibition on Brokerage Commission Arrangements:**

Rule 12b-1 **prohibits** funds from paying for distribution with brokerage commissions on portfolio transactions, and requires funds to implement policies reasonably designed to prevent broker-dealers' promotional efforts from influencing brokerage allocation decisions.¬π‚Å∑
