**Exception - Compensatory Disgorgement May Be Insurable:**

Recent case law provides a nuanced exception. In *J.P. Morgan Securities Inc. v. Vigilant Insurance Co.*, 183 N.E.3d 443 (N.Y. 2021), New York's highest court held that a $140 million SEC payment labeled as "disgorgement" was insurable under a D&O policy because the payment was calculated based on client gains and investor losses, serving a **compensatory purpose rather than a punitive one**, and did not constitute a "penalty imposed by law."‚Å∏

**Legal Standard:** The term "penalty" is "commonly understood to reference a monetary sanction designed to address a public wrong that is sought for purposes of deterrence and punishment rather than to compensate injured parties for their loss."‚Åπ If an award serves both compensatory and punitive purposes, it is not "punitive" for purposes of insurance coverage and is insurable under New York law.¬π‚Å∞

#### 4. Claims-Made Policy Considerations

**Most management and professional liability policies‚Äîlike E&O, D&O, and EPL‚Äîare written on a "claims-made" basis,** which means they provide coverage based on when the claim is first made against the policyholder, not when the underlying wrongful act occurred.¬≤‚Å∞

**a. Notice of Circumstances (NOC)**

Most claims-made policies allow for **notice of circumstances (NOC)** to preserve rights under an existing policy should a claim materialize in the future. If the policyholder provides timely notice of circumstances, the insurer will consider any later claim that arises out of the noticed circumstances to have been **first made during the policy period in which the notice was given**.¬≤¬π

**Critical Issue for Pinnacle:**

The October 2023 SEC examination represents a "circumstance that may give rise to a claim." Pinnacle should have reported this examination to its E&O carrier **immediately** in October 2023 to preserve coverage under the 2023-2024 policy period, even if no formal claim (Wells notice) had yet been made.

**If Pinnacle failed to report the October 2023 examination:**
- Coverage may be denied under the current policy if the claim is deemed to relate to the October 2023 examination (prior act exclusion)
- This is one of the most common reasons for claim denials under claims-made policies¬≤¬≤

**b. Prior Acts Coverage**

Claims-made policies typically include a "retroactive date." Coverage applies only to wrongful acts occurring after the retroactive date. If Pinnacle's current E&O policy has a retroactive date after October 2023, coverage for SEC examination deficiencies may be excluded as "prior acts."
