### A. SEC Enforcement Precedents - Custody Rule Violations (Rule 206(4)-2)

#### 1. Findings

**Rule 206(4)-2 Overview**: The custody rule requires registered investment advisers who have custody or possession of client assets to maintain those assets with a qualified custodian and ensure appropriate safeguards. Key requirements include annual surprise examinations by independent public accountants or distribution of audited financial statements to pooled vehicle investors within 120-180 days of fiscal year-end.

**Recent Enforcement Actions (2020-2024)**:

- **Violation**: Failed to obtain required surprise examinations from 2018-2024 despite having custody through signatory authority and power of attorney
- **Settlement**: $50,000 civil penalty
- **Factors**: Technical violation, no client funds missing, cooperation with SEC
- **Citation**: SEC Order, IA-6901-s (2024), https://www.sec.gov/enforcement-litigation/administrative-proceedings/ia-6901-s [VERIFIED]

#### 2. Legal Framework

**17 C.F.R. Â§ 275.206(4)-2** (Custody Rule):
- (a)(1): Adviser with custody must maintain client funds/securities with qualified custodian
- (a)(4): Annual surprise examination by independent public accountant OR
- (a)(7): Distribution of audited financial statements (120/180/260 days depending on fund type)
- (b)(3): Pooled vehicle exception requires GAAP audit + timely distribution to investors

**Investment Advisers Act Section 206(4)**: Makes it unlawful for investment advisers to "engage in any act, practice, or course of business which is fraudulent, deceptive, or manipulative"

**Qualified Custodian Definition** (Rule 206(4)-2(d)(6)): Bank, savings association, registered broker-dealer, registered futures commission merchant, or foreign financial institution meeting specified criteria

**Crypto Asset Considerations** (Galois Capital precedent): Trading platforms generally do not qualify as "qualified custodians" unless they meet specific regulatory criteria; advisers bear burden of due diligence
