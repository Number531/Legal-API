### A. Massachusetts Noncompetition Agreement Act (M.G.L. c. 149, ¬ß 24L)

#### 1. Statutory Framework (Effective October 1, 2018)

The Massachusetts Noncompetition Agreement Act fundamentally reformed restrictive covenant law in Massachusetts. To be enforceable, a noncompetition agreement must satisfy strict statutory requirements established in M.G.L. c. 149, ¬ß 24L [VERIFIED via Massachusetts Legislature website, accessed January 22, 2026].¬π

**Key Statutory Requirements:**

**(a) Consideration Requirement** - A noncompetition agreement must provide either:

1. **Garden Leave Clause**: Payment on a pro-rata basis during the entirety of the restricted period of at least 50% of the employee's highest annualized base salary paid within the 2 years preceding termination; OR

2. **Other Mutually-Agreed Upon Consideration**: Specified in the noncompetition agreement and agreed to by both employer and employee.¬≤

Recent case law clarifies that employment alone does not constitute sufficient "mutually-agreed upon consideration." In *KPM Analytics North America Corporation v. Blue Sun Scientific*, the court held that noncompetition agreements lacking either a garden leave clause or specifically articulated "other mutually-agreed upon consideration" violate the Act and are not legally binding.¬≥

**(b) Maximum Duration**: 12 months from termination of employment (statutory maximum).‚Å¥

**(c) Reasonable Scope**: Geographic scope and industry scope must be necessary to protect a legitimate business interest, including:
- Trade secrets (as defined by M.G.L. c. 93, ¬ß 42)
- Confidential information that does not rise to trade secrets
- Goodwill associated with ongoing customer relationships‚Åµ

**(d) Blue-Pencil Modification**: Massachusetts courts may modify overbroad non-competes by reducing duration or narrowing geographic/industry scope to render them reasonable.‚Å∂

**(e) Excluded Workers** - Noncompetition agreements are NOT enforceable against:
- Employees classified as nonexempt under FLSA
- Students in internships or short-term employment programs
- Employees terminated without cause or laid off
- Employees age 18 or younger‚Å∑

**(f) Choice of Law**: No choice-of-law provision can avoid Massachusetts requirements if the employee is, and has been for at least 30 days immediately preceding cessation of employment, a Massachusetts resident or employed in Massachusetts at termination.‚Å∏

### B. Non-Solicitation Agreements Distinguished from Non-Competes

#### 1. Traditional Reasonableness Standard

Following *Miele*, non-solicitation covenants are analyzed under traditional Massachusetts contract law, not the MNAA. Massachusetts courts enforce non-solicitation agreements if they are:

1. **Necessary to protect legitimate business interest** (customer relationships, confidential information)
2. **Reasonable in temporal scope** (typically 1-2 years)
3. **Reasonable in geographic scope** (aligned with actual customer base)
4. **Not unduly burdensome to employee** (does not prevent all employment in industry)¬π‚Å¥

**Pinnacle's Non-Solicitation Terms**:

The hypothetical employment agreements include:
- **Client Non-Solicitation**: 1-year prohibition on soliciting Pinnacle clients to transfer assets or terminate advisory relationships
- **Employee Non-Solicitation**: 1-year prohibition on recruiting Pinnacle investment professionals

**Enforceability**: These provisions are **highly enforceable** under Massachusetts law. Courts consistently uphold 1-year client non-solicitation agreements in the investment advisory industry, recognizing that client relationships constitute protectable goodwill.¬π‚Åµ

#### 2. Critical Distinction: Active Solicitation vs. Client-Initiated Contact

**Enforceable**: Non-solicitation prohibits the departing PM from **actively soliciting** clients (phone calls, emails, meetings to encourage asset transfers).

**Not Enforceable**: Non-solicitation **cannot prevent** clients from independently choosing to follow the PM to a new firm if the client initiates contact. Massachusetts courts recognize clients' freedom to choose their investment adviser.¬π‚Å∂

#### 1. Standard Key Person Clause Structure
