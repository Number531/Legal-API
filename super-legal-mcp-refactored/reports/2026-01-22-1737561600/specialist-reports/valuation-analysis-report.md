**Key Requirements:**

**Pinnacle Application**: Pinnacle Investment Management serves as the board-designated valuation designee for the Credit Opportunities Fund and Opportunity Fund. This designation was formalized in June 2024 following SEC examination deficiencies.

### B. ASC 820 Fair Value Measurement Hierarchy

#### 1. Three-Level Fair Value Hierarchy

**ASC 820** (FASB Accounting Standards Codification Topic 820) establishes a framework for measuring fair value and requires disclosure about fair value measurements.Â¹Â¹ The standard defines fair value as "the price that would be received to sell an asset or paid to transfer a liability in an orderly transaction between market participants at the measurement date."

**Fair Value Hierarchy:**Â¹Â²

**Level 1: Quoted Prices in Active Markets**
- Unadjusted quoted prices in active markets for identical assets or liabilities
- Examples: Publicly traded stocks on NYSE, exchange-traded derivatives
- **Pinnacle**: None of the $360M illiquid positions qualify as Level 1

**Level 2: Observable Inputs Other Than Quoted Prices**
- Observable inputs other than Level 1 quoted prices, including:
  - Quoted prices for similar assets or liabilities in active markets
  - Quoted prices for identical or similar assets in inactive markets
  - Observable inputs other than quoted prices (interest rates, yield curves, credit spreads)
  - Inputs corroborated by market data
- Examples: Corporate bonds valued using credit spreads, interest rate swaps valued using SOFR curve
- **Pinnacle**: OTC derivatives ($220M notional) qualify as Level 2 because they are valued using broker quotes based on observable SOFR curve

**Level 3: Unobservable Inputs**
- Unobservable inputs used when relevant observable inputs are unavailable
- Reflects entity's own assumptions about what market participants would use
- Requires significant management judgment
- Examples: DIP loans in bankruptcy, private equity pre-IPO positions, complex derivatives with no market activity
- **Pinnacle**: $360M in Credit Opportunities Fund distressed debt and Opportunity Fund PIPE positions are Level 3

#### 2. Level 3 Disclosure Requirements

Because Level 3 fair value relies on unobservable inputs, **enhanced disclosure and documentation** are required:Â¹Â³

1. **Quantitative Details About Unobservable Inputs**:
   - Discount rates, growth rates, terminal multiples
   - Recovery rate assumptions in bankruptcy scenarios
   - Comparable company selection criteria and adjustments
   - Probability-weighting for scenario analysis

2. **Description of Valuation Process**:
   - How unobservable inputs were developed
   - Whether inputs were derived from market data or entity-specific assumptions
   - Frequency of recalibration and backtesting

3. **Sensitivity Analysis**:
   - Impact on fair value from changes in significant unobservable inputs
   - Range of reasonably possible alternative inputs

**Pinnacle Application**: The June 2024 enhancement of valuation policies requires documentation of all Level 3 inputs. However, the 18-month stale marks on PIPE positions suggest inadequate sensitivity analysis. If comparable public companies declined 40% but Pinnacle did not adjust PIPE marks, this indicates:
- Failure to consider reasonably available observable inputs (public comparables)
- Over-reliance on single data point (last financing round)
- Insufficient sensitivity analysis of market condition changes

#### 3. Prioritization of Inputs - Observable vs. Unobservable

ASC 820 requires that entities **maximize the use of observable inputs and minimize the use of unobservable inputs** when determining fair value.Â¹â´

**Implication for PIPE Positions**: Even though PIPE positions are private securities (suggesting Level 3), the existence of comparable public companies that trade actively provides Level 2 observable inputs. Best practice requires:
- Identify comparable public companies in same industry, similar size, growth stage
- Apply public company multiples (EV/Revenue, EV/EBITDA, P/E) to private company metrics
- Apply **discount for lack of marketability** (DLOM) typically 20-40% for pre-IPO companies
- Reconcile back to last financing round, explaining variance

### C. DCF Modeling for Distressed Debt

#### 1. Methodology Framework

**Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) analysis** for distressed debt requires scenario-based modeling reflecting the binary outcomes common in bankruptcy: emergence as going concern vs. liquidation.Â¹âµ

**General Approach:**Â¹â¶
1. Forecast unlevered free cash flows over 3-5 year period (assuming successful reorganization)
2. Apply discount rate reflecting distress risk (typically 15-25% for companies in bankruptcy)
3. Calculate terminal value using exit multiples (4-7Ã— EBITDA depending on industry and restructured capital structure)
4. Discount terminal value and interim cash flows to present value
5. Compare to liquidation scenario (asset sale, recovery rate assumptions)
6. Probability-weight scenarios if appropriate

**Discount Rate Selection:**Â¹â·
For distressed companies, the discount rate must reflect **heightened risk**:
- **Range**: 15-25% (compared to 8-12% for healthy companies)
- **Components**: Risk-free rate + equity risk premium + distress premium + company-specific risk
- **Pinnacle's 15% rate**: At low end of range, appropriate only if emergence probability is high and post-reorganization capital structure is conservative

**Beta Adjustment**: The cost of equity beta should be re-estimated using the **updated debt-to-equity ratio** post-reorganization, as leverage typically declines dramatically (debt converted to equity).Â¹â¸

#### 2. Recovery Rate Analysis - Bankruptcy Scenarios

**Recovery rates** vary dramatically by seniority and bankruptcy outcome:Â¹â¹

### D. Private Equity PIPE Position Valuation

#### 1. PIPE (Private Investment in Public Equity) Overview

**PIPE transactions** involve private placements of securities in already-public companies to selected institutional accredited investors.Â²âµ In the context of Pinnacle's Opportunity Fund, the term "PIPE" appears to refer to **pre-IPO private equity positions** acquired through private placements, with the anticipation of future IPO liquidity.

**Valuation Challenge**: Private company securities lack observable market prices, requiring mark-to-model approaches.

#### 2. Valuation Methodologies for Private Equity

**Three Common Approaches:**Â²â¶

**(a) Publicly Traded Comparable Companies Method:**
- Identify public companies in same industry, similar business model, comparable size
- Calculate valuation multiples (EV/Revenue, EV/EBITDA, P/E)
- Apply multiples to private company's metrics
- Adjust for differences (growth rate, profitability, scale)
- Apply discount for lack of marketability (DLOM)

**(b) Transaction Comparables Method:**
- Review recent M&A transactions or financing rounds in sector
- Analyze transaction multiples paid
- Adjust for differences in deal terms, market conditions, timing
- Apply to private company metrics

**(c) Discounted Cash Flow Method:**
- Project future cash flows over 5-10 years
- Apply appropriate discount rate (cost of equity for minority interest)
- Calculate terminal value using exit multiples or perpetuity growth
- Discount to present value

**Pinnacle's Methodology**: Marking PIPE positions at **last financing round** valuation. This is the **transaction comparables approach**, using the most recent capital raise as the fair value indicator.

#### 3. Stale Marks Problem

**Critical Issue**: Pinnacle's PIPE marks are based on financing rounds from **18 months ago**, during which:
- Comparable public companies declined 40%
- Macroeconomic conditions changed (interest rates, inflation, growth expectations)
- Sector-specific headwinds may have emerged

**Stale Marks Analysis:**Â²â·
During the COVID-19 pandemic, public markets quickly adjusted valuations in March 2020, but private equity valuations lagged until Q2 financials were released in August-September 2020 (5-6 month lag). During significant market-moving events, private equity managers tend to be **more conservative** with valuations, leading to lower volatility than public markets.

**However**: 18-month lag without adjustment is **excessive** and fails to incorporate observable market information from comparable public companies.

**International Private Equity and Venture Capital Valuation Guidelines (IPEV):**Â²â¸
- Recommend **backtesting** - comparing actual liquidity events (sale, IPO, subsequent financing) to prior fair value estimates to assess valuation accuracy
- Require consideration of **market calibration** - using public market multiples to calibrate private valuations
- Emphasize **consistency** - applying methodology consistently across quarters, with adjustments when market conditions change materially

**Pinnacle's Failure to Adjust**: By maintaining 18-month-old marks despite 40% decline in comparables, Pinnacle:
- Violated the market calibration principle
- Failed to maximize use of observable inputs (ASC 820 requirement)
- Created **material risk** of NAV overstatement
