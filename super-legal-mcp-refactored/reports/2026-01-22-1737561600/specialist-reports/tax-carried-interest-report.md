### A. IRC ยง 1061 - CARRIED INTEREST TAXATION (3-Year Holding Period Requirement)

#### 1. Legislative Background and Statutory Framework

**Tax Cuts and Jobs Act (TCJA) - Section 1061 Addition**

Section 1061 was added to the Internal Revenue Code on December 22, 2017, as part of the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act (Pub. L. 115-97), applying to taxable years beginning after December 31, 2017.ยน The provision fundamentally altered the taxation of carried interest by extending the holding period required for long-term capital gains treatment from more than one year to more than three years for "applicable partnership interests" (APIs).ยฒ

**Statutory Citation**: 26 U.S.C. ยง 1061 [VERIFIED via Cornell Legal Information Institute]
**Regulatory Framework**: Treasury Regulation ยง 1.1061-1 et seq. (Final Regulations TD 9945, published January 19, 2021) [VERIFIED via Federal Register 86 FR 5534]

**Policy Rationale**: Congress enacted Section 1061 to address concerns that certain investment fund managers were converting what was essentially compensation for services (ordinarily taxed at rates up to 37%) into long-term capital gains (taxed at preferential 20% rates) through the use of carried interest structures.ยณ

#### 2. Applicable Partnership Interests (APIs) - Definition and Scope

**Statutory Definition** (IRC ยง 1061(c)(1)):

An "applicable partnership interest" means any interest in a partnership which, directly or indirectly, is transferred to (or is held by) the taxpayer in connection with the performance of substantial services by the taxpayer, or any other related person, in any applicable trade or business.โด

**Applicable Trade or Business** (IRC ยง 1061(c)(2)):

An "applicable trade or business" means any activity conducted on a regular, continuous, and substantial basis which consists, in whole or in part, of:
- Raising or returning capital, AND
- Either investing in or disposing of specified assets (or identifying specified assets for such investing or disposing), OR
- Developing specified assetsโต

**Specified Assets** include securities, commodities, real estate held for rental or investment, cash equivalents, and options or derivative contracts with respect to such assets.โถ

**Application to Pinnacle's Hedge Funds**:

Both the Opportunity Fund LP and Credit Opportunities Fund LP qualify as applicable trades or businesses:
- **Opportunity Fund** ($4.8B AUM): 130/30 long/short equity strategy involves continuous trading of securities (specified assets)
- **Credit Opportunities Fund** ($1.5B AUM): Distressed debt and DIP loans constitute securities and financial instruments (specified assets)

The performance fees (20% carried interest) earned by Pinnacle's general partner and senior portfolio managers constitute APIs because:
1. The interests are transferred/held in connection with performance of substantial investment management services
2. The services relate to an applicable trade or business (raising capital, investing in/disposing of specified assets)
3. The interests provide profit allocations tied to investment performance, not commensurate capital contributions

#### 3. Three-Year Holding Period Requirement - Recharacterization Mechanics

**Recharacterization Rule** (IRC ยง 1061(a)):

If an API holder (taxpayer) has net long-term capital gain from the partnership that would otherwise be treated as long-term capital gain, such gain is recharacterized as **short-term capital gain** (taxed at ordinary income rates up to 37%) to the extent the gain is attributable to assets held by the partnership for **three years or less**.โท

**Critical Distinction from General Partnership Rules**:
- **Standard Rule** (IRC ยง 1222): Long-term capital gain requires holding period of more than one year
- **Section 1061 Override**: For APIs, long-term capital gain treatment requires holding period of more than three years
- **Tax Rate Impact**: Short-term capital gains taxed at ordinary rates (10%-37%), long-term capital gains at preferential rates (0%-20%)โธ

**One-Year Look-Through Rule** (Treas. Reg. ยง 1.1061-4(b)(9)):

For purposes of determining whether capital gain allocated to an API holder qualifies for long-term treatment, the partnership's holding period in the underlying asset is tested. The taxpayer cannot aggregate or "tack" holding periods from related partnerships except in limited circumstances.โน

#### 5. Exceptions and Planning Opportunities

**Capital Interest Exception** (IRC ยง 1061(c)(4)(B)):

Section 1061 does NOT apply to any interest in a partnership that provides a right to share in partnership capital **commensurate with the amount of capital contributed** by the partner (or amounts paid for such interest).ยนยฒ

**Application**: If Pinnacle's GPs/PMs hold separate capital interests (e.g., as limited partners investing their own capital alongside institutional investors), allocations attributable to those capital interests are NOT subject to the 3-year holding period. Only the performance fee/carried interest allocation is subject to Section 1061.

**Contemporaneous Documentation Requirement**: Treas. Reg. ยง 1.1061-3(c)(2) requires partnerships to maintain **contemporaneous documentation** distinguishing API capital from non-API capital to preserve the capital interest exception. Failure to maintain such records results in the entire interest being treated as an API.ยนยณ

**Corporate Exception** (Treas. Reg. ยง 1.1061-1(a)):

Section 1061 does NOT apply to corporations. If a C corporation holds a carried interest, the 3-year holding period does not apply. However, this provides limited benefit because:
- Corporate tax rate is 21% (higher than 20% LTCG rate but lower than 37% ordinary rate)
- Double taxation occurs when distributions are made to individual shareholders (corporate tax + dividend tax)ยนโด

**Pinnacle Structure**: As a C corporation, Pinnacle Investment Management, Inc. itself is not subject to Section 1061. However, performance fees are typically allocated to individual GPs/PMs through the partnership structures, and those individuals ARE subject to Section 1061.

#### 6. Qualified Dividend Income (QDI) and Section 1256 Exemptions

**Certain Income Types Exempt from 3-Year Rule**:

Per Treas. Reg. ยง 1.1061-4(b)(7), the following income types allocated to an API holder retain their character and are NOT subject to recharacterization even if the underlying assets are held <3 years:

- **Qualified Dividend Income (QDI)**: Dividends from domestic corporations and qualified foreign corporations meeting IRC ยง 1(h)(11) requirements remain taxed at 20% preferential rate regardless of holding periodยนโต
- **Section 1256 Contracts**: Gains from regulated futures contracts, foreign currency contracts, and certain options on broad-based indices (marked-to-market, 60% long-term / 40% short-term treatment regardless of actual holding period)ยนโถ

**Application to Pinnacle**:

- **Opportunity Fund** (long/short equity): Likely receives substantial QDI from long equity positions. This provides partial relief from Section 1061 recharacterization.
- **Credit Opportunities Fund** (distressed debt): DIP loans and distressed debt typically generate interest income (ordinary) rather than QDI, so limited benefit from this exception.

**Estimated Benefit**: If 15-25% of Opportunity Fund's gains are QDI, approximately $2.2M-$3.7M of the $14.7M estimated carry might escape ordinary income treatment via the QDI exception, reducing the additional tax burden by $370K-$630K.
