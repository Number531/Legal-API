### C. Private Fund Investor Protection Rules‚ÄîLegal Uncertainty Post-Vacatur

#### 1. SEC's August 2023 Private Fund Adviser Rules

On August 23, 2023, the SEC adopted comprehensive "private fund adviser rules" under SEC Release No. IA-6383, imposing significant new requirements on registered investment advisers to private funds.¬≤¬≤

**Four Major Rule Categories:**

**a. Quarterly Fee Statement Rule (Proposed 17 CFR 275.211(h)(1)-2):**
- **Requirement**: Private fund advisers must prepare and distribute quarterly statements to investors containing:¬≤¬≥
  - Fund performance (gross and net returns)
  - Detailed itemization of all fees and expenses (management fees, performance fees, administrative fees, regulatory fees, portfolio company-level fees)

- **Compliance Date**: March 14, 2025
- **Pinnacle Impact**: Would require significant operational build-out; quarterly statements for Opportunity Fund ($4.8B, ~100 LPs) and Credit Opportunities Fund ($1.5B, ~40 LPs)

**b. Preferential Treatment Rule (Proposed 17 CFR 275.211(h)(2)-3):**
- **Prohibition #1**: Advisers cannot provide preferential treatment regarding redemption rights or portfolio holdings/exposure information that the adviser reasonably expects would have a material negative effect on other investors¬≤‚Å¥
- **Prohibition #2**: Any other preferential treatment (fees, liquidity, transparency, governance) must be disclosed to all other investors in the same fund
- **Compliance Date**: September 14, 2024 (large advisers $1.5B+); March 14, 2025 (small advisers)
- **Pinnacle Impact**: Side letters granting MFN provisions to 8 LPs and enhanced transparency to 5 LPs could violate preferential treatment restrictions if not disclosed to all LPs

**c. Adviser-Led Secondaries Rule (Proposed 17 CFR 275.211(h)(2)-4):**
- **Requirement**: Fairness opinion from independent opinion provider; financial statements for target assets; disclosure of all adviser conflicts
- **Compliance Date**: September 14, 2024 (large advisers)
- **Pinnacle Impact**: If Pinnacle conducts continuation fund or other adviser-led secondary, must obtain fairness opinion ($200K-$500K cost)

**d. Restricted Activities Rule (Proposed 17 CFR 275.211(h)(2)-2):**
- **Prohibitions**: (1) Charging fees/expenses for regulatory/compliance services; (2) reducing clawback obligations; (3) seeking indemnification for adviser's breach or negligence; (4) charging borrowing costs unless disclosed

#### 2. Fifth Circuit Vacatur‚ÄîJune 5, 2024

On June 5, 2024, a unanimous panel of the U.S. Court of Appeals for the Fifth Circuit issued its opinion in **National Association of Private Fund Managers v. SEC, No. 23-60471** (5th Cir. June 5, 2024), **vacating the private fund adviser rules in their entirety**.¬≤‚Åµ

**Legal Holdings:**

**a. Section 211(h) Does Not Apply to Private Fund Investors:**

The court held that Section 211(h) of the Investment Advisers Act‚Äîthe SEC's asserted statutory authority for the rules‚Äîapplies only to "retail customers," not sophisticated private fund investors.¬≤‚Å∂

Key reasoning:
- Section 211(h) was enacted as part of Title IX of Dodd-Frank (Section 913), which created a "retail customer" standard of conduct for broker-dealers
- Title IX explicitly limited its scope to "retail customers"
- Dodd-Frank made clear that "section 913 applies to 'retail customers,' not private fund investors" and "has nothing to do with private funds"¬≤‚Å∑
- Congress purposely limited SEC rulemaking authority under Title IX to retail customer relationships
- Private fund investors are sophisticated (accredited investors, qualified purchasers), not "retail customers"
- Section 913 was enacted during 2007-2008 financial crisis focused on consumer protection; Congress "clearly chose not to impose the same [rules] on private funds" as it did for registered investment companies¬≤‚Å∏

**b. Section 206(4) Antifraud Authority Insufficient:**

The court rejected the SEC's alternative reliance on Section 206(4) (antifraud provision), finding the SEC's justification "pretextual" and lacking rational connection to fraud prevention:¬≤‚Åπ
- The rules failed to define the fraud they supposedly addressed
- The SEC cited misconduct by only 0.05% of investment advisers‚Äîinsufficient basis for industry-wide rules
- A failure to provide disclosures required by the rules could not constitute fraud without an associated duty to disclose
- Fiduciary duty to disclose extends to the fund entity, not individual investors in the fund

**c. Entire Rule Vacated:**

The Fifth Circuit vacated the private fund rules **in their entirety**, holding that "no part of [the Private Fund Rules] can stand."¬≥‚Å∞ This includes:
- Quarterly fee statements
- Preferential treatment restrictions
- Adviser-led secondaries fairness opinion requirement
- Restricted activities prohibitions

**Compliance Dates Nullified**: The September 14, 2024 and March 14, 2025 compliance dates were nullified; no part of the rules went into effect.¬≥¬π
