### D. Alternative Terminal Availability - Bay Area

**Six Bay Area Seaports:**
1. **Port of Oakland** (primary container terminal)
2. Port of San Francisco
3. **Port of Richmond** (third largest CA tonnage: 19M short tons annually)
4. **Port of Benicia**
5. Port of Redwood City
6. Port of Stockton

**Port of Richmond:**
- **Cargo Profile:** Liquid bulk, automobiles, break-bulk (steel, lumber, heavy machinery)
- **Container Handling:** Limited/none (specialized in Ro-Ro and bulk)
- **Capacity Status:** Operating at or near capacity for Ro-Ro operations
- **Facilities:** 5 City-owned terminals + 10 privately-owned terminals

**Port of Benicia:**
- **Cargo Profile:** Automobiles, petrocoke
- **Container Handling:** No container terminal facilities
- **Capacity Status:** Operating near capacity for auto Ro-Ro

**CRITICAL FINDING:** No viable alternative container terminal exists in the Oakland/Bay Area region. Port of Richmond and Port of Benicia are specialized for Ro-Ro/bulk cargo and operating near capacity. Relocating PMSC's Oakland container operations would require moving to Los Angeles/Long Beach complex (330+ miles south) or Port of Seattle/Tacoma (815+ miles north), incurring substantial customer service disruption and logistics costs.
