#### 2. Facility Classification Analysis

**Container Terminals vs. CDC Facilities:**

Container terminals (Seattle, Oakland, Los Angeles, Long Beach) are **NOT** classified as "covered facilities" under Section 11804(c) unless they handle **Certain Dangerous Cargoes (CDC)** in bulk:

**CDC Definition (33 CFR 160.203):**
- Division 1.1 or 1.2 explosives
- Division 1.5D blasting agents (in bulk requiring permits)
- Division 2.3 poisonous gases (>1 metric ton per vessel)
- Division 5.1 oxidizing materials (in bulk requiring permits)
- **All bulk liquefied gas cargo that is flammable and/or toxic** (including LNG, LPG)

**33 CFR ยง 105.253(a) Risk Group A Classification:**

Facilities in Risk Group A (requiring TWIC readers) include:

**(1)** Facilities receiving vessels certificated to carry **>1,000 passengers** (effective June 8, 2020 - already in effect)

**(2)-(4)** Beginning **May 8, 2029**:
- Facilities that handle CDC in bulk and transfer such cargoes from or to a vessel
- Facilities that handle CDC in bulk but do not transfer it from or to a vessel
- Facilities that receive vessels carrying CDC in bulk but do not transfer it during vessel-to-facility interface

**Container Terminal Classification:**

Standard container terminals handling dry cargo containers and non-bulk liquid containers are **NOT** classified as Risk Group A facilities for TWIC reader purposes unless they:
- Receive cruise ships/ferries with >1,000 passenger capacity (unlikely for cargo terminals), OR
- Handle CDC in bulk (liquefied gas terminals, not typical container operations)

#### 3. Facility Security Officer (FSO) Designation

**33 CFR ยง 105.205 Requirements:**

Facility owners or operators must **designate, in writing, by name or by title**, a Facility Security Officer (FSO) and identify how the officer can be contacted at any time.

**FSO Qualifications (33 CFR ยง 105.205(b)):**

The FSO must have general knowledge, through training or equivalent job experience, in:
- Vessel and facility security measures
- MARSEC Levels and implementation
- Emergency preparedness, contingency planning, and response
- Security equipment and systems
- Methods of conducting audits, inspections, control, and monitoring techniques
- Relevant international laws and codes (ISPS Code for vessel/facility interface)
- Recognizing behavioral patterns of persons who may threaten security
- Handling sensitive security information (SSI under 49 CFR Part 1520)

**Certification Status:**

Under current regulations, FSOs are **NOT required** to undergo formal training and certification. However, Coast Guard Authorization Act of 2010, Section 821, requires the Secretary of Homeland Security to establish comprehensive FSO training requirements designed to provide full security training leading to certification.
