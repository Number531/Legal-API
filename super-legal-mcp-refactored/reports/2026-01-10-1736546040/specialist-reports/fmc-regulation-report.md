## IV. DETAILED ANALYSIS

### A. Ocean Common Carrier Licensing Requirements

#### 1. Statutory Framework

**Definition of Ocean Common Carrier**

Under 46 U.S.C. ¬ß 40102(6), an "ocean common carrier" means a person that:
(i) holds itself out to the general public to provide transportation by water of passengers or cargo between the United States and a foreign country for compensation;
(ii) assumes responsibility for the transportation from the port or point of receipt to the port or point of destination; and
(iii) uses, for all or part of that transportation, a vessel operating on the high seas or the Great Lakes between a port in the United States and a port in a foreign country.¬π

**Vessel-Operating Common Carriers (VOCCs) Distinguished from NVOCCs**

PMSC operates as a VOCC, which has different licensing requirements than Non-Vessel-Operating Common Carriers (NVOCCs). VOCCs are not subject to the licensing and financial responsibility bonding requirements that apply to Ocean Transportation Intermediaries under 46 CFR Part 515.¬≤

#### 3. Tariff Publication Requirements

**Statutory Obligation**

All VOCCs must publish a tariff under 46 CFR ¬ß 520.3. Tariffs must be:
- Open for public inspection
- Show all rates, charges, classifications, rules, and practices between all points or ports on their service routes‚Å∂
- Maintained in an electronic format accessible to the public
- Updated to reflect current rates and terms

**Enforcement Implications**

Failure to maintain a current, published tariff violates 46 U.S.C. ¬ß 41104(a) and subjects the carrier to civil penalties under 46 U.S.C. ¬ß 41107. As of January 15, 2024, maximum penalties are:
- **$73,045** for knowing and willful violations
- **$14,608** for violations that are not knowing and willful‚Å∑

#### 4. Financial Responsibility for VOCCs

**No Bonding Requirement for VOCCs**

Unlike NVOCCs (which must maintain $75,000 surety bonds) and Ocean Freight Forwarders (which must maintain $50,000 bonds), vessel-operating common carriers are **not subject to financial responsibility bonding requirements** under 46 CFR Part 515.‚Å∏

The financial responsibility provisions in 46 CFR Part 515 apply only to Ocean Transportation Intermediaries (OTIs), not to vessel operators. PMSC's acquisition by Global Logistics Partners does not trigger any FMC bonding requirements.

#### 5. License Transfer vs. Business Change Notification

**Key Distinction**

FMC regulations distinguish between:
- **License transfers** (applicable to OTI licenses under 46 CFR ¬ß 515.20, requiring Form FMC-18 and $125 filing fee)
- **Business information updates** (applicable to VOCCs under 46 CFR ¬ß 520.3, requiring updated Form FMC-1)

PMSC's change of ownership constitutes a business information update rather than a license transfer. The FMC does not issue "licenses" to VOCCs in the same manner as it licenses NVOCCs and Ocean Freight Forwarders. Instead, VOCCs operate under the general regulatory framework of the Shipping Act of 1984, as amended.‚Åπ
