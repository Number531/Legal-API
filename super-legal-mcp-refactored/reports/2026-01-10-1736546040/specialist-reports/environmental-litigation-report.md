#### 3. Penalty Calculation Methodology - EPA Policy

EPA calculates actual penalties using the **Clean Air Act Stationary Source Civil Penalty Policy** (1991, as amended), considering:

**Gravity-Based Penalty Factors:**
1. **Economic benefit of noncompliance** (avoided compliance costs, delayed expenditures)
2. **Seriousness of violation:**
   - Extent of deviation from regulatory requirement
   - Health/environmental harm potential
   - Duration of violation
3. **Size of business** (ability to pay)
4. **Compliance history** (prior violations)
5. **Good faith efforts to comply**
6. **Other unique factors**

**Penalty Adjustment Factors:**
- **Litigation risk** (strength of EPA's case)
- **Pollutant-specific multipliers** (hazardous air pollutants receive higher penalties)
- **Ability to pay** (may reduce penalty for demonstrated financial hardship)

**Multi-Day Violations:**
Each day of continuing violation constitutes a separate violation for penalty calculation purposes. For ongoing operational violations (e.g., diesel truck emissions exceeding permitted levels), penalties accrue **daily** until compliance achieved.
