## IV. DETAILED ANALYSIS

### A. FINRA Regulatory Framework for Variable Annuity Suitability

#### 1. FINRA Rule 2111 - General Suitability Obligations

FINRA Rule 2111 requires that broker-dealers and associated persons "have a reasonable basis to believe that a recommended transaction or investment strategy involving a security or securities is suitable for the customer, based on the information obtained through the reasonable diligence of the [firm] or associated person to ascertain the customer's investment profile."¬π A customer's investment profile includes critical factors: age, other investments, financial situation and needs, tax status, investment objectives, investment experience, investment time horizon, liquidity needs, and risk tolerance.¬≤

Rule 2111 imposes three distinct suitability obligations:¬≥

**a. Reasonable-Basis Suitability:** The broker-dealer must have a reasonable basis to believe that the recommendation is suitable for at least some investors based on adequate due diligence to understand the product's features, risks, and costs.

**b. Customer-Specific Suitability:** The broker-dealer must have a reasonable basis to believe that the recommendation is suitable for the particular customer based on that customer's specific investment profile.

**c. Quantitative Suitability:** The broker-dealer must have a reasonable basis to believe that a series of recommended transactions, even if suitable when viewed in isolation, is not excessive and unsuitable for the customer when taken together in light of the customer's investment profile.

#### 2. FINRA Rule 2330 - Specific Requirements for Deferred Variable Annuities

FINRA Rule 2330 (Members' Responsibilities Regarding Deferred Variable Annuities) establishes heightened sales practice standards for variable annuities.‚Å¥ Prior to recommending the purchase or exchange of a deferred variable annuity, a registered representative must make reasonable efforts to determine the customer's:‚Åµ

- Age
- Annual income
- Investment experience

#### 4. Unsuitable Variable Annuity Sales to Seniors - Regulatory Concerns

FINRA has identified variable annuity sales to seniors as a high-risk area for suitability violations due to:¬π¬≥
