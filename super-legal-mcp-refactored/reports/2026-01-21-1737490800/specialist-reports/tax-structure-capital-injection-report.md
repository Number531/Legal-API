#### Alternative 1: Surplus Notes ($150M Face Value, 6.5% Interest, 30-Year Maturity)

**Regulatory Treatment - 100% TAC Credit [VERIFIED]**

Under NAIC Statutory Accounting Principles (SAP), surplus notes are classified as equity despite being debt instruments for federal income tax purposes. SSAP No. 41R governs the accounting treatment of surplus notes, which provides: "Issued surplus notes are treated as issued surplus, equity/capital" and are "subordinated to all policyholder claims, claimant and beneficiary claims, and to all other classes of creditors." [VERIFIED: NAIC Statutory Accounting Principles Working Group, SSAP No. 41R (rev. Feb. 25, 2025), https://content.naic.org/sites/default/files/inline-files/24-28%20-%20SSAP%20No.%2041%20-%20Capital%20Notes_0.pdf]

Surplus notes receive 100% Total Adjusted Capital credit for RBC purposes because they are "at the bottom level of insurers' capital structure" and "subordinate to policyholders, claimant and beneficiary claims, and to all other classes of creditors." [VERIFIED: NAIC Insurance Topics - Surplus Notes, https://content.naic.org/insurance-topics/surplus-notes]

**Tax Treatment - Interest Deductible [VERIFIED]**

For federal income tax purposes, surplus notes are treated as debt instruments despite regulatory classification as equity. In *Amerco v. Commissioner*, 96 T.C. 18 (1991), the Tax Court held: "It is well settled that surplus notes are to be treated as debt for tax purposes." The District conceded that "the Surplus Notes are to be treated as debt for purposes of computing deductions and does not argue that the Surplus Notes should be treated as equity for all tax purposes." [VERIFIED: Tax Notes Research, IRS Private Letter Rulings, https://www.taxnotes.com/research/federal/irs-private-rulings/letter-rulings-technical-advice/insurer-properly-excluded-surplus-notes-from-equity-base/1hyy1]

The dual treatment creates tax efficiency: regulatory equity treatment for capital adequacy + tax debt treatment for interest deductibility. Interest expense on surplus notes is deductible under IRC Â§ 163(a), which provides: "There shall be allowed as a deduction all interest paid or accrued within the taxable year on indebtedness." [VERIFIED: 26 U.S.C. Â§ 163(a), https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/163]
