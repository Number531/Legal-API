#### 2. SEC April 2022 Prospectus Delivery Deficiency â€“ 12 Policyholders Affected

The SEC Office of Compliance Inspections and Examinations (OCIE) conducted an inspection of Liberty Life Securities LLC in April 2022 and identified a **prospectus delivery violation** affecting 12 policyholders who purchased variable universal life insurance policies from Separate Account A. [HYPOTHETICAL SCENARIO - specific inspection report requires verification]

**Nature of Violation**: The mailing fulfillment vendor used by Liberty Life Securities to distribute prospectuses sent the required prospectuses approximately **2 weeks after** policy applications were submitted, rather than delivering prospectuses **before** the application or within the required timeframe under Section 5(b)(2) of the Securities Act. This constitutes a violation of the fundamental requirement that securities cannot be carried through the mails or interstate commerce for sale or delivery after sale "unless accompanied or preceded by a prospectus."

**Root Cause**: The violation stemmed from inadequate vendor oversight and lack of automated controls to track prospectus delivery timing. Liberty Life Securities relied on a third-party fulfillment vendor without implementing sufficient monitoring to ensure compliance with Securities Act requirements. This demonstrates a supervision weakness under FINRA Rule 3110(a), which requires broker-dealers to establish and maintain a supervisory system reasonably designed to achieve compliance with applicable securities laws and regulations.

**Remediation Implemented** (May 2022):
- **Fulfillment Vendor Change**: Terminated the non-compliant vendor and engaged a new fulfillment provider with demonstrated securities compliance expertise
- **Automated Tracking System**: Implemented technology that generates date-stamped proof of mailing for each variable product application, creating an audit trail
- **Monthly Prospectus Delivery Audits**: Established compliance department review of a statistical sample of applications to verify prospectus delivery within required timeframe
- **Exception Reporting**: Created system alerts that flag any application where prospectus delivery exceeds 5 business days from application date, triggering immediate investigation

**SEC Resolution**: The SEC OCIE inspection finding was resolved through remediation with no monetary penalty imposed. The matter is administratively closed with no continuing oversight or follow-up required. [PENDING VERIFICATION - SEC correspondence or data room documents required to confirm no open matters]

**Significance**: While the violation affected only 12 policyholders and resulted in no monetary penalty, the prospectus delivery deficiency demonstrates supervision weaknesses that appear across multiple regulatory examinations. The joint SEC/NASD report on variable insurance products (2004) historically emphasized prospectus delivery as a fundamental compliance requirement, noting that a broker-dealer is required to deliver a prospectus on or before the transaction settlement date.

#### 3. FINRA October 2023 Examination â€“ 4 Violations, $75,000 Fine

FINRA conducted a cycle examination of Liberty Life Securities LLC in October 2023 and identified **4 violations**: 3 suitability violations under Rule 2111 and 1 supervision deficiency under Rule 3110. [HYPOTHETICAL SCENARIO - specific FINRA examination report requires verification]

**A. Suitability Violations (3 Cases) â€“ FINRA Rule 2111**

FINRA examiners determined that registered representatives recommended **variable universal life insurance policies to customers age 75 or older** with limited fixed income ($35,000-$45,000 annually from Social Security and pension sources), violating the customer-specific suitability requirement of FINRA Rule 2111.

**Specific Deficiencies Identified**:

1. **Age and Time Horizon Mismatch**: VUL policies are long-term products with breakeven periods typically 10-15 years. At age 75+, policyholders have limited life expectancy, substantially reducing the probability of realizing cash value accumulation benefits. FINRA Regulatory Notice 07-43 emphasizes that certain products or strategies pose risks that may be unsuitable for many seniors because of time horizon considerations.

2. **Cost Structure Unsuitability**: The VUL policies carried high costs that compound the suitability concerns:
   - Mortality and expense (M&E) charges: 0.90% of account value annually
   - Cost of insurance (COI) charges: Age-based and increasing exponentially at advanced ages (age 75+ COI charges can consume significant premium)
   - Administrative fees: $50-$75 annually plus percentage-based charges
   - Surrender charges: 8-year declining schedule starting at 8%, creating liquidity constraints

3. **Limited Income Insufficient to Sustain Premiums**: Customers with annual income of $35,000-$45,000 lacked sufficient disposable income to sustain the premium payments required for VUL policies over the long term. VUL requires ongoing premium payments to maintain coverage and avoid policy lapse. Given fixed income sources with limited growth potential (Social Security COLA adjustments typically 2-3% annually), the risk of premium payment cessation and policy lapse was substantial.

4. **Liquidity Concerns**: Senior customers age 75+ typically require liquidity for medical expenses, assisted living costs, or emergency needs. VUL policies with 8-year surrender charges and limited cash value accumulation in early years (due to high front-loaded costs) provide poor liquidity, making them unsuitable for customers who may need access to funds.

**Regulatory Context**: FINRA has identified variable products as being "at the sweet spot of complex products marketed to retirees and people about to retire," and enforcement officials have emphasized that variable annuities and variable life "are just very frequently involved in our cases." The 2025 FINRA Annual Regulatory Oversight Report highlights unsuitable variable annuity exchanges and inadequate supervision as common examination findings.

**B. Supervision Deficiency (1 Case) â€“ FINRA Rule 3110**

FINRA examiners identified a supervision deficiency in which a **branch manager failed to obtain timely principal review** for 8 variable product applications before policy issuance.

**Specific Deficiency**: The branch manager, who was not registered as a principal (lacking Series 24 or Series 26 qualifications), allowed 8 variable product applications to be submitted electronically to the home office for underwriting and policy issuance without first obtaining review and approval from a registered principal as required by FINRA Rule 3110 (supervision) and Rule 2330 (variable annuity principal review).

**Supervision Framework Violated**: FINRA Rule 3110(b)(2) requires supervisory procedures for the review by a registered principal, evidenced in writing, of all transactions relating to the firm's securities business. FINRA Rule 2330 specifically requires that "a registered principal shall review and determine whether he or she approves of the recommended purchase or exchange of the deferred variable annuity" before execution.

**Risk Created**: This supervision failure created the risk that unsuitable variable product sales could proceed through to policy issuance without the critical oversight function that principal review is designed to provide. Principal review serves as a control to catch unsuitable recommendations before customers are bound by contracts.

**C. FINRA Sanctions and AWC Settlement**

Liberty Life Securities LLC entered into an **Acceptance, Waiver and Consent (AWC)** settlement with FINRA, without admitting or denying the findings, agreeing to:

- **Fine**: $75,000
- **Individual Agent Sanctions**: 3 agents involved in the suitability violations suspended for 30 days
- **Retraining Requirement**: All 3 suspended agents required to complete FINRA-approved suitability training before resuming variable product sales
- **Effective Date**: Sanctions became effective December 2023

**AWC Process**: The AWC process is governed by FINRA Rule 9216. When FINRA's Department of Enforcement has reason to believe a violation has occurred and the member does not dispute the violation, FINRA may prepare a letter accepting a finding of violation, consenting to the imposition of sanctions, and agreeing to waive the right to a hearing. The AWC describes the act or practice, the rule violated, and the sanctions imposed.

**D. Enhanced Remediation Procedures Implemented** (November 2023)

Following the examination findings, Liberty Life Securities implemented enhanced procedures:

**Senior Customer Protection (Age 65+)**:
1. **Mandatory Home Office Review**: All variable product applications for customers age 65+ must receive review by a designated home office principal (Series 24 qualified) in addition to branch-level principal review, creating dual-layer oversight
2. **Expanded Disclosure Forms**: Created supplemental plain-language disclosure documents for senior customers explaining: long-term nature of variable products, surrender charge schedules, increasing cost of insurance with age, alternative products that may be more suitable (term life, immediate annuities)
3. **Income and Net Worth Verification**: For customers age 65+ with annual income below $75,000, representatives must obtain documentation verifying income (tax returns, Social Security statements) and liquid net worth to substantiate suitability
4. **System Controls**: Implemented technology controls that prevent submission of variable product applications for customers age 65+ until principal e-signature approval is obtained, eliminating the bypass risk identified in the supervision deficiency

**Branch Manager Training**: All branch managers received training on principal registration requirements (Series 24/26), delegation of supervisory authority procedures, FINRA Rule 3110 supervision obligations, and documentation requirements for principal review.
