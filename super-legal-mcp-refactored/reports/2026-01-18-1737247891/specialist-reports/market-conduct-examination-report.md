##### Interstate Cooperation Principles

The NAIC recognizes that **interstate cooperation is essential** for effective state regulation. Although each jurisdiction retains responsibility for examining company practices within its jurisdiction, cooperation avoids duplication of effort and maximizes use of resources. [VERIFIED: GAO Report GAO-09-372, [Insurance Reciprocity and Uniformity](https://www.gao.gov/assets/a288236.html)]

**Challenge Identified:** With each state conducting its own examinations, some insurance companies face **simultaneous examinations by several states**, while others may not be examined at all. The NAIC coordination mechanisms attempt to address this inefficiency.

##### Lead State Examination Model

In multi-state coordinated examinations (such as the LLIC examination):
1. **Lead State Designation**: One state (Nebraska for LLIC) serves as lead examiner
2. **Participating States**: Other states (12 additional states for LLIC) participate through the lead state
3. **Unified Examination**: Single examination team reviews practices across all participating states
4. **Separate Findings**: Each state retains authority to make independent findings and levy penalties
5. **Coordination of Remediation**: States coordinate on corrective action requirements

#### 1. Policy Illustration Violations (5 Identified Violations)

##### NAIC Life Insurance Illustration Model Regulation (Model #582)

The [NAIC Life Insurance Illustration Model Regulation](https://content.naic.org/sites/default/files/model-law-582.pdf) provides rules for life insurance policy illustrations that **protect consumers and foster consumer education**, providing illustration formats, prescribing standards, and specifying required disclosures. [VERIFIED: NAIC Model Law #582]

**Key Requirements:**
- **Guaranteed vs. Non-Guaranteed Elements**: Clear distinction between guaranteed policy elements (fixed) and non-guaranteed elements (variable based on company experience)
- **Illustration Certification**: Agent and insurer must certify that illustration complies with regulation
- **Disciplined Current Scale**: Illustrated rates must reflect current company practices, not overly optimistic projections
- **Consumer Notice**: Conspicuous statement that illustration is not a contract and values are not guaranteed

##### Actuarial Guideline 49 (AG49) â€” Indexed Universal Life Illustrated Rate Caps

**AG49 Background:**
Adopted by NAIC in June 2015 as "interim guidance" to address varying practices in application of Model 582 to indexed universal life (IUL) products. [VERIFIED: [SOA AG49 Analysis](https://www.soa.org/sections/product-dev/product-dev-newsletter/2023/june/pm-2023-06-hoffer/)]

**AG49 Evolution:**
- **AG49 (2015)**: Original illustrated rate caps based on Benchmark Index Account (S&P 500, 0% floor, 100% participation)
