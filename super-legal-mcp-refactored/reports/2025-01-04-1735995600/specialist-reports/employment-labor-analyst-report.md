#### 5. Colorado Wage Payment Law: Immediate Payment Required, $634K-$2.4M Penalty Risk

**Finding**: Colorado C.R.S. ¬ß 8-4-109 imposes **strict final paycheck timing requirements**:
- **If employer terminates employee**: Final wages + **accrued vacation** due **immediately** upon termination
- **If employee quits**: Final wages due on next regular payday

**"Earned and Determinable" Vacation**: All accrued but unused vacation must be paid at termination. However, employment agreements may **forfeit vacation if employee fails to give 2-week notice** or is terminated for cause (lawful under Colorado Wage Claim Act).

**Penalties for Non-Payment** (C.R.S. ¬ß 8-4-109(3)):
- Employee sends **written demand** to employer
- Employer has **14 days to pay** after receiving notice
- If employer fails:
  - **Negligent non-payment**: 2x unpaid wages or $1,000 (whichever greater)
  - **Willful non-payment**: 3x unpaid wages or $3,000 (whichever greater)

**Application to MSWC Workforce Reductions**:
- **Average accrued vacation**: 2-3 weeks (10-15 days) per employee
- **Average wage**: $75,000/year √∑ 52 weeks = $1,442/week √ó 2-3 weeks = **$2,885-$4,327 per employee**

### B. Successor Employer Obligations Under NLRA ¬ß 8(a)(5)

**[CONTINGENT ANALYSIS - Applies only if MSWC is unionized]**

If MSWC's workforce is unionized, **AWI becomes a "successor employer" under the National Labor Relations Act** and inherits specific collective bargaining obligations.

#### 1. NLRA ¬ß 8(a)(5) Statutory Framework

NLRA ¬ß 8(a)(5) makes it an unfair labor practice for an employer "**to refuse to bargain collectively with the representatives of its employees**" subject to Section 9(a).¬π¬π When an acquiring company purchases a business with a unionized workforce, **successor employer doctrine** determines the extent to which the successor inherits the predecessor's labor obligations.

#### 2. Successor Employer Rights and Obligations

**Initial Terms Exception ("Burns Doctrine"):**

A successor employer may **initially set terms and conditions of employment without bargaining** with the incumbent union, provided it is not a "perfectly clear" Burns successor.¬π¬≤ However, this right ends once the successor has hired a **"substantial and representative complement of employees,"** a majority of whom are drawn from the predecessor's workforce.¬π¬≥

**"Perfectly Clear" Exception:**

A successor **cannot set initial terms** without bargaining if it makes "perfectly clear" that it plans to retain all (or substantially all) of the predecessor's employees **without informing them they will work under different terms**.¬π‚Å¥ If AWI publicly announces retention of MSWC's entire 1,200-employee workforce at existing compensation levels, it may trigger immediate bargaining obligations.

**Duty to Bargain Arises:**

The NLRB held that a successor employer violated ¬ß 8(a)(5) by **refusing to bargain** when it made a "conscious decision" to purchase and maintain the predecessor's business in **substantially unchanged form** and hire a majority of the predecessor's employees.¬π‚Åµ

#### 3. Application to MSWC-AWI Transaction

**Factors Supporting Successor Employer Status:**
- **Stock purchase transaction**: AWI acquires MSWC stock (Delaware corporation), inheriting all assets, liabilities, contracts, and employees
- **Business continuity**: MSWC continues operating 28 public water systems, serving 485,000 connections‚Äîbusiness operations remain **substantially unchanged**
- **Workforce retention**: Regulated utility requiring **certified water treatment operators** (Colorado CDPHE certification), specialized maintenance staff, engineers for $1.4B-$1.95B lead service line program‚Äî**substantial workforce continuity inevitable**
- **CPUC local management requirement**: Research plan notes CEO/COO/CFO/Chief Engineer retention required for 5 years post-closing for CPUC approval (see Section IV.C below)

**Bargaining Obligation Likely Immediate:**

Given the above factors, AWI will likely be deemed a **"perfectly clear" successor** and must:
1. **Honor existing CBA terms** until expiration (cannot unilaterally change wages, benefits, working conditions)
2. **Bargain in good faith** with incumbent union over CBA renewal/modifications
3. **Refrain from unilateral changes** to terms and conditions of employment covered by the CBA

**90-Day Aggregation Rule for WARN Act:**

If AWI plans workforce reductions post-closing, it must comply with WARN Act aggregation rules (see Section IV.E below). Successor employer doctrine **does not eliminate WARN Act obligations**‚Äîboth MSWC (pre-closing) and AWI (post-closing) may trigger notice requirements depending on timing.

### D. WARN Act Applicability and Federal Requirements

#### 1. Federal WARN Act Statutory Framework

The **Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification Act** (29 U.S.C. ¬ß 2101 et seq.) requires covered employers to provide **60 days' advance written notice** before:
- **Plant closing**: Permanent or temporary shutdown of a single site affecting **50+ employees**, OR
- **Mass layoff**: Employment loss at a single site for (1) **500+ employees**, OR (2) **50-499 employees if ‚â•33% of active workforce**¬≤‚Å∑

**Covered Employers**: Private businesses with **100+ full-time employees** (excluding those with <6 months tenure or <20 hours/week average).¬≤‚Å∏

**Notice Recipients**:
1. Affected employees (or their union representatives)
2. State Dislocated Worker Unit (Colorado Department of Labor and Employment)
3. Chief elected official of local government where facility is located¬≤‚Åπ

#### 2. MSWC-AWI Transaction WARN Analysis

**MSWC Workforce: 1,200 employees** ‚Üí **Covered employer** under WARN Act

**Stock Purchase Transaction**: Acquisition itself is **not a WARN triggering event**.¬≥‚Å∞ However, **post-closing workforce reductions** could trigger notice requirements.

**33% Threshold Calculation:**
- Total workforce: 1,200 employees
- **33% = 396 employees**
- Mass layoff triggered if AWI terminates:
  - **500+ employees** (regardless of percentage), OR
  - **50-396 employees** representing ‚â•33% of workforce

**50-Employee Minimum**: Even if <33%, termination of **50+ employees at single site within 30-day period** may trigger WARN if combined with other factors.¬≥¬π

**90-Day Aggregation Rule (Critical):**

WARN Act requires **aggregating layoffs within 90 days** unless employer demonstrates "**separate and distinct actions and causes**."¬≥¬≤ If AWI plans:
