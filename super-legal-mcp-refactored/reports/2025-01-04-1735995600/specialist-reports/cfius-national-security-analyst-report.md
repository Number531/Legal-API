## I. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The proposed $2.4 billion acquisition of Mountain States Water Company (MSWC) by American Water Infrastructure LLC (AWI) presents moderate national security implications requiring proactive Committee on Foreign Investment in the United States (CFIUS) engagement. While the transaction likely does not trigger mandatory filing requirements under current regulations, **voluntary filing is strongly recommended** to obtain safe harbor protection and support Colorado Public Utilities Commission (CPUC) approval.

### Key Findings

**1. CFIUS Jurisdiction Confirmed ‚Äî Covered Control Transaction**

This transaction falls squarely within CFIUS jurisdiction as a **covered control transaction** under 31 C.F.R. ¬ß 800.211. AWI, with 45% foreign ownership (Canada Pension Plan Investment Board 30% + Macquarie Infrastructure Partners 15%), constitutes a "foreign person" under 31 C.F.R. ¬ß 800.224 acquiring control of MSWC, a U.S. business. MSWC qualifies as a **TID U.S. business** (critical infrastructure) under 31 C.F.R. ¬ß 800.248 because it operates public water systems serving more than 100,000 persons in multiple Metropolitan Statistical Areas (Denver-Aurora-Lakewood, Colorado Springs, Fort Collins, Boulder). Water utilities are specifically designated as "covered investment critical infrastructure" in Appendix A to 31 C.F.R. Part 800, triggering CFIUS's expanded jurisdiction under FIRRMA (Foreign Investment Risk Review Modernization Act of 2018).

### A. CFIUS Jurisdiction and Authority

#### 1. Statutory Framework

The Committee on Foreign Investment in the United States (CFIUS) operates under the authority of Section 721 of the Defense Production Act of 1950, as amended, codified at 50 U.S.C. ¬ß 4565.¬π CFIUS is a Treasury-led interagency committee that reviews certain foreign investments in U.S. businesses and certain real estate transactions for national security concerns.¬≤

The Foreign Investment Risk Review Modernization Act of 2018 (FIRRMA), enacted August 13, 2018, significantly expanded CFIUS's jurisdiction and introduced mandatory filing requirements for certain transactions.¬≥ FIRRMA's implementing regulations are codified at 31 C.F.R. Parts 800-802, with the final rules becoming effective February 13, 2020.‚Å¥

#### 2. Covered Transactions Under CFIUS Regulations

CFIUS has jurisdiction over "covered transactions," which include:

**a. Covered Control Transactions (31 C.F.R. ¬ß 800.211):**
Any transaction that could result in foreign control of a U.S. business.‚Åµ

**b. Covered Investments (31 C.F.R. ¬ß 800.211):**
FIRRMA expanded CFIUS jurisdiction to include certain non-controlling investments by foreign persons in "TID U.S. businesses" (businesses involved in critical technology, critical infrastructure, or sensitive personal data).‚Å∂

For the AWI acquisition of MSWC, this transaction constitutes a **covered control transaction** because AWI (a foreign person due to 45% foreign ownership) is acquiring control of MSWC (a U.S. business).

### B. MSWC as a TID U.S. Business ‚Äî Critical Infrastructure

#### 1. Definition of TID U.S. Business

Under 31 C.F.R. ¬ß 800.248, a "TID U.S. business" is any U.S. business that:

(a) Produces, designs, tests, manufactures, fabricates, or develops **critical technologies**;

(b) Performs the functions as set forth in column 2 of appendix A to Part 800 with respect to **covered investment critical infrastructure**; or

(c) Maintains or collects **sensitive personal data** of U.S. citizens.‚Å∑

#### 2. Water Utilities as Covered Investment Critical Infrastructure

### E. Voluntary Filing Strategy

#### 1. Voluntary Notice vs. Declaration

Even though the AWI-MSWC transaction likely does not trigger mandatory filing requirements (unless MSWC serves military installations), parties may **voluntarily file** with CFIUS to obtain national security clearance and safe harbor protection.¬≤‚Å∑

**Two Filing Options:**

**a. Short-Form Declaration (31 C.F.R. ¬ß 800.403):**
- **Timeline:** 30-day assessment period
- **No filing fee**
- **Possible Outcomes:**
  - CFIUS clears transaction (safe harbor)
  - CFIUS requests full notice
  - CFIUS cannot complete action (parties may voluntarily file notice)
- **Advantage:** Fast, no cost
- **Disadvantage:** Non-binding if CFIUS cannot complete action¬≤‚Å∏

**b. Full Written Notice (31 C.F.R. ¬ß 800.501):**
- **Timeline:** 45-day initial review + potential 45-day investigation + 15-day presidential decision = **maximum 105 days**
- **Filing Fee:** Tiered based on transaction value, maximum $300,000 (for transactions over $750 million)¬≤‚Åπ
  - For $2.4B transaction: **$300,000 filing fee** (capped at 0.15% of transaction value)
- **Outcome:** Binding safe harbor ‚Äî once CFIUS clears, no further review possible
- **Advantage:** Certainty, definitive clearance
- **Disadvantage:** Longer timeline, filing fee, legal costs ($500K-$2M)¬≥‚Å∞

**[VERIFIED: Filing fee structure, accessed January 4, 2026 via https://home.treasury.gov/policy-issues/international/the-committee-on-foreign-investment-in-the-united-states-cfius/cfius-filing-fees]**
