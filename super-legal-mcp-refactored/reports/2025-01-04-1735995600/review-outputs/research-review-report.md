### 3.2 Adverse Authority Analysis

**Definition:** Adverse authority = legal precedents, regulations, or factual circumstances unfavorable to acquirer's position that a competent opposing counsel would cite.

**Exemplary Adverse Authority Acknowledgment:**

**T1 (Securities) - Prop 218 Adverse Precedent:**
> "Historical Prop 218 approvals in California municipal utility transactions show 35% failure rate where foreign ownership exceeds 20%. *See* City of Claremont v. Golden State Water Co., where voters rejected acquisition by American States Utility Services (43% foreign ownership) despite CPUC approval. While distinguishable on rate increase magnitude (Claremont: 28% vs. MSWC projected: 12-15%), this precedent demonstrates voter sensitivity to foreign ownership that could undermine our 65-70% approval probability estimate."

**T8 (Insurance) - Pollution Exclusion Doctrine:**
> "California appellate courts have consistently interpreted pollution exclusions broadly to preclude coverage for gradual environmental contamination, even where insured lacked knowledge of discharge. *MacKinnon v. Truck Ins. Exch.*, 73 Cal. App. 4th 1205, 1214 (1999) ('[P]ollution exclusion applies to all pollution-related claims regardless of whether discharge was sudden or gradual'). This precedent directly undermines any argument that MSWC's lead service lines or PFAS contamination would trigger coverage under standard CGL policies."

**Assessment:** Both examples demonstrate attorney acknowledging controlling adverse precedent that weakens client's position, then providing factual distinction or analysis of impact. This is the hallmark of objective legal analysis.
