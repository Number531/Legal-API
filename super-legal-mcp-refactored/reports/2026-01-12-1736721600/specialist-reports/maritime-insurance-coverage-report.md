## I. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### Overview

This report analyzes the adequacy of Pacific Maritime Services Corporation's (PMSC) maritime insurance programs in connection with the proposed $4.8B acquisition by Global Logistics Partners LLC. PMSC maintains a comprehensive insurance portfolio including Protection & Indemnity (P&I) club coverage through the American Club ($1B per vessel), Hull & Machinery insurance with Lloyd's of London, LHWCA workers' compensation (ACE American Insurance, $500M aggregate), and business interruption coverage ($150M aggregate). While this program provides adequate protection for routine maritime operations, this analysis identifies **$78M-$105M in probability-weighted uninsured and under-insured exposures** arising from critical coverage gaps in Section 905(b) vessel negligence claims, labor disruption business interruption, environmental regulatory penalties, and port security violations.

**Key Finding:** PMSC's insurance program receives a **C+ rating** ‚Äî adequate for standard maritime operations but deficient for known high-severity risks identified in this transaction. Three critical coverage gaps require immediate pre-closing action: (1) Section 905(b) MEL coverage gap ($2M-$5M exposure), (2) ILWU strike business interruption shortfall ($175M-$225M exposure), and (3) environmental penalty exclusions ($2M-$8M exposure). Enhanced insurance coverage costing $700K-$1.03M annually would reduce uninsured exposure by $63M-$80M, delivering an 85:1 to 98:1 benefit-cost ratio.

### Critical Insurance Coverage Issues

#### 1. Section 905(b) Vessel Negligence - Martinez Claim ($2M-$5M Uninsured Risk)

**Issue:** American Club P&I is providing defense for the Martinez v. PMSC Section 905(b) vessel negligence claim **under reservation of rights**, citing potential "willful misconduct" exclusion if PMSC knew of the hatch cover defect. If American Club ultimately denies indemnity coverage, the acquirer faces $2M-$5M uninsured exposure for the Q3 2025 trial verdict (post-closing event).

**Facts:** Longshoreman Martinez was crushed by a defective hatch cover on M/V Pacific Titan in September 2023, claiming $8.5M in damages ($1.2M medical + $2.8M lost wages + $4.5M pain/suffering). PMSC's LHWCA carrier (ACE American Insurance) correctly denied coverage, as Section 905(b) creates a third-party tort claim outside the LHWCA workers' compensation system (33 U.S.C. ¬ß 905(b)). This shifts liability to P&I club coverage or Maritime Employers Liability (MEL) insurance.

**Coverage Analysis:**
- **P&I Club Position:** American Club's reservation of rights preserves the option to deny coverage based on "willful misconduct" if investigation reveals PMSC had constructive knowledge of vessel maintenance deficiencies. Evidence supporting potential willful misconduct exclusion includes: (1) M/V Pacific Titan's extensive corrosion history ($18.7M November 2024 drydock repair, 15 shell plates corroded), (2) Lloyd's 22% premium increase citing "poor maintenance history," and (3) pattern of deferred maintenance suggesting PMSC "should have known" of equipment risks.
- **PMSC's Defense:** Regular hatch cover inspections (August 2023 inspection passed), no actual knowledge of specific defect, industry-standard maintenance protocols followed, immediate corrective action post-incident.
- **Willful Misconduct Standard:** Insurers bear burden of proof to establish exclusion applies. Standard requires showing owner/manager "knew or should have known" of dangerous condition and failed to correct it ‚Äî a higher threshold than mere negligence but lower than intentional acts.

**MEL Coverage Gap:** The research brief does not confirm whether PMSC maintains a separate Maritime Employers Liability (MEL) policy specifically designed to cover Section 905(b) claims. MEL insurance provides broader employer liability coverage under Admiralty Law (Jones Act, Maintenance & Cure, Unseaworthiness, and Section 905(b) third-party tort claims) compared to standard P&I club coverage. If PMSC lacks MEL coverage, total Section 905(b) exposure falls to American Club with reservation of rights risk.

**Probability Assessment:** 40-60% risk that American Club denies indemnity coverage based on willful misconduct exclusion, resulting in $1.2M-$3M probability-weighted exposure.

**Recommendation:** **CRITICAL PRE-CLOSING ACTION** ‚Äî (1) Request American Club to waive reservation of rights and provide firm coverage commitment for Martinez claim, OR (2) bind standalone MEL policy ($25M/$50M limits, $120K-$180K annual premium) as closing condition. If neither achieved, require seller indemnification with $5M escrow holdback for 18 months (through trial outcome).

#### 2. ILWU Strike Business Interruption - $175M-$225M Coverage Shortfall

**Issue:** PMSC's $150M business interruption policy contains a 90-day waiting period and $50M Strikes, Riots and Civil Commotion (SRCC) sublimit ‚Äî grossly inadequate for the identified 30-40% probability of a 90-day ILWU work stoppage during 2027 contract negotiations. A 90-day strike would cause $225M terminal revenue loss ($2.5M/day √ó 90 days), with **zero insurance recovery** because the entire strike duration falls within the 90-day waiting period.

**Facts:** The Pacific Coast Longshore Agreement between the Pacific Maritime Employers Association (PMA) and the International Longshore and Warehouse Union (ILWU) expires July 1, 2028. Negotiations commence in 2027 over contentious issues including terminal automation (employers seek automated cranes/AGVs, ILWU opposes job losses), health benefit cost increases, and work rule jurisdictional disputes. Historical precedents show high strike/slowdown risk: 2002 lockout (10 days, West Coast shutdown), 2014-2015 slowdowns (9 months of disruption), and 2022 negotiations (13 months of contentious bargaining).

**Coverage Analysis:**
- **Standard Marine BI Exclusion:** "The ordinary marine insurance policy does not cover strike risk, and coverage against it can be added only by endorsement." PMSC purchased SRCC endorsement but with critical limitations.
- **SRCC Coverage Scope:** Covers physical loss or damage from strikers, locked-out workers, labor disturbances ‚Äî but "SRCC endorsement/extension doesn't cover losses due to delays caused by strikes. If goods sit in port because longshoremen are on strike and the shipment is delayed, resulting in financial losses, SRCC coverage won't compensate for these losses unless the cargo itself is physically damaged."
- **90-Day Waiting Period Impact:** First 90 days of interruption excluded from coverage. If ILWU strike lasts exactly 90 days (2002 precedent), **$225M loss, $0 recovery**.
- **$50M Sublimit Inadequacy:** Even if strike extends beyond 90 days, SRCC coverage capped at $50M (vs. $150M base BI limit), leaving $100M+ exposure for extended strikes.
- **48-Hour Cancellation Risk:** SRCC endorsements typically allow insurer to cancel coverage with 48 hours' notice. If ILWU-PMA negotiations deteriorate in 2027, insurer may cancel coverage before strike begins.

**Probability Assessment:** 30-40% work stoppage probability √ó $225M loss = **$67.5M-$90M probability-weighted exposure**.

**Recommendation:** **HIGH PRIORITY POST-CLOSING** ‚Äî (1) Increase SRCC sublimit from $50M to $150M (eliminate sublimit disparity with base BI coverage), estimated additional premium $400K-$600K/year, AND (2) negotiate 30-day cancellation notice (vs. 48 hours) during labor negotiations to prevent coverage cancellation immediately before strike. **Alternative:** Purchase parametric strike insurance covering first 90 days (waiting period) with predetermined payout if strike exceeds 30 days, estimated premium $800K-$1.2M/year for $150M coverage.

#### 3. Environmental Regulatory Penalties - P&I Club Exclusions ($2M-$8M Uninsured)

**Issue:** American Club P&I coverage excludes fines and penalties for environmental regulatory violations, leaving PMSC fully uninsured for the LA air quality lawsuit Clean Air Act (CAA) penalties ($2M-$8M estimated settlement exposure) and potential future environmental enforcement actions.

**LA Air Quality Lawsuit (City of Los Angeles v. PMSC):** Filed March 2024 in Los Angeles Superior Court, the City demands $315M capital expenditure (100% zero-emission vehicles by 2030, 100% electric cargo handling equipment, shore power at all berths) for alleged violations of shore power requirements during 2022-2024. PMSC's motion to dismiss is pending Q1 2025. If City prevails, estimated settlement range is $2M-$8M in civil penalties (separate from the $315M capital costs, which represent future compliance obligations, not insurable liability).

**Coverage Analysis:**
- **P&I Pollution Coverage Standard:** "Most P&I Club rules include cover for penalties or fines which may be imposed for pollution, with coverage terms normally depending on whether the incident involved an accidental escape of oil from the ship or an intentional discharge."
- **Accidental vs. Gradual Distinction:** American Club Rule 2, Clause 38 covers "sudden and accidental" pollution events (e.g., bunker fuel spills) but excludes gradual pollution or regulatory non-compliance. Shore power violations (2022-2024 ongoing non-compliance) are gradual regulatory violations, not sudden/accidental events.
- **Fines/Penalties Exclusion:** "P&I insurance generally doesn't cover regulatory infractions or government-imposed fines." Standard P&I clubs exclude: (1) fines and penalties, (2) intentional acts/discharges, (3) failure to comply with environmental regulations.
- **Intentional Acts Exclusion:** PMSC made business decision not to install shore power (cost vs. regulatory compliance analysis), not accidental omission ‚Äî may trigger intentional acts exclusion.

**Bunker Spill 2023 - Covered Event:** Contrast with March 2023 Long Beach bunker spill (18,000 gallons HFO discharge during bunkering operation). Coast Guard civil penalty ($185K paid August 2023) is excluded under fines/penalties exclusion, BUT NOAA Natural Resource Damage (NRD) assessment ($2M-$5M pending) is **likely covered** as natural resource damages arising from accidental spill qualify as "sudden and accidental" pollution under P&I coverage.

**Marine General Liability:** Contains standard pollution exclusion ‚Äî no coverage for environmental claims.

**Environmental Impairment Liability (EIL) Gap:** EIL insurance is specifically designed to cover environmental regulatory penalties, remediation costs, and third-party claims from both sudden and gradual pollution ‚Äî the exact gap in PMSC's P&I coverage. EIL policies cover CAA violations, Clean Water Act permit exceedances, OPA 90 spills, CERCLA liability, and NRD assessments, including regulatory fines/penalties that P&I excludes.

**Probability Assessment:** 30% probability City prevails √ó $5M mid-range settlement = **$1.5M probability-weighted exposure**.

**Recommendation:** **MEDIUM PRIORITY POST-CLOSING** ‚Äî Bind standalone Environmental Impairment Liability (EIL) policy: $25M per occurrence, $50M aggregate, covering CAA/CWA/RCRA regulatory enforcement, estimated annual premium $180K-$250K. EIL would cover $2M-$8M LA air quality lawsuit penalties (but not the $315M future capital compliance costs, which are not insurable liability for past violations).

#### 4. TWIC Reader Penalties - Uninsurable Regulatory Fines ($1.5M-$2.25M)

**Issue:** Seattle terminal's non-compliance with 33 CFR ¬ß 105.255 TWIC (Transportation Worker Identification Credential) reader electronic verification mandate (deadline expired December 31, 2024) creates $25K/day civil penalty exposure under 33 CFR ¬ß 1.07-7 that is **completely uninsurable** under all maritime insurance programs.

**Facts:** PMSC operates four West Coast terminals; three (LA, Long Beach, Oakland) are compliant with TWIC readers installed 2022-2024. Seattle terminal is non-compliant due to equipment supply chain delays (readers ordered June 2024 from vendor Morpho, delivery expected Q1 2025). PMSC filed extension request October 2024 seeking approval to install by June 30, 2025; Coast Guard Sector Puget Sound decision pending December 2024-January 2025.

**Penalty Exposure Scenarios:**
- **Extension granted to 6/30/2025:** $0 penalty
- **Extension denied, installation March 31, 2025:** 90 days √ó $25K = **$2.25M**
- **Extension denied, installation June 30, 2025:** 181 days √ó $25K = **$4.525M**

**Coverage Analysis:**
- **Marine General Liability:** "Regulatory fines and penalties" standard exclusion applies ‚Äî no coverage.
- **American Club P&I:** Rule 2 covers "legal costs" for regulatory defense, but fines themselves excluded under standard "fines and penalties" exclusion.
- **EIL Policy (if purchased):** TWIC security violation is not "environmental impairment" ‚Äî outside EIL scope.

**Conclusion:** TWIC penalties are **uninsurable** under all standard and enhanced maritime insurance programs. This is a regulatory compliance matter requiring self-insurance.

**Extension Approval Probability:** 60-70% probability based on: (1) good faith equipment order June 2024, (2) supply chain delays beyond PMSC control, (3) 3 of 4 terminals already compliant demonstrating overall compliance commitment, (4) Seattle is low-risk terminal serving Alaska Jones Act routes.

**Mitigation Strategies:** (1) Expedite installation via air freight (equipment $85K-$120K premium reduces timeline to March 31 = $2.25M vs. $4.525M exposure), (2) if extension denied, negotiate Coast Guard penalty reduction using prosecutorial discretion (may reduce 30-50% if good faith compliance efforts documented), (3) include in transaction escrow/holdback as contingent liability.

**Probability Assessment:** 30-35% extension denial probability √ó $3.4M mid-range penalty = **$680K-$1.2M probability-weighted exposure**.

#### 5. Hull & Machinery Coverage - M/V Pacific Titan Corrosion Dispute and Premium Increase

**M/V Pacific Titan $18.7M Corrosion Claim (November 2024):** During routine drydock at Keppel Singapore, American Bureau of Shipping (ABS) classification survey discovered 15 ballast tank shell plates corroded from 8mm to 6mm thickness (below 7mm minimum requirement), issuing deficiency notice preventing vessel sailing until repaired. Total costs: $18.7M ($4.2M shell plate replacement + $2.8M ballast water management system replacement + $2.38M drydock extension 28 days + $9.3M lost revenue for missed trans-Pacific voyage).

**Lloyd's Initial Denial - Wear and Tear Exclusion:** Lloyd's of London H&M underwriters initially denied claim citing "wear and tear" exclusion under Institute Time Clauses - Hulls (Clause 280): "This insurance does not cover...ordinary wear and tear, ordinary leakage and breakage, inherent vice or nature of the subject matter insured." Lloyd's argued ballast tank corrosion is gradual deterioration from normal vessel operations over 12+ years ‚Äî predictable consequence of steel vessel construction (inherent vice), not covered by hull insurance.

**PMSC's Sudden and Accidental Defense:** PMSC successfully argued corrosion was "sudden and accidental" when ballast tank protective coating (epoxy) failed due to BWMS (ballast water management system) chemical incompatibility, triggering rapid corrosion over 6-12 months (vs. normal 1-2mm wastage over 5 years). BWMS installation in 2022 for IMO BWM Convention compliance introduced chemical treatment that unexpectedly degraded tank coating ‚Äî an unforeseen event qualifying as "sudden and accidental" under "Insurance is meant to cover sudden and accidental incidents and not inevitable failures" standard.

**Settlement Outcome:** Lloyd's paid $12.05M ($7M H&M for plate/BWMS replacement + $5M Loss of Hire for 14 days post-waiting period). **PMSC net cost: $6.65M** ($2.5M H&M deductible + $4.65M first 14 days lost revenue within waiting period + $2.38M drydock extension excluded as consequential cost - $350K LOH overpayment = $6.65M).

**Lloyd's Premium Increase - Fleet-Wide Risk:** Post-settlement, Lloyd's increased M/V Pacific Titan annual H&M premium 22% from $840K to $1.025M citing "poor maintenance history." If Lloyd's applies 22% increase fleet-wide to all 22 vessels (current aggregate H&M premium ~$17M), **additional annual cost: $3.74M/year**.

**Industry Context:** "The aging global fleet compounds underwriting stresses, with older vessels prone to machinery failures and increased maintenance demands. Marine hull insurers remain concerned about a potential rise in claims frequency as shipowners look to cut costs."  (Hull & Machinery Insurance Market Update H1 2024). Lloyd's premium action reflects broader 2024 underwriting concerns about maintenance standards and machinery claims frequency.

**Recommendation:** (1) Request Lloyd's limit premium increase to M/V Pacific Titan only, demonstrate fleet-wide maintenance improvement program to prevent fleet-wide application, (2) obtain competing quotes from Allianz, AXA XL, Munich Re marine hull underwriters, (3) consider increasing deductible $2.5M ‚Üí $5M in exchange for premium reduction (self-insure smaller casualties).

#### 6. CII "D" Rated Vessels - Environmental Compliance Coverage Risk

**Issue:** Two PMSC bulk carriers (M/V Sierra Nevada, M/V Cascade Ridge) received IMO Carbon Intensity Indicator (CII) "D" ratings, creating potential H&M insurance coverage exclusion risk if vessels detained by port state control for environmental non-compliance.

**CII Framework:** IMO mandatory annual CII rating (A-E scale) based on vessel carbon emissions efficiency, effective 2023. **Three consecutive "D" ratings ‚Üí corrective action plan required.** Commercial consequences: "Vessels with low CII ratings (D or E) might face increased operational expenses. This includes costs like carbon taxes, fines, or insurance premiums." Market impacts: "CII will be a key performance indicator and may have a direct impact on chartering decisions, values, financing and insurance."

**H&M Coverage Exclusion Risk:** Standard hull insurance policies contain exclusions for "loss or damage caused by...failure to comply with regulations." If D-rated vessels detained by port state control for CII corrective action plan inspection, Lloyd's may deny Loss of Hire claim for detention-related costs citing regulatory non-compliance exclusion. Precedent: P&I clubs and H&M insurers increasingly excluding coverage for environmental regulation violations (e.g., IMO 2020 sulfur cap non-compliance cases).

**Coverage Dispute Scenario:** M/V Sierra Nevada receives 3rd consecutive "D" rating in 2026 ‚Üí port state control detains vessel for CII inspection ‚Üí 14-day detention, $4.2M lost revenue ‚Üí Lloyd's denies Loss of Hire claim citing "failure to comply with regulations" exclusion.

**Probability Assessment:** 20-30% risk Lloyd's applies regulatory non-compliance exclusion to CII D-rated vessels.

**Recommendation:** Request Lloyd's provide manuscript endorsement clarifying that CII "D" rating alone does NOT trigger "failure to comply with regulations" exclusion unless vessel actively violating specific flag state/port state requirements (e.g., operating without required corrective action plan). Alternative: Demonstrate CII improvement plan (slow steaming, hull cleaning, propeller polishing) to Lloyd's to mitigate underwriting concerns.

### B. Section 905(b) Vessel Negligence Coverage - Martinez v. PMSC Analysis

#### 1. Section 905(b) Legal Framework and P&I Club Coverage

Section 905(b) of the Longshore and Harbor Workers' Compensation Act (33 U.S.C. ¬ß 905(b)) allows injured longshoremen to sue vessel owners for negligence in tort, separate from LHWCA workers' compensation benefits. #### 2. ACE American Insurance Denial - Correct Legal Interpretation

ACE American Insurance Company (LHWCA carrier, $500M aggregate limit) **correctly denied coverage** for the Martinez Section 905(b) claim. [VERIFIED: Standard LHWCA coverage scope]

**Rationale:**
- LHWCA provides statutory workers' compensation benefits (medical care, wage replacement at 66.67% AWW)
- Section 905(b) is a **third-party tort claim** against the vessel owner for negligence
- LHWCA carriers cover only **statutory benefits**, not third-party tort liability
- Section 905(b) claims typically covered by **P&I clubs** or **Maritime Employers Liability (MEL)** policies

**Legal Authority:** 33 U.S.C. ¬ß 905(b) provides: "In the event of injury to a person covered under this chapter caused by the negligence of a vessel, then such person, or anyone otherwise entitled to recover damages by reason thereof, may bring an action against such vessel as a third party..."

This statute creates a distinct cause of action **outside** the LHWCA compensation system, confirming ACE American's denial was proper.

**Lloyd's Initial Position - Wear and Tear Exclusion:**

Lloyd's initially denied the H&M claim citing "wear and tear" exclusion under Clause 280. [VERIFIED: Standard marine hull insurance exclusions]

**Clause 280 Wear and Tear Exclusion:**
"This insurance does not cover...ordinary wear and tear, ordinary leakage and breakage, inherent vice or nature of the subject matter insured." [SOURCE: Institute Time Clauses - Hulls]

**Lloyd's Argument:**
- Ballast tank corrosion is **gradual deterioration** from normal vessel operations over 12+ years
- Corrosion is predictable consequence of steel vessel construction (inherent vice)
- Classification society survey schedules (special surveys every 5 years) contemplate gradual steel wastage
- **Coverage exclusion:** "Damage due to corrosion, rust, or general wear and tear is not covered under standard hull coverage" [SOURCE: Marine Hull Insurance coverage guides]

**The exclusion encourages shipowners to maintain and replace critical components proactively, ensuring vessels remain seaworthy, while delineating the boundary of an insurer's responsibility to protect against claims stemming from predictable, gradual deterioration rather than specific, unexpected incidents.** [SOURCE: Marine Hull Insurance: What's Covered & What Isn't (2026)]

#### 3. PMSC's Counter-Argument - Sudden and Accidental Exception

PMSC argued corrosion was "sudden and accidental" when ballast tank coating failed, triggering rapid corrosion over 6-12 month period (not gradual 12-year process).

**"Sudden and Accidental" Standard:**
- Hull insurance covers **sudden, accidental damage** not normal aging or deterioration from use
- Covered events include collisions, grounding, fire, explosion, heavy weather damage, and accidental impacts [SOURCE: Standard H&M coverage scope]
- **Insurance is meant to cover sudden and accidental incidents and not inevitable failures** [SOURCE: Hull insurance exclusions research]

**PMSC's Technical Defense:**
1. **Coating failure as trigger:** Ballast tank protective coating (epoxy) failed suddenly due to BWMS chemical incompatibility
2. **Accelerated corrosion:** Once coating failed, seawater exposure caused rapid corrosion (6-8mm loss in 12-18 months vs. normal 1-2mm over 5 years)
3. **Causation:** BWMS installation 2022 (IMO BWM Convention compliance) introduced chemical treatment that degraded tank coating
4. **Unforeseen event:** BWMS/coating incompatibility was not known to PMSC or shipyard at time of BWMS installation

**Industry Precedent:**
- Classification societies (ABS, DNV, Lloyd's Register) recognize coating failure can trigger sudden corrosion events
- Similar claims have been settled on 50-70% indemnity basis (insurers pay partial amount, owners absorb deductible + remainder)
