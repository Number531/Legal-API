#### Section 7.1  Vermont Captive Letter of Credit

The obligations of Buyer to consummate the Closing shall be subject to satisfaction or waiver of the following condition: Seller shall have caused Liberty Reinsurance VT LLC to obtain, and shall deliver to Buyer at Closing, an irrevocable, clean, unconditional letter of credit (the "**Vermont Captive LOC**") in the form attached as **Exhibit G**, satisfying the following requirements:

(a) **LOC Amount**: Four Hundred Million Dollars ($400,000,000);

(b) **LOC Issuer**: A U.S. banking institution rated AA- or better by Standard & Poor's Rating Services or Fitch Ratings, or Aa3 or better by Moody's Investors Service (acceptable issuers include JPMorgan Chase Bank, N.A., Citibank, N.A., or Bank of America, N.A.);

(c) **Beneficiary**: Liberty Life Insurance Company (the "**Company**");

(d) **Term**: Five (5) years from the Closing Date, automatically renewable for successive one-year periods unless the issuing bank provides ninety (90) days' prior written notice of non-renewal to the Company and Buyer;

(e) **Draw Conditions**: Beneficiary may draw upon the Vermont Captive LOC upon presentation to the issuing bank of beneficiary's certificate stating that (i) the Nebraska Department of Insurance has issued a written notice to the Company disallowing all or any portion of the reserve credit relating to the reinsurance transactions between the Company and Liberty Reinsurance VT LLC, or (ii) the Nebraska Department of Insurance has commenced formal proceedings to disallow such reserve credit and the Company reasonably determines that such disallowance is probable;

(f) **Purpose**: The Vermont Captive LOC shall provide collateral support for the reserve credit taken by the Company for reinsurance ceded to Liberty Reinsurance VT LLC, in compliance with Nebraska insurance laws and regulations and Actuarial Guideline 48 as adopted by the National Association of Insurance Commissioners.

For the avoidance of doubt, Buyer's obligation to close is contingent upon delivery of the Vermont Captive LOC at Closing. Seller shall bear all costs and expenses associated with obtaining the Vermont Captive LOC, including arrangement fees, issuance fees, and annual renewal fees. Seller shall execute a letter of credit term sheet with an acceptable LOC Issuer within sixty (60) days after the execution of this Agreement, and shall provide Buyer with written status updates every ten (10) Business Days thereafter until the Vermont Captive LOC is delivered. If Seller fails to deliver the Vermont Captive LOC at Closing, Buyer may terminate this Agreement pursuant to Section 9.1(d) without liability.

#### Section 7.2  AFH Direct Capital Funding

The obligations of Buyer to consummate the Closing shall be subject to satisfaction or waiver of the following condition: Assured Financial Holdings, Inc. ("**AFH**") shall have committed to provide, and shall provide at Closing, the capital support described below:

(a) **Direct Capital Injection**: AFH shall wire transfer to the Company at Closing the amount of One Hundred Fifty Million Dollars ($150,000,000) in immediately available funds. Such funds shall be applied by the Company to the purchase of surplus notes issued by the Company to AFH (the "**AFH Surplus Notes**") in accordance with the Surplus Notes Purchase Agreement in the form attached as **Exhibit H**.

(b) **Surplus Notes Terms**: The AFH Surplus Notes shall have the following terms:
    (i) **Principal Amount**: $150,000,000;
    (ii) **Maturity Date**: Ten (10) years from the Closing Date;
    (iii) **Interest Rate**: 5.1% per annum, calculated on an after-tax basis;
    (iv) **Interest Payment**: Quarterly in arrears, subject to prior written approval of the Nebraska Department of Insurance in accordance with Neb. Rev. Stat. section 44-510.02;
    (v) **Prepayment**: Permitted with fifteen (15) days' prior written notice, subject to prior written approval of the Nebraska Department of Insurance;
    (vi) **Subordination**: Subordinated to all policyholder claims and all other obligations of the Company except obligations expressly subordinated to the AFH Surplus Notes.

(c) **Regulatory Pre-Approval**: Seller shall obtain written approval from the Nebraska Department of Insurance for the issuance of the AFH Surplus Notes and the terms thereof no later than sixty (60) days prior to the anticipated Closing Date. Seller shall file the application for such approval within thirty (30) days after the execution of this Agreement and shall cooperate with Buyer to respond promptly to any questions or requests for additional information from the Nebraska Department of Insurance.

(d) **Parental Guarantee Assumption**: Concurrently with the Closing, AFH shall execute and deliver to the Company a guarantee in the form attached as **Exhibit I** (the "**AFH Guarantee**") pursuant to which AFH guarantees all obligations of Liberty Reinsurance VT LLC to the Company under the Reinsurance Agreement dated June 15, 2023. Upon execution and delivery of the AFH Guarantee, Liberty Life Holdings, Inc. shall be released from all obligations under the Parental Guarantee dated June 15, 2023 in favor of the Company (the "**Existing Parental Guarantee**"), and the Existing Parental Guarantee shall terminate and be of no further force or effect. The aggregate liability of AFH under the AFH Guarantee shall not exceed Seven Hundred Thirty Million Dollars ($730,000,000).

(e) **Funding Mechanics**: The $150,000,000 wire transfer described in subsection (a) shall be made directly from AFH to the Company. Under no circumstances shall such funds be transferred to or through Liberty Life Holdings, Inc. or any other Affiliate of Seller (other than the Company).

If AFH fails or refuses to provide the Direct Capital Injection or execute the AFH Guarantee, Buyer may (i) terminate this Agreement pursuant to Section 9.1(d) without liability, or (ii) proceed to Closing with a Purchase Price reduction of One Hundred Fifty Million Dollars ($150,000,000).

#### Section 7.3  Agent and Producer Retention Program

The obligations of Buyer to consummate the Closing shall be subject to satisfaction or waiver of the following condition: Seller shall have established, funded, and implemented an agent and producer retention program (the "**Retention Program**") satisfying the following requirements:

(a) **Captive Agent Retention Component**:
    (i) **Aggregate Funding**: Twenty-Two Million Dollars ($22,000,000);
    (ii) **Eligible Participants**: All captive agents of the Company actively producing business as of the date of this Agreement (approximately 650 agents);
    (iii) **Payment Schedule**: Fifty percent (50%) of each eligible agent's retention payment shall be paid twelve (12) months after the Closing Date, and the remaining fifty percent (50%) shall be paid twenty-four (24) months after the Closing Date, in each case subject to the agent's continued active production of business for the Company;
    (iv) **Participation Threshold**: Retention agreements shall have been executed by agents representing at least ninety percent (90%) of the total number of eligible captive agents prior to Closing.

(b) **Independent Producer Retention Component**:
    (i) **Aggregate Funding**: Twenty-Four Million Dollars ($24,000,000);
    (ii) **Eligible Participants**: The top two hundred (200) independent insurance producers ranked by annual premium production volume for the Company during the twelve (12) months preceding the date of this Agreement;
    (iii) **Incentive Structure**: Production bonus equal to five percent (5%) of gross written premium produced by such independent producer during the first twelve (12) months following the Closing Date, payable quarterly in arrears;
    (iv) **Participation Threshold**: Production bonus agreements shall have been executed by independent producers representing at least eighty-five percent (85%) of the aggregate premium production volume of the top two hundred (200) independent producers during the twelve (12) months preceding the date of this Agreement.

(c) **Funding Mechanism**: At Closing, Seller shall deliver evidence reasonably satisfactory to Buyer that (i) the aggregate amount of Forty-Six Million Dollars ($46,000,000) has been deposited into an escrow account with an escrow agent mutually acceptable to Buyer and Seller pursuant to an escrow agreement in the form attached as **Exhibit J**, or (ii) such amount has been accrued as a liability on the balance sheet of the Company in accordance with GAAP and is reflected in the Closing Statement delivered pursuant to Section 2.5.

(d) **Program Documentation**: No later than thirty (30) days after the execution of this Agreement, Seller shall provide Buyer with the proposed terms and conditions of the Retention Program for Buyer's review and reasonable approval. Seller shall use commercially reasonable efforts to execute retention agreements with eligible participants within thirty (30) days after the Closing Date.

If the participation thresholds set forth in subsections (a)(iv) and (b)(iv) are not achieved prior to Closing, Buyer may (i) waive this condition and proceed to Closing, (ii) terminate this Agreement pursuant to Section 9.1(d) without liability, or (iii) proceed to Closing with a Purchase Price reduction of One Hundred Million Dollars ($100,000,000).

#### Section 7.4  IUL Class Action Settlement Approval

The obligations of Buyer to consummate the Closing shall be subject to satisfaction or waiver of the following condition: The class action litigation styled *Thompson v. Liberty Life Insurance Company*, Case No. CV-2023-1847, pending in the District Court of Douglas County, Nebraska (the "**IUL Class Action**"), shall have been settled and such settlement shall have received final judicial approval, subject to the following requirements:

(a) **Settlement Execution**: The Company shall have executed a definitive class action settlement agreement with counsel for the plaintiff class (the "**Settlement Agreement**") on or before March 31, 2025. The Settlement Agreement shall provide for the establishment of a settlement fund to compensate class members for claims relating to alleged misrepresentations regarding the performance of indexed universal life insurance policies issued by the Company.

(b) **Court Approval**: The District Court of Douglas County, Nebraska shall have entered an order granting final approval of the Settlement Agreement on or before June 30, 2025. Such final approval order shall not be subject to any appeal or motion for reconsideration, or the time for filing any such appeal or motion shall have expired without any such appeal or motion having been filed.

(c) **Settlement Amount**: The total settlement fund under the Settlement Agreement shall not exceed Forty Million Dollars ($40,000,000), inclusive of all class member payments, notice and administration costs, attorneys' fees and costs, and any incentive awards to class representatives.

(d) **E&O Insurance Confirmation**: The Company's errors and omissions insurance carrier, Federal Insurance Company (Chubb) (the "**E&O Insurer**"), shall have confirmed in writing that (i) the E&O Insurance Policy No. 8352-1947 provides coverage for the claims asserted in the IUL Class Action in an aggregate amount not less than Thirty-Five Million Dollars ($35,000,000), (ii) no fraud or intentional misconduct exclusion applies to bar or limit such coverage, and (iii) the E&O Insurer has no basis to rescind or deny coverage under the policy.

(e) **Pre-Closing Funding**: Seller shall fund the settlement amount (excluding any portion covered by the E&O Insurer) prior to the Closing Date. Evidence of such funding reasonably satisfactory to Buyer shall be delivered at Closing. Under no circumstances shall the Company be required to fund any portion of the settlement amount from the Company's assets or capital.

(f) **Mediation Process**: Seller shall participate in good faith in mediation of the IUL Class Action during the period from January 1, 2025 through March 31, 2025, and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to achieve a negotiated settlement within the parameters set forth in subsection (c).

If the Settlement Agreement is not executed by March 31, 2025, Buyer may (i) terminate this Agreement pursuant to Section 9.1(d) without liability, or (ii) elect to proceed to Closing subject to (A) an increase in the Tier 3 Escrow Amount (as defined in Section 2.4(c)) by Fifty Million Dollars ($50,000,000), and (B) a Purchase Price reduction of Twenty-Five Million Dollars ($25,000,000). If final court approval is not obtained by June 30, 2025, but a Settlement Agreement has been executed and preliminary approval has been granted, Buyer may elect to proceed to Closing subject to the escrow increase and price reduction described in the preceding sentence. Notwithstanding anything to the contrary in this Agreement, if the District Court schedules the IUL Class Action for trial on a date prior to the anticipated Closing Date, Buyer may terminate this Agreement pursuant to Section 9.1(d) without liability.

#### Section 7.5  Global Re Consent

The obligations of Buyer to consummate the Closing shall be subject to satisfaction or waiver of the following condition: Global Reinsurance Corporation ("**Global Re**") shall have consented to the assignment or change of control of the Reinsurance Agreement dated December 1, 2021 between the Company and Global Re (the "**Global Re Reinsurance Agreement**") in accordance with Section 12.8 thereof, subject to the following requirements:

(a) **Consent Request**: Within ten (10) Business Days after the execution of this Agreement, Seller shall submit to Global Re a written request for consent to the change of control of the Company resulting from the transactions contemplated by this Agreement (the "**Consent Request**"). The Consent Request shall be in the form attached as **Exhibit K** and shall include such information regarding Buyer and its financial condition as Global Re may reasonably require.

(b) **Consent Standard**: Global Re shall have delivered to Seller and Buyer a written consent to the change of control (the "**Global Re Consent**") that does not contain any modifications to the Global Re Reinsurance Agreement that are materially adverse to the Company or Buyer. For purposes of this Section 7.5, a modification shall be deemed "materially adverse" if it would reasonably be expected to result in a net present value impact to the Company or Buyer exceeding Fifty Million Dollars ($50,000,000), including but not limited to:
    (i) Any increase in ceding commission rates, recapture charges, or other fees payable by the Company to Global Re;
    (ii) Any reduction in reinsurance coverage limits or expansion of exclusions from coverage;
    (iii) Any acceleration of recapture rights or modification of recapture provisions that would permit Global Re to recapture reinsured policies prior to the recapture dates set forth in the Global Re Reinsurance Agreement as in effect on the date of this Agreement;
    (iv) Any increase in collateral or letter of credit requirements imposed on the Company.

(c) **Review Period**: Global Re shall have a period of ninety (90) days from the date of the Consent Request to review and respond to the Consent Request. Seller and Buyer shall cooperate to provide Global Re with any additional information reasonably requested by Global Re during such review period.

(d) **Anti-Recapture Negotiation**: If Global Re conditions its consent upon modifications to the Global Re Reinsurance Agreement relating to recapture provisions, Seller shall negotiate with Global Re in good faith to minimize the economic impact of such modifications. Seller's authority to agree to economic concessions in connection with such negotiations shall not exceed Forty Million Dollars ($40,000,000) in net present value without Buyer's prior written consent.

(e) **Alternative Closing Conditions**: If Global Re has not delivered the Global Re Consent as of the Closing Date, but has not denied or conditioned its consent in a manner that would violate subsection (b), Buyer may elect to proceed to Closing subject to an increase in the Tier 2 Escrow Amount (as defined in Section 2.4(b)) by Fifty Million Dollars ($50,000,000), which additional escrow amount shall be released in accordance with Section 2.4(b) upon receipt of the Global Re Consent in accordance with this Section 7.5.

If Global Re denies the Consent Request or conditions its consent upon modifications that are materially adverse to the Company or Buyer (as determined under subsection (b)), Buyer may (i) terminate this Agreement pursuant to Section 9.1(d) without liability, or (ii) elect to proceed to Closing with such modifications if Buyer determines in its sole discretion that the modifications are acceptable notwithstanding their material adverse nature.
