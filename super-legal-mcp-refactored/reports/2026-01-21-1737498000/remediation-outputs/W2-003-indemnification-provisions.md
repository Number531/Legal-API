## Section 10.2  Indemnification by Buyer

Subject to the limitations set forth in this Article X, from and after the Closing, Buyer shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Seller and its Affiliates, officers, directors, employees, agents, successors, and assigns (collectively, the "**Seller Indemnified Parties**") from and against any and all Losses arising out of or resulting from:

(a) **Breach of Representations and Warranties**. Any breach of any representation or warranty made by Buyer in this Agreement or in any certificate delivered by Buyer pursuant to this Agreement;

(b) **Breach of Covenants**. Any breach of any covenant or agreement of Buyer contained in this Agreement;

(c) **Post-Closing Operations**. Any Losses arising out of the operation of the Company's business following the Closing, except to the extent such Losses arise from or relate to matters for which Seller has an indemnification obligation under Section 10.1; and

(d) **Post-Closing Taxes**. Any Taxes of the Company for any Post-Closing Tax Period (as defined in Section 8.4), except to the extent Seller has an indemnification obligation under Section 10.1(g).

## Section 10.3  Limitations on Indemnification

(a) **Basket**. Seller shall not have any liability under Section 10.1(a) (indemnification for breach of representations and warranties) until the aggregate amount of Losses with respect to such matters exceeds Five Million Dollars ($5,000,000) (the "**Basket**"), and then only for the amount by which such Losses exceed the Basket; *provided, however*, that the Basket shall not apply to:

> (i) Losses arising out of or resulting from breaches of the Fundamental Representations (as defined in Section 10.4(b));
>
> (ii) the Specific Indemnities set forth in Section 10.1(c) through (g);
>
> (iii) Losses arising from fraud or willful misconduct; or
>
> (iv) Losses arising from breaches of covenants under Section 10.1(b).

(b) **Cap**. The aggregate liability of Seller under Section 10.1(a) (indemnification for breach of representations and warranties) shall not exceed Five Hundred Fifty-Two Million Dollars ($552,000,000) (the "**Cap**"), which amount represents twenty percent (20%) of the Adjusted Purchase Price as finally determined pursuant to Section 2.3; *provided, however*, that the Cap shall not apply to:

> (i) Losses arising out of or resulting from breaches of the Fundamental Representations;
>
> (ii) the Specific Indemnities set forth in Section 10.1(c) through (g), each of which shall be subject to its own stated limit or threshold;
>
> (iii) Losses arising from fraud or willful misconduct; or
>
> (iv) Losses arising from breaches of covenants under Section 10.1(b).

(c) **Buyer Indemnity Limitations**. The aggregate liability of Buyer under Section 10.2(a) (indemnification for breach of representations and warranties) shall be subject to a basket of Five Million Dollars ($5,000,000) and a cap of One Hundred Million Dollars ($100,000,000), except in the case of fraud or willful misconduct.

(d) **Mitigation**. Each Indemnified Party shall use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate any Losses for which it seeks indemnification under this Article X, including by pursuing recovery under applicable insurance policies and from third parties. The amount of any Losses for which indemnification is available under this Article X shall be reduced by:

> (i) any insurance proceeds or other third-party recoveries actually received by the Indemnified Party with respect to such Losses (net of any costs of collection and any increase in insurance premiums attributable to such claim); and
>
> (ii) any net Tax benefit actually realized by the Indemnified Party as a result of such Losses in the taxable year in which the Loss is incurred or the immediately succeeding taxable year.

(e) **No Double Recovery**. Notwithstanding anything to the contrary in this Agreement, no Indemnified Party shall be entitled to recover more than once for the same Loss, whether through adjustment to the Purchase Price, indemnification under this Article X, recovery from the Escrow Amount, or otherwise.

(f) **No Consequential Damages**. Notwithstanding anything to the contrary in this Agreement, no party shall be liable to any other party for any punitive, exemplary, special, incidental, indirect, or consequential damages (including lost profits or diminution in value) in connection with this Agreement or the transactions contemplated hereby, except to the extent:

> (i) such damages are reasonably foreseeable and directly caused by fraud or willful misconduct; or
>
> (ii) such damages are awarded to a third party in a Third-Party Claim (as defined in Section 10.6) for which indemnification is required under this Article X.

## Section 10.5  Claims Procedures

(a) **Notice of Claims**. An Indemnified Party seeking indemnification under this Article X (a "**Claimant**") shall promptly notify the party from whom indemnification is sought (the "**Indemnifying Party**") in writing of any claim for indemnification under this Article X (a "**Claim Notice**"). The Claim Notice shall describe in reasonable detail:

> (i) the facts and circumstances giving rise to such claim;
>
> (ii) the basis under this Article X for such claim;
>
> (iii) copies of any written materials relating to such claim (to the extent reasonably available);
>
> (iv) the amount of Losses claimed or a good faith estimate thereof (which estimate shall not be conclusive of the final amount of such claim); and
>
> (v) the methodology for calculating such Losses.

(b) **Failure to Notify**. The failure to provide a Claim Notice in accordance with Section 10.5(a) shall not relieve the Indemnifying Party of its indemnification obligations under this Article X, except to the extent that the Indemnifying Party is actually prejudiced by such failure (including by losing the ability to effectively defend against, mitigate, or settle the claim).

(c) **Response Period**. Within thirty (30) days after receipt of a Claim Notice relating to a claim that does not involve a Third-Party Claim (as defined in Section 10.6), the Indemnifying Party shall notify the Claimant in writing either:

> (i) acknowledging its obligation to indemnify the Claimant for all Losses to which the Claim Notice relates (subject to the limitations set forth in this Article X);
>
> (ii) disputing its obligation to indemnify the Claimant for any or all Losses to which the Claim Notice relates, in which case the notice shall describe in reasonable detail the basis for such dispute; or
>
> (iii) acknowledging its obligation to indemnify the Claimant for certain Losses and disputing its obligation with respect to other Losses, specifying which Losses are acknowledged and which are disputed.

(d) **Dispute Resolution**. If the Indemnifying Party disputes all or any portion of a claim in accordance with Section 10.5(c)(ii) or (iii), and the parties are unable to resolve such dispute within thirty (30) days following the Claimant's receipt of the Indemnifying Party's response, either party may submit the dispute for resolution in accordance with Section 11.9 (Dispute Resolution).

(e) **Payment**. The Indemnifying Party shall pay any undisputed amounts to the Claimant within thirty (30) days after the earlier of:

> (i) the Indemnifying Party's acknowledgment of its indemnification obligation under Section 10.5(c)(i); or
>
> (ii) the final resolution of any dispute through negotiation, arbitration, or litigation as provided in Section 10.5(d).

Payment shall be made by wire transfer of immediately available funds to an account designated by the Claimant, or, in the case of indemnification obligations of Seller, by notice to the Escrow Agent to release funds from the Escrow Amount to the extent available.

## Section 10.6  Third-Party Claims

(a) **Notice of Third-Party Claims**. If a Claimant receives notice of any claim, demand, lawsuit, or other proceeding by a third party (a "**Third-Party Claim**") that may give rise to indemnification under this Article X, the Claimant shall provide a Claim Notice to the Indemnifying Party within fifteen (15) days after receipt of such notice; *provided, however*, that the failure to provide such notice shall not relieve the Indemnifying Party of its indemnification obligations except to the extent the Indemnifying Party is actually prejudiced thereby.

(b) **Defense of Third-Party Claims**. Upon receipt of a Claim Notice relating to a Third-Party Claim, the Indemnifying Party may, at its option and expense, assume the defense of such Third-Party Claim by providing written notice to the Claimant within thirty (30) days after receipt of the Claim Notice; *provided, however*, that the Indemnifying Party may not assume the defense of any Third-Party Claim if:

> (i) the Third-Party Claim seeks non-monetary or equitable relief against the Claimant;
>
> (ii) the Third-Party Claim relates to or arises in connection with any criminal proceeding, action, indictment, allegation, or investigation;
>
> (iii) the Third-Party Claim involves a material customer, supplier, or other business relationship of the Company and the conduct of the defense could reasonably be expected to materially adversely affect such relationship;
>
> (iv) the Indemnifying Party does not have sufficient financial resources to satisfy the potential liability (as reasonably determined by the Claimant);
>
> (v) the Third-Party Claim is reasonably likely to result in Losses exceeding the amount available under the Cap or the Escrow Amount; or
>
> (vi) there is a reasonable likelihood of a conflict of interest between the Indemnifying Party and the Claimant with respect to such Third-Party Claim.

(c) **Indemnifying Party Control**. If the Indemnifying Party assumes the defense of a Third-Party Claim in accordance with Section 10.6(b):

> (i) the Indemnifying Party shall have the right to conduct the defense of such Third-Party Claim with counsel of its choice reasonably acceptable to the Claimant, at the Indemnifying Party's sole cost and expense;
>
> (ii) the Claimant shall cooperate with the Indemnifying Party in the defense of such Third-Party Claim and may participate in (but not control) the defense at its own expense;
>
> (iii) the Indemnifying Party shall keep the Claimant reasonably informed of the status of such Third-Party Claim and provide copies of all material pleadings, correspondence, and other documents;
>
> (iv) the Claimant shall make available to the Indemnifying Party all relevant information and documents reasonably requested by the Indemnifying Party; and
>
> (v) the Indemnifying Party shall not settle or compromise any Third-Party Claim without the prior written consent of the Claimant (such consent not to be unreasonably withheld, conditioned, or delayed) if such settlement or compromise:
>
>> (A) does not include an unconditional release of the Claimant from all liability with respect to such Third-Party Claim;
>>
>> (B) includes any admission of wrongdoing or fault by the Claimant;
>>
>> (C) imposes any non-monetary obligations on the Claimant; or
>>
>> (D) involves a material customer, supplier, or other business relationship of the Company.

(d) **Claimant Control**. If the Indemnifying Party does not assume (or is not permitted to assume) the defense of a Third-Party Claim in accordance with Section 10.6(b):

> (i) the Claimant shall have the right to conduct the defense of such Third-Party Claim with counsel of its choice, at the Indemnifying Party's cost and expense (subject to the limitations in Section 10.3);
>
> (ii) the Indemnifying Party may participate in (but not control) the defense at its own expense;
>
> (iii) the Claimant shall keep the Indemnifying Party reasonably informed of the status of such Third-Party Claim and provide copies of all material pleadings, correspondence, and other documents;
>
> (iv) the Indemnifying Party shall make available to the Claimant all relevant information and documents reasonably requested by the Claimant; and
>
> (v) the Claimant shall not settle or compromise any Third-Party Claim without the prior written consent of the Indemnifying Party (such consent not to be unreasonably withheld, conditioned, or delayed) if such settlement or compromise involves any payment or other liability by the Indemnifying Party.

(e) **Allocation of Costs**. If a Third-Party Claim involves matters for which some Losses are subject to indemnification under this Article X and others are not:

> (i) the parties shall use good faith efforts to allocate defense costs and liability in a fair and reasonable manner based on the relative merits and potential exposure of the indemnifiable and non-indemnifiable matters; and
>
> (ii) if the parties cannot agree on such allocation within thirty (30) days, the dispute shall be submitted to the Independent Accounting Firm for resolution, whose determination shall be final and binding on the parties.

## Section 10.8  Treatment of Indemnification Payments

All payments made by an Indemnifying Party to a Claimant under this Article X shall be treated as adjustments to the Purchase Price for all Tax purposes, unless otherwise required by applicable Law or a final determination (as defined in Section 1313(a) of the Code) to the contrary.

## Section 10.9  Subrogation

Upon payment in full of any claim for indemnification under this Article X, the Indemnifying Party shall be subrogated to the extent of such payment to all rights and remedies of the Indemnified Party with respect to the matter giving rise to such claim. The Indemnified Party shall execute all documents and instruments reasonably requested by the Indemnifying Party to evidence and perfect such subrogation rights, including assignments, powers of attorney, and other instruments of transfer.

## Section 10.10  Right of Set-Off

Buyer shall have the right (but not the obligation) to set off any amounts owed to Buyer Indemnified Parties by Seller under this Article X against any amounts owed by Buyer to Seller under this Agreement (including any Earnout Payments under Section 2.5 and any Purchase Price adjustment under Section 2.3), upon prior written notice to Seller specifying the basis for such set-off and the amount thereof. Any such set-off shall constitute full satisfaction of the obligation of Buyer to pay such amount to Seller and full satisfaction (to the extent of the set-off) of the indemnification obligation of Seller. For the avoidance of doubt, nothing in this Section 10.10 shall be construed as limiting Buyer's right to seek recovery from the Escrow Amount in accordance with the Escrow Agreement.

## Section 10.11  Escrow as Primary Source

Notwithstanding anything to the contrary in this Agreement, following the Closing and prior to the expiration of the Escrow Period (as defined in the Escrow Agreement), the Escrow Amount shall be the primary source for satisfaction of any indemnification obligations of Seller under this Article X. Buyer shall first seek recovery from the Escrow Amount before seeking payment directly from Seller; *provided, however*, that:

(a) if the Escrow Amount has been exhausted or is insufficient to satisfy any indemnification claim, Buyer may seek recovery of the deficiency directly from Seller, subject to the limitations set forth in this Article X;

(b) Buyer may seek recovery directly from Seller (without first seeking recovery from the Escrow Amount) with respect to any indemnification claim that exceeds the Cap or relates to fraud or willful misconduct; and

(c) nothing in this Section 10.11 shall limit Buyer's right to assert claims against the Escrow Amount or directly against Seller within the applicable survival periods set forth in Section 10.4.

## Section 10.12  Insurance Proceeds

(a) **Pursuit of Insurance**. Prior to seeking indemnification under this Article X for any Loss that may be covered by insurance maintained by the Company or Seller, Buyer shall use commercially reasonable efforts to pursue recovery under all applicable insurance policies. Seller shall cooperate with such efforts and shall not take any action to impair the Company's ability to recover under such policies.

(b) **Reduction for Recoveries**. The amount of any Losses for which Seller is obligated to provide indemnification under this Article X shall be reduced (including retroactively) by any insurance proceeds or other third-party recoveries actually received by any Buyer Indemnified Party with respect to such Losses, net of:

> (i) any deductibles, co-payments, or uncovered amounts under the applicable policy;
>
> (ii) any reasonable costs and expenses incurred in obtaining such proceeds;
>
> (iii) any increase in insurance premiums attributable to such recovery over the three (3) year period following such recovery (discounted to present value using a discount rate equal to the Prime Rate); and
>
> (iv) any Taxes payable with respect to such proceeds.

(c) **No Subrogation Waiver**. Nothing in this Agreement shall constitute a waiver of any insurance coverage or any rights of subrogation that any insurance carrier may have with respect to any Loss.

For purposes of this Article X:

**"Losses"** means any and all losses, liabilities, damages, costs, expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees, expert fees, and costs of investigation), judgments, fines, penalties, settlements, deficiencies, interest, and charges; *provided, however*, that "Losses" shall not include any diminution in value of the Company or the Company Shares, except to the extent such diminution directly results from a liability, cost, or expense otherwise constituting a Loss.

**"Pre-Closing Tax Period"** means any taxable period ending on or before the Closing Date and, with respect to any Straddle Period (as defined in Section 8.4(d)), the portion of such period ending on and including the Closing Date.
