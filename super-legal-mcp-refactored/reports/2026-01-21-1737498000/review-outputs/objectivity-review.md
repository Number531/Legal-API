## Advocacy Concerns (Minimal - 3 Instances)

### 1. Report: commercial-contracts-analyst-report.md
**Finding**: "Combined recapture scenario is catastrophic; acquisition cannot close if both recaptures are probable."

**Why Concerning**: Use of "catastrophic" is hyperbolic (though scenario does involve potential $885M loss). More neutral phrasing: "Combined recapture scenario creates $885M exposure, rendering transaction economically unviable without massive capital injection."

**Recommended Revision**: Replace "catastrophic" with "creates material economic unviability" or "$885M combined exposure exceeds acquirer's capital capacity."

**Severity**: MINOR - single instance in 100+ page report, and underlying analysis is sound (scenario IS economically catastrophic in factual sense).

### 2. Report: financial-impact-analysis.md
**Finding**: "THE SINGLE MOST CRITICAL RISK is Vermont captive AG48 non-compliance..."

**Why Concerning**: ALL CAPS emphasis resembles advocacy framing. More neutral: "Vermont captive AG48 non-compliance represents the highest-severity risk..."

**Recommended Revision**: Remove ALL CAPS; use neutral comparative language.

**Severity**: MINOR - emphasis reflects genuine materiality (10-15% × $730M = $73M-$109.5M probability-weighted, highest single risk), but presentation style should be neutral.

### 3. Report: securities-researcher-report.md (minor)
**Finding**: Section uses "clearly" 3 times in factual contexts (e.g., "rider fee disclosure 0.95% stated clearly").

**Why Acceptable**: All uses are factual descriptions (documents "clearly" stated something, not legal argument "clearly" the law requires). This is NOT advocacy.

**No Revision Needed**: "Clearly" used appropriately in factual context.

## Recommendations for Memo Synthesis

1. **Maintain Objectivity Standard**: Memo-section-writers should emulate specialist objectivity standards. Use probability ranges, acknowledge uncertainty, cite authority neutrally.

2. **Avoid ALL CAPS Emphasis**: Even for critical risks, use neutral comparative language ("highest-severity risk" not "THE SINGLE MOST CRITICAL RISK").

3. **Replace "Catastrophic" with Neutral Terms**: Use "economically unviable," "creates material insolvency risk," or "$XXM exposure exceeds capital capacity."

4. **Preserve Favorable Findings**: Nebraska punitive damages prohibition is FAVORABLE to acquirer but must be presented neutrally (not "LLIC clearly has strong defense").

5. **Cross-Reference Uncertainty**: When probability estimates depend on data room verification (E&O policy, holding company financials, treaty language), memo must flag these contingencies.
