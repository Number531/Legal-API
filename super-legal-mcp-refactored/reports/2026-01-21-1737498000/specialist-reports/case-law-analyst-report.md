### Overview and Critical Findings

This memorandum analyzes Thompson v. Liberty Life Insurance Company, an alleged class action involving 850 indexed universal life (IUL) policyholders claiming misrepresentation and omissions related to sales illustrations projecting 8.5% annual crediting rates. The case was allegedly filed in Nebraska District Court in August 2023 with claimed damages of $85M-$125M. However, **extensive public database searches did not locate case records matching this caption**, requiring verification through Seller's data room. Advancing mediation to Q1 2025 (February/March) and achieving settlement by August/September 2025 before Q3 2025 acquisition closing is feasible and strongly recommended.

LLIC's anticipated defense relies on technical compliance with NAIC Model Regulation #582 (Life Insurance Illustrations Model Regulation), adopted by majority of U.S. jurisdictions including Nebraska:

**LLIC's Compliance Evidence**:
- ✅ Illustrations clearly labeled "non-guaranteed" values
- ✅ Showed guaranteed column alongside illustrated column for comparison
- ✅ Included required disclosure language: "values not guaranteed, actual results may vary"

**Precedent Support**: Allianz EIA case (Finding 6) demonstrates technical compliance with disclosure regulations does not preclude "deceptive materials" finding and $250M settlement. Minnesota federal jury found Allianz used "misrepresentation or deceptive practice" despite regulatory compliance.

### D. NAIC Model Regulation 582 — Life Insurance Illustration Requirements

**FINDING 8: NAIC Model Regulation #582 Compliance Standards [VERIFIED]**

NAIC Model Regulation #582 (Life Insurance Illustrations Model Regulation) governs life insurance policy illustrations to protect consumers and foster consumer education. The regulation has been adopted by a majority of U.S. jurisdictions, including Nebraska.

**Key Requirements**:

1. **Three Types of Illustrations**:
   - Basic illustration (provided at policy delivery)
   - Supplemental illustration (additional scenarios beyond basic)
   - In-force illustration (periodic updates during policy life)

2. **Non-Guaranteed Values Disclosure**:
   - Illustrations must clearly label non-guaranteed values as "NOT GUARANTEED"
   - Must show guaranteed column alongside illustrated column for comparison
   - Required disclosure language: "Values not guaranteed, actual results may vary"

3. **Disciplined Current Scale**:
   - Illustration actuary must certify that illustrated rates conform to Actuarial Standard of Practice (ASOP) for compliance with NAIC Model Regulation 582
   - Crediting rates must be based on disciplined current scale methodology (not arbitrary optimistic assumptions)

4. **Illustration Actuary Appointment**:
   - Board of directors must appoint one or more illustration actuaries
   - Actuary certifies illustration methodology complies with actuarial standards

5. **Policy Delivery Process**:
   - If policy marketed with illustration and applied for as illustrated, applicant and agent must sign illustration at application
   - If no illustration used, both must sign form acknowledging no illustration provided

6. **Purpose and Goals**:
   - "Ensure that illustrations do not mislead purchasers of life insurance"
   - "Make illustrations more understandable"
   - Prescribe illustration formats and disclosure standards

**Why LLIC's Motion Likely Failed**:
