**2. Payer rate negotiations create $400M NPV exposure; physician retention revenue risk adds $250M; contract consent issues add $150M. Total weighted exposure: $800M (52% probability). **See** Section IV.K (Commercial Contracts and Payer Agreements) for detailed analysis.

**3. Probably Yes.** Because eight employed gastroenterologists collectively own 33.3% of Mercy Endoscopy Center LLC and refer designated health services (colonoscopies, upper endoscopies) to that entity for Medicare/Medicaid patients without qualifying for any regulatory exception under 42 C.F.R. Section 411.355-.357. The ASC fails the "same building" requirement for the in-office ancillary services exception, and the "whole hospital" exception applies only to hospitals. Expected value exposure: $41.9M (60% self-disclosure scenario at $18.5M; 30% government investigation at $65M; 10% qui tam litigation at $217.5M). **See** Section IV.A (STARK Law and Anti-Kickback Statute Compliance) for detailed analysis.

**4. Probably Yes.** Because the physician-owners receive approximately $35,000 annual profit distributions from the ASC while 85% of the ASC's procedures originate from their referrals, creating a direct financial incentive for referrals that constitutes prohibited remuneration under the one-purpose test established in *United States v. Greber*, 760 F.2d 68 (3d Cir. 1985). The same ASC arrangement that violates STARK also violates the AKS, and a single OIG Self-Disclosure Protocol filing would address both violations. Exposure included in the $41.9M STARK/AKS calculation above. **See** Section IV.A (STARK Law and Anti-Kickback Statute Compliance) for detailed analysis.

**5. Probably No.** Because automatic assignment of Medicare provider agreements under 42 C.F.R. Rejection of assignment would constitute voluntary termination requiring 3-12 month re-certification with $216M-$864M revenue loss exposure, but rational acquirers always accept assignment. The primary risk is timely Form 855A submission to avoid payment suspension ($72M/month). Total weighted exposure: $73M (including readmission penalties of $6.9M/year and payer contract renegotiation risks). **See** Section IV.I (Medicare Provider Agreements and Reimbursement) for detailed analysis.
