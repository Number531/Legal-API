## I. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This report analyzes Medicare provider agreement assignment requirements, Conditions of Participation (CoPs) compliance, and Medicare payment program implications for National Healthcare Partners LLC's proposed $2.4 billion acquisition of Mercy Regional Health System's four acute care hospitals. The transaction involves conversion from 501(c)(3) nonprofit to for-profit ownership, requiring careful navigation of Medicare change of ownership (CHOW) regulations to preserve $864 million in annual Medicare revenue.

### Key Findings and Risk Assessment

**CRITICAL FINDING: Automatic Assignment is Mandatory to Avoid Deal-Blocking Revenue Gap**

Under 42 CFR ¬ß 489.18(c), Mercy's four Medicare provider agreements (CCN 360001, 360285, 360312, 360198) are automatically assigned to National Healthcare Partners unless the buyer **affirmatively rejects** assignment. Rejection constitutes voluntary termination, requiring the facilities to undergo complete Medicare re-certification as initial applicants, creating a 3-12 month payment gap with **$216M-$864M revenue loss** exposure. - Existing CCN numbers and provider agreement continuity
- $27M annual Graduate Medical Education (GME) payments (6 ACGME programs, 180 residents)
- $8.5M annual Disproportionate Share Hospital (DSH) payments (Mercy South)
- Joint Commission deemed status (exemption from routine CMS surveys)
- Medicare Advantage and Medicaid managed care contract continuity

42 CFR ¬ß 424.516(e) requires submission of CMS Form 855A to Medicare Administrative Contractor J15 (CGS Administrators) **within 30 days** of closing for all four hospitals. ## IV. DETAILED ANALYSIS

### A. Medicare Provider Agreement Assignment and Change of Ownership Requirements

#### 1. Legal Framework for Assignment Under 42 CFR ¬ß 489.18

**Automatic Assignment Rule:**
Under 42 CFR ¬ß 489.18(c), when a change of ownership occurs, the existing Medicare provider agreement is **automatically assigned** to the new owner unless the new owner affirmatively **rejects assignment** before the transfer date [VERIFIED: 42 CFR ¬ß 489.18(c)].¬π

The assigned agreement remains subject to "all applicable statutes and regulations and to the terms and conditions under which [it was] originally issued," including:
- Compliance with ownership and financial interest disclosure requirements (42 CFR ¬ß 420.200 et seq.)
- Civil rights compliance requirements (45 CFR Parts 80, 84, and 90)
- All Conditions of Participation under 42 CFR Part 482¬≤

**Change of Ownership Defined:**
42 CFR ¬ß 489.18(a) defines "change of ownership" to include:
- Transfer of title and control of the provider entity (stock sale or asset sale)
- Lease arrangements where operational control transfers to lessee
- In partnerships, the removal, addition, or substitution of a partner (unless partners expressly agree otherwise under applicable state law)¬≥

For **Mercy Regional Health System's transaction** (nonprofit-to-for-profit conversion via stock purchase by National Healthcare Partners LLC), this constitutes a change of ownership requiring notification to CMS within specified timelines [METHODOLOGY: Regulatory interpretation applied to transaction facts].

#### 2. CMS Notification Requirements and Timeline - Form 855A

**30-Day Notification Requirement (CRITICAL):**
Changes of ownership must be reported to CMS **within 30 days** of the effective date of the change per 42 CFR ¬ß 424.516(e) [VERIFIED: 42 CFR ¬ß 424.516(e)].‚Å¥ This is a **stricter timeline** than the standard 90-day reporting requirement for other enrollment changes.

**Form CMS-855A Submission Process:**
- **Old Owner's Obligation:** Must submit completed CMS-855A notifying CMS of the CHOW, including:
  - Effective date of ownership transfer
  - New owner's identifying information
  - Sales/lease agreement documentation (must be received within **90 days** after application receipt or application will be rejected)‚Åµ

- **New Owner's Obligation:** Must submit CMS-855A within **14 days** of receipt of old owner's application indicating acceptance or rejection of automatic assignment‚Å∂

**Submission Method:**
CMS encourages use of **Provider Enrollment, Chain, and Ownership System (PECOS)** for electronic submission. PECOS applications process faster than paper applications (typically 60-90 days for fully completed applications, though CHOW determinations may take additional 6-9 months).‚Å∑

**Medicare Billing Consequences During Transition:**
If new owner fails to (1) submit Form CMS-855A and (2) indicate acceptance of assignment within **30 calendar days** after contractor contact, the contractor shall **stop payments** [VERIFIED: CMS Manual guidance].‚Å∏

**APPLICATION TO MERCY TRANSACTION:**

42 CFR Part 482 establishes the health and safety requirements that hospitals must meet to participate in Medicare and Medicaid programs [VERIFIED: 42 CFR Part 482].¬π¬≤ Section 1866 of the Social Security Act (42 U.S.C. ¬ß 1395cc) authorizes CMS to establish these conditions and enforce compliance through provider agreement provisions.

**Structure of 42 CFR Part 482:**
The regulations cover multiple subparts including:
- **Subpart A:** General requirements (¬ß¬ß 482.1-482.15)
- **Subpart B:** Administration (¬ß¬ß 482.21-482.23)
- **Subpart C:** Basic hospital functions (¬ß¬ß 482.24-482.30)
  - ¬ß 482.24: Emergency services (EMTALA)
  - ¬ß 482.22: Medical staff
  - ¬ß 482.25-482.28: Nursing, pharmaceutical, diagnostic services
- **Subpart D-E:** Optional hospital services and specialized facilities

**Compliance Verification:**
Hospitals demonstrate CoPs compliance through either:
1. **Direct CMS Surveys:** State survey agencies conduct on-site inspections (triennial + complaint-driven)
2. **Deemed Status:** Accreditation by CMS-approved organization (e.g., Joint Commission) deems compliance per 42 CFR ¬ß 488.5¬π¬≥

#### 2. Graduate Medical Education (GME) Payments

**Direct Graduate Medical Education (DGME):**
Per 42 U.S.C. ¬ß 1395ww(h) and 42 CFR ¬ß 413.75 et seq., Medicare pays teaching hospitals for the direct costs of graduate medical education, including resident salaries, faculty salaries, and overhead costs allocated to training activities [VERIFIED: 42 CFR ¬ß 413.75].¬≤‚Å∏

**Indirect Medical Education (IME):**
Per 42 U.S.C. ¬ß 1395ww(d)(5)(B) and 42 CFR ¬ß 412.105, teaching hospitals receive an additional payment adjustment to IPPS payments reflecting higher patient care costs due to teaching activities [VERIFIED: 42 CFR ¬ß 412.105].¬≤‚Åπ

**IME Payment Calculation Formula:**
IME adjustment = **c √ó [(1 + r)^0.405 - 1]** where:
- **c** = multiplier set by Congress (currently **1.35** for FY 2003 and thereafter)
- **r** = ratio of residents to beds¬≥‚Å∞

The multiplier of 1.35 represents a **5.5% increase in IME payment for every 10% increase in the resident-to-bed ratio**.

**Resident FTE Cap (Critical Constraint):**
Section 1886(h)(4)(F) of the Social Security Act established limits on FTE residents hospitals may count for DGME/IME payments, typically capped at the hospital's FTE count for its most recent cost reporting period ending on or before **December 31, 1996** (Balanced Budget Act of 1997 cap) [VERIFIED: 42 U.S.C. ¬ß 1395ww(h)(4)(F)].¬≥¬π

**Recent Cap Expansion:**
Section 126 of the Consolidated Appropriations Act (CAA), 2021, provides **1,000 additional FTE resident cap slots** phased in at no more than 200 slots per year beginning FY 2023.¬≥¬≤

#### 3. Disproportionate Share Hospital (DSH) Payments

**Legal Authority:**

**DSH Patient Percentage Calculation:**
DSH patient percentage = **Medicare fraction + Medicaid fraction** where:¬≥‚Åµ

**Medicare Fraction** = (Medicare Part A + SSI patient days) √∑ (Total Medicare Part A patient days)

**Medicaid Fraction** = (Medicaid-only patient days, excluding Medicare Part A) √∑ (Total patient days)

**Eligibility Threshold:**
Hospitals with DSH patient percentage **exceeding 15%** are eligible for DSH payment adjustment.¬≥‚Å∂

**Payment Reduction (Affordable Care Act):**

### F. Provider Agreement Termination and Appeal Rights

#### 1. Grounds for Termination Under 42 CFR ¬ß 489.53

**Statutory Authority:**
CMS may terminate a Medicare provider agreement if it finds the provider "is not complying with the provisions of title XVIII and the applicable regulations of this chapter or with the provisions of the agreement" per 42 CFR ¬ß 489.53(a) [VERIFIED: 42 CFR ¬ß 489.53(a)].‚Åµ‚Å¥

**Specific Termination Grounds Include:**
- Failure to comply with Conditions of Participation (42 CFR Part 482)
- Failure to grant immediate access to state survey agency for CoPs inspections
- EMTALA violations posing immediate jeopardy (42 U.S.C. ¬ß 1395dd)
- Failure to permit examination of fiscal records by CMS
- Failure to disclose ownership information (42 CFR ¬ß 420.200 et seq.)
- Civil rights compliance failures (45 CFR Parts 80, 84, 90)
- Medicare enrollment revocation (discussed below)‚Åµ‚Åµ

**Notice Requirements:**
CMS must provide **15 days' notice** before effective date of termination, **except** for immediate jeopardy situations where shorter notice applies [VERIFIED: 42 CFR ¬ß 489.53(d)].‚Åµ‚Å∂

#### 2. Medicare Enrollment Revocation - 42 CFR ¬ß 424.535

**Relationship to Provider Agreements:**
Per 42 CFR ¬ß 489.53(a)(4)(i), if a provider's **enrollment in the Medicare program is revoked**, CMS may terminate the provider agreement [VERIFIED: Cross-reference ¬ß 489.53(a)(4)(i) and ¬ß 424.535].‚Åµ‚Å∑

**Enrollment Revocation Grounds (42 CFR ¬ß 424.535):**
CMS or Medicare Administrative Contractor may revoke Medicare billing privileges for:‚Åµ‚Å∏
- **Exclusion from federal health care programs** (provider, owner, managing employee, or delegated official)
- **Felony convictions** (federal or state, within preceding 10 years)
- **False or misleading information** on enrollment application
- **Failure to comply with enrollment requirements** (reporting changes, revalidation)
- **Abuse of billing privileges** (claims for services not furnished, pattern of improper billing)
- **Failure to repay Medicare overpayments**
- **Revocation or termination from State Medicaid or other federal health care program**

**Re-enrollment Bar Period:**
If billing privileges revoked, provider is **barred from re-enrollment for 1-3 years** depending on severity of violation [VERIFIED: 42 CFR ¬ß 424.535(c)].‚Åµ‚Åπ

**APPLICATION TO MERCY TRANSACTION:**

- Medicare overpayment monitoring and timely repayment (within 60 days of identification)

#### 3. Appeal Rights - 42 CFR Part 498

**Provider Agreement Termination Appeals:**
Any provider dissatisfied with termination of its provider agreement is entitled to a **hearing before an ALJ** (Administrative Law Judge) per 42 CFR ¬ß 498.5(a) [VERIFIED: 42 CFR ¬ß 498.5(a)].‚Å∂‚Å∞

**Appeal Process:**
1. **Request for ALJ Hearing:** File within **60 days** of receipt of termination notice
2. **ALJ Decision Timeline:** ALJ must issue decision within **180 days** of appeal filing for enrollment matters (¬ß 498.79)‚Å∂¬π
3. **Departmental Appeals Board Review:** If dissatisfied with ALJ decision, request DAB review
4. **Judicial Review:** Right to seek judicial review of DAB decision in U.S. District Court

**ALJ Scope of Review:**
ALJ may consider **new issues** even if not addressed in CMS initial determination, but **cannot consider issues arising on or after effective date of termination** [VERIFIED: 42 CFR ¬ß 498.60].‚Å∂¬≤

**Interim Participation During Appeal:**
Provider **may continue Medicare participation** during appeal process **unless** CMS determines immediate jeopardy to health/safety exists (EMTALA violations, immediate jeopardy CoPs findings).
