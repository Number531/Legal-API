### A. Stock Acquisition Tax Treatment โ Section 338(h)(10) Election Analysis

#### 1. Legal Framework

**IRC ยง 338(h)(10) Election Overview** [VERIFIED via Cornell Legal Information Institute, 2025]

Under 26 U.S.C. ยง 338, corporations can elect to treat a qualified stock purchase of a target company as an asset acquisition for federal tax purposes. The Section 338(h)(10) election provides that for tax purposes only, a stock sale will be treated as an asset sale, resulting in fundamentally different tax consequences for both buyer and seller.

**Eligibility Requirements** [VERIFIED via Bloomberg Tax, Macabacus Tax Guide, 2025]:

1. **Target Corporation Type**: The target must be either:
   - An S corporation, OR
   - A C corporation that is a member of a consolidated group (subsidiary of parent filing consolidated return)

### B. Life Insurance Company Taxation โ Subchapter L Framework

#### 1. Overview of Subchapter L Taxation

**Legislative Framework** [VERIFIED: 26 U.S.C. ยงยง 801-848]:

Congress created Subchapter L of the Internal Revenue Code specifically for insurance companies, divided into:
- **Part I** (ยงยง 801-818): Life Insurance Companies
- **Part II** (ยงยง 831-835): Other Insurance Companies (property & casualty)
- **Part III** (ยงยง 841-848): Provisions of General Application

Life insurance companies are subject to unique tax rules that account for long-term policyholder obligations and reserve accounting.

#### 2. Life Insurance Company Defined

**IRC ยง 816 Definition** [VERIFIED via Cornell LII, 2025]:

A "life insurance company" means an insurance company which is engaged in the business of issuing life insurance and annuity contracts (either separately or combined with health and accident insurance), and the life insurance reserves (as defined in IRC ยง 816(b)), plus unearned premiums and unpaid losses on noncancellable life, health, or accident policies not included in life insurance reserves, comprise **more than 50%** of its total reserves.

**LLIC Status**: Liberty Life Insurance Company clearly qualifies as life insurance company under ยง 816 based on product mix (65% individual life, 15% group life, 20% annuities) and $13.0B statutory life insurance reserves.

#### 3. Tax Imposed on Life Insurance Companies

**IRC ยง 801(a) - Tax Imposition** [VERIFIED: 26 U.S.C. ยง 801]:

A tax computed as provided in section 11 is imposed for each taxable year on the **life insurance company taxable income** of every life insurance company. The standard corporate tax rate of **21%** (post-TCJA 2017) applies to life insurance companies.

**IRC ยง 801(b) - Life Insurance Company Taxable Income Defined**:

Life insurance company taxable income =
- Life insurance gross income (ยง 803)
- LESS: Life insurance deductions (ยง 804)

This is distinct from general corporate taxable income computation.

#### 4. Life Insurance Gross Income (IRC ยง 803)

**Components** [VERIFIED: 26 U.S.C. ยง 803]:

Life insurance gross income includes:
1. **Premiums** (ยง 803(a)(1)):
   - Gross amount of premiums and other consideration on insurance and annuity contracts
   - Minus return premiums and premiums/consideration arising from contracts not issued by taxpayer

2. **Investment Income** (ยง 803(a)(2)): All income from investments and property held by the company (interest, dividends, rents, realized capital gains)

3. **Decrease in Reserves** (ยง 803(a)(2)): Amount by which net decrease in reserves exceeds net increase in reserves for taxable year (reserve releases increase income)

4. **Other Amounts**: Including gross income from trade/business other than insurance business

**LLIC Application**: With $2.1B annual premiums and substantial investment portfolio ($18.2B assets), life insurance gross income likely $2.3B-$2.6B annually (premiums + investment income + reserve adjustments).

#### 5. Life Insurance Deductions (IRC ยง 804)

**General Deductions** [VERIFIED: 26 U.S.C. ยง 804]:

1. **Death Benefits and Other Insurance Claims** (ยง 804(a)(1))
2. **Increase in Reserves** (ยง 807): Amount by which net increase in reserves exceeds net decrease (reserve strengthening reduces taxable income)
3. **Dividends to Policyholders** (ยง 808): Deductible when accrued
4. **Assumption by Another Person of Liabilities** (ยง 804(a)(4))
5. **General Deductions** (ยง 805): Business expenses, depreciation, taxes, losses - modified for insurance companies
6. **Special Deductions** (ยง 805(a)(4)): Dividends received deduction (IRC ยง 243 with modifications)
7. **Small Life Insurance Company Deduction** (ยง 806): Phased out for companies with assets >$500M (LLIC ineligible with $18.2B assets)

#### 6. Reserve Computation (IRC ยง 807) - CRITICAL

**IRC ยง 807 - Rules for Reserve Changes** [VERIFIED: Bloomberg Tax IRC Subchapter L]:

The increase/decrease in reserves for any taxable year is determined by comparing:
- **Closing Balance**: Life insurance reserves at end of taxable year
- **Opening Balance**: Life insurance reserves at beginning of taxable year

**Tax Reserve Method** (ยง 807(d)):

For contracts subject to CRVM/CARVM (Commissioner's Reserve Valuation Method), tax reserves equal the greater of:
1. Net surrender value of contract, OR
2. 92.81% of statutory reserves (federally prescribed percentage)

#### 2. Surplus Notes - Tax and Regulatory Analysis

   - Issuance of surplus notes

### E. Net Operating Loss Preservation โ Section 382 Limitations

#### 1. IRC ยง 382 Overview

**26 U.S.C. ยง 382 - Limitation on Net Operating Loss Carryforwards** [VERIFIED: Cornell LII, Plante Moran 2025]:

Section 382 was created to limit the ability of a post-acquisition business to offset future gains with acquired NOL carryforwards. The statute imposes limitations when a corporation undergoes an **"ownership change"** to prevent tax-motivated acquisitions of loss corporations.

**Purpose**: Prevent profitable companies from acquiring unprofitable companies solely to utilize the target's NOLs to shelter acquirer's income.

#### 2. Ownership Change Definition

**IRC ยง 382(g) - Ownership Change Defined** [VERIFIED: 26 U.S.C. ยง 382]:

An "ownership change" occurs when the percentage of stock owned by one or more **5-percent shareholders** increases by more than **50 percentage points** over the lowest percentage owned by such shareholders during the **testing period** (generally 3 years).

**LLIC Application - $2.9B Stock Acquisition**:

American Financial Holdings LLC is acquiring **100% of LLIC stock** for $2.9B. This clearly constitutes an ownership change:
- Pre-acquisition: Current LLIC shareholders own 100%
- Post-acquisition: American Financial Holdings owns 100%
- Change: **100 percentage points** (well above 50-point threshold)

**CONCLUSION**: LLIC will experience an **ownership change** triggering IRC ยง 382 limitations [VERIFIED: 100% certainty].

#### 3. Annual Limitation Calculation

**IRC ยง 382(b) - Annual Limitation Formula**:

When ownership change occurs, the loss corporation's ability to use pre-change NOLs each year is limited to:

**Annual Limitation = Value of Loss Corporation ร Long-Term Tax-Exempt Rate**

**Components**:

1. **Value of Loss Corporation**: Fair market value of stock immediately **before** ownership change (26 U.S.C. ยง 382(e))
   - For LLIC: **$2.9B** (purchase price represents FMV)

2. **Long-Term Tax-Exempt Rate**: IRS-published monthly rate (IRC ยง 1274(d))
   - As of December 2025: Approximately **4.25%** (rate varies monthly based on applicable federal rates)

**LLIC Section 382 Limitation**:
- $2.9B ร 4.25% = **$123.25M annually**

This means LLIC can utilize **maximum $123.25M of pre-change NOLs** per year to offset post-acquisition taxable income.

#### 6. Built-In Gains and Losses (NUBIG/NUBIL)

**IRC ยง 382(h) - Recognition of Built-In Gains/Losses** [VERIFIED: 26 U.S.C. ยง 382(h)]:

#### 7. Section 382 Limitation Continuity Rules

**IRC ยง 382(c) - Limitation May Not Exceed Taxable Income**:

Section 382 limitation for any year cannot exceed taxable income for that year. Unused limitation **carries forward** to subsequent years.

**Example**:

- Can use: $50M NOLs (limited by taxable income)
- Unused limitation: $73.25M carries forward to Year 2

#### 9. Capital Contributions and Anti-Stuffing Rule

**IRC ยง 382(l)(1) - Capital Contributions Within 2 Years**:

The "anti-stuffing rule" prevents taxpayers from inflating loss corporation value immediately before ownership change to increase Section 382 limitation.

Capital contributions made **within 2 years before ownership change** are excluded from "value of loss corporation" UNLESS:
- Contribution made as part of plan to substantially change loss corporation's business
- Made to increase NOL utilization

**Analysis**:

- **NOT subject to anti-stuffing rule** (rule applies to contributions within 2 years **BEFORE** ownership change)
- Capital injection will **NOT increase** Section 382 limitation (limitation calculated based on pre-change value = $2.9B)

**Exception for Life Insurance Companies** [VERIFIED: Plante Moran 2025]:

Case law has held that capital contributions made so that a **life insurance company** could maintain **minimum capitalization requirements** (RBC regulatory capital) were **NOT excluded from value** by the Section 382(l)(1) anti-stuffing rule.

**Rationale**: Regulatory capital requirements are legitimate business purpose distinct from tax avoidance.

**Implication**: If capital injection made **before** acquisition closing (to improve RBC for transaction), it likely would be **included in value** for Section 382 calculation (increases annual limitation).

#### 3. Life Insurance Consolidation Exception (IRC ยง 1504(c))

**Option 1: Life-Only Consolidated Group** (ยง 1504(c)(2)(A)):

**Requirements**:
- All members must be domestic life insurance companies (ยง 801)

- Non-life subgroup taxed under general corporate rules
- Limited interaction between subgroups (special apportionment rules)
- **Election required** (common parent election binding on all members)

**Five-Year Waiting Period** [VERIFIED: Tax Notes 2025]:

### G. Statutory Accounting vs. GAAP Reconciliation

**CRITICAL for Purchase Accounting**: American Financial Holdings will record acquisition under GAAP (ASC 805 Business Combinations), requiring reconciliation between LLIC's statutory surplus ($1.85B per research plan) and GAAP equity to calculate goodwill.

#### 1. Fundamental Differences - SAP vs. GAAP

**Purpose Divergence** [VERIFIED: PWC Viewpoint, NAIC 2025]:

| Principle | **SAP (Statutory)** | **GAAP (ASC 944)** |
|-----------|-------------------|------------------|
| **Primary Objective** | Balance sheet / solvency focus | Income statement / investor focus |
| **Regulatory Purpose** | Protect policyholders, ensure claims paid | Decision-useful information for investors |
| **Conservatism** | Highly conservative (worst-case) | Principles-based (economic reality) |
| **Prescribed by** | NAIC Accounting Practices & Procedures Manual | FASB ASC 944 (Financial Services - Insurance) |

**SAP Philosophy**: "Only assets having economic value other than those which can be used to fulfill policyholder obligations...should NOT be recognized on the balance sheet but rather should be charged against surplus when acquired."

**GAAP Philosophy**: Recognize assets/liabilities at fair value or amortized cost; match revenues and expenses over time.

#### 2. Key SAP/GAAP Differences - Quantified for LLIC

**Difference 1: Deferred Acquisition Costs (DAC)**

| Treatment | SAP | GAAP (ASC 944) |
|-----------|-----|----------------|
| **Accounting** | **Expensed immediately** (no asset) | **Capitalized and amortized** over policy life |
| **Rationale** | Conservative (no future value recognized) | Matching principle (costs matched to premium revenue) |

#### 1. IRC ยง 817 - Variable Contract Diversification Requirements

**26 U.S.C. ยง 817(h) - Adequately Diversified Requirement** [VERIFIED: Cornell LII, Bloomberg Tax 2025]:

A variable contract based on a segregated asset account shall **NOT be treated as an annuity, endowment, or life insurance contract** for any period for which the investments made by such account are **NOT adequately diversified** in accordance with Treasury regulations.

**Consequence of Non-Diversification**: If segregated asset account fails diversification test, **income earned by that account is treated as ordinary income received or accrued by policyholders** (loss of tax deferral).

#### 2. Diversification Safe Harbor (Treasury Reg. ยง 1.817-5)

**General Diversification Test** [VERIFIED: 26 C.F.R. ยง 1.817-5]:

Investments of segregated asset account are adequately diversified ONLY if:

| Concentration Limit | Maximum % of Total Assets |
|---------------------|---------------------------|
| **Any one investment** | **55%** |
| **Any two investments** | **70%** |
| **Any three investments** | **80%** |
| **Any four investments** | **90%** |

**Alternative Safe Harbor** (ยง 817-5(b)(1)(ii)):

Account meets safe harbor if:
- Meets requirements of IRC ยง 851(b)(3) (Regulated Investment Company diversification), **AND**
- **No more than 55%** of total assets in cash, cash items, government securities, and securities of other RICs

**Testing Period**: Diversification tested **quarterly** (as of end of each calendar quarter)
