#### 2. Jones Act Crew Non-Compliance - Catastrophic Tail Risk with Simple Cure

**Critical Finding:** 2 of PMSC's 6 Jones Act vessels operate with **73% U.S. citizen crew**, violating the statutory 75% minimum threshold (46 U.S.C. § 55102, 46 U.S.C. § 8103). The Jones Act mandates that master, all officers, and **at least 75% of unlicensed crew** must be U.S. citizens (not more than 25% may be aliens lawfully admitted for permanent residence).

**Penalty Structure (46 U.S.C. § 55102(b)):**
- **$1,100 per ton of merchandise** transported in violation
- **Vessel forfeiture** to U.S. Government (extreme remedy, rarely invoked)
- Penalty applies **per voyage**

**Quantified Exposure (Example: 36,000 DWT bulk carrier):**
- **Penalty per voyage:** 36,000 tons × $1,100/ton = **$39,600,000**
- **Annual exposure (12 voyages/year):** $475,200,000
- **Enforcement probability:** 5-10% (Low-Medium) - Coast Guard audits infrequent, typically triggered by casualties, whistleblowers, or competitor complaints

**Cure Strategy - De Minimis Cost:**
- **Hire 2 additional U.S. crew per vessel** via Seafarers International Union (SIU) hiring halls (Norfolk, Houston, Honolulu)
- **Cost:** $155,000 annually per vessel ($310,000 for both vessels)
- **Timeline:** 60-90 days from SIU contact to vessel assignment
- **Immediate action required:** Contact SIU within 14 days of closing to ensure cure before potential Coast Guard audit

**Recent Legislative Change - Enhanced Transparency:** Coast Guard Authorization Act of 2024 amended 46 U.S.C. § 55102 to require public disclosure of Jones Act penalties in the Customs Bulletin and Decisions within 14 days of issuance, including vessel name, owner, fine amount, and misconduct summary. No published penalties exist for PMSC vessels as of January 10, 2026, indicating either no audits occurred or compliance was cured pre-inspection.

**Recommendation:** **IMMEDIATE PRIORITY 1 ACTION.** Contact SIU hiring halls within 14 days of closing. Escrow $10M-$20M pending cure completion (90-day timeline). The $310K annual cure cost is de minimis relative to $39.6M per-voyage penalty risk, justifying immediate corrective action.

### B. JONES ACT CABOTAGE RESTRICTIONS

**Statutory Requirement:** 46 U.S.C. § 55102 mandates that vessels engaged in coastwise trade must be:

### E. IMO ENVIRONMENTAL COMPLIANCE (MARPOL, CII, EU ETS)

#### 1. IMO Carbon Intensity Indicator (CII) - MEPC.328(76) Framework

**Regulatory Basis:** IMO adopted CII regulations through **MARPOL Annex VI amendments** via **MEPC Resolution 328(76)** (June 17, 2021), entering into force January 1, 2023. [Source: IMO - EEXI and CII FAQ](https://www.imo.org/en/mediacentre/hottopics/pages/eexi-cii-faq.aspx)

**CII Rating System:** Vessels rated annually on fuel efficiency (grams CO₂ per cargo capacity-nautical mile):
- **A:** Major superior performance
- **B:** Minor superior performance
- **C:** Moderate performance (baseline compliance)
- **D:** Minor inferior performance (**PMSC has 2 D-rated bulk carriers**)
- **E:** Inferior performance

**Corrective Action Requirement (MEPC.338(76) Guidelines):**

A ship rated **D for three consecutive years, or E for one year**, must submit a **corrective action plan** to the flag state Administration showing how the required index of **C or above** will be achieved. [Source: Britannia P&I - CII Regulatory Overview](https://britanniapandi.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/Regulatory-Overview-of-Carbon-Intensity-Indicator-CII-and-Energy-Efficiency-Existing-Ship-Index-EEXI-June-2025.pdf)

**Ship Energy Efficiency Management Plan (SEEMP) Part III:**
The corrective action plan forms **SEEMP Part III** and must be submitted to and approved by the Administration (flag state or Recognized Organization) **before the Data Collection System (DCS) Statement of Compliance** for that year can be issued.

**Plan Contents:**
- Analysis of why required CII was not achieved
- Identification of operational inefficiencies
- Proposed corrective measures (speed reduction, route optimization, hull cleaning, propeller polishing, waste heat recovery systems)
- Revised implementation plan with timeline

[Source: Sustainable Ships - CII Regulations](https://www.sustainable-ships.org/rules-regulations/cii)

#### 3. Corrective Action Options and Costs

**Option A: Speed Reduction (Most Common)**
- Reduce vessel speed by **5-10%** to lower fuel consumption
- **Revenue impact:** Reduced voyage frequency = $6M-$12M annual revenue loss per vessel (fewer cargoes carried)
- **CII improvement:** Speed reduction of 10% can improve CII rating by **1-2 grades** (D to C, or D to B)
- **Cost:** $12M annually for 2 vessels

**Option B: Hull and Propeller Optimization**
- **Hull cleaning and coating:** $150K-$300K per vessel
- **Propeller polishing:** $80K-$150K per vessel
- **CII improvement:** 3-5% fuel efficiency gain = **potential to move from D to C**
- **Total cost:** $460K-$900K (one-time, plus recurring every 18-24 months)

**Option C: Energy-Saving Devices (ESDs)**
- Install **pre-swirl ducts, rudder bulbs, air lubrication systems**
- **Capital cost:** $500K-$2M per vessel
- **CII improvement:** 5-10% fuel efficiency gain = **likely move from D to C or B**
- **Payback period:** 3-5 years (fuel savings offset capital cost)

**Option D: Scrapping and Replacement**
- **Scrap value:** $23M-$37M per vessel (based on lightweight tonnage, current scrap prices ~$450-$550/LDT)
- **New vessel cost:** $60M-$85M per modern eco-design bulk carrier
- **Net investment:** $37M-$48M per vessel after scrap credit
- **CII improvement:** New vessels designed for **A or B ratings**
- **Recommendation:** Only if vessels are >20 years old AND require major capital repairs (like M/V Pacific Titan)

**Option E: Operational Efficiency Measures (No Capital Cost)**
- **Weather routing optimization:** Use advanced meteorological data to minimize fuel consumption
- **Just-in-time arrival:** Coordinate with port to reduce anchorage waiting time
- **Trim optimization:** Adjust ballast to optimize hull resistance
- **CII improvement:** 2-4% fuel efficiency gain = **may move from low-D to high-D or low-C**

## VI. CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS

### A. Summary of Conclusions

1. **FMC Detention/Demurrage Compliance:** PMSC's 47 D&D complaints represent **manageable settlement exposure** ($120K-$500K range) following D.C. Circuit's December 2025 partial vacatur of FMC's "properly issued invoices" provision. COVID-era billing disputes are industry-wide phenomena, not unique to PMSC. **Settlement negotiations should commence immediately** to resolve prior to acquisition closing.

2. **Jones Act Crew Non-Compliance is the Highest Regulatory Priority:** 2 vessels operating at **73% U.S. crew** (below 75% threshold) face theoretical **$39.6M per voyage penalty** if Coast Guard audits occur. While audit probability is low (5-10%), the **catastrophic downside** justifies **immediate corrective action**. Cost to cure ($310K annually for 2 vessels) is de minimis relative to penalty risk. **Hiring must commence within 14 days of closing** to ensure 90-day cure timeline.

3. **Offshore Wind Vessels Require Immediate Strategic Decision:** 2 COSCO-built CTVs are **permanently ineligible** for Jones Act trade. PMSC faces binary choice:
   - **Cancel COSCO contracts:** $5M-$8M termination cost + forfeit $35M strategic opportunity

   - **Recommendation:** Re-order from U.S. shipyard to capture **$500M-$1B annual offshore wind charter market** (30 GW by 2030 target). NPV analysis favors U.S. construction despite higher upfront cost.

4. **TWIC Reader Non-Compliance is a Non-Issue:** Seattle Terminal's alleged "December 30, 2025 deadline" **does not exist** in current Coast Guard regulations. Actual deadline is **May 8, 2029** for Risk Group A facilities, and **likely inapplicable** to container terminals handling general cargo (not Certain Dangerous Cargoes in bulk). **No penalty exposure identified.**

5. **M/V Pacific Titan Hull Corrosion Signals Fleet-Wide Maintenance Risk:** $6.65M net drydock cost overage indicates potential **deferred maintenance** across aging fleet. Prudent due diligence requires:
   - **Obtain drydock inspection reports** for all 22 vessels (last 3 years)

   - **Increase annual drydock budget** from current (unknown) level to **$11M-$17.6M annually** (22 vessels ÷ 5-year cycle × $2.5M-$4M per vessel)

   - **Speed reduction 5-10%:** $12M annual revenue loss (most effective CII improvement)
