### B. NAIC ACTUARIAL GUIDELINE 48 (AG48) COMPLIANCE ANALYSIS

#### 1. AG48 Framework and Purpose

**NAIC Actuarial Guideline XLVIII (AG48)** was adopted by the NAIC Executive Committee and Plenary in December 2014 and became effective January 1, 2015.¬π¬≤ AG48 defines the rules for new XXX and AXXX reserve financing transactions executed after the effective date, addressing regulatory concerns about captive reinsurance arrangements used for term life insurance with level premiums (XXX) and universal life insurance policies with secondary guarantees (AXXX).

**Historical Context - "Shadow Insurance" Controversy:**

Prior to AG48, life insurers increasingly turned to captive reinsurers to finance redundant statutory reserves on term and universal life products when reserves were viewed as excessive relative to underlying economic risk.¬π¬≥ This practice, termed "**shadow insurance**" by the New York State Department of Financial Services in 2013, depicted arrangements whereby insurers shifted blocks of insurance policy claims to special out-of-state (or out-of-country) shell companies, or captives, which carriers then used to reinsure existing policy claims‚Äîan arrangement DFS called a "complex shell game" that enables carriers to divert policy reserves to other purposes while the parent company remains "on the hook" through parental guarantee.¬π‚Å¥

A 2013 New York State DFS report revealed that New York-based insurers were held accountable for at least **$48 billion in hidden 'shadow insurance' transactions** through captive and reinsurance entities.¬π‚Åµ Regulators warned that failing to regulate shadow insurance could short-change tax-payers and, in a worst case scenario, place the entire economy at risk from cascading failures.¬π‚Å∂

**AG48 Response:**

AG48 established a consistent method for calculating the **economic reserve** (plus a margin for conservatism) and the level of "**primary security**" (i.e., higher quality assets), even when held at a captive reinsurer.¬π‚Å∑ The remainder of the statutory reserve is allowed to be backed by a wider variety of assets, referred to as "**other security**," and the ceding company's appointed actuary is required to analyze transactions to determine whether the required amount of high-quality assets are being held to back the reserve and whether the remainder of the reserve is sufficiently backed by other types of assets.¬π‚Å∏

#### 2. AG48 Key Definitions and Requirements

**Primary Security:**¬π‚Åπ

"Primary Security" refers to high-quality assets that must be held by the reinsurer (including captive reinsurers) to support the **Required Level of Primary Security**. Acceptable Primary Security assets include:
- Cash and cash equivalents
- Investment-grade bonds (NAIC 1-2 designation, equivalent to BBB-/Baa3 or higher)
- U.S. Treasury and agency securities
- Certain loan obligations secured by real property
- Other assets specifically approved by the ceding company's domiciliary commissioner

**Other Security:**¬≤‚Å∞

"Other Security" refers to collateral beyond Primary Security that may be used to support the remaining statutory reserve requirement. Other Security may include:
- **Parental guarantees** (subject to conditions: parent RBC >300%, net worth > guarantee amount, A rating or better from recognized rating agency)
- Letters of credit from qualified U.S. financial institutions
- Assets that do not meet Primary Security quality standards (e.g., below-investment-grade bonds, certain equity securities)
- Other forms of collateral approved by the domiciliary commissioner

**Required Level of Primary Security (Actuarial Method):**¬≤¬π

AG48 specifies an "Actuarial Method" to calculate the Required Level of Primary Security, which represents the **economic reserve layer**‚Äîthe amount of reserves actuarially necessary to support policyholder obligations under reasonable assumptions. The Actuarial Method calculation is designed to identify a reserve level that is sufficiently conservative to provide for the payment of all policyholder claims, but that is less conservative than the currently required gross XXX or AXXX reserving levels.¬≤¬≤

**Appointed Actuary Certification:**¬≤¬≥

The ceding company's appointed actuary must provide an actuarial opinion certifying that:
1. The Required Level of Primary Security has been calculated in accordance with AG48 methodology
2. Primary Security assets held by the reinsurer meet or exceed the Required Level
3. Other Security is sufficient to cover the remaining statutory reserve requirement
4. The total collateral (Primary Security + Other Security) is adequate to support the ceded reserves

If these conditions are not met, the appointed actuary must issue a **qualified opinion**, which may trigger regulatory action by the ceding company's domiciliary insurance department.

- Concurrent stressors: -8 to -13 percentage points (per research plan stress scenarios)

#### 2. Change of Control Consent Requirements

**Standard Reinsurance Treaty Provisions:**‚Å¥‚Åµ

Reinsurance agreements typically include change of control provisions requiring:

1. **Notice to Reinsurer:** Ceding company must notify reinsurer within **30-60 days** of change of control event

4. **Recapture Rights Triggered:** Change of control may activate **early recapture rights** for reinsurer (even if treaty has not reached normal recapture eligibility period)
5. **Renegotiation Rights:** Reinsurer may condition consent on revised terms (increased ceding commission, additional collateral, modified experience refund provisions)

- **20-30% of treaties** include "termination for convenience" clauses allowing reinsurer to terminate without cause (typically 180-365 days notice)

### E. LETTER OF CREDIT (LOC) COLLATERAL STRATEGY

#### 1. LOC Structure and Requirements

**Letter of Credit as Reinsurance Collateral:**‚Åµ¬π

NAIC regulations stipulate that certain reinsurance recoverables due from certified or unauthorized reinsurers require collateral to secure the recoverable amounts, and the collateral requirement is often satisfied through the use of a letter of credit. By some estimates, **three out of every four funded collateral programs use LOCs**.‚Åµ¬≤

**LOC Characteristics for Statutory Reserve Credit:**‚Åµ¬≥

Under the NAIC's Credit for Reinsurance Model Regulation (MO-786), LOCs must meet the following criteria:

1. **Clean:** No documentary requirements for draw beyond certification of amount due
2. **Irrevocable:** Cannot be cancelled or modified without beneficiary consent
3. **Unconditional:** No conditions precedent to draw (other than certification of amount due and presentation of complying documents)
4. **Qualified U.S. Financial Institution:** Issued or confirmed by a bank meeting NAIC QUSFI standards
5. **Evergreen:** Automatically renews annually unless bank provides 30-90 days non-renewal notice

**Qualified U.S. Financial Institution (QUSFI) Standards:**‚Åµ‚Å¥

A QUSFI is defined as an institution that:
- Is organized or licensed under U.S. or state laws
- Is regulated and supervised by U.S. federal or state authorities having regulatory authority over banks and trust companies
- Has been determined by either the commissioner or the NAIC Securities Valuation Office to meet financial soundness standards (typically **AA- or better credit rating** from S&P/Moody's/Fitch)

The NAIC Securities Valuation Office publishes an annual "**List of Qualified U.S. Financial Institutions**" identifying banks approved for issuing reinsurance LOCs.‚Åµ‚Åµ
