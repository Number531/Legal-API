#### E. GMWB Liability & Hedging Analysis (Section IV.E)

**GMWB Rider Mechanics:**

Guaranteed Minimum Withdrawal Benefit (GMWB) riders on variable annuities guarantee annuitants can withdraw specified percentage of initial investment (typically 5-7% annually) for life, **regardless of subaccount performance**. Insurer risks:
- **Market risk**: Equity declines reduce subaccount values while guaranteed withdrawal base remains fixed/grows via roll-up provisions
- **Longevity risk**: Annuitants outliving life expectancy exhaust subaccount but continue receiving guaranteed withdrawals from insurer's general account
- **Interest rate risk**: Prolonged low rates reduce investment income available to fund guarantee obligations

**Liberty Life GMWB Exposure:**

- Separate Account B: $800M variable annuity assets
- Industry average GMWB penetration: 30-40% of VA contracts include living benefit riders
- **Estimated GMWB exposure: $240M-$320M**

**NAIC VM-21 Reserve Requirements:**

VM-21 (Variable Annuity Reserves) under NAIC Valuation Manual establishes principle-based reserves (PBR) for variable annuities with guarantees: stochastic modeling (10,000 economic scenarios - equity returns, interest rates, volatility), C3 Phase II RBC charges (8-12% of reserves = $19M-$38M additional capital), Clearly Defined Hedging Strategy (CDHS) qualification allows reserve smoothing provisions.

**Assuming Liberty Life maintains hedge program:**

#### 1. FINRA Rule 2111 - Three-Part Suitability Obligation

**Regulatory Framework:**

FINRA Rule 2111 imposes a **three-part suitability obligation** on broker-dealers and registered representatives when recommending securities transactions or investment strategies:¬≤‚Å¥

| Suitability Component | Definition | Application to Variable Products |
|----------------------|------------|----------------------------------|
| **Reasonable-Basis Suitability** | Member or associated person must have a reasonable basis to believe that the recommendation is suitable for at least some investors based on reasonable diligence to understand the potential risks/rewards.¬≤‚Åµ | Firm MUST conduct due diligence on all variable annuity and variable life products offered, understanding fee structures, surrender charges, caps/floors, participation rates, and subaccount investment options. |
| **Customer-Specific Suitability** | Member or associated person must have a reasonable basis to believe the recommendation is suitable for the particular customer based on customer's investment profile (age, financial situation, tax status, investment objectives, experience, time horizon, liquidity needs, risk tolerance).¬≤‚Å∂ | Representative MUST document customer-specific analysis showing variable product aligns with customer's age, income, liquid net worth, time horizon, and need for insurance/investment features. |
| **Quantitative Suitability** | Member or associated person with actual or de facto control over a customer account must have a reasonable basis for believing that a series of recommended transactions, even if suitable when viewed in isolation, are not excessive and unsuitable for the customer when taken together.¬≤‚Å∑ | Applies to frequent exchanges between variable annuity contracts (churning) or excessive switching among subaccounts generating transaction charges. |

### E. GMWB LIABILITY & HEDGING ANALYSIS

#### 1. Guaranteed Minimum Withdrawal Benefit (GMWB) Overview

**GMWB Rider Mechanics:**

GMWB riders on variable annuities guarantee annuitants can withdraw a specified percentage of initial investment (typically 5-7% annually) for life, **regardless of subaccount performance**.‚Å∏¬≥ Key risk for insurers:

- **Market risk**: Equity market declines reduce subaccount values while guaranteed withdrawal base remains fixed or grows via roll-up provisions‚Å∏‚Å¥
- **Longevity risk**: Annuitants living longer than expected exhaust subaccount value but continue receiving guaranteed withdrawals from insurer's general account‚Å∏‚Åµ
- **Interest rate risk**: Prolonged low interest rates reduce investment income available to fund guaranteed payments‚Å∏‚Å∂
