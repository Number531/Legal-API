## I. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This comprehensive employment and labor law analysis addresses four critical workforce compliance issues affecting American Power Holdings' (APH) proposed $3.2 billion acquisition of Great Lakes Nuclear Power Company LLC (GLNPC). The research examines: (1) Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification Act (WARN Act) compliance thresholds for plant closings and mass layoffs; (2) Nuclear Regulatory Commission (NRC) technical qualification requirements necessitating retention of key licensed management personnel; (3) ongoing age discrimination litigation (Peterson v. GLNPC) arising from a statistically significant April 2024 reduction-in-force; and (4) access authorization requirements under 10 CFR § 73.56 that impose restrictions on foreign Board members' facility access. Collectively, these issues present financial exposure ranging from $12 million to $58 million and create potential deal-blocking risks if NRC-licensed personnel depart or license transfer technical qualifications cannot be demonstrated.

### Workforce Overview and Transaction Context

GLNPC operates the Great Lakes Nuclear Station in Homer Township, Illinois with **1,850 full-time employees** distributed across four functional departments:

- **Operations/Engineering (750 employees, 41%):** Reactor operations, shift supervision, engineering design, technical support, regulatory compliance. Includes NRC-licensed Senior Reactor Operators (SROs) responsible for directing reactor operations.
- **Maintenance (400 employees, 22%):** Preventive and corrective maintenance, equipment reliability programs, refueling outage support.
- **Security (350 employees, 19%):** Armed security officers, access control, physical protection systems compliance (10 CFR Part 73).
- **Corporate/Administrative (350 employees, 19%):** Executive management, finance, human resources, legal, regulatory affairs.

The workforce includes critical NRC-licensed personnel whose retention is essential for license transfer approval: **Plant Manager Susan Rodriguez** (SRO-licensed) and **Operations Manager Michael Thompson** (SRO-licensed). Additionally, **Chief Nuclear Officer Robert Chen** provides executive oversight and serves as primary interface with NRC Region III.

The acquisition occurs against the backdrop of significant workforce disruption. In April 2024, GLNPC conducted a 42-employee reduction-in-force (RIF) that disproportionately affected older workers, with **83% of terminated employees age 55 or older**. This RIF triggered pending age discrimination litigation with potential class action exposure of $36.75 million to $42 million if all 35 age-protected plaintiffs pursue claims.

### Key Takeaways

- GLNPC is subject to both federal WARN Act (29 U.S.C. §§ 2101-2109) and Illinois WARN Act (820 ILCS 65/1 et seq.)
- Federal threshold: 60-day advance notice required for layoffs affecting ≥50 employees (plant-closing) or ≥500 employees (mass layoff)
- Illinois threshold: **More restrictive** - 60-day advance notice required for layoffs affecting ≥**25 employees** (plant-closing)
- April 2024 RIF (42 employees) did NOT trigger WARN obligations (below 50-employee federal threshold)
- **90-day aggregation rule:** Multiple layoffs within 90 days are aggregated; future APH restructuring must account for this
- **Liability exposure:** $18,000-$20,000 per affected employee × 60 days if WARN notice not provided
- **Example exposure (100-employee post-acquisition RIF):** $1.8M-$2.0M in back pay/benefits plus $30,000 civil penalties

## IV. DETAILED ANALYSIS

### A. WARN Act Compliance (29 U.S.C. §§ 2101-2109)

#### 1. Statutory Framework

The Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification (WARN) Act, codified at 29 U.S.C. §§ 2101-2109, requires employers to provide advance notice of plant closings and mass layoffs.¹ The Act applies to employers with **100 or more employees** (excluding part-time employees), or employers with 100 or more employees who work at least 4,000 hours per week in the aggregate.²

**Key Definitions:**

**Plant Closing:** The permanent or temporary shutdown of a single site of employment if the shutdown results in an **employment loss** for **50 or more employees** (excluding part-time) during any 30-day period.³

**Mass Layoff:** A reduction in force which (1) is not the result of a plant closing, and (2) results in an employment loss during any 30-day period for:
- At least **33 percent of employees** AND at least **50 employees** (excluding part-time); OR
- At least **500 employees** (excluding part-time)⁴

**Employment Loss:** (A) an employment termination (other than discharge for cause, voluntary departure, or retirement), (B) a layoff exceeding 6 months, or (C) a reduction in hours of work of more than 50% during each month of any 6-month period.⁵

#### 2. Notice Requirements

An employer must provide **60 days advance written notice** before ordering a plant closing or mass layoff to:
1. Each representative of affected employees (or if no union, to each affected employee individually); and
2. The State dislocated worker unit and the chief elected official of the unit of local government where the closing/layoff will occur.⁶

**Aggregation Rule:** Employment losses for 2 or more groups at a single site of employment that are each less than the minimum thresholds, but which **in the aggregate exceed those thresholds** and occur within any **90-day period**, are considered a plant closing or mass layoff unless the employer demonstrates the losses are from separate and distinct actions and causes.⁷

**Penalties for Violation:** Back pay and benefits for each day of violation, up to 60 days, for each affected employee. Additionally, up to $500 per day civil penalty for failure to notify local government.⁸

### C. Age Discrimination Litigation - Peterson v. GLNPC

#### 1. ADEA Statutory Framework and Disparate Impact

The Age Discrimination in Employment Act (ADEA), 29 U.S.C. §§ 621-634, prohibits employment discrimination against individuals age 40 or older.²² The Supreme Court in *Smith v. City of Jackson* established that disparate impact claims are cognizable under the ADEA.²³ However, liability is precluded when the adverse impact is attributable to a "reasonable factor other than age" (RFOA).²⁴

**80% Rule (Four-Fifths Rule) for Disparate Impact:**
Derived from the Uniform Guidelines on Employee Selection Procedures (1978), the 80% rule states that a selection rate for a protected group should be at least 80% of the selection rate for the non-protected group.²⁵ If the selection rate falls below 80%, this creates a rebuttable presumption of adverse impact requiring employer justification.

**Formula:** (Selection Rate for Protected Group) / (Selection Rate for Non-Protected Group) ≥ 0.80

#### 2. Peterson v. GLNPC Case Background

**Plaintiff:** Peterson (age 62 at termination, April 2024)
**Claims:** Age discrimination under ADEA (disparate impact and disparate treatment), wrongful termination
**RIF Statistics (April 2024):**
- **Total positions eliminated:** 42 (2.3% of 1,850 workforce)
- **Employees age 55+ terminated:** 35 (83% of RIF)
- **Employees under age 55 terminated:** 7 (17% of RIF)

**Workforce Age Demographics (Estimated):**
- **Employees age 55+:** Approximately 40% of total workforce (740 of 1,850 employees)
- **Employees under age 55:** Approximately 60% of total workforce (1,110 of 1,850 employees)

#### 3. Disparate Impact Analysis - 80% Rule Application

**Selection Rate Calculation:**

| Group | Total Population | Terminated in RIF | Termination Rate | 80% Rule Analysis |
|-------|------------------|-------------------|------------------|-------------------|
| **Age 55+ (Protected)** | 740 | 35 | **4.73%** | Protected group rate |
| **Under Age 55 (Non-Protected)** | 1,110 | 7 | **0.63%** | Non-protected group rate |

**80% Rule Test:**
- Protected group termination rate: 4.73%
- Non-protected group termination rate: 0.63%
- **Ratio:** 4.73% / 0.63% = **7.51 (or 751%)**

**CRITICAL FINDING:** The termination rate for age 55+ employees was **7.5 times higher** than for younger employees. Applying the 80% rule in reverse (protected group should NOT be selected at >125% of non-protected group rate for adverse actions), this **grossly exceeds** allowable disparity thresholds, creating strong statistical evidence of disparate impact.

**Alternative Statistical Analysis:**
- If RIF were age-neutral (proportional to workforce composition):
  - Age 55+: 40% of 42 RIF positions = 16.8 employees expected
  - **Actual:** 35 employees (208% of expected)
- Age 55+ overrepresentation in RIF: **+18.2 employees** beyond proportional share

#### 5. RFOA Defense and Business Justification

GLNPC must demonstrate the April 2024 RIF was based on a "reasonable factor other than age" (RFOA) to avoid liability.²⁹ Potential defenses:
- **Performance-based selection:** If RIF targeted poor performers using objective criteria (performance ratings, disciplinary records), and age 55+ employees disproportionately had lower ratings
- **Skills-based selection:** If RIF eliminated positions requiring outdated skills held predominantly by longer-tenured (older) workers
- **Seniority-based selection:** Paradoxically, if REVERSE seniority used (retain newer employees), may be RFOA if business justification exists

**GLNPC's Challenge:** 83% age 55+ representation in 42-person RIF is **extraordinarily high** and suggests age was a determining factor absent compelling RFOA evidence.

#### 6. Recommendations for Future RIFs

**If APH plans post-acquisition layoffs:**

**Pre-RIF Adverse Impact Analysis:**
1. Calculate workforce age demographics (age 40+, age 50+, age 55+, age 60+)
2. Model proposed RIF selections across age groups
3. Apply 80% rule: Ensure selection rate for each protected age group is ≥80% of non-protected group (or prove RFOA)
4. Document business justification: Performance data, skills assessments, position eliminations tied to business needs

**Selection Criteria:**
1. Use **objective, age-neutral criteria:** Job eliminations, performance ratings, skills assessments, business unit consolidations
2. **Avoid subjective criteria** vulnerable to age bias: "Adaptability," "energy level," "long-term potential," "cultural fit"
3. **Document everything:** Selection criteria, decision-making process, business justification, individual employee assessments

**Legal Review Gate:**
1. Conduct adverse impact analysis **before finalizing RIF selections**
2. If adverse impact exceeds 80% rule threshold, revise selections or document compelling RFOA
3. Retain employment counsel to review RIF plan before implementation

### D. Access Authorization Requirements (10 CFR § 73.56)

#### 1. Statutory and Regulatory Framework

Under 10 CFR § 73.56, nuclear power plant licensees must establish, implement, and maintain an **access authorization program** for granting unescorted access to protected areas and vital areas.³⁰ All individuals requiring unescorted access must undergo comprehensive background investigations to ensure trustworthiness and reliability.³¹

**Protected Areas:** Areas encompassing all controlled areas within the security perimeter
**Vital Areas:** Areas containing vital equipment (reactor control room, spent fuel pool, emergency response facilities, security systems) where sabotage could directly threaten public health/safety³²

#### 2. Background Investigation Requirements

**Mandatory Elements (10 CFR § 73.56(d)):**
1. **FBI Criminal History Check:** Fingerprint-based background check through FBI and state databases
2. **Employment History Verification:** 7-year employment history (or since age 18, whichever is shorter)
3. **Education Verification:** Degree verification for claimed credentials
4. **Credit History Check:** Financial responsibility assessment (identifies coercion vulnerabilities)
5. **Character and Reputation Inquiry:** Interviews with references, former employers, associates
6. **Psychological Evaluation:** Fitness-for-duty assessment by licensed psychologist/psychiatrist
7. **Drug and Alcohol Testing:** Pre-access testing; ongoing random testing (10% quarterly for non-supervisors, 50% annually for ALL personnel)

**Disqualifying Conditions:**
- **Felony convictions** within 7 years (or since age 18)
- **Drug/alcohol abuse** within 5 years
- **Mental illness** affecting reliability (clinical determination)
- **Dishonorable discharge** from military
- **Adverse credit history** indicating financial irresponsibility or coercion vulnerability

#### 3. Foreign National Restrictions

**Immigration Status Verification (Enhanced Post-2022):**
NRC Regulatory Issue Summary 2022-02 (May 2022) requires licensees to **verify employment eligibility** for non-immigrant foreign nationals as part of true identity verification under § 73.56(d)(1).³³ Licensees must:
1. Validate non-immigrant status (visa classification) using DHS SAVE database
2. Verify employment authorization (work permit validity, expiration dates)
3. Confirm identity documents (passport, I-94, employment authorization document)
