**1. Two-Tier Nuclear Liability Insurance System**

**Primary Layer (First-Dollar Coverage):**

- **Coverage:** Off-site third-party bodily injury, property damage, environmental cleanup
- **GLNS Premium:** $1.3M-$1.5M annually (estimated)

**Secondary Layer (Retrospective Premium Mechanism):**
- **Amount per reactor:** $158,026,000 (as of October 2023 inflation adjustment)
- **GLNS total exposure (2 reactors):** **$316,052,000**

**Combined Coverage:** $15.5 billion per nuclear incident ($500M primary + $15.0B secondary), increasing to $16.3 billion with 5% surcharge if damages exceed available funds.

**2. Assessment Trigger: Industry-Wide Exposure, Not Facility-Specific**

A critical feature of the retrospective premium mechanism is that it applies to **all 95 U.S. commercial reactors** when an "extraordinary nuclear occurrence" at **any single reactor** generates public liability claims exceeding $500 million. GLNS would be assessed $316M even if the nuclear incident occurred at Vogtle (Georgia), Palo Verde (Arizona), or any other U.S. facility - not just at GLNS itself.

**Extraordinary Nuclear Occurrence Definition (42 USC Â§ 2014(j)):**
- Substantial discharge of radioactive material OR substantial radiation levels off-site; AND
- Will probably result in "substantial damages" to persons or property off-site

**Assessment Payment Terms:**
- Annual installments capped at $24.714M per reactor (GLNS total: $49.428M annually)
- Payment period: Approximately 6.4 years to reach full $316M assessment

**3. Historical Probability: 69 Years, Zero Assessments - Exceptionally Low Risk**

The most compelling evidence supporting manageable risk is the **complete absence of retrospective premium assessments** in the Price-Anderson Act's 69-year history (1957-2026).

**Three Mile Island (1979) - The Only Significant Test:**

The March 28, 1979 partial core meltdown at Three Mile Island Unit 2 (Pennsylvania) represents the **worst U.S. commercial nuclear incident** and the only event that generated substantial Price-Anderson claims:

- **Total claims paid:** $71 million ($42M indemnity + $29M litigation costs)
- **Primary insurance available (1979):** $160 million
- **Retrospective premium assessment:** **ZERO** - damages represented only 44% of primary layer
- **Outcome:** All claims satisfied from primary insurance without triggering secondary layer

**Why TMI-2 Did Not Exceed Primary Insurance:**

2. **Prompt evacuation:** Precautionary evacuation of pregnant women and children within 5 miles limited exposure
3. **Limited off-site contamination:** Average public exposure 1 millirem (equivalent to chest X-ray)
4. **Claim structure:** Primarily economic losses from evacuation, not bodily injury or property damage
