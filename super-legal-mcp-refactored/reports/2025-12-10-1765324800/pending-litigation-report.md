- GLNS Unit 2 began operations: **1982** (42 years ago)
- **GLNPC Argument**: Claims accrued when plant began operations, not when plaintiffs filed suit (2024-2025)

**Comparable Nuclear Plant Precedents:**

While specific property value class actions against nuclear plants are not well-documented in public case law databases, the statute of limitations defense is typically strong in long-operating facility cases.² The **Indian Point** nuclear plant in New York faced regulatory challenges where courts held that claims challenging decades-old operations were time-barred.³

- Tritium levels below EPA limits (no actual harm)

**Peterson v. Great Lakes Nuclear Power Company LLC** (N.D. Illinois or Illinois state court - venue TBD)

  - Peterson's position: Senior Operations Engineer, 18 years service

#### 2. ADEA Legal Framework

**Age Discrimination in Employment Act (29 USC § 623):**

(a) It shall be unlawful for an employer to fail or refuse to hire or to discharge any individual or otherwise discriminate against any individual with respect to his compensation, terms, conditions, or privileges of employment, because of such individual's age.⁶

**Protected Class**: Employees age 40 and older (29 USC § 631)

**Two Theories of Liability:**

1. **Disparate Treatment** (*Gross v. FBL Financial Services*, 557 U.S. 167 (2009)): Plaintiff must prove age was "but-for" cause of adverse action (not just "motivating factor")⁷
2. **Disparate Impact**: Facially neutral policy has disproportionate adverse impact on older workers (*Smith v. City of Jackson*, 544 U.S. 228 (2005))

#### 3. Disparate Impact Analysis - Peterson's Strongest Claim

**Statistical Evidence:**

- **RIF affected age 55+**: 35 of 42 employees (83%)
- **GLNPC workforce age 55+**: Assume 40% (typical nuclear plant demographics)
- **Chi-square test**: Likely statistically significant disparity (p < 0.05)

**GLNPC Defense: Reasonable Factor Other Than Age (RFOA)**

29 USC § 623(f)(1) allows employment decisions based on "reasonable factors other than age." GLNPC will argue:

- RIF based on **position eliminations** (operational restructuring), not individual employee ages
- Job functions consolidated, **Senior Operations Engineer** role redundant
- Business justification (cost savings, operational efficiency)

**Precedent**: *Gross v. FBL Financial Services* established "but-for" causation standard for disparate treatment claims, making them harder to prove. However, *Gross* does not eliminate disparate impact claims under ADEA.⁸ If Peterson can demonstrate statistically significant age disparity in RIF (83% age 55+), he has viable disparate impact claim even without proving intentional discrimination.
