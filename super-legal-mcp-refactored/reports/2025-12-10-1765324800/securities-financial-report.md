## IV. DETAILED ANALYSIS

### A. NRC Financial Qualifications Regulatory Framework

#### 1. Core Regulatory Requirements - 10 CFR 50.33(f)

**Statutory Basis**: The NRC financial qualifications requirement derives from 10 CFR 50.33(f), which mandates that applicants for nuclear facility licenses demonstrate adequate financial resources to conduct licensed activities safely.¹

**Operating License Requirements** (10 CFR 50.33(f)(2)): For operating licenses, applicants must submit:
- Information demonstrating possession of, or reasonable assurance of obtaining, funds necessary to cover estimated operation costs for the license period
- Estimates for total annual operating costs for each of the **first five years** of facility operation
- Indication of source(s) of funds to cover these costs²

**Electric Utility Exemption**: Electric utility applicants for licenses to operate utilization facilities are exempt from financial qualifications requirements under 10 CFR 50.33(f), as they are presumed financially qualified due to rate regulation.³ However, this exemption does **not apply** to non-utility entities like APH (PE-backed entity).

**Change of Status** (10 CFR 50.76): When an electric utility licensee ceases to be an electric utility (which would occur if GLNPC transitions to APH ownership), the entity must provide financial qualifications information **no later than 75 days prior** to the change, covering the first full **five years of operation** after ceasing to be an electric utility.⁴

#### 2. License Transfer Specific Requirements - 10 CFR 50.80

**NRC Consent Requirement**: 10 CFR 50.80 prohibits the transfer, assignment, or disposal of any nuclear facility license "unless the Commission gives its consent in writing."⁵

**Application Content**: License transfer applications must include "as much of the information described in §§ 50.33 and 50.34 with respect to the identity and technical and financial qualifications of the proposed transferee as would be required by those sections if the application were for an initial license."⁶

**Key Implication**: APH must demonstrate financial qualifications equivalent to an initial license applicant, which includes:
- Five-year operating cost projections (2026-2030 for this transaction)

- Decommissioning funding assurance (NDT adequacy)

#### 3. NUREG-1577 Standard Review Plan

**Guidance Document**: NUREG-1577 Rev. 1, "Standard Review Plan on Power Reactor Licensee Financial Qualifications and Decommissioning Funding Assurance," describes the NRC staff process for reviewing financial qualifications.⁷

**Review Context**: This Standard Review Plan was updated to reflect the electric utility industry's move from rate regulation toward greater competition—directly applicable to PE-backed acquisitions like APH's purchase of GLNPC.⁸

**Review Criteria** (from NUREG-1577):
- **Technical qualifications**: Retention of qualified management and operations personnel
- **Financial qualifications**: Demonstration of adequate resources for normal operations and decommissioning
- **Decommissioning funding**: Adequacy of decommissioning trust funds (NDT balance vs. NRC minimum)
- **Spent fuel management**: Plans and funding for ongoing spent fuel storage

### D. NRC Precedents for PE-Backed Nuclear Acquisitions

#### 1. Holtec International Acquisitions (Private Company Model)

Holtec International, a privately held company, established critical precedents for non-utility nuclear acquisitions:

**Oyster Creek (Exelon → Holtec, 2019)**:
- NRC approved license transfer in **9 months** from application date³⁰
- NRC finding: "Holtec met the regulatory, legal, technical and financial requirements to merit qualification as the successor licensee"³¹
- Financial qualification basis: "Holtec has no history of any long-term debt and enjoys a platinum credit rating from the financial markets"³²
- Transaction: Decommissioning-focused acquisition

**Pilgrim (Entergy → Holtec, 2019)**:
- NRC approval: "Holtec met the regulatory, legal, technical and financial requirements" for decommissioning³³
- Massachusetts regulatory proceedings included extensive financial qualifications review
- Holtec demonstrated adequate decommissioning trust fund plus corporate financial backing

**Indian Point (Entergy → Holtec, 2020)**:
- NRC determination: "Holtec possesses the required technical and financial qualifications to decommission Indian Point safely and in accordance with NRC requirements"³⁴
- Multiple-unit transaction (Indian Point Units 2 and 3)

**Key Precedent Value**: Holtec's approvals demonstrate NRC willingness to approve license transfers to privately held entities based on:
- Strong corporate balance sheet (no long-term debt)
- Adequate decommissioning funding
- Technical expertise and qualified personnel
- Clear source of funds for operations

**Distinguishing Factor**: Holtec acquisitions were decommissioning-focused (shut-down plants), whereas APH acquisition involves operating reactors with 11-15 years of remaining license term. Operating license transfers require more extensive financial qualifications (ongoing operations + decommissioning).

#### 2. Vistra/Energy Harbor Acquisition (Corporate PE-Style Transaction, 2023)

**Background**: Vistra acquired Energy Harbor's three nuclear plants (Beaver Valley, Davis-Besse, Perry) for approximately $3 billion, following Energy Harbor's emergence from FirstEnergy Solutions bankruptcy.

**NRC Approval** (September 29, 2023):
- NRC conclusion: "Vistra is financially and technically qualified to conduct the activities authorised by the licences"³⁵
- License transfer approved for three operating nuclear plants totaling ~3,700 MW capacity³⁶
- Transaction size comparable to APH/GLNPC ($3B vs. $3.2B)

**Financial Qualifications Review**:
- Vistra: Publicly traded company (NYSE: VST), investment-grade credit rating
- Demonstrated strong balance sheet and cash flow from diversified generation portfolio
- NRC approval cited "Vistra's strong technical and financial qualifications"³⁷

**Key Precedent Value**: Vistra/Energy Harbor demonstrates:
- NRC approval for **$3B+ acquisition** of multiple operating nuclear plants
- Acceptance of non-traditional utility ownership (competitive generator vs. rate-regulated utility)
- Review completed in standard 6-12 month timeframe
- Financial qualifications focus: balance sheet strength, cash flow adequacy, operational experience

**Applicability to APH**: Vistra precedent is highly relevant—similar transaction size, operating reactors, non-utility acquirer. Key difference: Vistra is publicly traded with transparent financials, whereas APH is PE-backed LLC requiring more detailed financial disclosure.

#### 3. FirstEnergy Solutions → Energy Harbor (Bankruptcy Emergence, 2019-2020)

**Background**: FirstEnergy Solutions filed bankruptcy in March 2018, emerged as Energy Harbor in February 2020.

**Key Precedent Value**: FirstEnergy/Energy Harbor demonstrates:
- NRC approval for **creditor-owned** entity (analogous to PE ownership structure)

**Applicability to APH**: Energy Harbor precedent supports PE-backed acquisition model. Creditor equity ownership post-bankruptcy is functionally similar to PE equity ownership—both involve non-utility financial investors acquiring nuclear assets. #### 4. Exelon Corporate Restructuring (2021)

**Background**: Exelon separated its regulated utility business (ComEd, PECO, BGE) from generation business (Constellation), transferring licenses for 23 operating and 5 decommissioning reactors.

**NRC Approval** (November 2021):
- NRC concluded: "SpinCo and its subsidiaries have the financial and technical qualifications to conduct the activities authorized by the licenses"⁴¹
- Review included decommissioning requirements: "SpinCo satisfies the NRC's decommissioning requirements since the plants' existing decommissioning funds will be transferred"⁴²
- Largest license transfer in NRC history (23 operating reactors)

**Financial Qualifications Review**:
- SpinCo (Constellation): Stand-alone competitive generator, no rate base
- Demonstrated financial qualifications through:
  - Pro forma financial statements (balance sheet, income statement, cash flow)
  - Power purchase agreements providing revenue certainty
  - Adequate decommissioning trust funds
  - Access to capital markets

**Key Precedent Value**: Exelon/Constellation demonstrates:
- NRC approval for transition from rate-regulated utility to competitive generator
- Acceptance of merchant generator financial model (similar to APH's non-utility status)
- Financial qualifications focus: cash flow adequacy, decommissioning funding, access to capital
- PPA revenue (like GLNPC's NIEC contract) provides financial assurance

**Applicability to APH**: Constellation precedent highly relevant for demonstrating that **competitive generators with PPA revenue** satisfy NRC financial qualifications. GLNPC's $1.42B annual PPA through 2035 provides similar revenue certainty to Constellation's contracts.

#### 5. NRC Approval Statistics and Timeframes

**Volume**: Since 1999 (Three Mile Island Unit 1 sale), NRC has reviewed **more than 115 license transfer applications**.⁴³

**Approval Rate**: NRC approves substantially all license transfer applications that meet regulatory requirements. Denials are rare and typically involve:
- Inadequate financial qualifications (inability to demonstrate funding)
- Unresolved foreign ownership issues (Section 103d violations)
- Technical qualifications deficiencies

**Timeline**:
- **Fast Track**: 9 months (Holtec/Oyster Creek)
- **Standard**: 6-12 months (most transactions)
- **Complex**: 12-18 months (multiple units, foreign ownership issues, public hearing)

**Public Hearing Risk**: License transfer applications are subject to Federal Register notice and opportunity for public hearing. Hearing petitions rarely granted unless:
- Significant new safety issues raised
- Legitimate standing of petitioner (local residents, states)
- Contention meets NRC procedural requirements (10 CFR Part 2)

**Projected Timeline for APH/GLNPC**:
- **Base Case**: 9-12 months (standard operating license transfer)
- **Potential Extension**: 12-15 months if foreign ownership (QIA 20%) requires extensive Negation Action Plan review (see T2 cross-reference)
- **Public Hearing**: Low probability (<10%) given no new safety issues; if hearing requested, adds 6-12 months
