#### D. Oil Pollution Act 1990 (OPA 90) Liability Framework

**Statutory Authority:**
- **33 U.S.C. ¬ß 2702**: Elements of liability (strict, joint and several liability for vessel oil discharges)¬≥‚Åµ
- **33 U.S.C. ¬ß 2704**: Limits on liability (vessel-specific caps based on gross tonnage)¬≥‚Å∂

**Strict Liability Standard:**
Responsible parties are **strictly liable, jointly and severally** under 33 U.S.C. ¬ß 2702(a) and (b) for removal costs and damages that result from oil discharge incidents.¬≥‚Å∑ No showing of fault required.

**Current Inflation-Adjusted Liability Limits (as of December 2022):**¬≥‚Å∏

For vessels other than tank vessels (applicable to container vessels, bulk carriers):
- **Greater of $1,300 per gross ton OR $1,076,000**

Example: 18,000 TEU container vessel (typical gross tonnage: 100,000-150,000 GT)
- Liability limit: $1,300 √ó 120,000 GT = **$156,000,000**
- Significantly exceeds minimum floor of $1,076,000

**Exceptions - Loss of Liability Limits:**¬≥‚Åπ
Responsible party loses right to limit liability if:
1. **Gross negligence or willful misconduct** in causing the spill
2. **Violation of applicable federal safety, construction, or operating regulation**
3. **Failure to report incident** when required by law
4. **Failure to provide reasonable cooperation** with removal activities
5. **Failure to comply without sufficient cause** with official removal orders

**USCG Cost Recovery:**
- Oil Spill Liability Trust Fund (OSLTF) advances federal response costs‚Å¥‚Å∞
- USCG seeks reimbursement from responsible party under 33 U.S.C. ¬ß 2712‚Å¥¬π
- Hypothetical PMSC bunker spill: $2.8M OSLTF reimbursement (realistic for boom deployment, vacuum trucks, absorbent materials, wildlife rescue, USCG personnel, waste disposal)
