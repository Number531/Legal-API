**Trigger Finding:** T2 (jones-act-compliance-report.md) identified potential Jones Act violations:
- **2 vessels (M/V Pacific Mariner, M/V Pacific Trader)** allegedly operating in U.S. coastwise trade with:
  - **73% U.S. citizen crew** (below 75% statutory requirement, 46 U.S.C. § 8103(a))
  - **Unverified U.S.-built status** (must be 75%+ U.S.-built, 46 U.S.C. § 12112)

**Regulatory Framework:**
- Jones Act (46 U.S.C. § 50501): Vessels engaged in U.S. coastwise trade must be:
  1. U.S.-built (at least 75% by weight)
  2. U.S.-flagged
  3. U.S.-citizen owned (at least 75%)
  4. **U.S.-crewed** (at least 75% U.S. citizens, 46 U.S.C. § 8103(a))

**Civil Penalty Exposure (46 U.S.C. § 55102(c)):**
- **$1,100 per distinct violation** (per 2024 inflation adjustment)
- Calculated exposure: 2 vessels × 30 non-compliant voyages × 600 miles/voyage × $1,100/violation = **$39.6M**
- Potential forfeiture: Cargo value or vessel value (extreme remedy, rarely invoked)

**Criminal Penalty Risk (46 U.S.C. § 55102(a)):**
- Knowing violations: $10,000 fine + up to 5 years imprisonment
- **Management-level exposure** if PMSC executives had actual knowledge of non-compliance

**Probability Assessment:**
- **IF non-compliant:** 80-90% probability Coast Guard detects upon audit
- **Coast Guard audit trigger probability:** 60-75% in context of $4.8B acquisition (high-profile transaction draws regulatory scrutiny)
