1. **CMS Requirements for Participation (42 CFR Part 483):**
   - Comprehensive standards for SNF participation in Medicare/Medicaid

   - Immediate jeopardy (J/K/L-tags): Sunset has 9 instances 2023-2024 (Orange County 3×, Desert Sun 1×, Sunrise Manor 1×, others)

   - Enforcement: DPNA (denial payment new admissions), CMPs (per-day $6,418-$106,966, per-instance $6,418-$21,393), termination (23-day notice)

2. **Five Star Quality Rating System:**
   - CMS Nursing Home Compare public reporting (www.medicare.gov/care-compare)
   - Overall star rating calculation: 3 components weighted
     - Health Inspections (35%): Based on 3 most recent annual surveys, scope/severity of deficiencies
     - Staffing (20%): Payroll-based journal (PBJ) hours per patient day (PPD), RN/LPN/CNA, compared to expected staffing based on case mix
     - Quality Measures (45%): MDS assessments, 16 clinical outcomes (pressure ulcers, falls, antipsychotics, hospitalizations, etc.)
   - Sunset portfolio: 0 five-star, 1 four-star (8%), 7 three-star (58%), 3 two-star (25%), 1 one-star (8%)
   - Concerns: Orange County 1-star (health 1, staffing 2, quality 1), Desert Sun 2-star (health 2, staffing 2, quality 3), Henderson 2-star (health 2, staffing 1, quality 3)
   - Implications: referrals, occupancy, payer mix

3. **Special Focus Facility (SFF) Program (42 CFR § 488.404):**
   - Designation criteria: bottom 5% nationally, persistent serious deficiencies

**T2 (case-law-analyst — False Claims Act Qui Tam Litigation):**

**Focus Areas:**
1. **Martinez v. Sunset Case Analysis:**
   - **Caption:** United States ex rel. Sarah Martinez v. Sunset Senior Living Group, LLC
   - **Court:** U.S. District Court, District of Arizona
   - **Filed:** May 2023 (under seal), unsealed December 2024
   - **Relator:** Sarah Martinez, former Director of Rehabilitation (employed 2019-2022, terminated December 2022 allegedly for raising concerns about therapy practices, claims retaliation)
   - **Allegations:**
     1. **Upcoding therapy minutes pre-PDPM (2019-October 2019):** RUG-IV ultra-high tier, instructed therapy staff provide unnecessary therapy reach 720 minutes/week quota, documentation inflated (60-90 minutes actual documented as 120-150), residents received therapy not medically necessary, example: resident John Doe post-hip replacement clinically needed 45 min PT 3×/week but facility scheduled 120 min/day 6 days/week to reach RUX tier, billed $850/day vs. appropriate $520/day, overcharge $330/day × 20 days = $6,600 per resident
     2. **Medically unnecessary services post-PDPM (October 2019-2022):** Even after PDPM eliminated minute-based payment, continued unnecessary therapy/nursing to justify higher case-mix classification, residents classified "extensive services" when "limited assistance" appropriate, nursing aides basic ADL documented as "rehabilitation nursing" to increase nursing component, example: resident Jane Smith post-stroke MDS documented "extensive" PT/OT/SLP but clinical notes show progressed quickly needed only "limited" after week 1, facility continued "extensive" all 28 days Medicare stay
     3. **Kickbacks to referring physicians (2019-2023):** Paid kickbacks to physicians to refer Medicare patients, disguised as "medical director" fees, Dr. Robert Johnson paid $15K/month "medical director" for minimal duties (1 meeting/month 1 hour, no other responsibilities), Dr. Johnson referred 150+ Medicare patients/year, kickbacks to induce referrals violates Anti-Kickback Statute
   - **Damages Sought:** 2,400 Medicare residents admitted 2019-2022, relator alleges 70% claims fraudulent (1,680 residents × average overpayment $8,000 = $13.4M single damages), treble damages $13.4M × 3 = $40.2M, per-claim penalties 1,680 × $11,000-$22,000 = $18.5M-$37M, **total exposure $58.7M-$77.2M**, relator share 15-30% ($8.8M-$23.2M if government intervenes)
   - **Government Investigation:** DOJ U.S. Attorney's Office District of Arizona investigating since June 2023, issued Civil Investigative Demands (CIDs) requesting documents therapy policies/billing records/medical director contracts, Sunset cooperating
   - **Status:** Case unsealed December 2024, government has not yet decided whether to intervene (typically 6-12 months from unsealing to intervention decision, expect Q1-Q2 2025)

2. **FCA Precedents — Therapy Upcoding:**
   - **United States ex rel. Paradies v. AseraCare, Inc.** (N.D. Ala., 11th Cir. 2019): Hospice medical necessity case, relator alleged patients not terminally ill, FCA requires proof services not medically necessary, clinical judgment not fraud unless no reasonable physician would certify, good faith clinical assessments defensible, AseraCare won summary judgment
   - **United States v. Baylor Scott & White Health** (W.D. Tex. 2018): Hospital admitted patients for inpatient care when observation status appropriate, upcoding inpatient vs. observation, settled $4.8M (single damages approximately $2.4M × 2 multiplier), avoided trial/treble damages
   - **United States ex rel. Wall v. Vista Hospice Care, Inc.** (N.D. Tex. 2016): Hospice billed for patients not terminally ill, medical necessity allegations, settled $25M (estimated single damages $12-15M × 1.7-2 multiplier), Corporate Integrity Agreement 5 years
   - **Relevance to Sunset:** Therapy upcoding/medical necessity allegations similar to AseraCare, Sunset defense = clinical judgment not fraud, therapy staff professional discretion, residents benefited even if exceeded strict medical necessity, MDS classifications subjective, relator exaggerated percentage of false claims (alleged 70%, likely 20-30% defensible)

3. **FCA Precedents — Anti-Kickback Statute:**
   - **United States v. Halifax Hospital Medical Center** (M.D. Fla. 2014): Hospital paid physicians excessive compensation to induce referrals, neurosurgeons paid $500K-$1M above FMV, settled $85M (single damages approximately $40M × 2 multiplier), AKS violations support FCA claims (kickbacks taint every claim referred by physician)
   - **United States ex rel. Drakeford v. Tuomey Healthcare System** (D.S.C., 4th Cir. 2015): Hospital part-time employment contracts with physicians, compensation above FMV to induce referrals, jury verdict $237M (treble damages), 4th Circuit affirmed, hospital ultimately settled $72.4M after bankruptcy
   - **Relevance to Sunset:** Dr. Johnson $15K/month ($180K annually) medical director fee, relator alleges excessive for 1 meeting/month 1 hour, Sunset defense = FMV benchmarked to Sullivan Cotter surveys, medical director role includes care planning/quality committee/infection control/staff education, referrals not based on compensation (employed physicians refer to network SNFs not paid per referral), employment safe harbor (if employee or independent contractor structured properly, FMV compensation, not based on referrals)

4. **Settlement Range Analysis:**
   - **Single damages estimate:** Sunset position = 20-30% of 2,400 residents had overpayments (480-720 residents × $8,000 average = $3.8M-$5.8M), government likely position = 40-50% (960-1,200 residents × $8,000 = $7.7M-$9.6M), negotiated settlement midpoint = $5M-$7M single damages
   - **Multiplier:** DOJ settlements typically 1.5-2× single damages if cooperative defendant, avoid trial/treble damages, Sunset cooperative (produced documents to CIDs, no obstruction), likely multiplier 1.75×
   - **Settlement range:** $5M × 1.75 = $8.75M to $7M × 1.75 = $12.25M, **$8M-$15M estimated settlement**
   - **Per-claim penalties:** Typically waived or reduced in settlements (focus on damages restitution, penalties added if egregious/non-cooperative, Sunset likely avoids most penalties)

5. **Corporate Integrity Agreement (CIA):**
   - **OIG requirements:** 5-year term standard, independent review organization (IRO) audits, compliance training, reporting obligations
   - **IRO audits:** Quarterly claims audits (50-100 claims per facility × 12 facilities = 600-1,200 claims/quarter), IRO reviews medical records/billing/MDS assessments, identifies potential overpayments, reports to OIG
   - **IRO cost:** $500K-$800K annually (Big 4 accounting firms Deloitte/EY/PwC/KPMG, healthcare compliance consultancies), $600K × 5 years = $3M total
