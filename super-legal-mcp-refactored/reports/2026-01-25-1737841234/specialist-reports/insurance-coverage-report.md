#### 5. Civil Monetary Penalties (CMPs): Completely Uninsurable

**Exposure**: CMS civil monetary penalties FY2024
- Desert Sun: $42,000 (pressure ulcer substandard quality, November 2023)
- Orange County: $95,000 (immediate jeopardy infection control, March 2024)
- **Total: $137,000**
- **Future CMPs**: $50K-$100K per immediate jeopardy incident (if additional citations occur)

**Insurability Analysis**:

âŒ **CMPs Uninsurable (0% Coverage)**: Federal and state law universally hold that fines and penalties are **uninsurable as against public policy**. Rationale: Allowing insurance coverage for fines/penalties would undermine deterrent effect (facilities would have no financial incentive to comply if insurers pay penalties). CMPs are explicitly designed to **punish wrongdoing** and **deter future violations** (42 U.S.C. Â§ 1395i-3(h)), not to compensate victims.

**Exception**: New Hampshire Supreme Court held civil penalties for negligent violations may be insurable (*City of Manchester v. National Union*, 1997), but Sunset's CMPs based on **immediate jeopardy** (Orange County) and **substandard quality of care** (Desert Sun) involve serious regulatory violations that most courts would deem uninsurable.

**Coverage: $0**, **Uninsured: $137K** (FY2024) + $50K-$100K future per incident

**Impact on Transaction**: Minor ($137K = 0.03% of $425M purchase price), but signals quality problems. Sunset/Silver Oak must pay out-of-pocket.

#### 2. D&O Coverage for FCA Settlement Amounts

**Key Issue: Fraud Exclusion and Restitution Characterization**

Most D&O policies contain exclusions for:

- **Fraud exclusions requiring "final adjudication" do not apply to settlements** (settlement includes no admission of wrongdoing)

- **Absent express exclusion, Delaware law permits coverage for fraudulent conduct** under D&O policies

Illinois law (governing jurisdiction for Astellas) places **burden on insurers** to prove settlement payments constitute uninsurable restitution (disgorgement of ill-gotten gains), rather than compensatory damages.â¹

Seventh Circuit in *Astellas* (2023) held:Â¹â°

### D. Uninsurable Exposures: Civil Monetary Penalties and DPNA

#### 1. Civil Monetary Penalties (CMPs) â€” Uninsurable

**FY2024 CMPs:**
- **Desert Sun (Scottsdale, AZ)**: $42,000 (per-day penalty for pressure ulcer substandard quality, November 2023)
- **Orange County (Santa Ana, CA)**: $95,000 (per-instance penalty for immediate jeopardy infection control, March 2024)
- **Total**: **$137,000**

**Insurability Analysis:**

**Public Policy Prohibition:**

Civil monetary penalties imposed by CMS under 42 U.S.C. Â§ 1395i-3(h) are **uninsurable in most jurisdictions** because:

1. **Punitive Nature**: CMPs are designed to **punish wrongdoing** and **deter future violations**, not to compensate victimsÂ²â°
2. **Against Public Policy**: Allowing insurance coverage for fines/penalties would **undermine deterrent effect** (facilities would have no financial incentive to comply if insurers pay penalties)Â²Â¹
3. **Explicit Policy Exclusions**: Most liability policies explicitly exclude "fines, penalties, sanctions, or taxes"

**State Law Variations:**

| State | Insurability of Fines/Penalties | Authority |
|-------|--------------------------------|-----------|
| **Arizona** | Uninsurable (public policy) | Arizona courts follow general rule that fines are uninsurable |
| **California** | Uninsurable (public policy) | Cal. Ins. Code Â§ 533 (insurance cannot indemnify for willful acts) |
| **Nevada** | Uninsurable (public policy) | Nevada follows majority rule |
| **Illinois** (Silver Oak HQ) | Uninsurable unless negligent conduct | Limited exception for civil penalties from negligence (not willful violations) |

**Exception: New Hampshire** (not applicable to Sunset):

New Hampshire Supreme Court held in *City of Manchester v. National Union Fire Insurance Co.*, 142 N.H. 176 (1997), that civil penalty for breach of nursing home's fiduciary duty was **insurable** when violation was **negligent** (not intentional), even if fine was "penal in nature."Â²Â²

However, Sunset's CMPs based on **immediate jeopardy** (Orange County) and **substandard quality of care** (Desert Sun) involve **serious regulatory violations** that most courts would deem **uninsurable**.

**Coverage Result:**

- **Pre-closing incidents** (e.g., Orange County COVID-19 outbreak March 2024, Desert Sun pressure ulcers November 2023)
