## I. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### Overview

This memorandum analyzes the federal excise tax (FET) implications of American Beverage Partners LLC's (ABP) proposed acquisition of ASBC, a brewery/distillery producing 485,000 barrels of beer and 100,000 proof gallons of spirits annually. ASBC currently pays $7.28M in annual FET using reduced rates under the Craft Beverage Modernization Act (CBMA). **The critical issue:** If ABP owns other breweries/distilleries (>50% common ownership), ASBC will become part of a "controlled group" under 26 U.S.C. § 1563, triggering mandatory aggregation of reduced FET rates among all group members. This could increase ASBC's FET liability by **$698,000 to $1.82 million annually**, depending on ABP's rate assignment strategy.

### Key Findings

**1. Controlled Group Determination (26 U.S.C. § 1563)**

If ABP owns >50% of ASBC and other beverage alcohol producers, controlled group status is automatic:
- **Parent-subsidiary group:** ABP owns >50% of ASBC + other breweries/distilleries
- **Brother-sister group:** 5 or fewer persons own >50% of multiple entities with identical ownership
- **Attribution rules apply:** Family attribution, trust attribution (26 U.S.C. § 1563(e))

**Controlled group status triggers FET aggregation rules under 26 U.S.C. §§ 5001(c)(3) and 5051(a)(5).**

**3. Assignment Mechanism and Default Rules**

**Regulatory Framework (27 C.F.R. §§ 25.167, 19.235):**
- Controlled group must elect allocation of reduced rates annually
- Filing: TTB Form 5130.10 (Brewer's Notice) or written agreement
- Deadline: January 1 of calendar year (irrevocable for 12 months)
- **Default rule:** If no election filed, TTB assigns proportionally based on production

**Proportional Assignment (Default):**
- ASBC receives: (485K / 685K) × 60K barrels = 42,482 barrels at $3.50/barrel
- ASBC receives: (100K / 180K) × 100K PG = 55,556 PG at $2.70/PG
- **Result:** ASBC FET increases $698K annually

**Optimal Assignment (Negotiated):**
- All 60,000 barrels assigned to ASBC → Beer FET unchanged ($7.01M)
- All 100,000 PG assigned to ASBC → Spirits FET unchanged ($270K)
- **Result:** ASBC FET unchanged; other group members pay higher rates

**Critical Insight:** Group-level FET is identical ($10.21M beer + $1.34M spirits = $11.55M total) regardless of assignment method. Only distribution among members differs. ABP has complete discretion to optimize assignment.

## IV. DETAILED ANALYSIS

### A. BEER FEDERAL EXCISE TAX - CONTROLLED GROUP RULES (26 U.S.C. § 5051)

#### 1. Statutory Framework

**26 U.S.C. § 5051(a)(1) - Base Rates:**
- $18.00 per barrel on first 6,000,000 barrels
- $18.00 per barrel on barrels over 6,000,000

**26 U.S.C. § 5051(a)(2) - Reduced Rates (CBMA rates):**
- $3.50 per barrel on first 60,000 barrels produced
- $16.00 per barrel on barrels over 60,000 up to 2,000,000 barrels

**26 U.S.C. § 5051(a)(5) - CONTROLLED GROUP RULE:**

*"In the case of a controlled group, the 60,000 barrel quantity specified in paragraph (2)(A) and the 6,000,000 barrel quantity specified in paragraph (1)(A) shall be applied to the controlled group, and the 60,000 barrel quantity shall be apportioned among the brewers who are members of such group in such manner as the Secretary or their delegate shall by regulations prescribe."*

#### 2. Controlled Group Definition

**27 C.F.R. § 25.11 (TTB Regulations):**

A controlled group is defined by reference to 26 U.S.C. § 1563(a), with modifications:
- **Parent-subsidiary group:** Common parent owns ≥50% voting power or value of stock
- **Brother-sister group:** 5 or fewer persons own ≥50% of each entity with common ownership >50%
- **Combined group:** Combination of parent-subsidiary and brother-sister

**PE Ownership Structure:**
If ABP (PE portfolio company) owns >50% of ASBC, Brewery A, Brewery B, and Distillery C, these constitute a controlled group.

#### 3. Assignment Methodology (27 C.F.R. § 25.26)

**TTB Assignment Rules:**
- Controlled group members must agree on assignment of 60,000 barrel reduced rate
- Assignment must be made on TTB Form 5130.9 (Brewer's Notice) or written agreement
- Assignment is irrevocable for the calendar year
- If members fail to agree, TTB may assign proportionally based on production

**Key Regulatory Provision (27 C.F.R. § 25.26(c)):**
*"The 60,000 barrel limitation shall be apportioned among the brewers who are members of a controlled group on the basis of the proportion which the production of beer by each brewer during the calendar year bears to the total production of beer by all members of the controlled group during that year, unless the members of the controlled group elect to apportion the limitation on an unequal basis."*

### B. SPIRITS FEDERAL EXCISE TAX - CONTROLLED GROUP RULES (26 U.S.C. § 5001)

#### 1. Statutory Framework

**26 U.S.C. § 5001(a) - Base Rate:**
- $13.50 per proof gallon

**26 U.S.C. § 5001(c) - Reduced Rates (CBMA rates):**

*(c)(1) IN GENERAL.—In the case of a distilled spirits operation, the otherwise applicable tax rate under subsection (a)(1) shall be—*
- *(A) $2.70 per proof gallon on the first 100,000 proof gallons of distilled spirits, and*
- *(B) $13.34 per proof gallon on the first 22,130,000 proof gallons of distilled spirits to which subparagraph (A) does not apply.*

**26 U.S.C. § 5001(c)(3) - CONTROLLED GROUP RULE:**

*"In the case of a controlled group, the proof gallon quantities specified in paragraph (1) shall be applied to the controlled group, and the 100,000 proof gallon quantity shall be apportioned among the distillers who are members of such group in such manner as the Secretary shall by regulations prescribe."*

#### 2. ASBC Controlled Group Scenario - Spirits

**ASSUMPTION:** ABP Controlled Group consists of:
