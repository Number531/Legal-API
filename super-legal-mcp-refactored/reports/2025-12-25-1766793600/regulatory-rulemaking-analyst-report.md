## I. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### Overview

This research memorandum addresses federal TTB (Alcohol and Tobacco Tax and Trade Bureau) regulatory compliance requirements for the $1.2 billion acquisition of Artisan Spirits & Brewing Company (ASBC), a craft alcoholic beverage producer operating 3 breweries, 1 distillery, 1 winery/meadery, and 18 taprooms across multiple states. The acquisition presents **material regulatory risks** requiring immediate pre-closing mitigation, including a **CRITICAL bourbon formula compliance issue** with $1.45M-2.66M exposure and complex permit transfer requirements affecting operational continuity.

### Executive Summary - Key Findings

#### 1. CRITICAL RISK: Bourbon Formula Investigation ($1.45M-2.66M Exposure)

**Issue:**
ASBC's Single Barrel Bourbon product uses **toasted wood staves** during the aging process to enhance flavor. A competitor filed a complaint with TTB in September 2024, alleging that wood staves constitute "added flavoring" prohibited under bourbon standards of identity (27 CFR ยง 5.143). TTB is investigating, with determination expected Q1 2026. **$4 million bourbon inventory** is at risk.

**Regulatory Standard:**
27 CFR ยง 5.143 defines bourbon as whisky aged in "charred new oak containers" with an **absolute prohibition** on adding "coloring, flavoring, or blending materials." Treatment with wood (staves, chips) beyond standard barrel aging requires labeling as "Whiskey - Colored and Flavored with Wood" and **disqualifies the product from bourbon designation**.

**Maker's Mark 46 Precedent:**
Maker's Mark 46 uses seared oak staves during aging and is **not labeled as bourbon** - it is classified as a Whiskey Specialty under 27 CFR ยง 5.39(c). This demonstrates TTB's position that wood staves constitute "added flavoring."

**Probability Assessment:**
- **70-80% likelihood** TTB denies formula (based on regulatory text and Maker's Mark precedent)
- **20-30% likelihood** TTB approves formula (no published precedent supports this interpretation)

**Financial Consequences if Formula Denied:**
- Inventory write-down: **$1.2M-2M** (30-50% value loss as bourbon commands premium pricing)
- Relabeling costs: **$200K-400K** (new COLAs, label printing, repackaging)
- Criminal penalties: **$1K-10K** per violation (26 U.S.C. ยง 5687 - up to $1,000 fine per violation + up to 1 year imprisonment)
- Civil penalties: **$50K-250K** (27 U.S.C. ยง 207 - TTB discretionary fines)
- Product recall and market disruption
- **TOTAL EXPOSURE: $1.45M-2.66M**

**Immediate Actions Required:**
1. **Request expedited TTB determination** before closing (contact TTB Formulations Division)
2. **Negotiate $2.66M escrow fund** in purchase agreement (released to seller only if formula approved or 18 months elapse with no adverse determination)
3. **Alternative:** Negotiate purchase price adjustment reflecting formula denial risk
4. **Halt production** of Single Barrel Bourbon until TTB determination obtained

## IV. DETAILED ANALYSIS

### A. TTB Permit Transfer Requirements (27 CFR Parts 1, 19, 24, 25)

#### 1. Legal Framework for Ownership Changes

##### a. Federal Alcohol Administration Act Requirements (27 CFR ยง 1.44)

**Automatic Termination Rule:**
Under 27 CFR ยง 1.44, basic permits issued under the Federal Alcohol Administration Act (FAA Act) are **non-transferable**. The regulation provides:

> "No basic permit shall be leased, sold, or otherwise voluntarily transferred, and, in the event of such lease, sale, or other voluntary transfer, such basic permit shall automatically terminate thereupon."

**30-Day Grace Period:**
If actual or legal control of the permittee is acquired (directly or indirectly, whether by stock ownership or otherwise), the permit automatically terminates after 30 days **unless** the transferee or permittee files an application for a new basic permit within that 30-day period. If timely filed, the outstanding permit continues in effect until TTB takes final action on the new application.

**Source:** [27 CFR ยง 1.44 - Automatic termination of permits](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-27/chapter-I/subchapter-A/part-1/subpart-C/subject-group-ECFR1861bc23bed83e8/section-1.44), [27 CFR ยง 1.42 - Change in ownership, management, or control](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/27/1.42)

##### b. Notification Requirements (27 CFR ยง 1.42)

Permittees must **immediately notify** the appropriate TTB officer of any change in ownership, management, or control. For corporations, this includes changes in officers, directors, or persons owning/controlling more than 10% of voting stock. Notification must include names and addresses of all new persons participating in the ownership, management, or control.

**Source:** [27 CFR ยง 1.42](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/27/1.42)

#### 2. Distilled Spirits Plant (DSP) Transfer Requirements (27 CFR Part 19)

##### a. Change in Proprietorship (27 CFR ยง 19.116)

**Qualification Requirement:**
The IRC and corresponding regulations require the **successor DSP to qualify in the same manner as the proprietor of a new distilled spirits plant** before commencing or continuing business. This includes:
- Filing new DSP registration (TTB Form 5110.41)
- Obtaining new operating permit
- Obtaining new FAA Act basic permit (if applicable)
- Filing new surety bond

**30-Day Continuity Provision:**
If the successor files an application for a new permit within 30 days of the ownership change, the outstanding basic permit and/or operating permit will remain in effect until TTB takes final action on the new application(s).

**Source:** [27 CFR ยง 19.116 - Change in proprietorship](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/27/19.116), [TTB DSP Change in Proprietorship Guidance](https://www.ttb.gov/regulated-commodities/beverage-alcohol/distilled-spirits/change-in-proprietorship-or-control)

##### b. Amended Registration Requirements (27 CFR ยง 19.112)

For changes affecting the DSP registration (short of full proprietorship change), proprietors must file an amended TTB Form 5110.41 within 30 days of any change to information in the current approved registration, unless another time period is specified.

**Source:** [27 CFR ยง 19.112 - General rules for amending a registration](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-27/chapter-I/subchapter-A/part-19/subpart-E/subject-group-ECFR4d0a6cd6ab515e4/section-19.112)

#### 3. Brewery Transfer Requirements (27 CFR Part 25)

##### a. Amended Brewer's Notice (27 CFR ยง 25.71)

Section 25.71 requires that, unless another time period is specified, a brewer submit an **amended notice within 30 days** of any change to information shown in the Brewer's Notice (TTB Form 5130.10).

**Corporate Changes:**
When there is a change in officers, directors, or stockholders of 10% or more, the brewer must submit, within 30 days, an amended Brewer's Notice on TTB Form 5130.10 in duplicate.

**Source:** [27 CFR ยง 25.71](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-27/chapter-I/subchapter-A/part-25), [TTB Brewery Changes After Qualification](https://www.ttb.gov/beer/brewery-changes-after-original-qualification)

##### b. Change in Proprietorship - Full Requalification

For a **complete change in proprietorship**, the IRC and regulations require the **successor brewer to qualify in the same manner as the proprietor of a new brewery** before commencing or continuing business. This requires:
- Filing new Brewer's Notice (TTB Form 5130.10)
- Filing new surety bond (if taxes exceed $50,000/year threshold)
- Meeting all federal qualification requirements

**Critical Timing:** To ensure continuity of operations, all qualifying documents must be filed **well in advance** of the proposed effective date of the change.

**Source:** [Federal Register - Modernization of Brewer's Notices](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2022/06/08/2022-11902/modernization-of-qualification-requirements-for-brewers-notices), [TTB Brewery Change in Proprietorship](https://www.ttb.gov/beer/brewery-changes-after-original-qualification)

#### 4. Winery/Basic Permit Transfer Requirements (27 CFR Part 24)

##### a. Successor Proprietor Requirements (27 CFR ยง 24.130)

If there is a change in proprietorship of wine premises qualified under Part 24:
- **Outgoing proprietor** must comply with requirements of ยง 24.140 (transfer of materials)
- **Successor proprietor** must, before commencing operations:
  - Apply for and obtain any required permits
  - File any required bonds
  - File application for and receive permission to operate (same manner as qualifying new wine premises)

**Source:** [27 CFR Part 24 Subpart D - Changes Subsequent to Original Establishment](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-27/chapter-I/subchapter-A/part-24/subpart-D/subject-group-ECFR277f7261fff7bb6)

##### b. Basic Permit Amendments

The proprietor must follow provisions of 27 CFR Part 1 to effect any change pertaining to a permit issued under the FAA Act. For name changes, the proprietor must file an amended application and, if a basic permit has been issued, an application for amendment of the basic permit.

**Source:** [27 CFR ยง 24.130](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-27/chapter-I/subchapter-A/part-24/subpart-D/subject-group-ECFR277f7261fff7bb6)

### B. Standards of Identity for Bourbon and Formula Approval (27 CFR Part 5, Subpart I)

#### 1. Bourbon Whisky Standards of Identity (27 CFR ยง 5.143)

**Note on Regulatory Reorganization:** The bourbon standards were previously codified at 27 CFR ยง 5.22 (pre-2020). TTB reorganized the labeling and advertising regulations in 2020-2022, moving standards of identity to Subpart I. Current bourbon standards are at **27 CFR ยง 5.143(c)**.

##### a. Production Requirements (27 CFR ยง 5.143(c)(2))

**Bourbon whisky** is defined as whisky produced:
1. **At not exceeding 160ยฐ proof** from a fermented mash
2. **Not less than 51% corn grain**
3. **Stored at not more than 125ยฐ proof**
4. **In charred new oak containers**

**Straight Bourbon:** Must be stored in charred new oak containers for 2 years or more.

**Source:** [27 CFR ยง 5.143 - Whisky](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/27/5.143), [eCFR Part 5 Subpart I - Standards of Identity](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-27/chapter-I/subchapter-A/part-5/subpart-I)

##### b. Prohibition on Additives (27 CFR ยง 5.143)

**Critical Prohibition:**
> "No coloring, flavoring, or blending materials may be used in the production of spirits designated as 'bourbon whisky' in accordance with ยง 5.22(b)(1)(i) or 'straight' whisky."

This prohibition is **absolute** for bourbon and straight bourbon. Any addition of coloring, flavoring, or blending materials **disqualifies** the product from being labeled as bourbon.

**Source:** [TTB Standards of Identity in Plain English](https://thewhiskeyjug.com/ttb-standards-of-identity-for-whiskey-in-plain-english/), [eCFR ยง 5.143](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/27/5.143)

#### 2. Treatment with Wood - Oak Staves and Chips (27 CFR ยง 5.39(c))

##### a. Regulatory Definition of "Treatment with Wood"

The term "coloring, flavoring, or blending material" includes **"infusion of oak chips when approved by the Administrator"** as a substance that can be customarily employed in distilled spirits products.

However, **any material whatsoever added to straight whisky is prohibited**, except that vodka may be treated with sugar and citric acid.

**Source:** [Tater-Talk - Label Says Bourbon But It's Not](https://tater-talk.com/2020/02/07/label-says-bourbon-but-its-not/)

##### b. Products with Added Wood - Loss of Bourbon Designation

**Regulatory Treatment:**
Whiskies that have **staves or spires** added to them (such as Maker's Mark 46) have these additional staves fall under **added HCFBM (harmless coloring, flavoring, or blending materials)**, specifically addressed by 27 CFR ยง 5.39(c) "Treatment with wood."

**Labeling Consequences:**
Treatment with wood during distillation or storage (other than through contact with the oak container) **requires the label to state** "colored and flavored with wood (insert chips, slabs, etc., as appropriate)" as part of the class and type designation. Products treated with additional wood (like staves) are **not bourbon** but are classified as **Whiskey Specialities**.

**Source:** [Bourbon as Defined by Law](https://www.talksonlaw.com/briefs/bourbon-as-defined-by-law), [TTB Whiskey Standards](https://thewhiskeyjug.com/ttb-standards-of-identity-for-whiskey-in-plain-english/)

##### c. Labeling When Bourbon Is Compromised

The TTB allows a producer to state "Bourbon" or "Straight Bourbon" on the label of products **as long as it is followed with the statement of what was done to it that made it no longer Bourbon**, and this is **required to be on the front label**.

Example: "Bourbon Whiskey Finished with Oak Staves" or "Bourbon Whiskey - Colored and Flavored with Wood Staves"

**Source:** [Tater-Talk - Label Issues](https://tater-talk.com/category/label-issues/)

#### 3. TTB Formula Approval Process (TTB Form 5100.51)

##### a. When Formula Approval Is Required (27 CFR Part 5, Subpart J)

An approved formula is required to **blend, mix, purify, refine, compound, or treat** distilled spirits in a manner that results in a change of class or type of the spirits. Formula must be filed on TTB Form 5100.51.

**Formula approval is required when:**
- Harmless coloring, flavoring, or blending materials (HCFBM) are added
- Wood chips, staves, or other wood additives are used beyond standard barrel aging

**Formula approval is NOT required for:**
- Straight bourbon whisky (no additives)
- Straight malt whisky
- Standard whisky production within standards of identity

**Source:** [TTB - Which Alcohol Beverages Require Formula Approval](https://www.ttb.gov/formulation/do-i-need-a-formula-spirits), [eCFR Part 5 Subpart J - Formulas](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-27/chapter-I/subchapter-A/part-5/subpart-J)

##### b. Formula Application Process

**Form:** TTB Form 5100.51 (Application for and Certification/Exemption of Formula)

**Filing Methods:**
- Electronic submission via Formulas Online system (https://www.ttb.gov)
- Paper form submission

**Processing Time:**
- TTB estimates **2 hours average burden** to complete form
- Approval timeline varies based on complexity and TTB workload
- **No specific approval timeline published** by TTB for formula approvals

**Production Prohibition:**
If formula approval is required, **production cannot begin** and distillers may **not bottle, sell, or market** a product until such approval is granted (subject to limited exceptions).

**Source:** [TTB Form 5100.51](https://www.ttb.gov/images/pdfs/forms/f510051.pdf), [Frost Brown Todd - Opening a Distillery: Formula and Label Approval](https://frostbrowntodd.com/opening-a-distillery-formula-and-label-approval/)

##### c. Formula Denials and Enforcement

**No Specific Bourbon Denial Precedent Found:**
Research did not identify published TTB rulings specifically addressing denials of bourbon formulas using toasted wood staves. This appears to be an **issue of first impression** or one handled through internal TTB investigations rather than published precedent.

**TTB Rulings Database:**
TTB publishes rulings on spirits standards, but specific bourbon wood stave rulings were not identified in the 2020-2025 period.

**Source:** [TTB Rulings](https://www.ttb.gov/media/72043/download?inline=)

#### 4. Penalties for Formula Violations (26 U.S.C. ยง 5687)

##### a. General Penalty Provision

**26 U.S.C. ยง 5687 - Penalty for Offenses Not Specifically Covered:**

Whoever violates any provision of Chapter 51 (Distilled Spirits, Wines, and Beer) or regulations issued pursuant thereto, for which a specific criminal penalty is not prescribed, shall be:

### C. Certificate of Label Approval (COLA) Transfer Requirements

#### 1. COLA Definition and Legal Framework

##### a. What Is a COLA?

Alcohol producers must apply for a **Certification/Exemption of Label/Bottle Approval (COLA)** and follow labeling and advertising regulations found in:
- 27 CFR Part 13 (Labeling Proceedings)
- 27 CFR Part 4 (Wine Labeling and Advertising)
- **27 CFR Part 5 (Distilled Spirits Labeling and Advertising)**
- **27 CFR Part 7 (Malt Beverages Labeling and Advertising)**
- 27 CFR Part 16 (Alcoholic Beverage Health Warning Statement)

**Source:** [TTB COLA Overview](https://www.ttb.gov/alfd/certificate-of-label-aproval-cola), [27 CFR Part 5 Subpart B](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-27/chapter-I/subchapter-A/part-5/subpart-B)

##### b. Certificate Holder Definition

The **certificate holder** is the permittee or brewer whose **name, address, and basic permit number, plant registry number, or brewer's notice number** appears on an approved TTB Form 5100.31.

**Source:** [TTB COLA FAQs](https://www.ttb.gov/faqs/colas-and-formulas-online-faqs)

#### 2. COLA Non-Transferability Upon Ownership Change

##### a. Critical Rule: COLAs Are NOT Transferable

**Change in Proprietorship Consequence:**
A change in proprietorship occurs when there is a change in the entity which owns and operates the business. When this occurs, the **certificate holder information** (name, address, permit number) on the original COLA is **no longer accurate**.

The existing COLAs are **tied to the specific permittee** whose information appears on the certificate and **cannot be transferred** to a new owner.

**Source:** [TTB Change in Proprietorship - Distillery](https://www.ttb.gov/distilled-spirits/change-in-proprietorship-or-control), [TTB Change in Proprietorship - Brewery](https://www.ttb.gov/beer/brewery-changes-after-original-qualification)

##### b. Requirement for New COLA Applications

**When ownership changes occur, new COLA applications must be filed by the new owner** since the certificate holder information will change. The new proprietor must:
1. Obtain new basic permit(s)
2. Receive new permit numbers
3. Re-submit COLA applications (TTB Form 5100.31) with **new permit holder information**
4. Await TTB approval before using labels

**ASBC Impact:**
- **280 approved COLAs** must be re-applied for under acquirer's new permit
- Each COLA requires separate Form 5100.31 submission
- Processing time for COLAs: Variable (see processing times below)

**Source:** [TTB COLA Certificate Holder Definition](https://www.ttb.gov/faqs/colas-and-formulas-online-faqs)

#### 3. COLA Application and Processing

##### a. COLA Application Methods

**Form:** TTB Form 5100.31 (Certificate/Exemption of Label/Bottle Approval)

**Filing Methods:**

**Source:** [TTB COLAs Online](https://www.ttbonline.gov/colasonline/publicSearchColasBasic.do), [27 CFR ยง 5.22 - COLA Rules](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-27/chapter-I/subchapter-A/part-5/subpart-B/section-5.22)

**Typical Timeline:**

### D. Tied House Laws and Three-Tier System Compliance (27 CFR Part 6, 27 U.S.C. ยง 205)

#### 1. Federal Tied House Legal Framework

##### a. Statutory Basis (27 U.S.C. ยง 205)

**Federal Alcohol Administration Act ยง 105(b)(3):**
Prohibits producers and wholesalers from inducing retailers to purchase products to the exclusion of competitors. This prohibition prevents "tied houses" - retailers controlled by producers/suppliers.

**Source:** [27 U.S.C. ยง 205](https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?req=granuleid:USC-prelim-title27-section205&num=0&edition=prelim)

##### b. Regulatory Implementation (27 CFR Part 6)

**27 CFR Part 6 - "Tied-House" Regulations:**

**Structure:**
- Subpart A: Scope of Regulations
- Subpart B: Definitions
- **Subpart C: Unlawful Inducements**
- **Subpart D: Exceptions**

**Last Amended:** November 6, 2024

**Source:** [27 CFR Part 6](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-27/chapter-I/subchapter-A/part-6), [Cornell Law - 27 CFR Part 6](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/27/part-6)

#### 2. Definition of "Industry Members"

**27 CFR Part 6, Subpart B:**

"Industry members" are persons engaged in business as:
- Distillers
- Brewers
- Rectifiers, blenders, or other producers
- Importers
- Wholesalers of distilled spirits, wine, or malt beverages

**ASBC Status:** As a brewer, distiller, and winery operator, ASBC is an **industry member** subject to tied house prohibitions.

**Source:** [27 CFR Part 6 Subpart B](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-27/chapter-I/subchapter-A/part-6)

#### 3. Prohibited Tied House Practices (Subpart C)

##### a. General Prohibition

Industry members are prohibited from:
1. **Direct or indirect financial interest** in retail establishments
2. **Furnishing, giving, renting, lending, or selling equipment, fixtures, signs, supplies** to retailers
3. **Extending credit** beyond lawful limits
4. **Paying for advertising** on behalf of retailers
5. **Providing free products** beyond permissible samples
6. **Any inducement** to purchase products to exclusion of competitors

**Source:** [27 CFR Part 6 Subpart C](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-27/chapter-I/subchapter-A/part-6), [Brewers Association - Tied House Laws](https://cdn.brewersassociation.org/wp-content/uploads/2019/02/Tied-House-Laws.pdf)

##### b. "Thing of Value" Standard

The basic premise of federal laws is to prevent control of retailers by manufacturers (tied houses) as was common before Prohibition. Generally prohibits brewers from directly or indirectly giving **anything "of value"** to retailers that compromises retailer independence.

**Source:** [Craft Beer Professor - Tied House Laws and Three-Tier System](https://www.craftbeerprofessor.com/2017/02/lets-make-sure-talking-things-tied-house-laws-three-tier-system/)

#### 4. Exceptions to Tied House Prohibitions (Subpart D)

##### a. On-Premise Consumption at Production Facility

**Critical Exception for Taprooms/Brewpubs:**

Federal law **authorizes a brewery to sell beer to consumers at the brewery** and to hold a **100% ownership interest in a retailer** co-located with the production facility.

Under **27 CFR ยง 25.25**, a brewer may use brewery premises for other purposes upon approval, including **operation of a tavern on brewery premises**.

**ASBC Application:**
- **18 taprooms** operated by ASBC appear to be **co-located** with breweries/distillery
- If taprooms are **on-premise** (same physical location as production), they fall within the federal tied house exception
- **No separate three-tier compliance issue** for on-premise taproom sales

**Source:** [27 CFR ยง 25.25](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/27/25.25), [27 CFR ยง 25.23 - Restrictions on use](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/27/25.23)

##### b. Self-Distribution Exception

**Federal Authorization:**
Federal law treats **brewers as manufacturers**, and federal law authorizes breweries to engage in limited self-distribution (direct sales to retailers without using wholesaler intermediary).

**Statutory Basis:**
There are **limited statutory exceptions** that allow manufacturers, such as breweries, to "self-distribute," though self-distribution is **heavily regulated** and whether a brewery qualifies depends on various factors.

**State-by-State Variation:**
- Some states follow federal model (treating brewers as manufacturers, authorizing retail sales on brewery premises and self-distribution)
- Other states treat brewpubs as retail operations similar to restaurants
- **Every state now allows some form of brewpub, taproom, and/or self-distribution for small producers**

**ASBC Self-Distribution (15% of Oregon volume = 10,875 barrels):**
- Must verify **Oregon state law** permits self-distribution for breweries/distilleries
- Federal law does not prohibit self-distribution by brewers
- Compliance depends on **state-specific three-tier laws**

**Source:** [Brewers Association - Tied House Laws](https://cdn.brewersassociation.org/wp-content/uploads/2019/02/Tied-House-Laws.pdf), [Craft Beer Professor](https://www.craftbeerprofessor.com/2017/02/lets-make-sure-talking-things-tied-house-laws-three-tier-system/)

##### c. Consumer Advertising Exception

Industry members may advertise directly to consumers. This includes:
- Newspaper, TV, radio, online advertising
- Social media marketing
- Point-of-sale materials provided to retailers (within limits)

**Source:** [Craft Brewing Business - Pay-to-Play Beer Laws](https://www.craftbrewingbusiness.com/featured/beer-law-review-of-tied-house-rules-on-advertising-involving-retailers/)

##### d. Product Samples Exception

Brewers and distillers may provide **product samples** to retailers under limited circumstances:
- Must be for **sampling/quality evaluation purposes**
- Must not exceed **reasonable quantities**
- May not be provided as **inducement** to purchase

**Note:** The research reference to "$50/month limit" for samples was not confirmed in federal regulations. This may be a **state-specific limit** rather than federal.

**Source:** [27 CFR Part 6 Subpart D](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-27/chapter-I/subchapter-A/part-6)

#### 5. Penalties for Tied House Violations

##### a. Civil Penalties

**Permit Suspension or Revocation:**
TTB may suspend or revoke FAA Act basic permits for tied house violations under 27 CFR Part 1.

**Cease and Desist Orders:**
TTB may issue cease and desist orders requiring termination of prohibited relationships.

**Source:** [27 CFR Part 1](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-27/chapter-I/subchapter-A/part-1)

##### b. Criminal Penalties

Violations of the Federal Alcohol Administration Act can result in criminal prosecution:
- **Fines** up to amounts specified in 27 U.S.C. ยง 207
- **Imprisonment** for willful violations

**Source:** [27 U.S.C. ยง 207](https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?req=granuleid:USC-prelim-title27-section207&num=0&edition=prelim)

##### c. State Penalties

State alcoholic beverage control agencies can impose additional penalties:
- State permit/license suspension or revocation
- State fines and civil penalties
- Exclusion from state market

**Source:** [Brewers Association - Tied House Laws](https://cdn.brewersassociation.org/wp-content/uploads/2019/02/Tied-House-Laws.pdf)

  2. **Label Reclassification:** Product becomes "Whiskey - Colored and Flavored with Wood Staves" (not bourbon)
  3. **COLA Revocation:** Existing COLAs for Single Barrel Bourbon invalidated
  4. **Market Value Destruction:** Bourbon commands premium pricing; reclassified whiskey has 30-50% lower market value
  5. **Criminal Penalties:** 26 U.S.C. ยง 5687 - Up to $1,000 fine per violation + up to 1 year imprisonment
  6. **Civil Penalties:** 27 U.S.C. ยง 207 - TTB discretionary civil fines
  7. **Permit Suspension Risk:** Serious violations can trigger FAA Act permit suspension

**Financial Impact - Scenario 2:**
- Inventory write-down: **$1.2M-2M** (30-50% value loss on $4M inventory)
- Relabeling costs: **$200K-400K** (new COLAs, label printing, repackaging)
- Criminal fines: **$1K-10K** (per violation; TTB discretion)
- Civil penalties: **$50K-250K** (TTB discretion)
- **Total Exposure: $1.45M-2.66M**

#### 3. Precedent Analysis - Maker's Mark 46

**Maker's Mark 46 Production Method:**
- Uses **seared French oak staves** inserted into barrels during aging
- Similar to ASBC's toasted wood staves methodology

**TTB Regulatory Treatment:**
- Maker's Mark 46 is **NOT labeled as bourbon**
- Product labeled as "Whisky Finished with Oak Staves"
- Classified as **Whiskey Specialty** under 27 CFR ยง 5.39(c)

**Implication for ASBC:**
If TTB applied Maker's Mark 46 precedent consistently, ASBC's Single Barrel Bourbon would lose bourbon designation.
