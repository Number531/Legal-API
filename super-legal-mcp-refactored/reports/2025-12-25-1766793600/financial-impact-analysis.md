## II. MONTE CARLO SIMULATION ANALYSIS

### A. Methodology

**Simulation Parameters:**
- **Iterations:** 10,000 runs per scenario
- **Distribution Type:** Triangular distribution for continuous variables (min, most likely, max); binomial for binary outcomes
- **Correlation Assumptions:**
  - Employment risks (R-005, R-007, R-008) assumed 60% correlated (common root cause: wage/hour compliance culture)
  - Regulatory risks (R-001, R-004) assumed 40% correlated (common factor: regulatory scrutiny of claims)
  - Litigation risks (R-002, R-003) assumed 30% correlated (both involve product liability/service issues)
  - Commercial/tax risks independent of litigation/regulatory

**Scenario Definitions:**

1. **Base Case (50th Percentile):** Median outcomes with strategic mitigation measures implemented
   - Distributor "Grow Into" strategy adopted (R-009a, not R-009)
   - FET optimal covenant implemented (R-010a, not R-010)
   - Wage/hour class assumes 30% opt-in (R-007, not R-008)
   - Settlement achieved for Texas Lone Star at recommended range ($2M-$2.5M vs. $2.8M-$4M exposure)

2. **Downside Case (75th Percentile):** Adverse outcomes on 2-3 major risks
   - Wage/hour class achieves 50% opt-in (between R-007 and R-008)
   - Texas Lone Star settles at high end ($3.5M-$4M)
   - TTB bourbon denial occurs (80% probability scenario materializes)
   - "Grow Into" strategy still implemented (distributor consolidation avoided)

3. **Severe Downside (95th Percentile):** Multiple adverse outcomes compound
   - TTB bourbon denial + full exposure ($2.66M)
   - Texas Lone Star proceeds to verdict ($4M+)
   - Wage/hour class achieves 70% opt-in ($4.5M-$7.2M)
   - Distributor "Grow Into" strategy encounters resistance ($60M-$100M vs. $30M-$100M expected)
