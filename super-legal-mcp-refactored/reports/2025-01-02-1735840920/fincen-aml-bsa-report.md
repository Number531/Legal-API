## IV. DETAILED ANALYSIS

### A. BSA/AML Statutory and Regulatory Framework

#### 1. Bank Secrecy Act - Statutory Foundation

**31 U.S.C. § 5318 - AML Program Requirement**

The Bank Secrecy Act requires all financial institutions to establish anti-money laundering programs with minimum requirements codified at 31 U.S.C. § 5318(h):

- **(A)** Development of internal policies, procedures, and controls
- **(B)** Designation of a compliance officer
- **(C)** Ongoing employee training program
- **(D)** Independent audit function to test programs¹

Money services businesses, including cryptocurrency exchanges, are explicitly covered financial institutions under the BSA framework.²

**Database Provenance:** [31 U.S.C. § 5318](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/31/5318) [VERIFIED via Cornell LII, accessed Jan 2, 2025]

#### 2. FinCEN Regulations for Money Services Businesses

**31 C.F.R. § 1022.210 - AML Program Requirements for MSBs**

Each money services business must develop, implement, and maintain an effective anti-money laundering program that is **reasonably designed** to prevent the MSB from being used to facilitate money laundering and terrorist financing.³

The regulation requires AML programs to include, at minimum:

1. **Policies, procedures, and internal controls** reasonably designed to assure BSA compliance
2. **Designated compliance officer** to assure day-to-day compliance
3. **Education and training** of appropriate personnel, including detection of suspicious transactions
4. **Independent review** to monitor and maintain an adequate program⁴

**Risk-Based Approach:** The program "shall be commensurate with the risks posed by the location and size of, and the nature and volume of the financial services provided by, the money services business."⁵

**Database Provenance:** [31 C.F.R. § 1022.210](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-31/subtitle-B/chapter-X/part-1022/subpart-B/section-1022.210) [VERIFIED via eCFR, accessed Jan 2, 2025]

#### 3. Suspicious Activity Reporting Requirements

**31 C.F.R. § 1022.320 - SAR Filing Obligations**

**30-Day Filing Deadline:** A money services business must file each SAR **no later than 30 calendar days** after the date of initial detection of facts that may constitute a basis for filing.⁶

**Immediate Notification:** In situations involving violations requiring immediate attention (such as ongoing money laundering schemes), the MSB must **immediately notify by telephone** an appropriate law enforcement authority in addition to filing a SAR.⁷

**Record Retention:** MSBs must maintain a copy of any SAR filed and supporting documentation for **five years** from the date of filing.⁸

**Confidentiality:** No MSB or its personnel may disclose a SAR or any information that would reveal the existence of a SAR.⁹

**Database Provenance:** [31 C.F.R. § 1022.320](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-31/subtitle-B/chapter-X/part-1022/subpart-C/section-1022.320) [VERIFIED via eCFR, accessed Jan 2, 2025]

#### 4. Customer Due Diligence (CDD) Rule

**31 C.F.R. § 1010.230 - Beneficial Ownership Requirements**

Covered financial institutions must establish written procedures reasonably designed to identify and verify beneficial owners of legal entity customers.¹⁰

**Two-Pronged Test:**

- **Ownership Prong:** Each individual who owns 25% or more of equity interests (up to 4 individuals)
- **Control Prong:** One individual with significant responsibility to control, manage, or direct the legal entity¹¹

All legal entity customers will have between **1 and 5 beneficial owners** total.¹²

**Required Information:** Name, date of birth, address, and identification number for each beneficial owner.¹³

**Database Provenance:** [31 C.F.R. § 1010.230](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-31/subtitle-B/chapter-X/part-1010/subpart-B/section-1010.230) [VERIFIED via eCFR, accessed Jan 2, 2025]

#### 5. FinCEN Cryptocurrency Guidance

**FIN-2019-G001 (May 9, 2019) - Application to CVC Business Models**

FinCEN's comprehensive guidance consolidated regulations, guidance, and administrative rulings relating to money transmission involving convertible virtual currencies (CVCs).¹⁴

**Key Principle:** Businesses or individuals involved with CVCs are money transmitters subject to the **same registration requirements** and anti-money laundering, recordkeeping, and reporting responsibilities as other money services businesses.¹⁵

**Regulatory Certainty:** The guidance does not establish new regulatory expectations but clarifies application of existing MSB rules to cryptocurrency platforms.¹⁶

**Companion Advisory:** FinCEN issued FIN-2019-A003 on Illicit Activity Involving CVCs to assist financial institutions in identifying and reporting suspicious activity related to criminal exploitation of cryptocurrencies.¹⁷

**Database Provenance:** [FinCEN Guidance FIN-2019-G001](https://www.fincen.gov/sites/default/files/2019-05/FinCEN%20Guidance%20CVC%20FINAL%20508.pdf) [VERIFIED via FinCEN.gov, accessed Jan 2, 2025]

### B. Civil Penalty Framework for BSA Violations

**31 U.S.C. § 5321 - Civil Penalties**

#### 1. Willful Violations

For **willful violations** of the BSA by a domestic financial institution or money services business, the civil penalty is **not more than the greater of:**

- The amount involved in the transaction (not to exceed **$100,000**), OR
- **$25,000** per violation¹⁸

**Willfulness Standard:** To establish willfulness, the government need only show the institution acted with either **reckless disregard** or **willful blindness**.¹⁹

#### 2. Negligent Violations

For **negligent violations**, the penalty may not exceed **$500** per violation.²⁰

For a **pattern of negligent violations**, FinCEN may impose an additional penalty of not more than **$50,000**.²¹

#### 3. Penalties for Specific Violations

**Section 5318(a)(2) Violations (CIP/KYC):** A separate violation occurs **for each day** the violation continues and at **each office, branch, or place of business** where the violation occurs.²²

**SAR Filing Violations:**
- Willful failure to file: **$25,000-$100,000** per violation
- Unauthorized SAR disclosure: Up to **$100,000** civil penalty per violation and **$250,000 criminal** penalty plus imprisonment up to **five years**²³

**MSB Registration Violations:** Up to **$5,000** per violation; each day a violation continues constitutes a separate violation.²⁴

#### 4. Inflation Adjustments

The Federal Civil Penalties Inflation Adjustment Act mandates annual inflation adjustments. For penalties assessed after August 1, 2016, FinCEN sets forth adjusted maximum penalty amounts annually.²⁵

**Database Provenance:** [31 U.S.C. § 5321](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/31/5321) [VERIFIED via Cornell LII, accessed Jan 2, 2025]

### D. Transaction Monitoring System Requirements and Alert Backlog Analysis

#### 1. FinCEN Expectations for Transaction Monitoring

**Five Key Components of Effective Monitoring:**

Effective suspicious activity monitoring and reporting systems must include:

1. **Identification or alert of unusual activity** (employee identification, law enforcement inquiries, referrals, transaction/surveillance monitoring output)
2. **Managing alerts** (investigation, documentation, escalation)
3. **SAR decision-making** (threshold determination, filing decision)
4. **SAR completion and filing** (within 30-day deadline)
5. **Monitoring system on-going evaluation** (effectiveness assessment, tuning)⁴³

**Risk-Based Approach:** The extent and parameters for monitoring should be **commensurate with the level of money laundering and terrorist financing risk** of the specific institution.⁴⁴

**Automated vs. Manual:** Monitoring typically includes employee identification, transaction-based manual systems, automated surveillance systems, or any combination.⁴⁵
