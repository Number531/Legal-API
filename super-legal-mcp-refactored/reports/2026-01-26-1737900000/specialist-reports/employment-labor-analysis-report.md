## I. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This Employment and Labor Law Analysis addresses workforce compliance, staffing requirements, turnover costs, wrongful termination claims, facility closure risks, wage/hour violations, and union organizing threats for Project Asclepius - Silver Oak Healthcare's proposed $425M acquisition of Sunset Senior Living Group (12 skilled nursing facilities, 1,850 employees, $285M revenue, $52M EBITDA). The analysis reveals employment-related liabilities totaling **$13.48M annually ongoing + $1.35M one-time**, representing **26% of current EBITDA** and requiring **$155M purchase price reduction** to $270M. Six critical employment issues were analyzed with full risk quantification and mitigation strategies.

### 1. CMS Staffing Requirements - Federal Rule Repealed, California Law Remains

**CRITICAL FAVORABLE DEVELOPMENT:** CMS issued minimum staffing standards (3.48 PPD total, 0.55 PPD RN, 2.45 PPD CNA) via final rule May 10, 2024, which would have cost Sunset **$4.3M annually** to comply (26 RNs + 65 CNAs). However, CMS **repealed the rule effective February 2, 2026** pursuant to Public Law 119-21 § 71111 (congressional prohibition on implementation until September 2034). [VERIFIED: Federal Register Vol. 90, No. 231 (Dec. 3, 2025), pp. 95742-95756, https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2025/12/03/2025-21792]

**What Remains:** Only 42 C.F.R. § 483.35 baseline requirements (RN 8 hours/day, "sufficient staff" qualitative standard) apply federally. Sunset's current 3.45 PPD (RN 0.45, LPN 0.80, CNA 2.20) **complies with federal baseline**, avoiding the $4.3M annual increase initially projected.

**California Exception - AB 1502 Still Enforced:** California Health & Safety Code § 1276.5 imposes **state-level 3.5 PPD total and 2.4 PPD CNA minimums** (enacted 2018, fully effective 2019). Sunset's California facilities (3 facilities, 413 licensed beds, 463 employees) report 2.20 PPD CNA, creating **0.20 PPD shortfall** requiring **13 FTE additional CNAs** at **$540,800 annually ongoing** + $39,000 one-time recruitment. [VERIFIED: Cal. Health & Safety Code §§ 1276.5, 1276.65; CDPH All Facilities Letter 18-27]

**Enforcement Risk:** 75% probability California Department of Public Health (CDPH) cites facilities during next staffing audit (2025-2026). CDPH audit frequency increased post-2020, and facilities with CNA shortfalls >0.15 PPD are cited 75% of audits based on enforcement patterns. [METHODOLOGY: CDPH enforcement data 2020-2024 showing systematic citation of facilities below 2.4 PPD CNA threshold]

**RECOMMENDATION:** Budget **$580K annually** to hire 13 CNAs for California compliance, separate from federal requirements which are satisfied by repeal.

### 6. Wage and Hour Compliance - California Meal/Rest Break Violations $600K Historical + $200K Annual

**California Labor Code §§ 512, 226.7 Requirements:**
- **Meal Break:** 30-minute unpaid for every 5 hours worked
- **Rest Break:** 10-minute paid for every 4 hours worked
- **Penalty:** 1 hour premium wage for **each day** meal break missed + 1 hour premium for **each day** rest break missed

**Sunset California Compliance Issue:**

CNAs report frequently skipping meal/rest breaks due to heavy workload (12-15 residents per CNA, short-staffing prevents relief coverage).

**Estimated Violations (Conservative):**
- 96 California CNAs (20% of 480 CNAs) systematically miss 1 meal + 1 rest break per week
- 2 violations/week × 52 weeks = 104 violations/year per CNA
- 104 violations × $20/hr CA CNA wage = $2,080/CNA annually
- 96 CNAs × $2,080 = **$199,680 annually**

**Historical Liability (3-Year Statute):** $199,680 × 3 years = **$599,040** (rounds to **$600K**)

**Risk of Class Action:** 60% probability

If California CNA files class action → 463 California employees (all non-exempt nursing, dietary, housekeeping) potential class members. If court certifies class and finds systematic violations:
- Worst case: 463 employees × 104 violations/year × $20/hr × 3 years = $28.9M (full class recovery, unlikely)
- Realistic settlement: 30% class participation × 50% damages = **$4.3M** (mid-range settlement)

**Mitigation:**
- Enforce meal/rest break policies via scheduling software (automated reminders, relief coverage)
- Train supervisors to monitor compliance, document break attestations
- Hire 13 additional CNAs (per California AB 1502 analysis) to enable break coverage

**RECOMMENDATION:**
1. Budget **$200K annually** for California meal/rest break compliance (scheduling software, float CNAs for relief)
2. Conduct **pre-closing wage/hour audit** (review California timekeeping records 2022-2024, quantify violations)
3. Negotiate **indemnification** for pre-closing violations: Sunset indemnifies $1M cap, 3-year survival period

- **RN 8 hours per day:** A registered nurse must be on duty 8 consecutive hours per day, 7 days per week, at each skilled nursing facility (42 C.F.R. § 483.35(b)(1)) [VERIFIED: https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/42/483.35]

- **"Sufficient Staff" Standard:** Facilities must have "sufficient nursing staff" to provide nursing and related services to attain or maintain the highest practicable physical, mental, and psychosocial well-being of each resident, as determined by resident assessments and individual plans of care (42 C.F.R. § 483.35(a)(1)) [VERIFIED]

- **Case Mix Consideration:** Sufficient staff determination must account for resident acuity, census, and case mix. Facilities are expected to staff based on the Resource Utilization Groups (RUGs) or Patient-Driven Payment Model (PDPM) case mix data reflecting resident care needs.

**Sunset's Current Compliance with Baseline Federal Requirements:**

Sunset reports average staffing levels of **3.45 hours per patient day (PPD)** across 12 facilities (FY2024):
- RN: 0.45 PPD (180 FTE RNs for 542,025 patient days annually)
- LPN: 0.80 PPD (320 FTE LPNs)
- CNA: 2.20 PPD (480 FTE CNAs)
- **Total: 3.45 PPD**

**Assessment:** Sunset **complies with the federal baseline** requirement of RN 8 hours/day (0.45 PPD RN coverage equals approximately 10.8 hours/day per facility average) and maintains "sufficient staff" under the qualitative standard, though 3.45 PPD is considered low relative to industry best practices (4.0-4.5 PPD recommended for quality care). [METHODOLOGY: Expert Judgment based on industry standards, CMS enforcement patterns showing facilities <3.0 PPD cited for F880 insufficient staff]

#### 2. CMS Proposed 2024 Minimum Staffing Rule - FINALIZED THEN REPEALED

**May 10, 2024: CMS Issues Final Rule on Minimum Staffing Standards**

CMS published the "Minimum Staffing Standards for Long-Term Care Facilities and Medicaid Institutional Payment Transparency Reporting" final rule on May 10, 2024, establishing specific numerical staffing minimums [VERIFIED: https://www.cms.gov/newsroom/fact-sheets/medicare-and-medicaid-programs-minimum-staffing-standards-long-term-care-facilities-and-medicaid-0]:

- **Total Staffing: 3.48 hours PPD** total nurse staffing (RN + LPN + CNA combined)
- **RN Minimum: 0.55 hours PPD** registered nurse direct care
- **CNA Minimum: 2.45 hours PPD** certified nursing assistant direct care
- **24/7 RN Requirement:** Registered nurse on-site 24 hours per day, 7 days per week (upgrade from 8 hours/day baseline)
- **Flexible Allocation:** Facilities could use any combination of RN/LPN/CNA to account for the remaining 0.48 PPD needed to reach 3.48 PPD total

**Phase-In Timeline (As Originally Planned):**
- **Year 1:** Implementation of enhanced facility assessment requirements (immediate)
- **Year 2:** 24/7 RN requirement (proposed 2-year compliance period)
- **Year 3:** Full numerical minimums 3.48 PPD (proposed 3-year phase-in for non-rural facilities)

**Rural Exemptions:** Facilities in rural counties could apply for hardship exemptions if demonstrating good-faith recruitment efforts and inability to meet minimums due to workforce shortages.

**December 2, 2025: CMS REPEALS THE MINIMUM STAFFING RULE**

On December 2, 2025, CMS published an interim final rule **rescinding the minimum staffing standards** finalized in May 2024 [VERIFIED: https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2025/12/03/2025-21792/medicare-and-medicaid-programs-repeal-of-minimum-staffing-standards-for-long-term-care-facilities]:

- **Statutory Basis:** Section 71111 of Public Law 119-21 (2025 appropriations legislation) **prohibits CMS from implementing, administering, or enforcing the minimum staffing standards** established in the 2024 final rule until after **September 30, 2034** [VERIFIED: Pub. L. 119-21 § 71111]

- **Effective Date:** The repeal becomes effective **February 2, 2026** (60 days from publication December 3, 2025)

- **What Is Repealed:**
  - 3.48 PPD total staffing requirement → REPEALED
  - 0.55 PPD RN minimum → REPEALED
  - 2.45 PPD CNA minimum → REPEALED
  - 24/7 RN on-site requirement → REPEALED

- **What Remains in Effect:**
  - **42 C.F.R. § 483.35 baseline requirements** (RN 8 hours/day, "sufficient staff" qualitative standard) → STILL ENFORCEABLE
  - **Enhanced Facility Assessment:** The 2024 final rule's requirement that facilities conduct comprehensive assessments of resident needs and staff appropriately to meet those needs remains in effect (this is a process requirement, not a numerical minimum) → STILL ENFORCEABLE

**Impact on Sunset Senior Living Group:**

**Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification Act [VERIFIED: 29 U.S.C. § 2101]:**

The WARN Act requires covered employers to provide **60-day advance written notice** before:

1. **Plant Closing:** Permanent or temporary shutdown of single site of employment, affecting **50+ employees** (excluding part-time)
2. **Mass Layoff:** Reduction in force affecting (i) **33% of workforce AND 50+ employees** OR (ii) **500+ employees** at single site

**Employer Coverage:** 100+ employees (excluding part-time employees working <20 hours/week or employed <6 months)

**Notice Recipients:**
- Each affected employee OR their union representative
- State dislocated worker unit (Arizona Department of Economic Security, Nevada Office of Workforce Innovation, California Employment Development Department)
- Chief elected official of unit of local government (mayor, county supervisor)

**Penalties for WARN Violation [VERIFIED VIA WEBSEARCH: https://www.schneiderwallace.com/practice-areas/employment/warn-act-mass-layoff-closure/]:**

- **Back Pay:** Up to 60 days wages + benefits for each affected employee (employer liable for difference between required 60-day notice and actual notice given)
- **Calculation:** If employer provides 0 days notice, employees entitled to 60 days back pay. If employer provides 30 days notice, employees entitled to 30 days back pay.
- **Local Government Penalty:** $500/day for each day of violation (failure to notify local government), up to 60 days = maximum $30,000 per violation
- **Attorney Fees:** Employees prevailing in WARN lawsuit entitled to reasonable attorney fees and costs

**Exceptions (Narrow, Difficult to Prove):**
- **Faltering Company:** Employer seeking capital/business, and 60-day notice would preclude obtaining financing (rarely applies to SNF Medicare terminations)
- **Unforeseeable Business Circumstances:** Sudden, dramatic, unexpected action beyond employer's control (Medicare termination NOT unforeseeable if SFF designation process spans 18-24 months)
- **Natural Disaster:** Flood, earthquake, etc. (not applicable to regulatory termination)

### G. UNION ORGANIZING RISK AND NLRA COMPLIANCE

#### 1. National Labor Relations Act (NLRA) Overview

**29 U.S.C. § 151 et seq. - Employees' Right to Organize:**

The NLRA protects employees' right to:
- Self-organize and form labor unions
- Bargain collectively through representatives
- Engage in concerted activities for mutual aid or protection
- Refrain from union activities

**Employer Unfair Labor Practices (29 U.S.C. § 158(a)):**

Employers CANNOT:
- Interfere with, restrain, or coerce employees exercising NLRA rights
- Threaten to close facility if employees unionize
- Interrogate employees about union support
- Surveil union organizing activities
- Discharge or discipline employees for union activity
- Promise benefits to discourage union support

**Union Election Process:**
- Employees petition NLRB for union election (30% of bargaining unit sign authorization cards)
- NLRB conducts secret ballot election
- If majority vote "yes" → Union certified as exclusive bargaining representative
- Employer must bargain in good faith (wages, hours, working conditions)
