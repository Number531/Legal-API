### B. Critical Deal Protections

This section provides complete draft contract language for seven essential transaction protections that MUST be incorporated into the definitive purchase agreement. #### 1. Purchase Price Adjustment Mechanism

The aggregate exposure of $1,962.5M (109% of purchase price) necessitates a comprehensive purchase price adjustment mechanism that provides dollar-for-dollar true-ups for adverse regulatory outcomes that exceed baseline assumptions. The adjustment mechanism below establishes clear thresholds tied to expected values from the probabilistic risk analysis, creates objective measurement criteria, and provides escrow mechanics to ensure funds remain available for adjustments.

**ARTICLE 2: PURCHASE PRICE**

**Section 2.1 Base Purchase Price.**

The aggregate purchase price for 100% of the issued and outstanding membership interests of CryptoTrade Exchange LLC (the "Interests") shall be One Billion Eight Hundred Million Dollars ($1,800,000,000) (the "Base Purchase Price"), subject to adjustment as set forth in Section 2.3 below.

**Section 2.2 Payment at Closing.**

At Closing, Purchaser shall deliver to Seller:

(a) **Cash Payment**: Nine Hundred Fifty-Seven Million Five Hundred Thousand Dollars ($957,500,000) by wire transfer of immediately available funds; and

(b) **Escrow Deposits**: Five Hundred Forty-Two Million Five Hundred Thousand Dollars ($542,500,000) deposited into escrow accounts as follows:

- (i) **General Regulatory Escrow**: Four Hundred Fifty Million Dollars ($450,000,000) deposited with Escrow Agent pursuant to Regulatory Escrow Agreement;

- (ii) **Litigation Escrow**: Seventeen Million Five Hundred Thousand Dollars ($17,500,000) deposited with Escrow Agent pursuant to Litigation Escrow Agreement;

- (iii) **Criminal Investigation Reserve**: Seventy-Five Million Dollars ($75,000,000) deposited with Escrow Agent pursuant to Criminal Investigation Escrow Agreement.

(c) **Total Escrowed Amount**: The aggregate escrowed amounts under Section 2.2(b) equal $542,500,000, representing 30.1% of the Base Purchase Price.

**Section 2.3 Regulatory Exposure Purchase Price Adjustment.**

(a) **Adjustment Triggers**. The Base Purchase Price shall be reduced dollar-for-dollar (without duplication) for the following Adverse Regulatory Outcomes occurring during the Adjustment Period (defined as 24 months following Closing Date):

- (i) **SEC Settlement Adjustment**: If the Company enters into settlement with the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission regarding the Wells Notice dated October 2024, or SEC files enforcement action resulting in judgment, consent decree, or settlement, for aggregate amount exceeding Four Hundred Million Dollars ($400,000,000), then the Base Purchase Price shall be reduced by 100% of the amount by which such SEC Settlement Amount exceeds $400,000,000, up to maximum adjustment of Six Hundred Ten Million Dollars (-$610,000,000);

- (ii) **NY BitLicense Capital Adjustment**: If the New York State Department of Financial Services (NYDFS) requires capital injection exceeding Fifty Million Dollars ($50,000,000) as condition of BitLicense approval or continued operation, then the Base Purchase Price shall be reduced by 100% of the amount by which such Capital Requirement exceeds $50,000,000, up to maximum adjustment of One Hundred Million Dollars (-$100,000,000);

- (iii) **CFTC Settlement Adjustment**: If the Company enters into settlement with the U.S. Commodity Futures Trading Commission regarding unregistered Futures Commission Merchant operations for amount exceeding Ten Million Dollars ($10,000,000), then the Base Purchase Price shall be reduced by 100% of the amount by which such CFTC Settlement exceeds $10,000,000, up to maximum adjustment of Fifteen Million Dollars (-$15,000,000);

- (iv) **State Licensing Penalty Adjustment**: If any State financial regulator (excluding New York) imposes civil monetary penalties, cease-and-desist orders requiring operational shutdown in such State, or license revocation, with aggregate financial impact exceeding Five Million Dollars ($5,000,000), then the Base Purchase Price shall be reduced by 100% of the amount by which such State Penalties exceed $5,000,000, up to maximum adjustment of Twenty Million Dollars (-$20,000,000);

- (v) **Token Classification Revenue Loss Adjustment**: If the Company is required by regulatory order or settlement to delist digital assets representing annual recurring revenue exceeding Forty Million Dollars ($40,000,000), measured on trailing twelve-month basis as of delisting date, then the Base Purchase Price shall be reduced by 5× the amount by which such Annual Revenue Loss exceeds $40,000,000, calculated as net present value over 10-year period at 8% discount rate, up to maximum adjustment of One Hundred Million Dollars (-$100,000,000);

- (vi) **Class Action Settlement Adjustment**: If any securities, consumer protection, or breach of contract class action litigation against the Company results in settlement, judgment, or arbitration award exceeding Fifteen Million Dollars ($15,000,000), then the Base Purchase Price shall be reduced by 100% of the amount by which such Litigation Settlement exceeds $15,000,000, up to maximum adjustment of Twenty Million Dollars (-$20,000,000);

- (vii) **Insurance Denial Adjustment**: If the Company's cyber insurance claim relating to the September 18, 2024 hot wallet security incident results in insurance payout less than Twenty-Two Million Four Hundred Thousand Dollars ($22,400,000), then the Base Purchase Price shall be reduced by 100% of the amount by which the insurance recovery falls short of $22,400,000, up to maximum adjustment of Twenty-Five Million Dollars (-$25,000,000).

(b) **Maximum Aggregate Downward Adjustment**. Notwithstanding Section 2.3(a), the maximum aggregate purchase price adjustment under all subsections (i) through (vii) shall not exceed Nine Hundred Forty-Five Million Dollars (-$945,000,000), such that the Adjusted Purchase Price shall not be reduced below Eight Hundred Fifty-Five Million Dollars ($855,000,000).

(c) **Adjustment Calculation and Payment**.

- (i) Within fifteen (15) Business Days following the earlier of (A) the expiration of the Adjustment Period or (B) the date on which all Adjustment Triggers have been finally resolved through settlement, judgment, or regulatory determination, Purchaser shall deliver to Seller a written statement (the "Adjustment Statement") setting forth Purchaser's calculation of the Adjusted Purchase Price, together with supporting documentation including copies of settlement agreements, consent decrees, regulatory orders, court judgments, insurance denial letters, and financial records demonstrating revenue loss.

- (ii) Seller shall have thirty (30) days following receipt of the Adjustment Statement to review and either (A) accept the calculation by written notice to Purchaser, or (B) dispute the calculation by delivering written notice specifying the basis for dispute with reasonable particularity.

- (iii) If Seller disputes the Adjustment Statement, Purchaser and Seller shall negotiate in good faith for fifteen (15) days to resolve the dispute. If the parties cannot reach agreement within such fifteen-day period, the dispute shall be submitted to the Independent Accounting Firm designated in Section 2.3(c)(iv) for binding determination.

- (iv) The "Independent Accounting Firm" shall be [Big Four Accounting Firm] or, if such firm declines to serve, another nationally recognized accounting firm mutually agreed by Purchaser and Seller. The Independent Accounting Firm shall act as expert (not arbitrator) and shall determine only those line items in dispute. The Independent Accounting Firm's determination shall be final, binding, and non-appealable.

- (v) Fees and expenses of the Independent Accounting Firm shall be allocated between Purchaser and Seller in proportion to the relative amounts by which each party's position differs from the Independent Accounting Firm's final determination.

(d) **Payment Mechanics**.

- (i) If the Adjusted Purchase Price is less than the Base Purchase Price due to one or more Adjustment Triggers, then the purchase price adjustment amount shall first be applied against the applicable Escrow Account(s) as follows:

  - (A) SEC Settlement Adjustments and Token Classification Revenue Loss Adjustments shall be applied against the General Regulatory Escrow;

  - (B) Class Action Settlement Adjustments and Insurance Denial Adjustments shall be applied against the Litigation Escrow;

  - (C) All other adjustments shall be applied against the General Regulatory Escrow.

- (ii) If the required purchase price adjustment exceeds the amounts then remaining in the applicable Escrow Account(s), Purchaser may draw first from any other Escrow Account with available funds, and second, may pursue indemnification claim against Seller pursuant to ARTICLE 8.

- (iii) If the required purchase price adjustment is less than the aggregate amounts in the Escrow Accounts at expiration of the Adjustment Period, the excess shall be released to Seller pro rata from each Escrow Account within five (5) Business Days following final determination of the Adjusted Purchase Price.

(e) **Illustrative Examples**.

**Example 1 (Optimistic Scenario — No Adjustment):**
- SEC settles for $260M (below $400M threshold) = $0 adjustment
- NY BitLicense requires $50M capital (at threshold) = $0 adjustment
- Token delisting affects $40M annual revenue (at threshold) = $0 adjustment
- **Result**: Full $542.5M escrow released to Seller; Seller receives total consideration of $1,800M

**Example 2 (Expected Scenario — Moderate Adjustment):**
- SEC settles for $540M ($140M above threshold) = -$140M adjustment
- NY BitLicense requires $141.5M capital ($91.5M above threshold) = -$91.5M adjustment
- Token delisting affects $54.82M annual revenue ($14.82M above threshold × 5× multiplier) = -$74.1M adjustment
- Class action settles for $17.5M ($2.5M above threshold) = -$2.5M adjustment
- **Total Adjustment**: -$308.1M
- **Result**: $308.1M retained from escrow by Purchaser; $234.4M released to Seller; Seller receives total consideration of $1,491.9M

**Example 3 (Conservative Scenario — Maximum Adjustment):**
- SEC settles for $910M ($510M above threshold, capped at -$610M) = -$610M adjustment
- NY BitLicense requires $150M capital ($100M above threshold) = -$100M adjustment
- Token delisting affects $85M annual revenue ($45M above threshold × 5× multiplier = -$225M, capped at -$100M) = -$100M adjustment
- CFTC settles for $18.6M ($8.6M above threshold) = -$8.6M adjustment
- Class action settles for $25M ($10M above threshold, capped at -$20M) = -$20M adjustment
- Insurance denied entirely ($22.4M shortfall, capped at -$25M) = -$22.4M adjustment
- **Total Adjustment Before Cap**: -$861M
- **Total Adjustment After Cap**: -$861M (below -$945M maximum)
- **Result**: $861M retained from escrow by Purchaser; $0 released to Seller from litigation and criminal escrows; Seller receives total consideration of $939M (after Purchaser claims against General Regulatory Escrow)

(f) **No Adjustment for Favorable Outcomes**. For clarity, if any Adverse Regulatory Outcome is resolved for amount BELOW the applicable threshold in Section 2.3(a), there shall be NO upward adjustment to the Base Purchase Price. The adjustment mechanism operates only to reduce the purchase price for adverse outcomes exceeding baseline assumptions; favorable outcomes inure solely to Purchaser's benefit.

#### 2. Escrow Structure with Milestone-Based Releases

The escrow structure balances three competing objectives: (1) providing seller with certainty regarding timing and conditions for release of escrowed funds; (2) ensuring sufficient funds remain available to cover reasonably anticipated regulatory settlements and litigation judgments; and (3) creating incentive for seller to cooperate in regulatory resolution processes post-closing.

**ARTICLE 3: ESCROW ARRANGEMENTS**

**Section 3.1 General Regulatory Escrow.**

(a) **Establishment and Initial Funding**. At Closing, Purchaser shall deposit Four Hundred Fifty Million Dollars ($450,000,000) with Escrow Agent (as defined in the Regulatory Escrow Agreement) to be held and released in accordance with the terms of this Section 3.1 and the Regulatory Escrow Agreement.

(b) **Release Schedule and Conditions**. The General Regulatory Escrow shall be released to Seller in five (5) tranches contingent upon achievement of the following milestones:

- **Tranche 1 (12 months post-Closing): $90,000,000 (20% of General Regulatory Escrow)**

  Release Conditions:
  - (i) No enforcement action filed by SEC relating to Wells Notice (filing of settlement agreement or consent decree does not constitute "enforcement action" for this purpose);
  - (ii) No criminal indictment filed by Department of Justice against the Company or any current or former officer, director, or employee relating to Company operations during Pre-Closing Period;
  - (iii) No license revocation, suspension, or operational cease-and-desist order issued by NYDFS relating to BitLicense;
  - (iv) No additional regulatory investigation initiated by any Federal or State agency (other than routine examinations) involving potential civil monetary penalties exceeding $5,000,000.

- **Tranche 2 (24 months post-Closing): $90,000,000 (20% of General Regulatory Escrow)**

  Release Conditions:
  - (i) All conditions for Tranche 1 remain satisfied;
  - (ii) If SEC has filed enforcement action or initiated settlement discussions, aggregate settlement discussions do not exceed $600,000,000 based on SEC's most recent settlement demand or Purchaser's reasonable good-faith estimate;
  - (iii) NY BitLicense application approved OR Company has implemented compliant capital structure to meet NYDFS requirements;
  - (iv) CFTC investigation (if any) resolved through settlement not exceeding $15,000,000 OR no enforcement action filed.

- **Tranche 3 (36 months post-Closing): $90,000,000 (20% of General Regulatory Escrow)**

  Release Conditions:
  - (i) All conditions for Tranche 2 remain satisfied;
  - (ii) SEC matter (if any) fully resolved through settlement, consent decree, or judgment for aggregate amount not exceeding $700,000,000;
  - (iii) No material adverse development in any ongoing regulatory investigation that would reasonably be expected to result in civil monetary penalties exceeding $25,000,000 in aggregate beyond amounts already accrued or reserved.

- **Tranche 4 (48 months post-Closing): $90,000,000 (20% of General Regulatory Escrow)**

  Release Conditions:
  - (i) All conditions for Tranche 3 remain satisfied;
  - (ii) FinCEN examination (if conducted) completed with aggregate civil monetary penalties (if any) not exceeding $5,000,000;
  - (iii) All State money transmitter licensing change-of-control applications approved (excluding any State representing less than 1% of Company revenue that denies approval);
  - (iv) OFAC matter relating to 248 transactions with Iranian nationals resolved through settlement not exceeding $500,000.

- **Tranche 5 (60 months post-Closing): $90,000,000 (20% of General Regulatory Escrow)**

  Release Conditions:
  - (i) All conditions for Tranche 4 remain satisfied;
  - (ii) All regulatory investigations, examinations, enforcement actions, and settlement obligations fully resolved with no remaining material contingent liabilities relating to Pre-Closing Period conduct;
  - (iii) No claims pending under the purchase price adjustment mechanism in Section 2.3 that would reasonably be expected to exceed remaining escrow amounts.

(c) **Retention for Purchase Price Adjustments**. Notwithstanding the release schedule in Section 3.1(b), Purchaser may issue written notice ("Adjustment Hold Notice") to Escrow Agent at any time during the Adjustment Period specifying that:

- (i) One or more Adjustment Triggers under Section 2.3(a) have occurred or are reasonably likely to occur within the following twelve (12) months; and

- (ii) A specific dollar amount (the "Adjustment Hold Amount") should be retained in the General Regulatory Escrow pending final resolution of such Adjustment Trigger(s).

Upon receipt of an Adjustment Hold Notice, Escrow Agent shall retain the Adjustment Hold Amount and release to Seller only the portion of any scheduled tranche that exceeds the Adjustment Hold Amount. The Adjustment Hold Amount shall be released to Seller or applied to purchase price adjustments in accordance with Section 2.3(d).

(d) **Interest and Investment of Escrow Funds**. Escrow Agent shall invest the General Regulatory Escrow in [short-term U.S. Treasury securities / money market funds with average maturity not exceeding 90 days]. All interest, dividends, and other investment earnings shall be allocated pro rata between Purchaser and Seller based on the ultimate disposition of the principal amounts.

(e) **Fees and Expenses**. All fees, expenses, and charges of the Escrow Agent shall be borne equally (50%/50%) by Purchaser and Seller.

**Section 3.2 Litigation Escrow.**

(a) **Establishment and Initial Funding**. At Closing, Purchaser shall deposit Seventeen Million Five Hundred Thousand Dollars ($17,500,000) with Escrow Agent to be held and released in accordance with the terms of this Section 3.2 and the Litigation Escrow Agreement.

(b) **Purpose**. The Litigation Escrow is established to secure (i) settlement payments, judgments, or arbitration awards in the class action litigation captioned *Chen, et al. v. CryptoTrade Exchange LLC*, Case No. 1:24-cv-08642 (S.D.N.Y. filed October 15, 2024) relating to the September 18, 2024 hot wallet security incident; and (ii) potential adverse determination in the Company's insurance coverage dispute with [Cyber Insurance Carrier] regarding the $37,000,000 claim relating to the same incident.

(c) **Release Schedule and Conditions**. The Litigation Escrow shall be released as follows:

- **Tranche 1 (upon final resolution of class action): $15,000,000 maximum**

  - (i) If class action settles, is dismissed with prejudice, or results in final judgment following trial, Escrow Agent shall pay directly to the settlement administrator, plaintiffs' counsel, or judgment creditor the amount required under the settlement agreement or judgment, not to exceed $15,000,000.

  - (ii) Any amount of Tranche 1 not applied to class action resolution shall be released to Seller within ten (10) Business Days following final resolution.

  - (iii) If class action settlement or judgment exceeds $15,000,000, Seller shall have no obligation to fund any excess; Purchaser acknowledges that recovery for amounts exceeding $15,000,000 may be pursued only through indemnification claim under ARTICLE 8.

- **Tranche 2 (upon final resolution of insurance coverage dispute): $2,500,000 maximum**

  - (i) If insurance coverage is denied (in whole or in part) and Company elects to pursue bad faith litigation against the insurer, Escrow Agent shall disburse to Company's litigation counsel reasonable attorneys' fees and expenses, not to exceed $2,500,000 in aggregate.

  - (ii) If insurance coverage is approved for amount equal to or exceeding $30,000,000, or if Company elects not to pursue bad faith litigation following partial or full denial, any remaining portion of Tranche 2 shall be released to Seller within ten (10) Business Days.

(d) **Release Deadline**. Any amounts remaining in the Litigation Escrow on the date that is thirty-six (36) months following the Closing Date shall be released to Seller within ten (10) Business Days, except to the extent: (i) litigation or insurance dispute remains pending and unresolved as of such date, in which case the specifically applicable Tranche shall continue to be held until final resolution; or (ii) Purchaser has delivered Adjustment Hold Notice pursuant to Section 3.1(c) specifically referencing the Class Action Settlement Adjustment or Insurance Denial Adjustment under Section 2.3(a)(vi) or (vii).

**Section 3.3 Criminal Investigation Reserve.**

(a) **Establishment and Initial Funding**. At Closing, Purchaser shall deposit Seventy-Five Million Dollars ($75,000,000) with Escrow Agent to be held and released in accordance with the terms of this Section 3.3 and the Criminal Investigation Escrow Agreement.

(b) **Purpose**. The Criminal Investigation Reserve is established to secure potential (i) criminal fines, penalties, restitution, or forfeiture in the event the Department of Justice files criminal charges against the Company relating to Bank Secrecy Act violations (31 U.S.C. § 5322), unlicensed money transmission (18 U.S.C. § 1960), or money laundering (18 U.S.C. § 1956); (ii) costs of criminal defense if such charges are filed; and (iii) civil monetary penalties or settlement payments to FinCEN relating to AML/CFT compliance deficiencies.

(c) **Release Schedule and Conditions**.

- **Tranche 1 (24 months post-Closing): $25,000,000 (33% of Criminal Investigation Reserve)**

  Release Conditions:
  - (i) No criminal charges filed by DOJ against the Company or any current or former officer, director, or employee relating to Company operations during Pre-Closing Period;
  - (ii) No grand jury subpoena issued to the Company (other than the existing FBI investigation of 18 customers suspected of romance scam participation, which relates to customer conduct rather than Company conduct);
  - (iii) FinCEN examination (if conducted) completed with no referral to DOJ for criminal prosecution.

- **Tranche 2 (36 months post-Closing): $25,000,000 (33% of Criminal Investigation Reserve)**

  Release Conditions:
  - (i) All conditions for Tranche 1 remain satisfied;
  - (ii) IRS Criminal Investigation Division John Doe summons resolved through civil tax assessments with no criminal tax prosecution initiated;
  - (iii) Statute of limitations for BSA criminal violations (5 years under 18 U.S.C. § 3282) has expired for any conduct occurring more than 60 months prior to the Closing Date.

- **Tranche 3 (48 months post-Closing): $25,000,000 (33% of Criminal Investigation Reserve)**

  Release Conditions:
  - (i) All conditions for Tranche 2 remain satisfied;
  - (ii) All statutes of limitations for potential criminal violations relating to Pre-Closing Period conduct have expired;
  - (iii) No ongoing criminal investigation or prosecution relating to Pre-Closing Period conduct.

(d) **Application to Criminal Defense Costs**. If DOJ files criminal charges against the Company relating to Pre-Closing Period conduct at any time prior to expiration of the Criminal Investigation Reserve release schedule, Purchaser may direct Escrow Agent to disburse from the Criminal Investigation Reserve reasonable and documented attorneys' fees, expert witness fees, and litigation expenses incurred in defense of such charges, up to maximum aggregate amount of Thirty-Five Million Dollars ($35,000,000). (e) **Application to Criminal Fines and Penalties**. Seller shall have no obligation to fund any criminal fines or penalties exceeding the $75,000,000 Criminal Investigation Reserve amount.

#### 3. Representations & Warranties Insurance

Given the magnitude and complexity of regulatory exposure (particularly the $562.5M expected SEC settlement exposure and 15-20% criminal prosecution risk), representations and warranties insurance serves as critical additional protection beyond the escrow structure. The R&W insurance policy must include several non-standard features tailored to digital asset regulatory risks.

**ARTICLE 4: REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES INSURANCE**

**Section 4.1 R&W Insurance Policy Procurement.**

(a) **Obligation to Procure**. Prior to Closing, Purchaser shall procure, at Purchaser's sole cost and expense, a buyer-side representations and warranties insurance policy (the "R&W Insurance Policy") with the following minimum terms:

- (i) **Policy Limit**: Not less than Two Hundred Fifty Million Dollars ($250,000,000) in aggregate coverage;

- (ii) **Retention (Deductible)**: Not more than Eighteen Million Dollars ($18,000,000) (representing 1.0% of Base Purchase Price);

- (iii) **Policy Period**: Not less than six (6) years from Closing Date for representations related to regulatory compliance, litigation, and governmental investigations; and not less than seven (7) years for tax-related representations;

- (iv) **Securities Enforcement Sublimit**: Dedicated sublimit of not less than One Hundred Million Dollars ($100,000,000) specifically covering losses arising from SEC enforcement actions, consent decrees, disgorgement, penalties, and prejudgment interest relating to (A) operation of unregistered national securities exchange, (B) operation as unregistered broker-dealer, (C) offers and sales of unregistered securities, and (D) operation of unregistered securities clearing agency;

- (v) **Regulatory Compliance Sublimit**: Dedicated sublimit of not less than Seventy-Five Million Dollars ($75,000,000) specifically covering losses arising from (A) FinCEN AML/CFT compliance violations, (B) OFAC sanctions violations, (C) state money transmitter licensing violations, (D) CFTC commodity derivatives violations, and (E) criminal fines and penalties if DOJ files charges relating to Pre-Closing Period conduct;

- (vi) **Knowledge Scrape Mitigation**: The R&W Insurance Policy shall not exclude coverage for matters disclosed in the Disclosure Schedules to this Agreement, except to the extent such matters involve actual fraud or intentional misrepresentation by Seller (the parties acknowledge that regulatory investigations disclosed in Disclosure Schedule 3.14 shall NOT be subject to knowledge exclusion).

(b) **Premium Allocation**. The premium for the R&W Insurance Policy shall be borne as follows:

- (i) Purchaser shall pay 100% of the premium for the base policy limit of $250,000,000 with $18,000,000 retention;

- (ii) If Purchaser elects to increase the policy limit above $250,000,000 or reduce the retention below $18,000,000, Purchaser shall pay 100% of the incremental premium for such enhanced coverage.

(c) **Policy Beneficiaries**. The R&W Insurance Policy shall name as insureds: (i) Purchaser, (ii) the Company (following Closing), (iii) Purchaser's lenders (if any) providing acquisition financing, and (iv) Purchaser's Affiliates that directly or indirectly own equity interests in Purchaser.

(d) **No Recourse Limitations**. The parties acknowledge and agree that:

- (i) Procurement of the R&W Insurance Policy shall NOT limit, reduce, or otherwise affect Purchaser's right to bring indemnification claims against Seller under ARTICLE 8 of this Agreement;

- (ii) Purchaser may elect, in Purchaser's sole discretion, whether to pursue recovery first from (A) the R&W Insurance Policy, (B) the Escrow Accounts, or (C) direct indemnification claim against Seller;

- (iii) Recovery from one source shall reduce Purchaser's total indemnifiable losses dollar-for-dollar but shall not waive Purchaser's right to pursue recovery from other sources for any shortfall;

- (iv) The R&W Insurance Policy shall expressly provide that the insurer waives all subrogation rights against Seller, except in cases of fraud or intentional misrepresentation.

**Section 4.2 Specific Coverage Requirements for Digital Asset Regulatory Risks.**

The R&W Insurance Policy shall specifically cover, without exclusion or sublimit reduction, losses arising from breach of the following representations and warranties:

(a) **Regulatory Compliance Representations (Section 3.14):**

- (i) Coverage for SEC enforcement relating to unregistered securities offerings, including disgorgement of profits, civil monetary penalties, and prejudgment interest;

- (ii) Coverage for CFTC enforcement relating to unregistered futures commission merchant operations;

- (iii) Coverage for FinCEN civil monetary penalties relating to Bank Secrecy Act violations, including SAR filing delays, transaction monitoring deficiencies, and independent testing failures;

- (iv) Coverage for OFAC civil monetary penalties relating to sanctions violations involving 248 transactions with 12 Iranian nationals totaling $1.8M between March 2022 and May 2023.

(b) **Licensing and Permits Representations (Section 3.16):**

- (i) Coverage for losses arising from operation in New York without BitLicense between 2019 and 2024, including civil monetary penalties, forced operational shutdown, and capital injection requirements to cure deficiencies;

- (ii) Coverage for state money transmitter licensing violations in 48 states, including change-of-control notification failures and bonding deficiencies.

(c) **Litigation Representations (Section 3.13):**

### E. Alternative Structures Considered

The transaction structure recommended in Sections V.B through V.D (equity purchase with multi-tier escrow, R&W insurance, and purchase price adjustment at $1.8B base price) represents the optimal balance of acquirer protection and seller economics given the $1,962.5M aggregate exposure. However, the deal team evaluated four alternative structures during due diligence, each of which was rejected for specific reasons detailed below.

#### Alternative 1: Asset Purchase (REJECTED)

**Structure Description**:

- Acquirer purchases substantially all assets of CryptoTrade Exchange LLC (customer contracts, technology platform, intellectual property, regulatory licenses) but does NOT purchase equity interests
- Seller retains liabilities relating to Pre-Closing Period, including SEC enforcement, CFTC settlement, FinCEN penalties, class action litigation, and tax obligations
- Purchase price: $1.2B-$1.4B (30% discount to equity purchase to compensate for operational complexity)

**Evaluation**:

**Advantages**:
- Clean break: Acquirer not directly liable for Pre-Closing Period regulatory enforcement or litigation
- Cherry-picking: Acquirer can exclude specific liabilities (e.g., hot wallet class action) from assumed liabilities schedule
- Tax efficiency: Acquirer obtains step-up in asset basis for tax depreciation purposes (vs. no step-up in equity purchase)

**Disadvantages** (FATAL FLAWS):

1. **Customer Contract Assignment Impracticable**: Asset purchase requires assignment of 8.4 million customer contracts (Terms of Service agreements); customer consent requirements vary by jurisdiction; customer contact rate for consent typically 30-40%; consent process would take 12-18 months with substantial customer churn

2. **Regulatory License Transfer Complexity**: State money transmitter licenses are entity-specific and generally non-transferable; asset purchase requires NEW license applications in 48 states (vs. change-of-control approvals in equity purchase); new license applications require 12-18 months processing in most states; Company would need to cease operations during license transfer period (commercially unworkable)

3. **Tax Inefficiency for Seller**: Asset sale triggers entity-level tax on gain (21% corporate tax rate) PLUS shareholder-level tax on liquidating distribution (20% qualified dividend rate); combined tax rate 36.4% vs. 20% capital gains rate in equity sale; tax inefficiency requires 15-20% price premium to make Seller economically whole, offsetting any benefit to acquirer

4. **Successor Liability Risk**: Despite clean legal separation, courts in regulatory enforcement contexts frequently apply "successor liability" doctrine if (i) acquirer continues same business, (ii) same management, (iii) same customers, and (iv) continuity of operations; SEC and FinCEN have asserted successor liability in digital asset enforcement cases (e.g., Poloniex acquisition by Circle)

**Rejection Rationale**: Customer contract assignment impracticability and state license transfer complexity make asset purchase operationally unworkable. Even if surmountable, successor liability risk eliminates primary advantage of asset purchase structure.

---

#### Alternative 2: Equity Purchase with Earnout (REJECTED at $1.8B base; see Alternative Recommendation)

**Structure Description**:

- Acquirer purchases 100% equity at reduced base purchase price ($800M-$1,000M)
- Seller receives contingent earnout payments based on achievement of operational milestones: (i) Year 1 EBITDA ≥$150M = $200M earnout, (ii) Year 2 EBITDA ≥$125M = $200M earnout, (iii) regulatory settlement at or below aggregate $600M = $300M earnout, (iv) no criminal charges filed within 36 months = $200M earnout
- Maximum total consideration: $800M base + $900M earnout = $1,700M (5% discount to $1.8B equity purchase)

**Evaluation**:

**Advantages**:
- Aligns seller incentives with favorable outcomes (regulatory settlements, operational performance)
- Reduces acquirer upfront cash requirement from $1,800M to $800M
- Shares risk between parties rather than placing 100% of downside on acquirer

**Disadvantages** (REJECTION FACTORS):

1. **Regulatory Uncertainty Makes Earnout Milestones Unworkable**: EBITDA earnout milestones are inherently uncertain given regulatory exposure; if SEC settlement is $910M (conservative scenario), pro forma EBITDA falls to negative $13M, making EBITDA earnouts impossible to achieve; seller has no ability to influence regulatory outcomes, making regulatory earnouts arbitrary

2. **Earnout Disputes are Frequent and Costly**: Earnout structures historically result in post-closing litigation in 30-40% of transactions; typical disputes involve: (i) EBITDA calculation methodology (what expenses are deductible?), (ii) acquirer operating decisions that impair earnout achievement (aggressive cost-cutting, product discontinuation), (iii) earnout payment timing and interest

3. **Earnout Accounting Treatment Unfavorable**: Under ASC 805 (Business Combinations), earnout liability must be recorded at fair value at acquisition date with subsequent changes in fair value recorded in earnings; volatility in earnout fair value creates earnings volatility for public company acquirers

4. **Seller Typically Rejects Earnout in Distressed Situations**: Earnout is seller-favorable structure in high-growth businesses with predictable revenue (e.g., SaaS acquisitions); earnout is seller-unfavorable in distressed regulatory situations where seller has no control over outcome; seller will demand higher expected value in earnout structure (e.g., $800M base + $900M earnout with 50% earnout probability = $1,250M expected value, vs. $1,200M cash-at-closing in alternative structure)

**Rejection Rationale at $1.8B Base Price**: Earnout structure inappropriate at current $1.8B valuation because regulatory uncertainty makes milestones unachievable. HOWEVER, earnout structure becomes ATTRACTIVE at substantially reduced base price ($150M-$200M) where earnouts represent upside optionality rather than core consideration. See Alternative Recommendation below.

#### Alternative 3: Stock Purchase with Holdback >30% (REJECTED)

**Structure Description**:

- Equity purchase with 40-50% of purchase price held back as seller financing or deferred payment
- Holdback amount: $720M-$900M (40-50% of $1.8B) held for 48-60 months
- Holdback released to Seller only if aggregate regulatory settlements and litigation judgments do NOT exceed $600M-$700M
- If regulatory exposure exceeds threshold, holdback is forfeited to acquirer

**Evaluation**:

**Advantages**:
- Provides acquirer with substantial cushion against regulatory exposure
- Reduces acquirer upfront cash requirement to $900M-$1,080M
- Economically efficient: holdback is "free" (no transaction costs, no insurance premiums, no escrow fees)

**Disadvantages** (REJECTION FACTORS):

1. **Seller Walkaway Threshold**: Industry standard for escrow/holdback in M&A transactions is 10-20% of purchase price; earnouts add another 10-20%; aggregate seller funds-at-risk typically does not exceed 30-35% without seller rejecting transaction; 40-50% holdback exceeds seller's walkaway threshold

2. **Seller Has Opportunity Cost**: $720M-$900M held for 48-60 months has time value of money cost; at 8% discount rate, present value of $720M received in 48 months is $523M (27% haircut); seller will demand price premium to compensate for time value, offsetting acquirer benefit

3. **Holdback Creates Dispute Magnet**: Unlike escrow (which has neutral third-party escrow agent and objective release conditions), holdback structures create acquirer-vs-seller disputes regarding: (i) whether regulatory settlements should be characterized as "relating to Pre-Closing Period" or "relating to Post-Closing Period," (ii) whether settlement was necessary or could have been avoided with different litigation strategy, (iii) calculation of "aggregate regulatory settlements" (does it include defense costs? compliance undertakings?)

4. **Seller Financing Creates Credit Risk**: If structured as seller financing (promissory note payable to Seller), acquirer's obligation to pay holdback is subject to acquirer's creditworthiness; if acquirer experiences financial distress, seller's recovery is impaired; seller typically demands parent company guarantee or letter of credit, increasing transaction costs

**Rejection Rationale**: 40-50% holdback exceeds seller's walkaway threshold; seller will demand price premium to compensate for time value and credit risk, making structure economically equivalent to lower base price with standard 25-30% escrow (which is the recommended structure).

#### Alternative 4: R&W Insurance Only (No Escrow, No Holdback) (REJECTED)

**Structure Description**:

- Equity purchase at $1.8B with 100% cash paid to Seller at Closing
- NO escrow, NO holdback, NO seller indemnification (except for fraud)
- Acquirer procures $500M R&W insurance policy to cover all representations and warranties breaches, including regulatory liabilities
- Seller achieves "clean exit" with no post-closing obligations

**Evaluation**:

**Advantages**:
- Seller receives 100% cash at Closing with no escrow or post-closing indemnification exposure ("seller's dream deal")
- Eliminates post-closing disputes between acquirer and seller regarding escrow releases or indemnification claims
- R&W insurance premium ($15M-$25M for $500M policy) is acquirer's cost, not shared expense

**Disadvantages** (REJECTION FACTORS - FATAL):

1. **R&W Insurance Market Capacity Constraint**: R&W insurance market capacity for KNOWN material exposures (e.g., pending SEC Wells Notice, ongoing CFTC investigation, disclosed class action) is LIMITED; insurers typically exclude known risks from coverage or sublimit coverage for disclosed matters; market capacity for SEC enforcement sublimit is approximately $100M-$150M (vs. $562.5M expected exposure); acquirer CANNOT obtain $500M R&W insurance policy covering disclosed regulatory matters

2. **R&W Insurance Does NOT Cover Punitive Damages or Criminal Fines**: Insurance policies exclude coverage for punitive damages, fines, and penalties as against public policy; SEC disgorgement and civil monetary penalties are insurable, but criminal fines under 31 U.S.C. § 5322 (BSA violations) or 18 U.S.C. § 1960 (unlicensed money transmission) are uninsurable; criminal investigation expected exposure ($28.25M) cannot be covered by R&W insurance

3. **R&W Insurance Claims Process is Slow**: R&W insurance claims typically take 12-24 months to resolve; insurer investigates claim, evaluates coverage, disputes causation and damages; acquirer needs IMMEDIATE access to funds to pay regulatory settlements (e.g., SEC settlement typically requires payment within 30 days of settlement execution); escrow provides immediate liquidity, R&W insurance does not

4. **Underwriting Diligence Burden**: R&W insurer will conduct independent underwriting diligence on regulatory matters, requiring 60-90 days and potentially uncovering additional adverse facts that impair transaction momentum; underwriter may decline to issue policy if regulatory exposure deemed too severe, killing deal

**Rejection Rationale**: R&W insurance market capacity is insufficient to cover $1,962.5M aggregate exposure; criminal fines uninsurable; claims process too slow for regulatory payment obligations. R&W insurance is SUPPLEMENTAL protection (as structured in recommended deal), not SOLE protection.

#### Selected Structure: Equity Purchase with Multi-Tier Escrow + R&W Insurance + Purchase Price Adjustment (RECOMMENDED)

**Rationale for Selection**:

The recommended structure combines three complementary protection mechanisms that collectively provide acquirer with adequate protection while remaining within seller's acceptance parameters:

1. **Multi-Tier Escrow ($542.5M, 30% of purchase price)**: Provides IMMEDIATE liquidity for regulatory settlements and litigation judgments; three separate escrow accounts (General Regulatory $450M, Litigation $17.5M, Criminal Investigation $75M) align funds with specific risks; milestone-based releases over 60 months align with regulatory resolution timeline; 30% escrow is at high end of market norms (10-20% typical) but justified given risk profile

2. **R&W Insurance ($250M policy with $100M SEC sublimit)**: Provides ADDITIONAL protection layer beyond escrow; covers representations and warranties breaches (including disclosed regulatory matters within sublimit); $18M retention (1% of purchase price) is commercially reasonable; insurance proceeds available if escrow insufficient

3. **Purchase Price Adjustment (up to -$945M)**: Provides LONG-TAIL protection for adverse outcomes exceeding baseline assumptions; dollar-for-dollar true-ups ensure acquirer not economically disadvantaged if SEC settles for $910M (conservative scenario) vs. $562.5M (expected scenario); adjustment mechanism objectively tied to observable events (settlement amounts, court judgments, revenue loss)

**Combined Protection**: $542.5M escrow + $250M R&W insurance + $945M purchase price adjustment cap = $1,737.5M total protection (96% of $1,800M purchase price), providing near-complete downside protection while permitting transaction to close

**Why This Structure Works**:
- Seller receives $957.5M cash at Closing (53% of purchase price), sufficient to satisfy Seller's immediate liquidity needs
- Seller has realistic path to receiving additional $542.5M (escrow releases) if regulatory outcomes are favorable (50th-75th percentile scenarios)
- Acquirer has adequate protection against 75th-90th percentile downside scenarios through escrow + insurance + purchase price adjustment
- Structure is FINANCEABLE: acquisition lenders typically permit 25-30% escrow in purchase price; $957.5M cash-at-closing can be financed with $400M-$500M equity contribution + $450M-$550M debt financing

### G. Walk-Away Scenarios

This section provides Purchaser's Board of Directors with objective decision framework for determining whether to terminate the transaction at various stages of the due diligence and pre-closing period. The analysis below synthesizes quantitative thresholds, qualitative factors, and legal termination rights to enable informed, timely decision-making.

#### Scenario 1: SEC Enforcement Action Filed Before Closing

**Trigger Event**: SEC files complaint in federal district court or institutes administrative proceeding against Company seeking disgorgement, civil monetary penalties, and injunctive relief

**Termination Rights**: Purchaser has automatic walk-away right under Section 9.1(g)(i) (Specific Regulatory Termination Events) with NO Reverse Break Fee obligation

**Quantitative Impact**:
- SEC enforcement action filing (vs. settlement) typically increases settlement amount by 30-50% due to loss of cooperation credit
- Expected settlement increases from $562.5M (base case with Wells response and settlement negotiations) to $730M-$845M (post-filing scenario)
- Litigation defense costs increase by $8M-$15M (vs. settlement negotiation costs)
- Settlement timeline extends from 12-18 months (negotiated settlement) to 24-36 months (litigated settlement)

**Qualitative Factors**:
- Enforcement action filing signals SEC's determination to pursue litigation rather than settlement, indicating more aggressive regulatory posture
- Public filing creates reputational damage to Company and Acquirer (assuming deal announced)
- Banking partners and payment processors may terminate relationships upon public enforcement action, creating existential operational threat
- Customer churn accelerates (estimated 10-15% immediate churn upon public enforcement filing vs. 3-5% from Wells Notice alone)

**Board Recommendation**: **WALK AWAY**
- SEC enforcement action filing increases expected aggregate exposure from $1,962.5M to $2,130M-$2,283M (9-16% increase)
- At 75th percentile, aggregate exposure reaches $2,500M-$2,700M (139-150% of purchase price)
- Probability of positive ROI decreases from 10% to 3-5%
- **Cost of walking away**: $15M Reverse Break Fee + $8M-$12M sunk costs = $23M-$27M total cost
- **Cost of proceeding**: Expected loss of $330M-$483M (expected aggregate exposure minus purchase price)
- **Net benefit of walking away**: $303M-$456M

**Exception**: Proceed only if SEC simultaneously offers binding settlement term sheet capping settlement at ≤$700M (unlikely in litigation context)
