## IV. DETAILED ANALYSIS

### A. New York BitLicense Gap Analysis

#### 1. Regulatory Framework: 23 NYCRR Part 200

New York's BitLicense regulation (23 NYCRR Part 200) requires any person conducting "Virtual Currency Business Activity" in or involving New York residents to obtain a license from the New York Department of Financial Services (NYDFS). The regulation became effective August 8, 2015, and covers cryptocurrency exchange, custody, and token issuance activities.

**Legal Authority:** 23 NYCRR Part 200.3(a) states: "No Person shall, without a license obtained from the superintendent as provided in this Part, engage in any Virtual Currency Business Activity."

**Source:** [N.Y. Comp. Codes R. & Regs. Tit. 23 Â§ 200.8 - Capital Requirements](https://www.law.cornell.edu/regulations/new-york/23-NYCRR-200.8); [Virtual Currency Business Licensing | Department of Financial Services](https://www.dfs.ny.gov/virtual_currency_businesses)

#### 2. Capital Requirements Under Section 200.8

Section 200.8 of 23 NYCRR Part 200 establishes capital requirements for BitLicense holders. The regulation uses a **principles-based approach** rather than a fixed formula, granting NYDFS discretionary authority to determine adequate capitalization.

**23 NYCRR Â§ 200.8(a) - Capital Maintenance Requirements:**

"Each Licensee must maintain at all times such capital in an amount and form as the superintendent determines is sufficient to ensure the financial integrity of the Licensee and its ongoing operations based on an assessment of the specific risks applicable to each Licensee."

**Factors Considered by NYDFS:**

The superintendent considers multiple factors when determining minimum capital requirements, including:

1. **Asset Composition:** Position, size, liquidity, risk exposure, and price volatility of each type of asset
2. **Liability Composition:** Size and repayment timing of each type of liability
3. **Business Activity Volume:** Actual and expected volume of Virtual Currency Business Activity
4. **Existing Regulatory Status:** Whether the licensee is already licensed under Financial Services Law, Banking Law, or Insurance Law
5. **Leverage:** Amount of leverage employed by the licensee
6. **Liquidity Position:** Overall liquidity of the entity
7. **Financial Protections:** Protections provided through trust accounts or surety bonds
8. **Customer Base:** Types of entities served
9. **Product Mix:** Types of products or services offered

**23 NYCRR Â§ 200.8(b) - Form of Capital:**

"Each Licensee shall hold capital required to be maintained in accordance with this Section in the form of cash, virtual currency, or high-quality, highly liquid, investment-grade assets, in such proportions as are acceptable to the superintendent."

**Source:** [N.Y. Comp. Codes R. & Regs. Tit. 23 Â§ 200.8](https://www.law.cornell.edu/regulations/new-york/23-NYCRR-200.8)

**CRITICAL FINDING:** The BitLicense regulation does **NOT** contain a fixed formula (such as "2% of first $5M in liabilities + 1% of liabilities above $5M") as stated in the task delegation. This appears to be a hypothetical calculation methodology. The actual regulation grants NYDFS case-by-case discretionary authority based on risk assessment.

**Capital Determination Practice:**

Based on BitLicense FAQ guidance and industry reports, NYDFS determines capital requirements during the application review process through individualized supervisory agreements. Upon BitLicense approval, NYDFS creates a detailed Supervisory Agreement that sets conditions and requirements tailored to the specific risks presented by the applicant's business model.

**Source:** [BitLicense FAQs](https://business.cch.com/bfld/BitLicenseFAQs.pdf); [BitLicense: The Ultimate Guide to New York's Crypto Regulation](https://uslawexplained.com/bitlicense)

#### 3. Capital Requirements in Practice: Industry Standards
