#### 2. Termination Provisions â€” Legal Framework

**Massachusetts Contract Law â€” At-Will Termination:**

Investment advisory agreements are services contracts governed by general Massachusetts contract law principles. Unlike employment relationships (which are presumptively at-will unless modified by contract), commercial services contracts are governed by their express terms. Pinnacle's institutional advisory agreements contain express termination-for-convenience provisions allowing either party to terminate upon 30-90 days' written notice.

**Typical Termination Clause:**
> "Either party may terminate this Agreement upon [30/60/90] days' prior written notice to the other party. Termination shall be effective as of the end of the notice period, at which time Client shall pay all fees accrued through the effective date of termination. No termination fees or penalties shall apply."

**Legal Effect [METHODOLOGY: Massachusetts contract law analysis]:**
- **No cause required:** Client need not establish breach, poor performance, or any justification
- **No penalty:** Unlike some European jurisdictions or specialized service contracts, no compensation for "termination without cause"
- **Short notice period:** 30-90 days provides minimal revenue visibility
- **Immediate revenue loss:** Upon termination, Pinnacle loses 100% of that client's management fees (no tail fees, no wind-down compensation beyond accrued fees through termination date)

**Restatement (Third) of Agency Â§ 1.01 (Fiduciary Duty Context):**
Even in at-will termination scenarios, investment advisers owe fiduciary duties to clients through the termination date. Pinnacle must:
- Continue managing assets prudently through termination effective date
- Provide orderly transition of records and account documentation
- Not abandon client (precipitous cessation could constitute fiduciary breach)
- Disclose any material conflicts of interest arising during wind-down period

**UCC Article 2 Inapplicability:**
Investment advisory services are not "goods" under UCC Â§ 2-105, so UCC Article 2's "perfect tender rule" and cure provisions do NOT apply. However, general contract principles of substantial performance and material breach remain applicable if client attempts to terminate "for cause" and Pinnacle contests.
