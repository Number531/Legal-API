### 1. CMS Staffing Minimums ‚Äî CRITICAL REGULATORY DEVELOPMENT (REPEALED)

**Background:** CMS finalized minimum staffing requirements May 10, 2024 (89 FR 40876) requiring skilled nursing facilities to provide 3.48 hours per resident day (HPRD) total nursing (RN 0.55 HPRD, CNA 2.45 HPRD) with 24/7 RN on-site presence. Sunset currently provides 3.45 HPRD (RN 0.45, LPN 0.80, CNA 2.20), creating compliance gap requiring 26 additional RN FTEs + 65 CNA FTEs at estimated cost $4.3 million annually ongoing + $344K-$800K one-time recruitment.

**DECEMBER 2025 REPEAL:** CMS published interim final rule December 3, 2025 (89 FR [TBD]) **REPEALING** the minimum staffing standards effective February 2, 2026. Repealed requirements include 3.48 HPRD total, 0.55 RN HPRD, 2.45 CNA HPRD, and 24/7 RN presence. Statutory basis: Public Law 119-21 (signed July 4, 2025) prohibited CMS from implementing/enforcing minimum staffing standards. Facility assessment requirements remain in effect (42 C.F.R. ¬ß 483.71), requiring facilities to staff based on resident acuity but without specific quantitative HPRD minimums.

**Legal Challenges Pre-Repeal:** Federal district courts in Texas (AHCA v. HHS) and Iowa (20 state AGs v. HHS) vacated the 2024 final rule May and October 2024 respectively, finding CMS exceeded statutory authority and violated Administrative Procedures Act. Trump administration DOJ initially defended rule April 2025 but subsequently repealed December 2025.

**Impact on Sunset:** Federal repeal means Sunset **NO LONGER required** to hire 91 FTEs or incur $4.3M annual staffing cost for federal compliance. **COST AVOIDANCE:** $4,326,400 annually + $344,500-$800,000 recruitment costs. Sunset can maintain current 3.45 HPRD staffing levels for Arizona (6 facilities) and Nevada (3 facilities) without federal penalty.

**California AB 1502 State Mandate (REMAINS IN EFFECT):** California Health & Safety Code ¬ß 1276.5 requires **3.5 HPRD total nursing** minimum for California skilled nursing facilities (effective July 2023), independent of federal CMS rule. Applies to Sunset's 3 California facilities (Sunrise Manor 150 beds, Orange County 145 beds, San Diego 130 beds). **Orange County Non-Compliance:** Currently 3.4 HPRD (below 3.5 minimum), must add **2.3 FTE** (CNAs or LPNs) at cost $86,112 annually. California CDPH can impose civil monetary penalties $100-$150 per resident per day for non-compliance ($435,000/month exposure if deficiency cited).

**Strategic Implications:** (1) **Positive:** Federal repeal avoids $4.3M annual cost, provides operational flexibility to staff based on facility-specific acuity. (2) **Remaining Obligations:** Orange County facility California AB 1502 compliance $86K annually, qualitative federal standard 42 C.F.R. ¬ß 483.35 "sufficient nursing staff" (F880 deficiency risk if inadequate staffing causes resident harm). (3) **Five Star Ratings:** Repeal does not eliminate Payroll-Based Journal reporting or staffing component (20% of overall rating). Sunset current 3.45 HPRD = 2.6 stars staffing component. Recommendation: **Maintain 3.45 HPRD minimum** to preserve current star ratings, avoid further degradation.

### 2. WARN Act Liability ‚Äî Orange County Facility Closure ($0-$4.5M Exposure)

**Triggering Scenario:** If Orange County facility designated full Special Focus Facility September 2025 (following March 2025 survey immediate jeopardy) ‚Üí fails to achieve substantial compliance within 18-24 months ‚Üí CMS terminates Medicare provider agreement March 2028 ‚Üí facility loses 88% revenue ($24.6M annually Medicare/Medicaid) ‚Üí closure inevitable due to financial non-viability ‚Üí **290 employees laid off** (128 residents √ó 2.27 FTE/resident industry average = nursing staff ~180, dietary/housekeeping ~60, therapists ~25, admin ~25).

**Federal WARN Act (29 U.S.C. ¬ß¬ß 2101-2109):** Sunset meets 100+ employee threshold (1,850 total employees). Orange County 290-employee layoff qualifies as **plant closing** (‚â•50 employees single site during 30-day period). **60-day advance written notice required** to: (1) affected employees, (2) California Employment Development Department, (3) City of Santa Ana chief elected official. Notice must specify closure date, job titles affected, bumping rights, company contact.

**Exceptions Unlikely to Apply:** (1) **Unforeseeable Business Circumstances:** CMS termination arguably FORESEEABLE (Orange County already SFF candidate September 2024, full SFF designation September 2025 provides 24 months advance warning of termination risk March 2028). Courts require "sudden, dramatic, unexpected" circumstances outside employer control (20 C.F.R. ¬ß 639.9(b)(1)), regulatory termination after 24-month improvement plan does NOT satisfy. (2) **Faltering Company:** Limited to plant closings where employer seeking capital/business and notice would preclude financing (not applicable to CMS regulatory termination). (3) **Natural Disaster:** Not applicable.

**Penalties for Non-Compliance:** (1) **Back Pay + Benefits:** $500/day per employee for each day of violation up to 60 days maximum. 290 employees √ó $189/day average wage √ó 60 days = $3,288,600 back pay + $464,000 benefits (health insurance 60 days) = **$3,752,600 employee liability**. (2) **Civil Penalty:** $500/day to City of Santa Ana for each day of violation, 60 days maximum = $30,000 (avoidable if employer pays all back pay/benefits to employees within 3 weeks of closure). (3) **Attorney Fees:** Prevailing employees entitled to reasonable fees, class action likely (290 employees), estimated $500K-$1M attorney fees. **TOTAL FEDERAL WARN EXPOSURE:** $3,752,600 + $30,000 + $750,000 = **$4,532,600** if ZERO days notice provided.

**California WARN Act (Cal. Labor Code ¬ß¬ß 1400-1408):** Stricter state requirements apply **in addition to** federal (no double recovery, employees elect higher recovery). California threshold 75+ employees (Sunset 1,850 meets threshold), triggering events include termination of operations (Orange County closure), 60-day notice to employees/California EDD/Local Workforce Investment Board/chief elected officials city and county. Penalties same as federal: back pay/benefits up to 60 days (or one-half days employed whichever smaller), civil penalty $500/day, attorney fees (asymmetric fee-shifting, prevailing employees recover fees but prevailing employer cannot).

**Mitigation Strategy ‚Äî ZERO LIABILITY:** **Provide full 60-day advance written notice January 2028** (60 days before March 2028 anticipated CMS termination effective date). Notice allows employees to seek new employment, file unemployment claims, coordinate with California EDD Rapid Response Services (job fairs, resume assistance, retraining programs). **Cost:** $0 WARN penalties (full compliance). **Additional Closure Costs (Not WARN-Related):** (1) Severance $825,300 (2 weeks pay per year of service typical, 290 employees √ó 3 years average tenure, voluntary not required by WARN but common practice reduces litigation). (2) Accrued PTO Payout $548,100 (California law requires vacation payout upon termination Lab. Code ¬ß 227.3). **Total Closure Costs:** $1,373,400 unavoidable regardless of WARN compliance.

**Transfer Option (Reduce WARN Headcount):** If other Sunset facilities have vacancies within reasonable commuting distance (50 miles), employees who accept transfers NOT counted as "employment loss" for WARN purposes (20 C.F.R. ¬ß 639.3(f)). San Diego facility 90 miles from Orange County (marginal commuting distance), assume 20-30 employees accept transfers. Revised WARN headcount: 290 - 25 transferred = **265 employees** subject to notice (modest reduction, still significant).

**Cross-Domain Impact:** WARN Act penalties typically **NOT covered** by Employment Practices Liability Insurance (EPLI) policies (statutory penalties excluded). Review Sunset EPLI policy, if WARN excluded, budget $3.75M-$4.5M uninsured exposure. Purchase agreement should allocate WARN liability between seller (Sunset management 2019-2025 caused SFF designation) and buyer (Silver Oak as successor employer responsible for post-closing compliance).

**Compliance Cost:** 144 California CNAs (480 total CNAs √ó 30% California allocation, 3 facilities of 12 total) √ó $4/hour average increase ($17 current midpoint ‚Üí $21 required) √ó 2,080 hours = **$1,197,120 annually**. **Enforcement:** California CDPH monitors SB 525 compliance, can impose civil monetary penalties, revoke licenses for repeated violations. Employee private right of action for unpaid wages (underpayment $16-$18 vs. required $21 = $3-$5/hour √ó 2,080 hours = $6,240-$10,400 per CNA annually √ó 144 CNAs √ó 3-year statute of limitations = **$2.7M-$4.5M class action exposure**).

**Meal/Rest Break Violations (California Class Action Risk):**

**California Labor Code Requirements:**
- **Meal Breaks (¬ß 512):** 30-minute unpaid, uninterrupted, duty-free required if shift >5 hours (second 30-minute if >10 hours). Must begin before end of 5th hour. Employee must be "relieved of all duty" (cannot respond to patient call lights, answer phones).
- **Rest Breaks (¬ß 226.7):** 10-minute paid rest break every 4 hours worked (or "major fraction thereof" >2 hours). 1 break for 4-6 hours shift, 2 breaks for 6-10 hours, 3 breaks for 10-14 hours.

**Premium Penalties:** **1 hour pay at regular rate** for EACH day employer fails to provide meal break OR rest break (2 hours if both violations same day, separate penalties not capped).

**Sunset Risk:** Understaffing 3.45 PPD prevents CNAs from taking timely duty-free meal breaks (patient call lights require response, no relief coverage during breaks ‚Üí NOT duty-free ‚Üí violation). High patient acuity/workload prevents CNAs from taking rest breaks every 4 hours.

**Class Action Exposure:**
- If systemic violations (CNAs miss breaks 2-3 days/week average due to understaffing), **30% violation rate** across 144 California CNAs
- Annual penalties: CNA $18/hour √ó 1 hour premium √ó 260 workdays √ó 30% violation rate = $1,404 per CNA/year
- **3-Year Lookback:** 144 CNAs √ó $1,404/year √ó 3 years statute of limitations (Cal. Code Civ. Proc. ¬ß 338) = **$606,528 class action exposure**
- **Ongoing Annual Penalties:** $202,176/year if violations continue

**Mitigation:** (1) Audit timekeeping records (100 California employees sample), verify meal break attestations, rest break compliance. (2) Implement mandatory relief coverage (hire **float CNAs** to cover patient care during meal/rest breaks, ensure duty-free breaks). (3) Automated meal break attestation (timekeeping system requires affirmative employee confirmation they received duty-free break, waived break, or were prevented from taking break ‚Üí documentation for defense). (4) If systemic violations discovered, consider voluntary remediation (pay owed wages/penalties proactively before class action filed, cuts off older claims).

**Arizona/Nevada Wage/Hour Compliance (LOW RISK):**

**Arizona:** Minimum wage $14.70/hour (2025), no state meal/rest break requirements (follows federal FLSA only: breaks <20 minutes paid if provided, meal breaks ‚â•30 minutes unpaid if duty-free, overtime 1.5√ó rate >40 hours/week only). Sunset CNAs $16-$18/hour **COMPLIANT** with Arizona minimum ‚úì.

**Nevada:** Minimum wage $12.00/hour (2025, two-tier system eliminated July 2024), daily overtime 1.5√ó rate for hours >8 in workday IF employee earns <$18/hour (Sunset CNAs $16-$18 subject to daily overtime), meal break 30-minute unpaid required for shifts ‚â•8 hours (Nev. ### 5. Union Organizing Risk ‚Äî SEIU-UHW ($20M Annual Labor Cost)

**Vulnerability Assessment: MODERATE-HIGH**

**Risk Factors:** (1) **Low Wages:** CNAs $16-$18/hour vs. unionized SNF CNAs $20-$25/hour under SEIU contracts (Oregon SEIU Local 503 Volare 2024-2027: CNAs $19-$24/hour experience-based), wage gap $4-$7/hour creates organizing incentive "vote yes for 25% raise." (2) **High Turnover:** 85% CNA turnover indicates workforce dissatisfaction (unions target dissatisfied workers). (3) **Understaffing:** 3.45 PPD below proposed CMS 3.5 (repealed, but employees aware), F880 deficiencies insufficient staffing cited Orange County/Desert Sun/Sunrise Manor ‚Üí employees experience excessive workload ‚Üí union leverages "vote yes, we'll negotiate safe staffing ratios." (5) **California SEIU-UHW Activity:** SEIU United Healthcare Workers West represents 100,000+ California healthcare workers, actively organizes SNFs, Sunset's 3 California facilities (Sunrise Manor San Jose, Orange County Santa Ana, San Diego) vulnerable.

**Union Organizing Process:**

**Authorization Card Drive:** SEIU organizers distribute authorization cards (physical/electronic), employees sign "I authorize SEIU-UHW to represent me for collective bargaining." **30% threshold** (294+ employees of 980 nursing staff) triggers NLRB secret ballot election petition. Timeline: 4-8 weeks card drive to election.

**NLRB Secret Ballot Election:** Bargaining unit determination (CNAs/LPNs/dietary/housekeeping grouped, RNs separate unit or excluded as supervisors if charge nurse duties), 4-6 weeks campaign period (union and employer communicate with employees, subject to NLRA restrictions). Election Day: NLRB conducts secret ballot vote at workplace, **>50% vote yes** certifies union as exclusive bargaining representative.

**Collective Bargaining:** Employer must bargain in good faith over wages, hours, working conditions (29 U.S.C. ¬ß 158(d)). First contract negotiation 6-18 months. Mandatory subjects: wages, health insurance, PTO, retirement, staffing levels, scheduling, grievance procedures, arbitration.

**Employer Unfair Labor Practices (NLRA 29 U.S.C. ¬ß 158(a)):**

**TIPS Rule (Prohibited Conduct):**
- **T**hreaten: Cannot threaten to close facility, reduce wages, eliminate benefits if union elected
- **I**nterrogate: Cannot ask employees how they plan to vote, who supports union
- **P**romise: Cannot promise wage increases/benefits conditioned on voting no (unless timing coincidental with pre-planned raises)
- **S**urveil: Cannot spy on union meetings, follow employees distributing cards

**Mitigation Strategy (Lawful Union Avoidance):**

**Pre-Emptive Retention Strategy (RECOMMENDED):** If Sunset implements wage increases ($20/hour CNAs), benefits enhancements (20 days PTO, $100/month health insurance, 3% 401k match), career development (tuition reimbursement) **BEFORE union organizing campaign begins**, reduces vulnerability:
- Employees less likely to sign authorization cards if already receiving competitive wages/benefits (union premium diminishes from $20M to $8-$10M residual)
- **Lawful:** Employer can implement pre-planned wage/benefits improvements as long as timing not motivated by union activity (NLRA permits bona fide business decisions independent of union campaign)

**Supervisor Training:** Train all DONs, ADONs, charge nurses on NLRA TIPS prohibitions (no threats, interrogation, promises, surveillance). Engage labor counsel (Jackson Lewis, Littler Mendelson specialize union avoidance) if authorization cards distributed.

**Lawful Employer Speech (29 U.S.C. ¬ß 158(c)):** Employer can express views/opinions about unions as long as no threats/promises:
- Lawful: "We value direct communication, believe union intermediary unnecessary"; "Union dues $50-$80/month reduces take-home pay"; "Union cannot guarantee wage increases, must negotiate, if no agreement could strike"
- Unlawful: "If union elected we'll close this facility" (threat); "Vote no and we'll increase wages to $20/hour" (promise conditioned on vote)

#### 1. Regulatory History and Current Status

**2024 Final Rule (89 FR 40876, May 10, 2024):**

- Example: Employee worked 90 days before layoff ‚Üí California penalty limited to 45 days (90 √∑ 2), not full 60 days

- **$500 per day** of violation (same as federal) payable to local government
- **Avoidable:** If employer pays all back pay/benefits to employees within **3 weeks** of layoff

**Attorney Fees (Cal. Labor Code ¬ß 1404):**
- **Prevailing employees** entitled to reasonable attorney fees
- **Prevailing employer** NOT entitled to attorney fees (asymmetric fee-shifting favors employees)

**CALIFORNIA WARN EXPOSURE (Orange County):**
- Same calculation as federal: **$3,752,600** maximum (back pay + benefits) + $30,000 (civil penalty) + $750,000 (attorney fees) = **$4,532,600**
- **NO DOUBLE RECOVERY:** Employees cannot collect both federal and California WARN damages for same violation, but can elect higher recovery (typically same amount, California law incorporated by reference to federal calculations)

#### 3. Meal and Rest Break Requirements

**California (Strict Meal/Rest Break Laws):**¬≥‚Åµ

**Meal Breaks (Cal. Labor Code ¬ß 512):**
- **First Meal Break:** 30 minutes unpaid, uninterrupted, duty-free required if shift **>5 hours**
  - Must begin **before end of 5th hour** of work
  - May waive by mutual consent if total shift ‚â§6 hours
- **Second Meal Break:** 30 minutes unpaid required if shift **>10 hours**
  - Must begin before end of 10th hour
  - May waive by mutual consent if total shift ‚â§12 hours AND first meal break not waived
- **Duty-Free Requirement:** Employee must be "relieved of all duty" during meal break (cannot answer calls, respond to patient needs, remain "on-call")
- **Sunset Risk:** Skilled nursing facilities often require CNAs/LPNs to respond to patient call lights during meal breaks ‚Üí **NOT duty-free** ‚Üí meal break violation

**Rest Breaks (Cal. Labor Code ¬ß 226.7):**

  - 6-10 hours shift: 2 rest breaks
  - 10-14 hours shift: 3 rest breaks
- **Timing:** Rest breaks should occur as close to middle of work period as practicable (cannot require employees to take rest break at beginning/end of shift)
- **Sunset Risk:** High patient acuity/understaffing may prevent CNAs from taking timely rest breaks ‚Üí violation

#### 3. Union Organizing Campaign Process

**Authorization Card Drive:**‚Åµ‚Å∑
- **Union Contacts Employees:** SEIU organizers distribute authorization cards (physical cards or electronic via email/text), employees sign cards stating "I authorize SEIU-UHW to represent me for collective bargaining"
- **30% Threshold for NLRB Election:** If ‚â•30% employees sign cards (294+ employees out of 980 nursing staff), union petitions NLRB for secret ballot election

**NLRB Secret Ballot Election:**‚Åµ‚Å∏

- **Election Day:** NLRB conducts secret ballot vote at workplace, employees vote yes/no for union representation
- **Majority Wins:** If >50% vote yes, union certified as exclusive bargaining representative

**Collective Bargaining:**‚Åµ‚Åπ

- **Mandatory Bargaining Subjects:** Wages, health insurance, PTO, retirement, staffing levels, scheduling, grievance procedures, arbitration
- **Management Rights:** Employer retains right to direct workforce (assign tasks, establish productivity standards), but changes to wages/benefits/working conditions require union negotiation
