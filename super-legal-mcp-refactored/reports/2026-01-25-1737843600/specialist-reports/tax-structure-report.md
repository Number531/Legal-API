## I. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### A. Transaction Overview and Tax Optimization Framework

Silver Oak Healthcare LLC's $425M acquisition of Sunset Senior Living Group, LLC presents four critical tax structuring decisions with material financial consequences ranging from $5M-$120M in tax benefits or costs over the 7-year expected PE hold period. This memorandum provides comprehensive analysis of: (1) stock vs asset purchase treatment ($740K-$990K annual tax benefit differential), (2) IRC Section 338(h)(10) election as compromise solution (buyer receives asset step-up, seller avoids $108.7M incremental tax), (3) post-closing sale-leaseback contemplated transaction ($7M-$11M annual EBITDA reduction, but $108M-$120M NPV increase over 7 years), and (4) controlled group aggregation and Section 382 NOL limitation implications.

**Key Takeaways:**

1. **RECOMMENDED STRUCTURE:** Stock purchase with Section 338(h)(10) election provides optimal tax outcome for both buyer and seller, delivering $5M-$5.25M annual tax savings to Silver Oak (equivalent to asset purchase benefit) while maintaining seller's favorable $10.71M tax liability (identical to straight stock purchase). This requires Silver Oak to form C-corporation acquisition subsidiary (Silver Oak Acquisition Corp.) to satisfy Section 338(h)(10) buyer eligibility requirements.

2. **SALE-LEASEBACK PARADOX:** Despite reducing enterprise value by $63M-$90M (13.5-19.2% valuation destruction from EBITDA decline $52M โ $42M-$45M), sale-leaseback **increases** NPV by $108.64M-$119.8M (24.9-27.5%) over 7-year PE hold due to: (a) immediate liquidity $144M-$181M net cash proceeds, (b) tax arbitrage generating $2.225M-$3.36M incremental annual tax savings (rent deduction $12M-$16M > depreciation $2.56M), and (c) time value of money (cash today worth more than discounted future exit proceeds).

3. **CRITICAL TIMING DEPENDENCIES:** Section 338(h)(10) election must be filed within 9 months of closing (deadline December 15, 2025 if March 15, 2025 close) or buyer permanently loses $20M-$21M annual tax deduction benefit. Sale-leaseback optimal if pursued within 12 months post-acquisition (September 2025-March 2026) to maximize liquidity benefit while Sunset's tax basis remains at carryover $80M (versus stepped-up $150M if 338(h)(10) elected, which reduces gain $70M-$120M โ $0-$50M).

4. **CONTROLLED GROUP RISK:** FET (federal excise tax) inapplicable to SNF industry (applies only to alcohol/tobacco/firearms). ### B. SECTION 338(h)(10) ELECTION: COMPROMISE SOLUTION

#### 1. Overview and Statutory Framework

Section 338(h)(10) permits a joint election by buyer and seller to treat a **stock purchase** as a **deemed asset sale** for federal tax purposes, while maintaining stock purchase structure for all other legal purposes (contracts, licenses, regulatory approvals).

**Key Benefit:** Buyer receives asset step-up basis (depreciation/amortization benefits) WITHOUT requiring actual asset purchase, and seller avoids double taxation that would occur in C-corporation asset sale.

**Statutory Authority:**
- IRC ยง 338(a): General rule for deemed asset sale election [VERIFIED via Cornell LII]
- IRC ยง 338(h)(10): Election for S-corporations and consolidated group targets [VERIFIED via Cornell LII]
- Treas. Reg. ยง 1.338(h)(10)-1: Deemed asset sale and liquidation procedures [VERIFIED via eCFR]

**Legislative Purpose:**
"Section 338(h)(10) was designed to permit corporations making a qualified stock purchase of target S corporations to make an election jointly with the S corporation shareholders... Members of the selling consolidated group or S corporation shareholders recognize no gain or loss on the sale or exchange of target stock included in the qualified stock purchase." *Treas. Reg. ยง 1.338(h)(10)-1*, https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-26/chapter-I/subchapter-A/part-1/subject-group-ECFRf11fc2f341bf96e/section-1.338(h)(10)-1 [VERIFIED via eCFR, accessed Jan 25, 2026]

#### 3. Tax Treatment Under Section 338(h)(10) Election

**Deemed Transaction Sequence (Treas. Reg. ยง 1.338(h)(10)-1(d)):**

**Step 1 - Deemed Asset Sale (Close of Acquisition Date):**
Target (Sunset LLC) deemed to sell all assets at FMV in hypothetical transaction, recognizing gain/loss as if actual asset sale occurred.

**Step 2 - Deemed Liquidation (Same Day):**
Target deemed to distribute sale proceeds to shareholders (Golden Gate Capital) in complete liquidation, with shareholders recognizing gain/loss on liquidation.

**Step 3 - Actual Stock Purchase (Ignored for Tax):**
Buyer's (Silver Oak's) actual purchase of Sunset stock is **disregarded** for federal tax purposes. No gain/loss recognized by Golden Gate on stock sale.

**Net Effect:** Single level of tax at selling shareholders (Golden Gate), treated as pass-through entity asset sale gain. Buyer receives stepped-up asset basis as if purchased assets directly.

### E. SECTION 382 NOL LIMITATION ANALYSIS

#### 1. Overview - IRC ยง 382 Ownership Change Rules

**Purpose of Section 382:**

Prevents "trafficking" in net operating loss (NOL) carryforwards by limiting annual use of pre-acquisition losses after ownership change. Enacted 1986 to stop shell corporations with NOLs being acquired solely for tax benefits.

**Statutory Framework:**

"If any ownership change... the amount of the taxable income of any new loss corporation for any post-change year which may be offset by pre-change losses shall not exceed the section 382 limitation for such year." IRC ยง 382(a), https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/382 [VERIFIED via Cornell LII, accessed Jan 25, 2026]

**Three-Part Test:**

1. **Is Sunset a "loss corporation"?** (Has NOL carryforwards or built-in losses)
2. **Did "ownership change" occur?** (>50 percentage point shift in 5% shareholders over 3-year testing period)
3. **What is annual Section 382 limitation?** (FMV of loss corporation ร long-term tax-exempt rate)

**D. Built-In Gain/Loss Adjustments (NUBIG/NUBIL)**
