## Technical Implementation Notes

### Parenthetical Writing Strategy:

1. **Present Participle Start**: All parentheticals begin with holding/establishing/explaining/applying/interpreting
2. **Holding Focus**: Describe what the court DECIDED, not background facts
3. **Relevance Clear**: Reader should immediately understand why case is cited
4. **Concise**: 15-30 words typical; maximum 40 words
5. **Case-Specific**: Avoid generic parentheticals like "(relevant case)" or "(on point)"

### Common Patterns Used:

| Pattern | Example | Use Case |
|---------|---------|----------|
| **holding that [legal rule]** | (holding that materiality requires showing government would not have paid) | Core legal holdings |
| **establishing [test/standard]** | (establishing "one purpose" test for AKS violations) | Seminal cases creating tests |
| **holding [fact] + [result]** | (holding physician compensation exceeding FMV violates AKS) | Fact-specific applications |
| **explaining [principle]** | (explaining that remedial statutes should be construed liberally) | Secondary authority, principles |
| **applying [law] to [facts]** | (applying WARN Act to contract-dependent business) | Analogous fact patterns |
| **interpreting [statute/reg]** | (interpreting 42 C.F.R. § 1001.952(i) employment safe harbor) | Regulatory construction |

### Avoided Anti-Patterns:

❌ **Generic parentheticals**: "(important case)" → Too vague
❌ **Fact recitals**: "(physician paid $500K for no services)" → Should focus on holding
❌ **Procedural posture**: "(affirming district court on summary judgment)" → Not relevant
❌ **Party names**: "(holding in favor of plaintiff)" → Doesn't explain legal principle
❌ **Citations within parentheticals**: "(citing Smith v. Jones)" → Too nested
