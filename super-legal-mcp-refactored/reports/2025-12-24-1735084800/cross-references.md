### C. Risk Aggregation and Non-Additivity

**Critical Finding**: Many quantified risk exposures are **non-additive** because they represent alternative scenarios rather than independent probabilities:

**Example 1: Student Refund Exposure**
- Student refund exposure ($435M-$539M, Section X) and HLC denial risk (Section I) represent the **same scenario**, not separate risks
- If HLC approves (80-85% probability), student refund exposure = $0
- If HLC denies (15-20% probability), student refund exposure = $435M-$539M
- **Correct risk aggregation**: $435M-$539M × 17.5% midpoint probability = **$76M-$94M weighted impact**
