## XI.A CONSOLIDATED RISK MATRICES AND SCENARIO ANALYSIS

### 1. Executive Risk Summary

American Education Holdings' proposed $780 million acquisition of Midwest University presents aggregate quantified risk exposure of $846M-$1.66B in worst-case scenarios, with probability-weighted expected impact of $163M-$373M across ten analyzed risk domains. The transaction's financial viability depends critically on three binary approval outcomes: (1) Higher Learning Commission change of control approval (denial creates $435M-$539M student refund catastrophe); (2) maintenance of programmatic accreditations, particularly ABA law school approval (denial eliminates $27.5M annual revenue); and (3) federal research funding transferability under nonprofit-to-for-profit conversion ($10M-$20M in non-transferable grants).

The five highest-weighted risks by expected financial impact are: (1) **Student tuition refund obligations** if HLC denies accreditation ($109M-$189M weighted impact, 60% of total risk exposure); (2) **Commercial vendor contract renegotiations** requiring 30-50% premium increases ($24.7M-$65.6M weighted); (3) **Federal research grant losses** from non-transferable NIH training grants and NSF restrictions ($22.7M-$42.8M weighted); (4) **Clinical placement agreement terminations** overlapping with accreditation loss ($28.6M-$40M weighted, non-additive to student refund); and (5) **Employment law exposure** from 680 tenured faculty contracts and pending EEOC discrimination charge ($25M-$75M most likely case).

Transaction feasibility assessment: **CONDITIONALLY VIABLE** subject to three absolute requirements that cannot be waived: (1) HLC approval obtained before closing (denies $435M-$539M catastrophic student refund exposure); (2) comprehensive title search confirms no ground lease restrictions on 350-acre core campus (denies $20M-$100M forced land acquisition or $200M-$500M campus relocation); (3) minimum 75% vendor consent rate for material contracts representing $49.4M annual spend. Expected aggregate risk costs of $163M-$373M (21-48% of $780M purchase price) justify immediate purchase price reduction of $100M-$150M, with additional $100M-$150M held in escrow for 18-24 months covering regulatory approval contingencies and successor liability tail exposure.

4. **Stakeholder Engagement**: Alumni councils, faculty advisory boards, student representation demonstrating responsive governance
5. **Performance Metrics**: Publish annual reports showing educational outcome improvements (graduation rates, job placement, graduate school admission) proving quality maintenance

**Expected Mitigation Effectiveness:** 40-60% reduction in stigma impact, lowering aggregate exposure to **$27M-$105M** (still material but manageable).

**Key Observations:**

### 5. Recommended Transaction Structure Adjustments

Based on consolidated risk analysis revealing $163M-$373M expected exposure (21-48% of purchase price), the following transaction structure adjustments are recommended to align risk allocation with economic reality:

**Purchase Price Reduction:**
- Original price: $780M
- Recommended: $680M-$730M (7-13% discount)
- Rationale: Reflects probability-weighted costs of $102.5M-$121M tax burden (5-year), $59.5M-$95M athletics losses (5-year), $45M-$77M research funding decline (5-year), and $27M-$105M stigma-related enrollment pressure
- Alternative: Maintain $780M price but include **earnout structure** where $50M-$100M contingent on achieving Base Case scenario outcomes (HLC approval within 14 months, 90/10 compliance Year 3-5, vendor consent >75%)

**Escrow Structure:**
- **Tier 1 Escrow - Regulatory Approval ($50M-$75M, 18-24 months):**
  - Covers student refund obligations if HLC denies ($109M-$189M weighted exposure)
  - Covers federal loan discharge recoupment if Title IV suspended ($56M-$115M weighted)
  - Release contingent on: (a) HLC final approval without show-cause orders, (b) Title IV recertification complete, (c) composite score >1.5 maintained for 18 months

  - Covers vendor contract renegotiation premiums exceeding 15% baseline
  - Covers insurance premium increases exceeding 35% baseline
  - Covers first-year Title IX/EEOC litigation settlements
  - Release contingent on: (a) 75% vendor consent achieved, (b) insurance renewals obtained, (c) Chen EEOC charge resolved

- **Tier 3 Escrow - Successor Liability Tail ($25M-$50M, 36 months):**
  - Covers environmental liabilities not discovered in Phase II assessments
  - Covers student litigation claims arising from pre-closing conduct
  - Covers employee claims (WARN Act, discrimination, tenure breach) related to pre-closing actions
  - Release: 3-year tail period matching statute of limitations for most claims

**Total Escrow:** $100M-$175M (13-22% of purchase price), representing middle-ground between Base Case ($163M expected) and Downside Case ($600M+ exposure)

**Debt Structure:**
- Original: $585M (75% LTV) at 8.5% interest
- Recommended: $475M-$525M (65-70% LTV) at 9.0-9.5% interest
- Covenant modifications:
  - **Accreditation maintenance covenant**: Event of default if HLC, ABA, or AACSB revoked
  - **Title IV compliance covenant**: Quarterly certification of 90/10 ratio and composite score >1.5
  - **Capital expenditure minimum**: $20M annually to maintain facilities supporting accreditation standards
  - **Restricted payments**: No dividends if composite score <2.0 or enrollment declines >10% year-over-year

**Closing Conditions (Absolute):**
