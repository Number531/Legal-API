**Medicare Home Health PDGM** (Patient-Driven Groupings Model, implemented January 2020):

**Replaced 60-Day Episodic Payment**:
- **Old system**: 60-day episodes (regardless of visits provided, one payment per 60-day episode, therapy thresholds 6/14/20 visits created upcoding incentive)
- **New PDGM**: 30-day payment periods (two 30-day periods replace one 60-day episode, more frequent assessment/payment, case-mix adjusted based on functional impairment not therapy)

**PDGM Case-Mix Variables**:

**1. Timing**: Early (first/only 30-day period of sequence) vs. Late (second+ 30-day period following early), early periods paid ~$400-$500 higher than late reflects higher resource utilization early in episode

**2. Admission Source**: Community (patient admitted from home/community setting) vs. Institutional (discharged from hospital/SNF/IRF inpatient rehabilitation within 14 days), institutional source paid ~$200-$300 higher reflects post-acute higher acuity

**3. Clinical Grouping**: MMTA (Musculoskeletal/MS-Rehab/Neuro-Rehab/Complex/Behavioral) based on principal diagnosis ICD-10 code:
- **MS-Rehab**: Musculoskeletal rehabilitation (orthopedic surgery, fractures, joint replacement, arthritis, most common 35% Gentle Transitions patients)
- **Neuro-Rehab**: Neurological rehabilitation (stroke, Parkinson's, MS, neuropathy, 25% patients)
- **Complex**: Complex nursing (wounds, IV therapy, diabetes management, COPD, CHF, 20% patients)
- **MMTA**: Medical management, teaching, assessment (routine chronic disease management, 15% patients)
- **Behavioral**: Mental health/substance abuse (rare in home health, 5% patients)

**4. Functional Impairment**: Low/Medium/High based on OASIS items ADL bathing/dressing/toileting/transferring/grooming/eating + cognitive function, high impairment paid ~$300-$500 more than low

**5. Comorbidity Adjustment**: Low/Medium/High tier based on secondary diagnoses, high comorbidity paid ~$150-$300 more, examples diabetes + hypertension + CKD = medium/high tier

**LUPA Threshold** (Low Utilization Payment Adjustment):
- **Threshold**: 2-6 visits per 30-day period (varies by clinical grouping, MS-Rehab 6 visits, Neuro-Rehab 6, Complex 4, MMTA 2)
- **LUPA payment**: If visits < threshold, agency receives per-visit payment instead of case-mix adjusted 30-day payment (per-visit $60-$150 vs. 30-day payment $1,500-$3,000, significant reduction)
- **Risk**: LUPA payments unprofitable (per-visit doesn't cover overhead/coordination/OASIS assessment, agencies target visits ≥ threshold to avoid LUPA)

**Gentle Transitions OASIS Accuracy Issues**:

**OIG Audit** (October 2023, randomly selected):
- **Audit scope**: Medicare Payment Advisory Commission MedPAC contracts with OIG to audit home health agencies OASIS accuracy, random sample 30 patients Jacksonville home health, compare OASIS submissions to clinical documentation visit notes
- **Findings**:

**STARK Law Analysis**:

**42 U.S.C. § 1395nn** — Physician self-referral prohibition:
- **DHS**: Home health services are "designated health services" under STARK (if physician has financial relationship with home health agency and refers Medicare patients, STARK violation unless exception applies)
- **Financial relationship**: Dr. Mitchell owns 15% equity + receives $1.44M annually medical director fees (compensation arrangement)
- **Referrals**: Dr. Mitchell refers Medicare patients to Gentle Transitions agencies (cardiology patients post-MI/CHF/cardiac surgery need home health monitoring/education/cardiac rehab, estimated 180 referrals annually from Dr. Mitchell to Gentle Transitions)

**Exceptions**:
- **Whole hospital exception**: § 1395nn(d)(3) (ownership in whole hospital, physician authorized to perform services, ownership proportional to services provided), BUT home health agencies are NOT hospitals, exception does NOT apply ✗
- **Rural provider exception**: § 1395nn(d)(2) (ownership in rural provider <50 beds, substantially all services to rural residents), Gentle Transitions in metro Atlanta/Jacksonville/Tampa/Savannah/Augusta/Charleston not rural, exception does NOT apply ✗
- **Employment exception**: § 1395nn(e)(2) (bona fide employment, compensation FMV not based on referrals), Dr. Mitchell is independent contractor not employee, exception does NOT apply ✗
- **Personal services exception**: § 1395nn(e)(3) (written agreement 1-year term, compensation set in advance FMV covers all services, not based on referrals), Dr. Mitchell medical director contracts arguably meet requirements if FMV and not based on referrals

**FMV Determination**:
- **Medical director fees**: $15K/month × 8 agencies = $120K/month = $1.44M annually (high, typical medical director home health $5K-$10K/month per agency, $40K-$80K annually, Dr. Mitchell $1.44M ÷ 8 = $180K per agency significantly above market)
- **Benchmark**: Sullivan Cotter surveys home health medical directors $60K-$100K annually depending on agency size/responsibilities, Dr. Mitchell $180K per agency 80-200% above benchmark
- **Conclusion**: Compensation likely exceeds FMV (even if Dr. Mitchell works 40 hours/month per agency = 320 hours/month total, $1.44M ÷ 320 = $4,500/hour implausible, more likely compensation inflated to disguise referral payments)

- **2020**: PE acquired 85%, Dr. Mitchell retained 15% + medical director, referred 200 patients (slight decline)
- **2021-2024**: Referrals averaged 180/year (stable, Dr. Mitchell continues to refer significant volume, financial relationship 15% equity + $1.44M fees)

**STARK Violation Likely**: Dr. Mitchell has financial relationship (ownership + compensation) + refers Medicare patients + no applicable exception (personal services exception questionable if compensation exceeds FMV, may be disguised referral payments)
